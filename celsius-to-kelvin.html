The Formula Explained

K = °C + 273.15

Glossary of Terms

  • Celsius (°C): A scale and unit of measurement for temperature where 0 °C is the freezing point of water.
  • Kelvin (K): The base unit of temperature in the International System of Units (SI), where 0 K is absolute zero.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Example: Convert 25°C to Kelvin.

Using the formula: K = 25 + 273.15 = 298.15 K

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the Kelvin scale?

The Kelvin scale is an absolute temperature scale that starts at absolute zero. It is primarily used in scientific contexts.

Why add 273.15 to Celsius to get Kelvin?

The offset of 273.15 is used to make the Kelvin scale align with the Celsius scale, with 0 °C being equivalent to 273.15 K.

Is Kelvin used outside of scientific contexts?

Kelvin is mainly used in scientific fields like physics and chemistry because it is an absolute scale.
