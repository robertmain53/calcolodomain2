The Formula Explained

We present results in today’s dollars using real returns.

Real rate from nominal return r and inflation i:

$$ r_{\mathrm{real}} = \frac{1+r}{1+i} - 1 $$

Accumulation phase (monthly compounding, contributions at end of month):

$$ \text{NestEgg} = PV\,(1+r_m)^n \;+\; PMT \cdot \frac{(1+r_m)^n - 1}{r_m} $$

where r_m is monthly real rate, n is number of months.

Retirement phase (annual withdrawals W at end of year, N years, real return R):

$$ \text{RequiredNestEgg} = W \cdot \frac{1 - (1+R)^{-N}}{R} $$

Rearranged for sustainable withdrawal given a nest egg:

$$ W = \text{NestEgg} \cdot \frac{R}{1 - (1+R)^{-N}} $$

Glossary of Variables

  • PV — Current retirement savings (today’s dollars).
  • PMT — Total monthly contributions (you + employer) in today’s dollars.
  • r — Nominal annual return before or after retirement.
  • i — Annual inflation rate.
  • r_m — Monthly real return during accumulation.
  • n — Number of months until retirement.
  • R — Annual real return during retirement.
  • N — Number of years in retirement.
  • W — Annual withdrawal from savings (excludes Social Security).

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Inputs: Age 35 → retire at 67; years in retirement 30; savings $50,000; you save $600/mo and employer adds $200/mo; returns 6% before, 4% after; inflation 2.5%; desired income $60,000 (today’s $); Social Security $24,000/yr.

Real return before: r_real = (1+0.06)/(1+0.025)−1 ≈ 3.41%. Monthly real r_m ≈ (1+0.0341)^(1/12)−1.

Months to retirement n = 32 × 12 = 384. PMT = 800.

Compute NestEgg via the FV formula above. Retirement real return R ≈ (1+0.04)/(1+0.025)−1 ≈ 1.46%.

Your desired portfolio withdrawal is W = $60,000 − $24,000 = $36,000. RequiredNestEgg = W × [1 − (1+R)^(−30)] / R.

Compare projected NestEgg to RequiredNestEgg. If short, solve for PMT that meets the target using the accumulation formula.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Are results shown in today’s dollars?

Yes. We convert nominal returns to real returns using your inflation input, so projections reflect today’s purchasing power.

Do you include taxes?

No. Taxes vary widely. Consider modeling taxes separately or consult a tax professional.

How accurate is Social Security estimation?

Our estimate is a simplified replacement-rate approach. For accuracy, use official SSA estimators and statements.

What if I plan to increase contributions over time?

Because the tool uses real dollars, increasing contributions with inflation is implicitly handled. For step-ups beyond inflation, increase the monthly contribution input accordingly.

Is the “recommended monthly contribution” my amount or total?

The recommendation shows your personal monthly contribution after accounting for your employer’s monthly match.

Which return should I use in retirement?

Use a conservative assumption reflecting a more defensive portfolio and sequence-of-returns risk (e.g., 3–4% nominal).

Does this tool factor market volatility?

No stochastic modeling is performed. It’s a deterministic projection. Consider scenario testing with different return assumptions.
