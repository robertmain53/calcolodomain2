Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the formulas and data provided by the Pet Nutrition Alliance. All calculations strictly adhere to these guidelines.

The Formula Explained

\( \text{Daily Calories} = 70 \times (\text{Weight in kg}^{0.75}) \times \text{Activity Level} \)

Glossary of Variables

  • Weight (kg): The dog's weight in kilograms.
  • Activity Level: A multiplier based on the dog's daily activity.
  • Calories per day: The number of calories your dog needs daily.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For a 20 kg dog with normal activity level:

\( \text{Daily Calories} = 70 \times (20^{0.75}) \times 1.8 = 1,099 \) calories.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How accurate is this calculator?

This calculator uses standard formulas that are widely accepted in veterinary nutrition. However, individual needs can vary.

Can I use this calculator for puppies?

This calculator is designed for adult dogs. Puppies have different caloric needs.

What should I do if my dog is overweight?

Consult with your veterinarian to create a tailored weight management plan.

Why is my dog's activity level important?

Activity level significantly affects caloric needs. A more active dog requires more calories.

What if my dog has health issues?

Always consult a veterinarian for advice tailored to your dog's health conditions.
