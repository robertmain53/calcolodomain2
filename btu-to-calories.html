Glossary of Terms

British Thermal Unit (BTU)
A traditional unit of heat energy used in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning industries. One BTU is the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit.
Calorie (cal)
A unit of energy commonly used in chemistry and nutrition. The thermochemical calorie is defined as exactly 4.184 joules.
Energy
The capacity to do work or produce heat, measured in various units including calories, BTU, joules, and kilowatt-hours.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a calorie?

A calorie is a unit of energy commonly used in chemistry and nutrition. The thermochemical calorie is defined as exactly 4.184 joules. Note that food "Calories" (with capital C) are actually kilocalories, equal to 1000 thermochemical calories.

How do I convert BTU to calories?

Simply divide the number of BTU by 0.00396567 to get the equivalent value in calories. Our converter does this calculation instantly for you.

Why is this conversion important?

This conversion is crucial for scientific calculations and engineering applications that require precise energy measurements, especially when working with different measurement systems or international standards.

What are common uses for calories?

Calories are commonly used in chemistry, nutrition science, and thermodynamics. They are particularly useful for measuring heat energy in chemical reactions and food energy content.

Who can benefit from this tool?

Engineers, scientists, chemists, nutritionists, students, and anyone working with energy measurements in different unit systems can benefit from this accurate conversion tool.
