Glossary

Bar
A metric unit of pressure equal to 100,000 pascals. While not an SI unit, it is widely used in meteorology, engineering, and industry.
PSI (Pounds per Square Inch)
A unit of pressure in the imperial system, commonly used in the United States for tire pressure, hydraulic systems, and various industrial applications.
Pressure
The force applied perpendicular to the surface of an object per unit area.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a bar?

Bar is a metric unit of pressure, not part of the International System of Units (SI), but widely used in industry. It equals 100,000 pascals or approximately 14.5 PSI.

What is PSI?

PSI stands for Pounds per Square Inch, a unit of pressure used in the U.S. customary units. It's commonly used for tire pressure, hydraulic pressure, and compressed air systems.

How accurate is this conversion?

This conversion uses the standard conversion factor of 14.5038, ensuring high accuracy for most practical applications. Results are displayed to two decimal places.

Can I convert PSI back to bar?

Yes, by dividing the PSI value by 14.5038, you can convert back to bar. Use our PSI to Bar converter for this reverse conversion.

Why is converting pressure units important?

Different industries and countries use different units; converting ensures compatibility and compliance with local standards. Engineers often need to convert between metric and imperial units.
