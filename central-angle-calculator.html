The Formula Explained

From arc length \(s\): \(\displaystyle \theta = \frac{s}{r}\)

From chord length \(c\): \(\displaystyle \theta = 2\arcsin\!\left(\frac{c}{2r}\right)\)

From sector area \(A\): \(\displaystyle \theta = \frac{2A}{r^{2}}\)

From arc percentage \(p\) (0–1): \(\displaystyle \theta = 2\pi p\)

Convert to degrees: \(\displaystyle \theta_{^\circ} = \theta_{rad}\cdot \frac{180}{\pi}\)

Glossary of Variables

  • r: circle radius (> 0)
  • s: arc length (≥ 0)
  • c: chord length (0 ≤ c ≤ 2r)
  • A: sector area (0 ≤ A ≤ πr²)
  • p: fraction of the full circumference (0 ≤ p ≤ 1); if you enter percent, p = % / 100
  • θ: central angle (in radians unless otherwise noted)

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Given: radius r = 8 m, arc length s = 6.28 m.

  1. Use \( \theta = \dfrac{s}{r} \Rightarrow \theta = \dfrac{6.28}{8} = 0.785\ \text{rad} \) (approx.).
  2. Convert to degrees: \( 0.785 \times \dfrac{180}{\pi} \approx 45^\circ \).

Result: θ ≈ 45° (0.785 rad).

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the angle output always shown in both degrees and radians?

Yes. You can choose a preferred unit, but the calculator displays both for clarity.

What if my chord is larger than the diameter?

The input is invalid; a chord cannot exceed 2r. The tool flags this with an error message.

Can I enter mixed units (e.g., cm for radius and m for arc)?

Avoid mixing units. Convert to the same unit before calculating to ensure correctness.

Does the calculator use degrees or radians internally?

All core formulas are in radians; degrees are provided via conversion.

What range of angles does the chord formula cover?

For c ∈ [0, 2r], the formula computes θ ∈ [0, π]. Larger angles correspond to the reflex arc; use arc length or percent if you need θ > π.
