APR is computed by solving for the annual rate that discounts payments against the net amount received (loan amount minus up-front fees).

How APR is calculated

With monthly compounding, let $P$ be principal, nominal annual rate $r$, monthly rate $i=r/12$, term $n$ (months), up-front fees $F$, and net proceeds $P_\mathrm{net}=P-F$.

  • Monthly payment: $PMT = P \\cdot \\dfrac{i}{1-(1+i)^{-n}}$
  • APR (effective, incl. fees): find annual $r_\\mathrm{APR}$ such that the present value of payments equals $P_\\mathrm{net}$: $$P_\\mathrm{net} = \\sum_{k=1}^{n} \\dfrac{PMT}{\\left(1+\\dfrac{r_\\mathrm{APR}}{12}\\right)^k}$$ Solve numerically for $r_\\mathrm{APR}$ and report as a percentage.

Tips

  • Higher fees raise APR even if the nominal rate is unchanged.
  • Longer terms reduce the fee impact on APR (fees amortize over more months).
  • Use APR for apples-to-apples comparisons between offers.

FAQ

Does APR include ongoing fees? Usually only up-front, but regulations vary. Always check disclosure.
