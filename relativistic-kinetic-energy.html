The Formula Explained

\[ KE = (\gamma - 1) \cdot m \cdot c^2 \] where \(\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}\)

Glossary of Terms

  • Mass (m): The mass of the object in kilograms.
  • Velocity (v): The velocity of the object in meters per second.
  • Speed of Light (c): Approximately \(3 \times 10^8\) m/s.
  • Relativistic Kinetic Energy (KE): The energy of the object due to its motion at relativistic speeds.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why does kinetic energy increase with speed?

As an object moves faster, it gains more energy due to its motion, which is reflected in its kinetic energy.

What is the significance of relativistic kinetic energy?

Relativistic kinetic energy becomes significant when dealing with speeds approaching the speed of light, where traditional Newtonian physics no longer apply.
