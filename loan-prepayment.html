How the prepayment calculation works

We assume you make a one-time lump-sum payment at the selected payment number and continue with the original monthly payment. The calculator recomputes the remaining number of payments using the standard amortization formula.

Monthly payment: \( P = \dfrac{L \cdot r}{1 - (1+r)^{-n}} \)

Remaining term after prepayment: \( n' = \dfrac{\ln \left(\dfrac{P}{P - L' r}\right)}{\ln(1+r)} \)

Where \( L \) is the original balance, \( L' \) the balance after prepayment, \( r \) the monthly rate, and \( P \) the unchanged payment.

Why make a lump-sum prepayment?

  • Reduce total interest paid over the life of the loan.
  • Eliminate monthly payments earlier to free up cash flow.
  • Prepare for selling or refinancing by shrinking the outstanding balance.

Tips before you prepay

  • Check for prepayment penalties or fees in your loan agreement.
  • Confirm the lender applies prepayments to principal immediately.
  • Consider saving for an emergency fund before committing large sums to debt payoff.

Frequently asked questions

Does a prepayment lower my required payment?

Most loans keep the scheduled payment the same and shorten the payoff period. Some lenders allow you to request a recast that recalculates a lower payment.

What if I can’t make the scheduled payment after prepaying?

Prepayments do not obligate you to pay extra each month. You continue making your standard payment; the loan simply finishes sooner.

How soon should I make the lump-sum?

Early prepayments produce the largest savings because more of the remaining balance is subject to interest. Use the “payment number” field to test different timings.
