How It Works

Watts measure power (energy per unit time) in the SI system, while BTU per hour measures thermal power in the imperial system. The conversion factor 3.41214 BTU/hr per watt is derived from the relationship between joules per second (watts) and British Thermal Units per hour. This conversion is essential when comparing electrical power to heating or cooling capacity.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert watts to BTU/hr?

To convert watts to BTU/hr, multiply the number of watts by 3.41214. For example, a 1500-watt heater produces 5118.21 BTU/hr of heat.

Why is this conversion important?

This conversion is important for comparing electrical power consumption with HVAC capacity. Air conditioners and heaters are often rated in BTU/hr, while electrical consumption is measured in watts.

How many BTU/hr is 1 watt?

1 watt equals 3.41214 BTU/hr. This means a 1000-watt device produces 3412.14 BTU/hr of thermal energy.

What's the difference between BTU and BTU/hr?

BTU (British Thermal Unit) is a unit of energy, while BTU/hr is a unit of power (energy per time). BTU measures total energy, BTU/hr measures the rate of energy transfer, similar to joules vs. watts.
