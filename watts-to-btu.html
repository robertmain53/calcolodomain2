Glossary

Watt (W)
The SI unit of power, equal to one joule per second. Named after James Watt, it measures the rate of energy transfer or consumption.
BTU/hr (British Thermal Unit per hour)
A unit of power commonly used in HVAC to measure heating and cooling capacity. It represents the energy transfer rate in BTU per hour.
Power
The rate at which energy is transferred or converted. Measured in watts (SI) or BTU/hr (imperial). Different from energy, which is measured in joules or BTU.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert Watts to BTU/hr?

To convert Watts to BTU/hr, multiply the number of Watts by 3.41214. This gives you the equivalent power in BTU per hour.

Why is this conversion important?

This conversion is important for comparing electrical power consumption with HVAC capacity, sizing air conditioners, and understanding equipment specifications that may use different units.

How many BTU/hr is 1 Watt?

One Watt equals 3.41214 BTU/hr. This means a 1,000-watt heater produces 3,412.14 BTU/hr of heat.

Can I convert BTU/hr back to Watts?

Yes, divide the BTU/hr value by 3.41214 to convert back to Watts. For example, 3,412.14 BTU/hr Ã· 3.41214 = 1,000 Watts.

What's the difference between BTU and BTU/hr?

BTU is a unit of energy, while BTU/hr is a unit of power (energy per time). BTU measures total energy, BTU/hr measures the rate of energy transfer, just like joules vs. watts.
