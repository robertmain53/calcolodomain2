The Formula Explained

Magnitude: \( |z| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2} \)

Phase: \( \theta = \tan^{-1}(\frac{b}{a}) \)

Glossary of Terms

  • Real Part: The real component of the complex number.
  • Imaginary Part: The imaginary component of the complex number.
  • Magnitude: The distance from the origin to the point in the complex plane.
  • Phase: The angle with the positive real axis.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a complex number?

A complex number is a number that comprises a real and an imaginary part.

How do I calculate the magnitude?

The magnitude is calculated using the formula: \( |z| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2} \).
