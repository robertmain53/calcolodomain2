The Formula Explained

The vertical jump is calculated using the formula: Vertical Jump = Jump Height - Standing Reach.

Glossary of Terms

  • Standing Reach: The height you can reach with one arm while standing flat-footed.
  • Jump Height: The maximum height you can reach with one arm while jumping.
  • Vertical Jump: The difference between your jump height and standing reach.

Example: How It Works

Suppose your standing reach is 80 inches and your jump height is 100 inches. Your vertical jump would be calculated as follows:

Vertical Jump = 100 - 80 = 20 inches

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How do I measure my standing reach?

Stand flat-footed against a wall and reach as high as possible with one arm. Measure the height from the ground to your fingertips.

What is a good vertical jump height?

A good vertical jump height varies by sport and athlete. Generally, 20 inches is a good benchmark for recreational athletes.

How can I improve my vertical jump?

Improving your vertical jump can be achieved through strength training, plyometrics, and proper technique.

Does height affect vertical jump?

Height does not directly affect your vertical jump, but longer limbs may give an advantage in reach.

Why is vertical jump important?

Vertical jump is a key indicator of explosive power, important in many sports such as basketball and volleyball.
