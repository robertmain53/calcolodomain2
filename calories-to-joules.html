Formula

The formula to convert calories to joules is:

\[ \text{Joules} = \text{Calories} \times 4.184 \]

One calorie (small calorie or gram calorie) is equal to 4.184 joules. Note: This is different from the dietary Calorie (capital C), which equals 1,000 small calories or 4,184 joules.

Data Source

All calculations are based on the standard thermochemical calorie where 1 cal = 4.184 J. This conversion is defined by international standards and is widely used in chemistry, physics, and nutritional science.

Glossary

Calorie (cal)
A unit of energy equal to the amount of heat needed to raise the temperature of 1 gram of water by 1°C. Also called a small calorie or gram calorie.
Dietary Calorie (Cal or kcal)
A kilocalorie, equal to 1,000 small calories. This is the unit used on food labels. 1 Cal = 1,000 cal = 4,184 J.
Joule (J)
The SI unit of energy. One joule equals the work done when a force of one newton moves an object one meter.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why convert calories to joules?

Joules are the standard unit of energy in the International System of Units (SI), making them more widely applicable in scientific contexts including physics, chemistry, and engineering.

What is the difference between a calorie and a joule?

A calorie is a unit of energy commonly used in food and nutrition, whereas a joule is the SI unit used in physics and engineering. They measure the same thing (energy) but in different units.

How accurate is this converter?

Our converter uses the precise conversion factor of 4.184 joules per calorie, ensuring high accuracy for all conversions. Results are displayed to two decimal places.

Can I convert joules back to calories?

Yes, simply divide the number of joules by 4.184 to convert back to calories. For example, 418.4 J ÷ 4.184 = 100 cal.

What about dietary Calories (kcal)?

Dietary Calories (with capital C) are kilocalories. To convert dietary Calories to joules, multiply by 4,184 instead of 4.184. Or multiply by 1,000 first to get small calories, then use this converter.
