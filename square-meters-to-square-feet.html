The Formula Explained

\( \text{sq ft} = \text{sq m} \times 10.7639 \)

Where sq m is the area in square meters and sq ft is the area in square feet.

Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the standard conversion rate: 1 square meter = 10.7639 square feet. This is the internationally recognized conversion factor for area measurements.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For example, to convert 50 square meters to square feet:

  1. Start with the formula: \( \text{sq ft} = \text{sq m} \times 10.7639 \)
  2. Substitute 50 for sq m: \( \text{sq ft} = 50 \times 10.7639 \)
  3. Calculate: \( \text{sq ft} = 538.195 \)

Result: 50 sq m = 538.20 sq ft

Glossary of Terms

  • Square Meters (sq m or m²): A metric unit of area measurement. One square meter is the area of a square with sides of one meter.
  • Square Feet (sq ft or ft²): An imperial/US customary unit of area measurement. One square foot is the area of a square with sides of one foot.
  • Conversion Factor: The fixed number (10.7639) used to convert square meters to square feet.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why convert square meters to square feet?

Square feet are commonly used in real estate and construction in the United States, while most of the world uses square meters. This conversion is essential for international property transactions and construction projects.

How many square feet are in one square meter?

One square meter equals approximately 10.7639 square feet. This means a square meter is slightly larger than 10 square feet.

Is this tool accurate?

Yes, this calculator uses the standard conversion factor (10.7639) and displays results to two decimal places, providing accuracy suitable for real estate, construction, and everyday applications.

Can I convert square feet back to square meters?

Yes! To convert square feet back to square meters, divide the square feet value by 10.7639. For example: 1000 sq ft ÷ 10.7639 = 92.90 sq m.

What's the difference between square meters and meters squared?

They mean the same thing! "Square meters" and "meters squared" both refer to m², a unit of area. The notation m² indicates a two-dimensional measurement.
