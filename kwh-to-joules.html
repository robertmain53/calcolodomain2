Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are rigorously based on the conversion factor: 1 kWh = 3,600,000 J. For more information, refer to the official energy conversion guidelines.

The Formula Explained

The conversion formula used is: Joules = Kilowatt-hours × 3,600,000

Glossary of Variables

  • Kilowatt-hours (kWh): A measure of electrical energy equivalent to a power consumption of 1,000 watts for 1 hour.
  • Joules (J): A derived unit of energy in the International System of Units.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a kilowatt-hour (kWh)?

A kilowatt-hour is a measure of energy representing one kilowatt (1,000 watts) of power used for one hour.

How is the conversion from kWh to Joules done?

The conversion is performed using the factor: 1 kWh equals 3,600,000 Joules.

Why do I need to convert energy units?

Different applications and regions use different energy units, so conversion is necessary for global standardization and understanding.
