Glossary of Terms

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a light-year?

A light-year is the distance that light travels in a vacuum in one year, approximately 5.878625 trillion miles.

Why use light-years instead of miles?

Light-years are used to measure astronomical distances because they are more practical for the vast scales involved in space.

How accurate is this conversion?

The conversion is based on the exact distance light travels in a year, providing a high level of accuracy.
