The Formula Explained

\[ \text{Volume} = \text{Height} \times \text{Length} \times \text{Depth} \] \[ \text{Weight} = \text{Volume} \times \text{Stone Density} \]

Glossary of Variables

  • Height: The vertical dimension of the wall.
  • Length: The horizontal dimension of the wall.
  • Depth: The thickness of the wall.
  • Stone Density: The mass per unit volume of the stones used.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For a wall with a height of 2 meters, a length of 5 meters, and a depth of 0.5 meters using stones with a density of 1600 kg/m3:

  • Step 1: Calculate Volume = 2 × 5 × 0.5 = 5 m3
  • Step 2: Calculate Weight = 5 × 1600 = 8000 kg

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a gabion wall?

A gabion wall is a retaining structure made of wire mesh containers filled with rock, concrete, or sometimes sand and soil.

How do gabion walls work?

Gabion walls stabilize slopes and prevent erosion through their weight and flexibility.

What materials are used in gabion walls?

Gabion walls typically use rocks, concrete, or other sturdy materials to fill the wire mesh containers.

How long do gabion walls last?

With proper design and materials, gabion walls can last over 50 years.

Are gabion walls environmentally friendly?

Yes, they are often considered eco-friendly due to their permeability and use of natural materials.
