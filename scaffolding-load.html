The Formula Explained

The load capacity formula used in this calculator is:

\( \text{Load Capacity} = \text{Base Area} \times \text{Material Strength} \)

Glossary of Terms

  • Scaffold Height: The vertical height of the scaffolding structure.
  • Scaffold Width: The horizontal width of the scaffolding structure.
  • Material Type: The type of material used for the scaffolding, affecting its load-bearing capacity.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For a scaffold of 5m height and 2m width made of steel, the load capacity is calculated as:

  1. Calculate the base area: \(5 \times 2 = 10 \, \text{m}^2\)
  2. Apply material strength factor for steel: \(10 \times 3000 = 30000 \, \text{kg}\)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the load capacity of scaffolding?

The load capacity depends on the scaffold type and the materials used.

How is scaffolding load calculated?

It is calculated based on the weight it needs to support, including workers, tools, and materials.
