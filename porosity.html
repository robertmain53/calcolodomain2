The Formula Explained

Porosity \( \phi \) = \(\frac{{\text{Bulk Volume} - \text{Grain Volume}}}{\text{Bulk Volume}} \times 100\)

Glossary of Terms

  • Bulk Volume: The total volume of the sample including grains and pore spaces.
  • Grain Volume: The volume of the solid grains within the sample, excluding pore spaces.
  • Porosity: The percentage of the bulk volume that is pore space.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Consider a sample with a bulk volume of 100 cc and a grain volume of 70 cc. The porosity is calculated as follows:

Porosity \( \phi \) = \(\frac{100 - 70}{100} \times 100 = 30\%\)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is porosity?

Porosity is a measure of the void spaces in a material, expressed as a percentage of the total volume.

Why is porosity important?

Porosity is crucial for determining the storage capacity of reservoirs and the movement of fluids through geological formations.

How do I measure bulk and grain volumes?

Bulk volume can be measured using methods such as water displacement, while grain volume can be measured by drying and weighing the sample.

Can porosity be greater than 100%?

No, porosity is a fraction of the bulk volume and cannot exceed 100%.

What factors affect porosity?

Factors such as grain size, shape, and packing can influence the porosity of a material.
