The Formula Explained

\[ \text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} \]

Glossary of Variables

  • Distance: The total distance traveled in kilometers.
  • Time: The total time taken in hours.
  • Speed: The average speed calculated as distance divided by time.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For instance, if you cycle 30 kilometers in 2 hours, your average speed is calculated as follows:

\[ \text{Speed} = \frac{30 \text{ km}}{2 \text{ hours}} = 15 \text{ km/h} \]
