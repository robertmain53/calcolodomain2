How this rebar weight calculator works

This tool computes rebar weight from the bar diameter, length, quantity, and steel density. It supports:

  • US bar sizes (#3, #4, #5, #6, #7, #8, #9, #10, #11, #14, #18)
  • Metric diameters (8–40 mm and more)
  • Custom diameters if your bar size is not listed

Rebar weight formula

The weight of a steel bar is based on its volume and the density of steel:

In SI units

Diameter in meters: \( d \) (m)

Length: \( L \) (m)

Steel density: \( \rho \approx 7850 \,\text{kg/m}^3 \)

Cross-sectional area: \( A = \pi \frac{d^2}{4} \)

Weight of one bar:

\[ W_\text{bar} = A \cdot L \cdot \rho \]

From this, we get the common unit weights:

  • Weight per meter: \( w_\text{m} = A \cdot \rho \) (kg/m)
  • Weight per foot: \( w_\text{ft} = w_\text{m} \times 0.3048 \) (kg/ft)

Imperial shortcut formula

In US practice, rebar weight per foot is often approximated directly from the diameter in inches:

Let \( d \) be the bar diameter in inches. Then:

\[ w_\text{ft} \approx 0.167 \, d^2 \quad (\text{lb/ft}) \]

For example, #5 rebar has \( d = 0.625 \,\text{in} \):

\[ w_\text{ft} \approx 0.167 \times 0.625^2 \approx 1.04 \,\text{lb/ft} \]

Step-by-step example

Suppose you need the total weight of 25 pieces of #5 rebar, 20 ft long each.

  1. #5 bar diameter = 0.625 in (≈ 15.9 mm).
  2. Weight per foot from table ≈ 1.043 lb/ft.
  3. Weight per bar = 1.043 × 20 = 20.86 lb.
  4. Total weight = 20.86 × 25 ≈ 521.5 lb (≈ 236.6 kg).

If you enter these values in the calculator, it will show essentially the same result using the exact geometric formula.

Typical uses of a rebar weight calculator

  • Estimating material orders – convert bar schedules into total tons or kilograms of steel.
  • Transport and lifting planning – check truck load limits or crane lift capacities.
  • Cost estimation – many suppliers quote prices per ton; you need total weight to estimate cost.
  • Concrete reinforcement takeoff – quickly summarize bar lists from drawings.

Assumptions and limitations

  • Uses nominal bar diameters from common US and metric standards.
  • Assumes straight bars; bends and hooks add a small amount of extra length and weight.
  • Density is assumed constant; you can override it if you have mill certificate data.

FAQ

Can I change the steel density?

Yes. If you are working with stainless steel or a specific alloy, enter its density in kg/m³. The calculator will recompute all weights using your value.

What if my bar size is not in the list?

Select “Custom diameter” under Bar standard and type the diameter directly (in inches or millimeters, depending on the current mode). The calculator will treat it like any other bar.

Are the results suitable for structural design?

The calculator is intended for quantity takeoff and logistics, not for checking structural capacity. For design, always follow the relevant building codes and use appropriate design software or spreadsheets.
