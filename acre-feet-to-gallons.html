The Formula Explained

\[ \text{Gallons} = \text{Acre-Feet} \times 325,851 \]

Glossary of Terms

  • Acre-Feet: A unit of volume commonly used in the United States in reference to large-scale water resources.
  • Gallons: A unit of volume for liquid measure equal to four quarts, in particular.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Suppose you have 2 acre-feet of water. To find the equivalent in gallons, you would multiply 2 by 325,851, resulting in 651,702 gallons.
