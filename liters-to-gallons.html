The Formula Explained

\( \text{gallons} = \text{liters} \times 0.264172 \)

Where liters is the volume in the metric system and gallons is the volume in US customary units.

Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the conversion factor provided by authoritative resources. 1 liter is approximately equal to 0.264172 US gallons. This is the standard conversion used in the United States.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For example, to convert 10 liters to gallons:

  1. Start with the formula: \( \text{gallons} = \text{liters} \times 0.264172 \)
  2. Substitute 10 for liters: \( \text{gallons} = 10 \times 0.264172 \)
  3. Calculate: \( \text{gallons} = 2.64172 \)

Glossary of Terms

  • Liters (L): A metric unit of volume commonly used worldwide. One liter equals 1,000 cubic centimeters or approximately 1.057 quarts.
  • Gallons (gal): A unit of volume in the US customary system. One US gallon equals 3.78541 liters or 128 fluid ounces.
  • Conversion Factor: The fixed number (0.264172) used to convert liters to US gallons.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How many gallons are in a liter?

There are approximately 0.264172 US gallons in one liter. This means 1 liter is slightly more than a quarter of a gallon.

Is this US gallons or Imperial gallons?

This converter uses US gallons, which are different from Imperial (UK) gallons. 1 US gallon = 3.78541 liters, while 1 Imperial gallon = 4.54609 liters.

Why use liters instead of gallons?

Liters are part of the metric system, which is used by most countries worldwide and in scientific contexts. The US primarily uses gallons for everyday measurements.
