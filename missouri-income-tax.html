Assumptions to keep the tool transparent and fast: we apply Missouri’s 2024 progressive rate schedule to Missouri taxable income approximated as Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) minus the federal‑equivalent standard deduction. Credits, exemptions, itemized deductions, and the federal tax deduction adjustment are not included. Optional city earnings tax is modeled at 1% of wages for Kansas City or St. Louis for planning purposes.

The Formula Explained

Let Gross be annual gross wages, PreTax be pre‑tax deductions, Ded be the deduction (standard or custom). Then:

AGI = Gross − PreTax
Missouri Taxable Income: TI = max(0, AGI − Ded)

2024 Missouri progressive tax (piecewise by bracket i with cap c_i and rate r_i):
Tax(TI) = Σ over i [ max(0, min(TI, c_i) − c_{i−1}) × r_i ] , with c_0 = 0

For 2024, brackets (USD):
0%: 0–1,000; 2.0%: 1,001–2,000; 2.5%: 2,001–3,000; 3.0%: 3,001–4,000;
3.5%: 4,001–5,000; 4.0%: 5,001–6,000; 4.5%: 6,001–7,000; 4.8%: 7,001+

City earnings tax (optional): CityTax = Gross × 0.01 (if Kansas City or St. Louis)

Effective rate: Eff = (StateTax + CityTax) / Gross

Glossary of Variables

Annual Gross Income: Total wages/salary before any taxes or deductions.
Pre‑tax Deductions: Amounts that reduce wages for tax (e.g., traditional 401(k), pre‑tax health premiums).
Filing Status: Used to determine the standard deduction amount.
Standard Deduction: Missouri follows the federal standard deduction (2024): Single/MFS $14,600; HOH $21,900; MFJ $29,200.
Missouri Taxable Income (TI): AGI minus the deduction, floored at zero.
Missouri State Income Tax: Computed via the progressive bracket schedule.
City Earnings Tax: Optional 1% for Kansas City or St. Louis; modeled on wages for planning.
Effective Rate: Total state + city tax divided by gross income.
Marginal State Rate: The rate applied to the last dollar of TI (top bracket reached).
Per‑paycheck: Annual tax divided by the number of pay periods selected.

How It Works: A Step‑by‑Step Example

Scenario: Single filer, annual gross income $65,000; pre‑tax deductions $5,000; standard deduction; no city tax; biweekly pay.

  1. AGI = $65,000 − $5,000 = $60,000.
  2. Standard deduction (Single, 2024) = $14,600. TI = $60,000 − $14,600 = $45,400.
  3. Apply brackets up to TI with the listed rates. The first $7,000 is taxed per the lower brackets; all remaining TI above $7,000 is taxed at 4.8%.
  4. Sum the bracketed amounts to get the annual Missouri state income tax.
  5. Per‑paycheck state tax = Annual state tax / 26 (biweekly). City tax is $0 in this scenario.

This mirrors the “Tax(TI)” piecewise formula above and yields the results shown by the calculator.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Which tax year does this calculator support?

Tax year 2024 for Missouri. Brackets and rates are based on Missouri DOR materials for 2024. We will update as official guidance changes.

Does it include the federal income tax deduction Missouri sometimes allows?

No. To keep results fast and transparent, we focus on the core bracket calculation and the deduction. Certain adjustments and credits (including any federal tax deduction interplay) are not included.

How accurate are per‑paycheck estimates?

They are the annual tax divided by your pay periods. Payroll withholding formulas and your W‑4 choices may produce different withholding amounts.

How are Kansas City and St. Louis earnings taxes handled?

If selected, a 1% earnings tax is applied to gross wages for planning. The actual base and residency/work rules may vary—confirm with city guidance.

Can I use itemized deductions?

You can approximate by switching to “Custom” and entering your total deduction amount. This tool does not evaluate itemized vs. standard for you.

Will my final return match this estimate exactly?

Not necessarily. Credits, additional income sources, adjustments, and city rules can change your outcome. Use this as an educational planning tool.

Where can I verify the latest Missouri tax tables?

Always refer to the Missouri Department of Revenue at dor.mo.gov for official forms, instructions, and updates.
