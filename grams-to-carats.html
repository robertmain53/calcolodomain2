Glossary of Terms

Gram (g)
A metric unit of mass equal to one thousandth of a kilogram. It is the base unit commonly used for measuring small weights in the SI (International System of Units).
Carat (ct)
A unit of weight for precious stones and pearls, equivalent to 200 milligrams or 0.2 grams. The metric carat was adopted internationally in 1907 to standardize gemstone measurements.
Gemstone Weight
The weight of a gemstone is typically measured in carats. Larger gemstones are exponentially more valuable than smaller ones of the same quality.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert grams to carats?

Simply divide the number of grams by 0.2 to get the equivalent value in carats. For example, 2 grams = 2 ÷ 0.2 = 10 carats.

What is a carat?

A carat is a unit of weight for gemstones and pearls, equivalent to 200 milligrams or 0.2 grams. It should not be confused with karat, which measures gold purity.

Why is precise conversion important?

Precise conversion ensures accuracy in measurement, critical in gemology, jewelry making, and precious stone trading where even small weight differences can significantly impact value.

Is a carat the same as a karat?

No. A carat (ct) measures gemstone weight, while a karat (kt) measures gold purity. For example, 24-karat gold is pure gold, while a 1-carat diamond weighs 0.2 grams.

Who can benefit from this tool?

Jewelers, gemologists, precious stone dealers, collectors, students, and anyone working with gemstone measurements can benefit from this accurate conversion tool.
