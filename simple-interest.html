The Formula Explained

Simple Interest Formula: Interest = Principal × Rate × Time

Glossary of Terms

  • Principal Amount: The initial sum of money invested or borrowed.
  • Annual Interest Rate (%): The yearly interest percentage applied to the principal.
  • Time Period (Years): The duration for which the money is invested or borrowed.
  • Total Interest: The total interest earned or owed over the period.
  • Total Amount: The sum of the principal and the total interest.

Practical Example

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Let's say you invest $1,000 at an annual interest rate of 5% for 2 years. The simple interest earned would be calculated as follows:

Interest = 1000 × 0.05 × 2 = $100

Thus, the total amount would be $1,000 + $100 = $1,100.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is simple interest?

Simple interest is a quick and easy method of calculating the interest charge on a loan.

How is simple interest different from compound interest?

Unlike compound interest, simple interest is calculated only on the principal amount, or on that portion of the principal amount which remains unpaid.

When is simple interest used?

Simple interest is often used in short-term loans or investments.

What happens if I miss a payment?

Missing a payment could lead to penalties and increased interest rates, depending on the loan agreement.

Can I earn interest on interest?

With simple interest, you do not earn interest on interest, unlike compound interest.
