The Formula Explained

The conversion from degrees to radians is given by the formula: \( \text{radians} = \text{degrees} \times \frac{\pi}{180} \).

Glossary of Terms

  • Degrees: A measure of angle, 360 degrees form a full circle.
  • Radians: The standard unit of angular measure, used in many areas of mathematics.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For example, to convert 90 degrees to radians: 90 degrees × π/180 = 1.5708 radians.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a radian?

A radian is a unit of angular measure used in mathematics. It is equal to the angle at the center of a circle whose arc is equal in length to the radius.

Why use radians instead of degrees?

Radians are often used in mathematical calculations because they provide a direct relationship between the angle and the arc length.

How do I convert radians back to degrees?

To convert radians to degrees, use the formula: degrees = radians × 180/π.
