The Formula Explained

\( 1~\mathrm{dwt} = \frac{1}{20}~\mathrm{ozt} \quad\) and \( \quad 1~\mathrm{ozt} = 31.1034768~\mathrm{g} \)

Therefore:

\( \displaystyle 1~\mathrm{dwt} = \frac{31.1034768}{20}~\mathrm{g} = 1.55517384~\mathrm{g} \)

Direct conversions:

\( \displaystyle g = \mathrm{dwt} \times 1.55517384 \quad\) and \( \quad \mathrm{dwt} = \frac{g}{1.55517384} \)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is the dwt↔g conversion factor exact?

Yes. It is derived from exact definitions in the troy-weight system mandated by standards bodies (NIST). No rounding is applied to the factor itself.

Why use pennyweight instead of grams?

Pennyweight is traditional in precious-metal trade because values are neatly related to the troy ounce (20 dwt = 1 ozt), which is often quoted in markets.

Does this work for avoirdupois ounces?

No. Avoirdupois ounces are used for everyday goods and are a different system; the converter targets the troy system used for bullion and jewelry.

How many dwt are in 5 grams?

\( \mathrm{dwt} = \frac{5}{1.55517384} \approx 3.21507 \,\mathrm{dwt} \).

What level of precision do you display?

Internally the tool uses full precision of the factor. Results are shown with up to 8 significant digits (configurable) to balance readability and accuracy.

Where can I verify these numbers?

Check NIST OWM conversion tables for troy units and grams, or the “Troy weight / Pennyweight” encyclopedic references for historical context.
