Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "roots calculator"

Current Landscape: Many sites including Symbolab, Mathway, and simple polynomial solvers deliver the roots of a polynomial or equation, but they usually just list the outputs with minimal context about multiplicity, complex conjugates, or the numerical method used.

To Surpass:
- Precision: Clarify whether the roots are exact radicals, approximated decimals (with tolerance), or complex pairs, and provide the residual (plugging the root back into the polynomial) so readers can trust the precision.
- Feature: Add a “root explorer” that graphs the polynomial on the real axis, highlights each root, reports multiplicity, and optionally shows the iteration process (Newton/Ruffini) if the roots were found numerically.
- Nuance: Provide a short theory note on why root finding is sensitive (condition number, Sturm sequences), mention when to prefer symbolic factorization vs numeric solvers, and link to further resources so curious readers can deepen their understanding.
