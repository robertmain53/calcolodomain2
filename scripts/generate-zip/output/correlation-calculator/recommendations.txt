### Topic: Correlation Calculator (`correlation-calculator`)

**User Persona & Goal:** A statistics student or researcher who needs to measure the strength and direction of a linear relationship between two variables.

**Critique of Existing Pages:** Many calculators compute the correlation coefficient (r) but don't provide the necessary context for interpretation.

**Recommendations for `correlation-calculator.html`:**
*   **Paired Data Input:** An interface to enter two columns of data (X and Y values).
*   **Scatter Plot:** The most crucial feature. Generate a scatter plot of the data so the user can visually assess the relationship.
*   **Calculate 'r' and 'r^2':** Calculate the Pearson correlation coefficient (r) and the coefficient of determination (r-squared).
*   **Interpret the Results:** Explain the meaning of the results. "r = [value]" indicates the strength and direction (positive or negative) of the linear relationship. "r^2 = [value]" means that [percentage]% of the variance in Y can be explained by the variable X. Add a warning: **"Correlation does not imply causation."**
