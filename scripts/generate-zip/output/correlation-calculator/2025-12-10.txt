Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "correlation calculator"

Current Landscape: RapidTables, Statology, and similar tools output Pearson/Spearman coefficients but rarely contextualize which statistic you just computed, what the underlying assumptions are, or whether the result holds when outliers are included.

To Surpass:
- Precision: Display the formula for the chosen correlation (Pearson, Spearman, Kendall), note the number of significant digits, and inform the user whether the coefficient is undefined due to zero variance so they can trust the report.
- Feature: Provide a “data inspector” that plots a scatter with the fitted line, highlights outliers, and recomputes the coefficient with robust trimming, giving scientists both the quick metric and reliability checks.
- Nuance: Add a short explanation of the theory (linear association vs rank-based), discuss confounding, and link to deeper resources about interpreting correlation versus causation so readers can dive into nuance beyond the number.
