### Topic: GCF Calculator (Greatest Common Factor) (`gcf-calculator`)

**User Persona & Goal:** A pre-algebra student learning to simplify fractions or factor polynomials.

**Critique of Existing Pages:** Most calculators give the GCF but don't show how it was found.

**Recommendations for `gcf-calculator.html`:**
*   **Multiple Number Input:** Allow the user to find the GCF of two or more numbers.
*   **Show the Factorization:** For each number entered, list all of its factors. Then, identify the common factors between the lists. Finally, point out the greatest of these common factors.
*   **Prime Factorization Method:** As an alternative, show the prime factorization of each number. The GCF is the product of the lowest powers of their common prime factors. This method is more advanced and provides deeper insight.
*   **Link to LCM:** The Greatest Common Factor (GCF) and Least Common Multiple (LCM) are closely related and often taught together. Provide a link to an LCM calculator.
