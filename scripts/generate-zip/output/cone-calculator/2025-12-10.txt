Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "cone calculator"

Current Landscape: Cone calculators (surface/volume) apply standard formulas but seldom note whether results include base, slant height, or how they tie into real-world flows/structures.

To Surpass:
- Precision: Spell out each formula (volume, lateral surface, total surface) and indicate the reference height (perpendicular vs slant) while letting users lock units and precision to suit documentation.
- Feature: Offer an interactive cone model, show both the profile and net (sector), compute center of mass or moment of inertia when relevant, and let the quick answer feed into design calculations.
- Nuance: Provide short notes on applications (funnels, nozzles, rooftops), highlight how thickness tolerances affect volume, and link to geometric references so readers can explore the theory after landing on the quick number.
