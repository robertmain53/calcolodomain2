Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "distance formula calculator"

Current Landscape: Distance formula helpers (MathWarehouse, Cuemath, Symbolab) apply √[(x2−x1)²+(y2−y1)²] or its 3D variant, but they usually just give a number and ignore units, coordinate systems, or interpretation in physics or navigation contexts.

To Surpass:
- Precision: Emphasize the Euclidean formula, allow units (meters, feet, kilometers) and keep the decimal precision consistent with instrument accuracy; show the squared difference step-by-step so readers can verify each part.
- Feature: Add a “trajectory builder” where you input two labeled points, display them on a mini coordinate grid, and overlay the computed distance along with the vector components and optionally the 3D path length if a Z coordinate is provided.
- Nuance: Provide a short explanation of alternative metrics (Manhattan, great-circle) depending on application, plus links to derivations so readers can explore why the formula works and when to adapt it.
