Keyword: "pyramid calculator"

Current Landscape: Pyramid calculators often compute volume = (1/3) × base area × height but they rarely state which base shape is assumed, whether slant heights are involved, or how lateral faces contribute to surface area.

To Surpass:

- Feature: Provide a 3D preview with adjustable height and base dimensions, show lateral surface with slant height calculation, and optionally compute centroid height for stability analysis.
- Nuance: Mention applications (monuments, roofs), note how the 1/3 factor arises via calculus, and link to further reading on the volume derivation so curious readers can trace the theoretical steps after the instant result.
