### 4. Topic: Bar to PSI (`bar-to-psi`)

**User Persona & Goal:** An automotive enthusiast, diver, or engineer working with pressure measurements. They need a quick conversion but would benefit from understanding the physical concepts of atmospheric vs. absolute pressure.

**Critique of Existing Pages:** Simple `bar` to `psi` calculators are common. They don't typically differentiate between gauge pressure (psig) and absolute pressure (psia) or explain that "bar" is already an absolute pressure unit close to atmospheric pressure at sea level.

**Recommendations for `bar-to-psi.html`:**
*   **Dual-Mode Converter:** Allow users to specify if their PSI value is gauge or absolute, and explain the difference.
*   **Physics Primer:** A concise explanation of pressure (Force/Area). Define atmospheric pressure, bar, and psi (pounds per square inch).
*   **Application Context:** Provide sections for different fields:
    *   **Automotive:** Tire pressure (typically psig).
    *   **Diving:** Tank pressure (bar).
    *   **Meteorology:** Atmospheric pressure (millibar).
*   **Reference Table:** Show a chart with common pressure points: sea level atmospheric pressure, a car tire, a pressure cooker, etc., in both bar and psi.
