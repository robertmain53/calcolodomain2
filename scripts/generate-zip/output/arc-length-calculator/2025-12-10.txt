Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "arc length calculator"

Current Landscape: Arc length converters apply s = rθ or s = (πrθ)/180 depending on units but seldom walk through the distinction between radians and degrees, or show how rounding impacts physical lengths (gear teeth, track segments).

To Surpass:
- Precision: Display both formulas, allow the user to pick radians or degrees, and show the decimal output with configurable precision, noting the choice explicitly for engineering reports.
- Feature: Provide a circle diagram with the arc highlighted, slider for radius and angle, and real-time display of chord length vs arc length to illustrate curvature.
- Nuance: Include short commentary on arc length in contexts like waveguides or planetary orbits, mention why radians simplify the formula, and link to deeper calculus resources for readers who want to study the derivation.
