Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "logarithm calculator"

Current Landscape: Sites like RapidTables, OmniCalculator and math tutorial blogs let you compute log values or change bases with a simple form, but they rarely explain the base selection, precision, or how to apply logs in signal processing, pH calculations, or decibel conversions.

To Surpass:
- Precision: Expose the chosen base (2, 10, e or custom), show the step-by-step base-change formula, and include the log’s derivative or inverse (exponential) with copy buttons so users can cite the math.
- Feature: Add a “log story” module where you can drop in pH, decibels, or Richter values and see how the log reflects orders of magnitude, including a slider that animates the log’s effect on scale.
- Nuance: Offer a short explanation on when to use different bases (binary for information, natural for calculus, common for engineering) and link to deeper references so curious scientists can refresh the theory behind the quick answer.
