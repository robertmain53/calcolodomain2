Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "chord calculator"

Current Landscape: Most chord calculators just apply s = 2r sin(θ/2) or Pythagorean steps, but they rarely contextualize whether the value is part of a circle arc, polygon, or instrumentation geometry.

To Surpass:
- Precision: Show the formula (2r sin(θ/2) or derived from endpoints), allow the user to toggle between angle and arc length inputs, and carry enough precision for optics/antenna alignment.
- Feature: Provide a circle diagram with draggable endpoints, show the chord highlighted, display the corresponding radius, central angle, and arc length, and optionally show chord sagitta for structural applications.
- Nuance: Include a short explanation on how chords behave in regular polygons, why they matter in mechanical linkages, and link to resources on circle theorems so readers can dive deeper after securing the quick value.
