### 5. Topic: Celsius to Fahrenheit (`celsius-to-fahrenheit`)

**User Persona & Goal:** A student, traveler, or home cook needing to convert temperatures. They might be curious about why these different scales exist and how they are defined.

**Critique of Existing Pages:** Countless converters exist. Very few explain the anchor points (freezing/boiling of water) for Celsius or the more obscure historical anchor points for Fahrenheit (brine, human body temperature).

**Recommendations for `celsius-to-fahrenheit.html`:**
*   **Interactive Slider:** A visual slider that shows both scales simultaneously, with key reference points marked.
*   **The "Why" Behind the Formulas:**
    *   `F = C * 9/5 + 32`
    *   `C = (F - 32) * 5/9`
    *   Break down why we multiply by 9/5 (the ratio of scale intervals) and add/subtract 32 (the offset of the freezing point).
*   **Historical Context:** Briefly explain how Anders Celsius and Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit developed their scales.
*   **Kelvin Connection:** Introduce the Kelvin scale as the absolute temperature scale used in science and explain its simple relationship with Celsius (K = C + 273.15).
