Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "square meters to square feet"

Current Landscape: UnitConverters, Metric-Conversions, Housing.com and CalculatorSoup quickly translate m² to ft² with a boxed calculator and a short table, but they rarely help the reader choose whether the conversion should include wall thickness, service shafts or how to express the same footprint for different building standards.

To Surpass:
- Precision: Spell out 1 m² = 10.7639104 ft² and offer toggles for rounding (courtroom precision vs quick estimation) so professionals can cite exactly the value they relied on.
- Feature: Introduce a “space profiler” that lets you enter length×width (or a room type) and overlays the converted area on a real reference (e.g., the size of a US studio apartment vs a UK living room) while showing both net and gross area, so the conversion feels actionable.
- Nuance: Discuss why different markets (UK developers, US leasing agents) report IPMS net internal area vs GIA, and include a short mention that architects often work in meters while contractors quote feet, giving curious readers a pathway to the operational theory.
