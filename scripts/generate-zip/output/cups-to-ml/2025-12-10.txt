Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "cups to ml"

Current Landscape: UnitConverters, InchCalculator, Exploratorium measurement tables and baking blogs all spit out 1 cup ≈ 236.588 mL (US) or the metric/imperial cousins, but they rarely force you to choose a standard or acknowledge how ingredient density skews the reading.

To Surpass:
- Precision: Make cup standards explicit (US customary 236.588 mL, US legal 240 mL, metric 250 mL, imperial 284.131 mL) and let readers lock the one they need, so the quick answer is citation-ready for recipes or lab protocols.
- Feature: Offer an “ingredient density” panel where you pick flour, sugar, water, or oil and the tool outputs the mass for the selected cup standard plus an error bar for heaped vs leveled spoons, satisfying the need for both accuracy and quick guidance.
- Nuance: Explain how professional kitchens and labs prefer mass, not volume, highlight the effect of packing and temperature, and point to deeper conversion resources so curious scientists can reinforce their theory.
