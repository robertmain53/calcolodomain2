### Topic: Cups to Milliliters (`cups-to-ml`)

**User Persona & Goal:** A home cook or baker following a recipe from a different region. They need precision for the recipe to succeed.

**Critique of Existing Pages:** Many sites provide a simple conversion but fail to address the critical differences between various international "cup" standards.

**Recommendations for `cups-to-ml.html`:**
*   **Multi-Standard Converter:** The calculator must allow users to select between US Legal Cup (240 mL), US Customary Cup (~236.59 mL), Imperial Cup (~284.13 mL), and other standards (e.g., Australian, Canadian, Japanese).
*   **"Which Cup Am I Using?":** Include a guide with images to help users identify their measuring cup's country of origin or standard.
*   **Ingredient Density Warning:** Add a note explaining that converting volume (cups) to weight (grams) is not direct and depends on the ingredient's density. Link to a cooking-specific volume-to-weight converter.
