Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "square root calculator"

Current Landscape: Square root helpers (RapidTables, OmniCalculator, Symbolab) mostly provide ±√n with a decimal and maybe a short explanation of perfect squares, but they rarely mention approximation methods, convergence, or the root’s role in equations.

To Surpass:
- Precision: Display the square root with optional radical form plus decimal to configurable precision, note whether the number is irrational, and include the difference between successive Newton iterations so readers see convergence.
- Feature: Provide a “root proof” card that outlines the Newton/Raphson steps, shows the error after each iteration, and optionally converts to fractional approximations for rationals, giving trust and a path to deeper numerics.
- Nuance: Offer a brief discussion on why square roots appear in RMS, standard deviation, or Pythagorean contexts, with links to theory so enthusiastic scientists can explore why the quick number matters.
