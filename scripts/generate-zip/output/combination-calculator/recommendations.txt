### Topic: Combination Calculator (`combination-calculator`)

**User Persona & Goal:** A probability or statistics student trying to calculate "n choose k" (C(n,k)).

**Critique of Existing Pages:** Often paired with permutations, but the distinction isn't always made clear.

**Recommendations for `combination-calculator.html`:**
*   **Clear Definition:** Start with a clear explanation: "Combinations are for when the **order does not matter**." Use an example: "Choosing 3 friends from a group of 5 for a committee."
*   **Formula and Inputs:** Provide inputs for `n` (total items) and `k` (items to choose). Display the combination formula `C(n,k) = n! / (k!(n-k)!)` and show the calculation with the user's numbers.
*   **Link to Permutations:** Provide a clear link to a "Permutation Calculator" and explain the difference (permutations are for when order *does* matter, like arranging letters in a word).
