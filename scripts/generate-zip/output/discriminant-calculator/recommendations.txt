### Topic: Discriminant Calculator (`discriminant-calculator`)

**User Persona & Goal:** An algebra student using the quadratic formula and needing to understand the nature of the roots before solving.

**Critique of Existing Pages:** Calculators will compute the discriminant, but often fail to explain what the result *means*.

**Recommendations for `discriminant-calculator.html`:**
*   **Formula First:** Prominently display the formula: `Discriminant = b^2 - 4ac`.
*   **Interpret the Result:** This is the most important feature. After calculating the discriminant, the page must provide a clear interpretation:
    *   If `b^2 - 4ac > 0`: "The discriminant is positive, so there are **two distinct real roots**."
    *   If `b^2 - 4ac = 0`: "The discriminant is zero, so there is **exactly one real root**."
    *   If `b^2 - 4ac < 0`: "The discriminant is negative, so there are **two complex conjugate roots**."
*   **Graphical Link:** Show a sample graph for each of the three cases (a parabola intersecting the x-axis twice, once, or not at all) to visually connect the discriminant's value to the behavior of the function.
