Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "sector area calculator"

Current Landscape: Sector area calculators typically compute A = 1/2r²θ (θ in radians) but many copies do not clarify whether the angle is given in degrees or radians or how arc length ties in.

To Surpass:
- Precision: Explicitly state the formula with the angle unit, allow on-the-fly conversion between degrees and radians, and show both the resulting area and corresponding arc length with configurable decimal precision.
- Feature: Display the sector on a circle, highlight the central angle, radius, arc, and area, and optionally allow toggling between small (thin slices) and large sectors to show precision behaviour near 360°.
- Nuance: Mention applications (turbine blades, missing sectors in detectors), explain why radian measure is natural for the formula, and link to circle geometry references so readers can extend their theory after the quick answer.
