Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "trapezoid calculator"

Current Landscape: Most trapezoid helpers compute area and maybe perimeter using the basic formula but don’t explain when to use median height concept, how to handle non-right trapezoids, or how precision shifts with skewed sides.

To Surpass:
- Precision: State the formula (area = (a + b)/2 × h) and allow input of side lengths plus angles, showing the derived height when necessary so that the quick area carries full justification.
- Feature: Provide a sketch with draggable vertices, highlight the height and bases, and show how the area/perimeter change in real time—ideal for architects and engineers wanting to see the trapezoid evolve.
- Nuance: Include a short explanation of trapezoid properties (isosceles, right), mention when you might compute side lengths from diagonal data, and link to geometric proofs for deeper exploration.
