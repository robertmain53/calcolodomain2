Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "pythagorean theorem calculator"

Current Landscape: Most offerings simply compute c = √(a² + b²) or missing leg, but they seldom note when units mismatch, when rounding affects the hypotenuse, or how the theorem generalizes to n dimensions.

To Surpass:
- Precision: Expose the formula, allow unit conversion, highlight whether the result is exact or approximated (with decimal precision control), and mention the underlying assumptions (right angle, Euclidean space).
- Feature: Provide a right triangle diagram with draggable legs/hypotenuse, show the squares on each side, and optionally compute altitude/projection to connect to advanced applications.
- Nuance: Mention where the theorem appears (distance metrics, wavefronts), link to vector dot-product interpretations, and include references to generalized Pythagorean relations so readers can explore the theory behind the quick number.
