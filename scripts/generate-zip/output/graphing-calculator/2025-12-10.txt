Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "graphing calculator"

Current Landscape: Graphing tools (Desmos-inspired, GeoGebra, Symbolab) let you enter functions and render their curves, but they often focus on visualizing alone without guiding the user through key features like axis intercepts, derivatives, or parametric behavior.

To Surpass:
- Precision: Keep the plotted window responsive but expose the coordinate grid, axis scales, and tick precision to ensure scientists can cite exact intercepts or turning points.
- Feature: Add a linked insights panel that automatically lists intercepts, maxima/minima, inflection points, and area estimates (where applicable), each with copyable expressions and links to the relevant theory.
- Nuance: Provide a short explanation on different plot types (Cartesian, parametric, polar) and include notes on how to interpret slices or derivatives, so readers can follow the deeper math while still getting their quick visual answer.
