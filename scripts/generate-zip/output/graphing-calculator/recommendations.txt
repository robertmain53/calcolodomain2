### Topic: Graphing Calculator (`graphing-calculator`)

**User Persona & Goal:** A student or professional who needs to visualize one or more functions.

**Critique of Existing Pages:** While powerful online graphing calculators exist (like Desmos), a self-hosted version can be superior by integrating deeper explanations and avoiding external dependencies.

**Recommendations for `graphing-calculator.html`:**
*   **Multi-Function Plotting:** Allow users to enter and plot multiple functions on the same axes, with different colors.
*   **Interactive Analysis:** Let users click or tap on the graph to find key points: intersections, roots (x-intercepts), y-intercepts, and local maxima/minima.
*   **Calculus Features:** For a given function, offer buttons to plot its derivative and integral. This is an incredibly powerful teaching tool.
*   **Parametric and Polar:** Go beyond basic `y=f(x)` by including modes for plotting parametric equations (`x(t), y(t)`) and polar equations (`r(θ)`).
