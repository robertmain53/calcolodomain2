Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "fibonacci calculator"

Current Landscape: Fibonacci calculators (MathIsFun clones, calculator sites) list the sequence up to n or compute the nth term via recursion, but they rarely show closed-form expressions, visual spirals, or applications linking the numbers to growth models.

To Surpass:
- Precision: Display both the recursive iteration and Binet’s formula (with √5) for the nth term, state whether the result is exact or rounded for large n, and expose the difference between the two methods when floating-point limits appear.
- Feature: Add a “spiral builder” that maps the sequence onto squares/rectangles and draws the golden spiral, letting users adjust n to see how the geometry evolves alongside the numeric values.
- Nuance: Include a short section on how Fibonacci numbers appear in nature, algorithms (e.g., search trees), and the golden ratio, with links to deeper reading so readers can go beyond the quick number.
