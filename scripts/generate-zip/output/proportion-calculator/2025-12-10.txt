Analysis Report - 2025-12-10
Persona: Enthusiast-Scientist
Keyword: "proportion calculator"

Current Landscape: Most proportion calculators (RapidTables, Omnicalculator, math help sites) let you solve a single ratio with cross multiplication but don't help you reason about units, scaling laws, or what happens when the quantities are not strictly proportional (affine relationships).

To Surpass:
- Precision: State the ratio clearly (a:b = c:?) along with unit checks, surface the computed constant of proportionality, and include a toggle for exact vs decimal outputs so engineers can document the exact multiplier.
- Feature: Add a “scaling lab” where you enter two known quantities, adjust a third, and see how the fourth shifts if the proportionality holds, plus allow plugging in a small perturbation to illustrate deviation from perfect proportion.
- Nuance: Offer a short discussion on how proportions relate to similarity in geometry or direct/inverse scaling in physics, and link to further reading so analytic readers can refresh the underlying theory beyond the arithmetic.
