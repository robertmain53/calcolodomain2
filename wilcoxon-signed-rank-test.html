Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test?

The Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test is a non-parametric test for comparing two related samples to assess whether their population mean ranks differ.

When should I use this test?

Use it when comparing two matched groups, especially when the data does not assume a normal distribution.

How do I interpret the results?

A small p-value (typically ≤ 0.05) indicates strong evidence against the null hypothesis, suggesting a significant difference between groups.
