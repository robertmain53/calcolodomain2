Glossary of Variables

  • Span Length (ft): The length of the diaphragm span.
  • Uniform Load (psf): The load distributed evenly across the diaphragm.
  • Shear Capacity (plf): The shear capacity of the diaphragm.
  • Deflection (inches): The displacement under load.
  • Shear Demand (plf): The shear force demand on the diaphragm.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a wood diaphragm?

A wood diaphragm is a structural element used in construction to resist lateral forces, typically in buildings.

How do I determine the shear capacity?

The shear capacity can be determined from material specifications and design codes such as the NDS/SDPWS.

What is the importance of deflection in diaphragm design?

Deflection is crucial as excessive deflection can lead to structural failures or serviceability issues.

How often should a diaphragm design be reviewed?

Diaphragm designs should be reviewed whenever there are changes in load conditions or structural modifications.

What standards govern diaphragm design?

The NDS/SDPWS standards provide guidelines for diaphragm design in wood structures.
