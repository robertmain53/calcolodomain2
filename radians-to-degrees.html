Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a radian?

A radian is a unit of angular measure used in mathematics. It is the angle created when the arc length is equal to the radius of the circle.

Why use radians instead of degrees?

Radians provide a natural way of measuring angles and are often used in calculus and other mathematical contexts for their simplicity and elegance.

How do I convert degrees to radians?

The formula to convert degrees to radians is: Radians = Degrees × π/180.

What is the relationship between π and radians?

π radians is equivalent to 180 degrees, which is why the conversion involves multiplying by 180/π.
