Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the standard conversion factor: 1 Nm = 0.737562 ft-lb. For more information, refer to the official data source.

The Formula Explained

The formula used for the conversion is:

ft-lb = Nm × 0.737562

Glossary of Variables

  • Nm: Newton-Meters, a unit of torque in the metric system.
  • ft-lb: Foot-Pounds, a unit of torque in the imperial system.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is torque?

Torque is a measure of the rotational force applied to an object.

Why convert from Nm to ft-lb?

Converting between these units allows professionals to work with different systems of measurement.

How accurate is the conversion?

The conversion uses a precise factor of 0.737562, ensuring high accuracy.

Where is this conversion applicable?

This conversion is commonly used in automotive and mechanical engineering.

Can I convert back from ft-lb to Nm?

Yes, by using the inverse of the conversion factor: 1 ft-lb = 1.35582 Nm.
