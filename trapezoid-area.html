The Formula Explained

The area of a trapezoid is calculated as follows:

Area = \\frac{(Base_1 + Base_2)}{2} \\times Height

Glossary of Terms

  • Base 1 (a): The length of the first base of the trapezoid.
  • Base 2 (b): The length of the second base of the trapezoid.
  • Height (h): The perpendicular distance between the two bases.
  • Area: The space contained within the trapezoid.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a trapezoid?

A trapezoid is a four-sided shape with at least one pair of parallel sides.

How do I find the height of a trapezoid?

The height is the perpendicular distance between the parallel bases of the trapezoid.

Can a trapezoid have more than two parallel sides?

No, a trapezoid can only have one pair of parallel sides.
