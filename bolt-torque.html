The Formula Explained

The torque required for a bolt is calculated using the following formula:
\( T = \frac{K \times F \times d}{2\pi} \)
Where \( T \) is the torque, \( K \) is the torque coefficient, \( F \) is the clamping force, and \( d \) is the diameter of the bolt.

Glossary of Variables

  • Bolt Diameter (d): The diameter of the bolt in millimeters.
  • Bolt Grade: The strength grade of the bolt, affecting its maximum torque capacity.
  • Clamping Force (F): The force that the bolt must withstand in kilonewtons.
  • Torque (T): The rotational force required to achieve the desired clamping force.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the importance of bolt torque?

Proper bolt torque ensures that the bolt is adequately tight to hold parts together without causing damage or failure.

How does bolt grade affect torque?

Higher grade bolts can handle more torque, making them suitable for demanding applications.

Can I use this calculator for all types of bolts?

This calculator is designed for standard bolts as per ISO 898-1:2013. Consult a specialist for non-standard bolts.

What happens if I apply too much torque?

Over-torquing can lead to bolt failure, stripping, or damage to the components being fastened.

Are there other factors affecting bolt torque?

Yes, lubrication and surface finish can affect the torque. Always consider these factors in practical applications.
