Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are strictly based on the formulas and data provided by the Electrical World. All calculations utilize standard engineering conversion methods.

The Formula Explained

The conversion from AWG to diameter in millimeters follows the formula:

Diameter (mm) = 0.127 × 92((36 - AWG)/39)

Glossary of Terms

  • AWG: American Wire Gauge, a standardized wire gauge system.
  • Diameter (mm): The calculated diameter of the wire in millimeters.
  • Cross-sectional Area (mm²): The area of the wire's cross-section in square millimeters.

Example Calculation

For an AWG size of 10, the diameter would be approximately 2.59 mm and the cross-sectional area would be approximately 5.26 mm².

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is AWG?

AWG (American Wire Gauge) is a standardized system used in the United States for measuring wire diameter.

Why convert AWG to mm?

Converting AWG to metric measurements is necessary for international applications and designs where metric systems are used.

How accurate is this converter?

This converter uses standard formulas for conversion, ensuring high accuracy for engineering purposes.

Can I use this for all wire types?

Yes, this converter is suitable for all wire types measured by the AWG standard.

Where can I find more information?

For more detailed calculations and standards, refer to official electrical engineering handbooks and guidelines.
