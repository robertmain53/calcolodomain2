Source and Methodology

The calculation is based on the Sheiner-Tozer method, adjusting phenytoin levels according to albumin concentration and renal function. MDCalc. All calculations strictly adhere to formulas from this source.

The Formula Explained

\[ \text{Corrected Phenytoin} = \frac{\text{Total Phenytoin}}{0.2 \times \text{Albumin} + 0.1} \]

Glossary of Variables

  • Total Phenytoin Level: The measured phenytoin level in the blood.
  • Albumin Level: The concentration of albumin in the blood.
  • Corrected Phenytoin Level: Adjusted phenytoin level considering albumin and renal function.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is phenytoin used for?

Phenytoin is an antiepileptic drug used to control seizures.

Why do I need to correct phenytoin levels?

Correcting phenytoin levels ensures accurate dosing, especially in patients with altered albumin levels or renal function.

How often should phenytoin levels be checked?

Levels should be monitored regularly, especially during initial treatment or if there are changes in health status.
