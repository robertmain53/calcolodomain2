Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the standard Elo rating change formula as used in various competitive sports. For more details, visit FIDE's Elo Rating System.

The Formula Explained

The formula used is: R_new = R_old + K * (S - E)

Glossary of Terms

  • R_new: New rating after the match.
  • R_old: Previous rating before the match.
  • K: A constant factor (usually between 10 and 40).
  • S: Actual score (1 for win, 0.5 for draw, 0 for loss).
  • E: Expected score.

FAQs

How does the Elo rating system work?

The Elo rating system calculates the relative skill levels of players in zero-sum games such as chess and soccer.

What is the K-factor?

The K-factor is a constant that determines the maximum possible adjustment per game, typically a value between 10 and 40.
