Data Source and Methodology

Our calculations are based on data from Numbeo, one of the largest databases in the world for cost of living information. Visit Numbeo for more detailed information.

All calculations are strictly based on the formulas and data provided by this source.

The Formula Explained

The Cost of Living Index is calculated based on the prices of consumer goods, including groceries, restaurants, transportation, and utilities. The index does not include accommodation expenses such as rent or mortgage.

Glossary of Variables

  • City 1 and City 2: The two cities you are comparing.
  • Cost of Living Index: A numerical value representing the overall cost of living in a city.
  • Difference: The difference in the cost of living index between the two cities.

Practical Example: Step-by-Step

Consider comparing New York City to San Francisco. If the cost of living index for New York City is 100 and for San Francisco is 90, the difference would be 10, indicating that San Francisco is 10% more affordable in general living costs.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the Cost of Living Index?

The Cost of Living Index is a theoretical price index that measures relative cost of living over time or regions. It is an index number that measures differences in the price of goods and services, allowing for comparison between cities.

How accurate is this calculator?

This calculator provides estimates based on data from Numbeo. While it is a useful tool for comparison, it is advisable to conduct further research for specific needs.

Does the calculator include housing costs?

No, the Cost of Living Index used here does not include accommodation costs such as rent or mortgage.

How often is the data updated?

The data is sourced from Numbeo and is updated regularly to reflect current prices and trends.

Can I use this calculator for international comparisons?

Yes, this calculator can be used to compare cities worldwide, provided that data is available for those locations in the Numbeo database.
