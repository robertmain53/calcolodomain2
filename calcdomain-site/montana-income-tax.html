The Formula Explained

Let taxable income be T, lower rate r_1, higher rate r_2, and threshold b. Montana’s two-rate structure is:

Tax components:

Lower-bracket portion: \( \text{LB} = \min(T,\, b)\times r_1 \)

Upper-bracket portion: \( \text{UB} = \max(T - b,\, 0)\times r_2 \)

Total tax before credits:

\( \text{Tax} = \text{LB} + \text{UB} \)

Marginal rate:

\( \text{MR} = \begin{cases} r_1, & T \le b \\ r_2, & T > b \end{cases} \)

Effective rate (if \(T>0\)):

\( \text{ER} = \dfrac{\text{Tax}}{T} \)

Glossary of Variables

Filing Status
Your Montana filing category (Single, Married Filing Jointly, Married Filing Separately, Head of Household).
Tax Year
The calendar tax year for which the tax is computed (currently 2024 or 2025).
Montana Taxable Income (T)
Your income subject to Montana tax after state adjustments.
Lower Rate (r₁)
The percentage applied to income up to the threshold.
Higher Rate (r₂)
The percentage applied to the portion of income above the threshold.
Threshold (b)
The income cutoff separating the lower and higher rates. It may differ by filing status and year.
Tax at Lower Rate (LB)
\( \min(T,b)\times r_1 \)
Tax at Higher Rate (UB)
\( \max(T-b,0)\times r_2 \)
Total Tax Before Credits
LB + UB, excluding refundable/nonrefundable credits.
Effective Rate (ER)
Total tax divided by T, shown as a percentage.
Marginal Rate (MR)
The rate that applies to your last dollar of income.

Worked Example: Step-by-Step

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Assume Tax Year = 2025, Filing Status = Single, Taxable Income \(T = \$60{,}000\). Defaults: lower rate \(r_1=4.7\%\) and higher rate \(r_2=5.9\%\). Threshold \(b=\$41{,}000\).

  1. Lower-bracket portion: \( \min(60{,}000, 41{,}000)\times 0.047 = 41{,}000\times 0.047 = \$1{,}927.00 \)
  2. Upper-bracket portion: \( \max(60{,}000-41{,}000, 0)\times 0.059 = 19{,}000\times 0.059 = \$1{,}121.00 \)
  3. Total tax before credits: \( 1{,}927.00 + 1{,}121.00 = \$3{,}048.00 \)
  4. Marginal rate: \( r_2 = 5.9\% \) (because \(T>b\))
  5. Effective rate: \( 3{,}048 / 60{,}000 = 5.08\% \)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Does the calculator include Montana credits?

No. This tool computes the statutory tax before credits. Apply nonrefundable or refundable credits after the result.

What if I don’t know my Montana taxable income?

Check your Montana return instructions or prior Form 2. For 2024+, Montana conforms more closely to federal taxable income with state adjustments. When in doubt, consult the MT DOR or a tax professional.

Are thresholds different by filing status?

Yes. The calculator loads status-specific thresholds for the selected tax year. Use Advanced to override if new values are published.

Is this calculator suitable for nonresidents?

Yes, for the Montana-source taxable income portion. Your taxable base must reflect Montana-source income allocation.

How often are parameters updated?

We monitor MT DOR releases annually. Thresholds are indexed; when new values appear, we update defaults and note the change log.

Can I switch to another state?

Yes. Browse the Taxation category to find other state calculators with the same accessible UX.

Will my data be stored?

No. All calculations run in your browser. We do not persist inputs.
