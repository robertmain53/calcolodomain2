How to convert lb/gal to kg/m³

Pounds per gallon (lb/gal) is a density unit commonly used in the US, especially in oil & gas, coatings, and chemical industries. Kilograms per cubic meter (kg/m³) is the SI density unit used in science and engineering.

The conversion factor depends on which gallon you use:

  • US gallon = 3.785411784 L
  • UK (imperial) gallon = 4.54609 L

Formulas (US gallon):

\[ \text{kg/m}^3 = \text{lb/gal (US)} \times 119.8264 \]

\[ \text{lb/gal (US)} = \frac{\text{kg/m}^3}{119.8264} \]

Formulas (UK gallon):

\[ \text{kg/m}^3 = \text{lb/gal (UK)} \times 99.7764 \]

\[ \text{lb/gal (UK)} = \frac{\text{kg/m}^3}{99.7764} \]

Worked example: 8.6 lb/gal (US) to kg/m³

Suppose a drilling mud has a density of 8.6 lb/gal (US). Convert it to kg/m³:

\[ \text{kg/m}^3 = 8.6 \times 119.8264 \approx 1{,}030.5 \text{ kg/m}^3 \]

So 8.6 lb/gal (US) ≈ 1,031 kg/m³ (rounded to 3 significant figures).

Worked example: 950 kg/m³ to lb/gal (US)

\[ \text{lb/gal (US)} = \frac{950}{119.8264} \approx 7.93 \text{ lb/gal} \]

So a fluid with density 950 kg/m³ has a density of about 7.93 lb/gal (US).

Why the gallon type matters

The difference between US and UK gallons is significant (about 20%). If you use the wrong gallon definition, your density in kg/m³ will be off by the same proportion.

  • US gallon: smaller volume → larger kg/m³ value for the same lb/gal.
  • UK gallon: larger volume → smaller kg/m³ value for the same lb/gal.

Always check your data source (datasheet, standard, or regulation) to see whether it assumes US or UK gallons. When in doubt for US-based industries, US gallons are usually intended.

Typical densities in lb/gal and kg/m³

  • Fresh water at ~4 °C: ≈ 8.345 lb/gal (US) ≈ 1000 kg/m³
  • Sea water: ≈ 8.55 lb/gal (US) ≈ 1025 kg/m³
  • Light crude oil: ≈ 7.0–7.5 lb/gal (US) ≈ 840–900 kg/m³
  • Heavy brine: > 10 lb/gal (US) > 1,200 kg/m³
