ISBN Validator

This tool checks if an International Standard Book Number (ISBN) is valid by verifying its format and checksum. It works for both 10-digit and 13-digit (EAN) formats. Ideal for authors, librarians, publishers, and booksellers who need to confirm the integrity of an ISBN.

ISBN Validation Tool

Enter a 10 or 13-digit ISBN. Hyphens and spaces are allowed and will be ignored during validation.

Authoritative Source & Methodology

An ISBN is a unique numeric identifier for books, standardized by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO). This validator adheres to the checksum verification algorithms defined in the official standard:

All calculations are based strictly on the weighted "modulo 11" (for ISBN-10) and "modulo 10" (for ISBN-13) algorithms prescribed by this standard.

The Formulas Explained

Validation is not a database lookup; it's a mathematical calculation on the number itself to see if the final "check digit" is correct.

ISBN-10 Checksum Formula

An ISBN-10 (e.g., $d_1 d_2 d_3 d_4 d_5 d_6 d_7 d_8 d_9 d_{10}$) is valid if the weighted sum of its digits is a multiple of 11. The 10th digit, $d_{10}$, can be 'X' to represent the value 10.

$ (d_1 \cdot 1) + (d_2 \cdot 2) + (d_3 \cdot 3) + \dots + (d_9 \cdot 9) + (d_{10} \cdot 10) \equiv 0 \pmod{11} $

ISBN-13 Checksum Formula

An ISBN-13 (e.g., $d_1 d_2 \dots d_{13}$) is part of the EAN-13 system. It uses alternating weights of 1 and 3. The entire sum, including the check digit, must be a multiple of 10.

$ (d_1 \cdot 1) + (d_2 \cdot 3) + (d_3 \cdot 1) + \dots + (d_{12} \cdot 3) + (d_{13} \cdot 1) \equiv 0 \pmod{10} $

Glossary of Variables

  • ISBN (International Standard Book Number): A unique identifier assigned to each book or book-like product.
  • ISBN-10: The 10-digit format used before 2007.
  • ISBN-13: The 13-digit format used since January 2007. All ISBNs are now issued in this format, typically starting with the "978" or "979" prefix.
  • EAN (European Article Number): A 13-digit barcode standard. The ISBN-13 is a specific type of EAN used for books.
  • Check Digit: The final digit of an ISBN, calculated from all the other digits. It acts as a safeguard against data entry errors.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Let's validate the ISBN-13: 978-0-306-40615-7.

  1. Strip hyphens: 9780306406157
  2. Apply weights (1, 3, 1, 3...) to the first 12 digits:
    $(9 \cdot 1) + (7 \cdot 3) + (8 \cdot 1) + (0 \cdot 3) + (3 \cdot 1) + (0 \cdot 3) + (6 \cdot 1) + (4 \cdot 3) + (0 \cdot 1) + (6 \cdot 3) + (1 \cdot 1) + (5 \cdot 3)$
  3. Calculate the sum:
    $9 + 21 + 8 + 0 + 3 + 0 + 6 + 12 + 0 + 18 + 1 + 15 = 93$
  4. Find the remainder when divided by 10:
    $93 \pmod{10} = 3$
  5. Subtract the remainder from 10:
    $10 - 3 = 7$. (If the remainder is 0, the check digit is 0).
  6. Compare to the check digit: The calculated check digit (7) matches the 13th digit of the ISBN (7).
  7. Conclusion: The ISBN is valid.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the difference between ISBN-10 and ISBN-13?

ISBN-10 was the 10-digit standard used until the end of 2006. To expand capacity (especially with the prefix "979"), the standard moved to 13 digits (ISBN-13) in January 2007, which integrates with the EAN-13 barcode system.

Can I convert an ISBN-10 to an ISBN-13?

Yes. You can convert any valid ISBN-10 to an ISBN-13 by prepending the prefix "978", removing the old 10th digit (check digit), and then calculating a new 13th digit using the ISBN-13 (mod 10) formula. For example, "0-306-40615-2" becomes "978-0-306-40615-7".

What does the 'X' mean in an ISBN-10?

The 'X' is the check digit. In the ISBN-10 (mod 11) algorithm, the check digit can be any value from 0 to 10. To represent '10' as a single character, the letter 'X' is used.

Does this tool tell me what book an ISBN belongs to?

No. This is a validator, not a search engine. It only confirms if the number itself is mathematically valid according to the ISO standard. To find a book, you would use a bookstore or library database.

Do the hyphens matter?

For validation, no. The hyphens are only for human readability, separating the prefix, registration group, registrant, and publication elements. Our validator ignores them automatically.

My ISBN starts with "979". Is that valid?

Yes. While "978" was the original prefix for converting ISBN-10s, the "979" prefix is now also used for new ISBNs to further increase the numbering capacity.
