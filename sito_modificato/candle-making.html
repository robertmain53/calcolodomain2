The Formula Explained

Total Wax = Wax Weight + (Fragrance Load / 100 × Wax Weight)

Glossary of Terms

  • Wax Weight: The weight of wax used in the candle.
  • Fragrance Load: The percentage of fragrance added to the wax.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

To calculate the total wax needed for a candle with a desired fragrance load, simply input the weight of the wax and the percentage of fragrance. For example, if using 500g of wax with a 10% fragrance load, the total fragrance needed would be 50g, resulting in 550g of total wax.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the ideal fragrance load for candles?

The ideal fragrance load typically ranges from 6% to 10% depending on the type of wax and personal preference.

Can I use any type of wax with this calculator?

This calculator is designed for use with most commonly available candle wax types, including soy, paraffin, and beeswax.

How accurate are the results?

The results are estimates based on standard candle making practices. Always perform a test batch to ensure satisfaction.

Why is my candle sweating?

"Sweating" can occur if too much fragrance is added. Ensure your fragrance load does not exceed the recommended percentage.

How do I store my candles?

Store candles in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to maintain their quality.
