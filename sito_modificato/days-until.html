How the Days Until calculator works

This tool counts the number of days between two calendar dates. By default it uses today as the start date, but you can change both the start and end dates, include or exclude today, and optionally count only business days (Monday to Friday).

Basic formula for days between dates

Raw days difference (exclusive)
\( \text{days} = \dfrac{\text{endDate} - \text{startDate}}{86\,400\,000} \)

where 86,400,000 is the number of milliseconds in one day.

If you include today
\( \text{daysIncludingToday} = \text{days} + 1 \) (when end date ≥ start date)

The calculator normalizes both dates to midnight in your local time zone to avoid off‑by‑one errors caused by time of day or daylight saving changes.

Counting business days only

When you enable Business days only, the calculator loops from the start date to the end date and counts only days that fall on Monday–Friday. Weekends (Saturday and Sunday) are excluded. Public holidays are not excluded automatically.

Understanding the outputs

  • Days – the main result, adjusted for your Include today choice.
  • Weeks – days divided by 7.
  • Months (approx.) – days divided by 30.44 (average days per month).
  • Years (approx.) – days divided by 365.25 (accounts for leap years on average).
  • Business days – Monday–Friday between the two dates.
  • Weekend days – Saturdays and Sundays between the two dates.

Examples

Example 1 – Days until New Year

Suppose today is October 1, 2025 and you want to know how many days are left until January 1, 2026.

  1. Set start date = 2025‑10‑01.
  2. Set end date = 2026‑01‑01.
  3. Leave Include today unchecked.

The calculator will show the exact number of days between those dates, plus the equivalent in weeks, months, and years.

Example 2 – Business days until a deadline

You start a project on March 4 and the deadline is March 29. You want to know how many working days you have.

  1. Start date = March 4.
  2. End date = March 29.
  3. Turn on Business days only.
  4. Choose whether to include today depending on when you start working.

The calculator will show the total calendar days, business days, and weekend days between those dates.

Common questions

FAQ

How do I calculate how many days are left until a date? ▸
Choose a start date (usually today) and an end date, then subtract the start date from the end date. Our calculator does this for you and also converts the result into weeks, months, and years.
Does this calculator include today in the day count? ▸
It’s up to you. When Include today is turned on and the end date is in the future, the calculator adds 1 day to the raw difference. This matches how many people think about countdowns (e.g., “3 days including today”).
Can I count only business days until a date? ▸
Yes. Enable Business days only. The calculator will count only Monday through Friday and ignore Saturdays and Sundays. Public holidays are not excluded automatically, so if you need to remove them you should subtract them manually.
What if the end date is before the start date? ▸
Then the date is in the past relative to the start date. The calculator will show the number of days since that date instead of days until, and the status badge will indicate that the target date is in the past.
Why do months and years show as approximate? ▸
Months and years have different lengths (because of 28–31 day months and leap years), so there is no single exact conversion. We use 30.44 days per month and 365.25 days per year, which are standard averages for calendar calculations.
