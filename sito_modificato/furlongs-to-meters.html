How to convert furlongs to meters

A furlong is a traditional English unit of length still used in horse racing. In the modern metric system, it has an exact definition:

Exact definition

1 furlong = 220 yards

1 yard = 0.9144 meters

Therefore:

1 furlong = 220 × 0.9144 m = 201.168 meters

Formula: furlongs to meters

meters = furlongs × 201.168

Example: Convert 6 furlongs to meters.

  1. Write the value: 6 furlongs
  2. Multiply by the factor: 6 × 201.168 = 1,207.008
  3. Result: 6 furlongs = 1,207.008 m (≈ 1,207.01 m)

Formula: meters to furlongs

To go the other way, divide by the same factor:

furlongs = meters ÷ 201.168

Example: Convert 1,200 meters to furlongs.

  1. Write the value: 1,200 m
  2. Divide by 201.168: 1,200 ÷ 201.168 ≈ 5.9649
  3. Result: 1,200 m ≈ 5.96 furlongs

FAQ: furlongs and meters

How many meters are in a furlong?

There are exactly 201.168 meters in one furlong. This is defined via the yard: 1 furlong = 220 yards and 1 yard = 0.9144 m, so 220 × 0.9144 = 201.168 m.

How many furlongs are in a mile?

There are 8 furlongs in a mile. Since 1 mile = 1,609.344 m and 1 furlong = 201.168 m, dividing 1,609.344 by 201.168 gives exactly 8.

Why are horse races measured in furlongs?

Furlongs come from historical English land measurement. A furlong was the length of a furrow in a ploughed field. Horse racing adopted this unit early on, and it remains a traditional way to describe race distances such as 5, 6, 7, or 8 furlongs.

Is a furlong an SI (metric) unit?

No. The furlong is not part of the SI system. It is a non‑SI unit that is defined in terms of yards and meters. For scientific and engineering work, meters are preferred, which is why conversions like this are useful.
