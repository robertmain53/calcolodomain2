US Gallon vs. UK Gallon: A Critical Difference

The most common error when converting "gallons" is not knowing which gallon you are using. The US and UK (Imperial) gallons are **not the same size**.

  • 1 US Liquid Gallon = 3.7854 Liters
  • 1 UK Imperial Gallon = 4.54609 Liters

As you can see, the **UK gallon is approximately 20% larger** than the US gallon. This is critical for recipes, fuel economy calculations, and scientific measurements. Our calculator includes both to prevent this error.

Conversion Formulas

US Gallon to Liters: Liters = US Gallons × 3.7854
Liters to US Gallons: US Gallons = Liters × 0.264172
UK Gallon to Liters: Liters = UK Gallons × 4.54609
Liters to UK Gallons: UK Gallons = Liters × 0.219969

US Cooking Conversions

For recipes, the smaller US units are all related:

  • 1 US Gallon = 4 Quarts
  • 1 US Quart = 2 Pints
  • 1 US Pint = 2 Cups
  • 1 US Cup = 8 Fluid Ounces
  • 1 US Fluid Ounce = 2 Tablespoons

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How many liters are in a gallon?

There are **3.7854 liters** in 1 **US Gallon**.
There are **4.546 liters** in 1 **UK (Imperial) Gallon**.

Is a US gallon the same as a UK gallon?

No. The UK (Imperial) gallon is about 20% larger than the US gallon. (1 UK Gallon = 1.201 US Gallons).

How many liters are in 5 gallons of gas?

In the US, this would be 5 US Gallons, so: 5 × 3.7854 = 18.927 Liters.
In the UK or Canada, this would be 5 Imperial Gallons, so: 5 × 4.546 = 22.73 Liters.
