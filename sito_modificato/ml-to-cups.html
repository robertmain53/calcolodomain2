A complete kitchen conversion tool. First, select your "Cup" type (Metric is 250ml, US is 236.56ml). Then, enter a value in any field to instantly convert between **ml**, **Cups**, **Tablespoons (tbsp)**, and **Teaspoons (tsp)**.

Not All Cups Are Equal: US vs. Metric

The most common point of confusion when converting `ml to cups` is that "cup" is not a universal standard. A US cup is different from a Metric cup (used in Australia, Canada, etc.).

  • 1 US Customary Cup (standard for US recipes) = **236.56 ml** (This is 1/2 US pint or 8 US fl oz).
  • 1 Metric Cup (standard for AU/NZ/CA recipes) = **250 ml**.
  • 1 US Legal Cup (used for US nutrition labels) = **240 ml**.

Our calculator defaults to the US Customary Cup (236.56 ml) but allows you to select the type you need.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How many milliliters (ml) in a cup?

It depends on the cup: 1 **Metric Cup** is 250 ml. 1 **US Customary Cup** (for recipes) is 236.56 ml. 1 **US Legal Cup** (for nutrition) is 240 ml. Use our calculator's dropdown to select the one you need.

Is 250ml one cup?

Yes, 250ml is exactly **1 Metric Cup**. This is the standard in Australia, Canada, and for most modern metric recipes. It is slightly larger than a US cup (236.56 ml).

How many ml in a tablespoon (tbsp)?

A US tablespoon is 1/16th of a US cup, which is **14.79 ml**. A Metric tablespoon (used in Australia/UK) is **15 ml**. This tool uses the US standard (14.79 ml).
