The formula explained

For a square with side \( s \):
Area: \( A = s^2 \)    Perimeter: \( P = 4s \)    Diagonal: \( d = s\sqrt{2} \)
Inradius (inscribed circle): \( r_{\text{in}} = \dfrac{s}{2} \)    Circumradius: \( r_{\text{circ}} = \dfrac{s}{\sqrt{2}} \).

Inversions:
\( s = \sqrt{A} = \dfrac{P}{4} = \dfrac{d}{\sqrt{2}} \).

Glossary of variables

  • s — side length (linear unit)
  • A — area (unit squared)
  • P — perimeter (linear unit)
  • d — diagonal (linear unit)
  • r in — inradius (linear unit)
  • r circ — circumradius (linear unit)

How it works: a step-by-step example

Suppose the diagonal is \( d = 20\text{ cm} \). Then \( s = d/\sqrt{2} \approx 14.1421\text{ cm} \). Area \( A = s^2 \approx 200\text{ cm}^2 \). Perimeter \( P = 4s \approx 56.5685\text{ cm} \). Inradius \( r_{\text{in}} = s/2 \approx 7.0711\text{ cm} \). Circumradius \( r_{\text{circ}} = s/\sqrt{2} \approx 10\text{ cm} \).

FAQ

Which input should I choose?

Pick the property you know with the most reliable measurement (side, area, perimeter, or diagonal). The tool will derive the rest.

How are units handled?

Linear results use the selected unit; areas use the squared unit. Changing the unit re-expresses outputs consistently.

Can I enter area directly?

Yes. Choose “Area” and enter a positive number. The calculator will compute side \( s=\sqrt{A} \) and all other properties.

What if I need more precision?

Increase “Significant digits” to show more rounded digits in outputs (between 2 and 10).

Is this valid for rectangles?

No—these identities assume all sides equal and right angles. Use a rectangle calculator for non-square rectangles.

Why do I see √2 in formulas?

The diagonal of a unit square is \( \sqrt{2} \) by the Pythagorean theorem, which scales with side length.
