decimal calculator

Convert fractions and percents to decimals, round with professional modes (half-up, banker's/half-even, floor, ceil, truncate), compare decimal values, and perform two-number operations. Built for educators, engineers, accountants, and students who need reliable, bias-aware results.

Interactive Calculator

Enter a fraction (a/b), a percent (like 7.5%), or a decimal (optionally scientific notation).

The formulas explained

Fraction to decimal : \\( d = \\dfrac{a}{b} \\), with \\( b \\neq 0 \\).

Percent to decimal : \\( d = \\dfrac{p}{100} \\).

Rounding (half-up) to \\(n\\) places : \\[ \\mathrm{round}_{0.5\\,\\uparrow}(x,n) = \\frac{\\left\\lfloor 10^n x + 0.5\\right\\rfloor}{10^n} \\]

Banker’s rounding (half-even) : ties at \\(5\\times 10^{-n-1}\\) go to the nearest even last kept digit.

Operations : \\( x \\circ y \\) with \\(\\circ \\in \\{+,-,\\times,\\div\\}\\); result rounded to selected places with chosen mode.

How it works: a step-by-step example

Example: Convert and round

  1. Input 3/8 into “Value to convert”.
  2. Select Output decimal places = 4 .
  3. The calculator evaluates \\(3/8 = 0.375\\). Fraction stays exact; display shows 0.3750 .

Example: Banker's rounding

  1. Enter 2.345 in “Number to round”.
  2. Choose Decimal places = 2 and Rounding mode = Banker's .
  3. Tie at the third decimal (5): last kept digit is 4 (even) → result 2.34 .

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do some decimals look odd in other calculators?

Many tools use binary floating-point directly, which can introduce tiny representation errors. This calculator uses string/rational math to avoid common pitfalls for typical inputs.

Can I paste values like “1,234.56”?

Yes—commas are ignored. You can also use scientific notation like 1.2e-3 .

When should I use half-even rounding?

Use it to minimize cumulative bias in financial/statistical aggregations—this is the essence of banker's rounding.

What happens on division by zero?

The calculator prevents it and shows a clear error message under the field.

Is there a maximum precision?

Display precision is capped at 10 places in the UI; internal exactness is preserved using rational forms where feasible.
