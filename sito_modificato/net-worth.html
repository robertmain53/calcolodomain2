The Formula Explained

Net Worth = Total Assets - Total Liabilities

Glossary of Terms

  • Assets: Everything you own that has value.
  • Liabilities: All your debts and financial obligations.
  • Net Worth: The difference between your assets and liabilities.

Practical Example

Example: If your assets total $500,000 and your liabilities total $150,000, your net worth is $350,000.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is net worth?

Net worth is the total of your assets minus your liabilities.

Why is calculating net worth important?

It helps you understand your financial health and plan for future financial goals.

How often should I calculate my net worth?

It's recommended to calculate it annually or whenever you experience significant financial changes.

What should I include as an asset?

Include cash, property, investments, and any other valuable items.

What should I include as a liability?

Include all debts, such as mortgages, loans, and credit card balances.
