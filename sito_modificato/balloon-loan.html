How to Use This Calculator

Enter the loan amount, the annual interest rate, the number of years for repayment, and the balloon payment percentage. Click Calculate to resolve the monthly installment and the final balloon amount. Inputs trigger a recalculation whenever you change them.

Methodology

The calculator applies the fixed-rate amortization formula to derive the monthly payment. It then simulates each period to accumulate interest and principal, preserving the balloon percentage so you understand the final lump sum due at term.

Results are estimates that mirror how lenders calculate balloon loans. Use this tool for planning and confirm final numbers with a financial advisor or loan officer.

The Formula Explained

To calculate the balloon payment and monthly payments, the following formula is used:

M = P × [r(1+r)ⁿ] / [(1+r)ⁿ - 1]

Glossary of Terms

  • Loan Amount: The principal amount borrowed.
  • Interest Rate: The annual interest rate applied to the loan.
  • Loan Term: The duration over which the loan is to be repaid.
  • Balloon Payment: A large payment due at the end of the loan term.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a balloon loan?

A balloon loan is a type of loan that does not fully amortize over its term, leaving a balance due at maturity.

How is the balloon payment calculated?

The balloon payment is determined as a percentage of the original loan amount, payable at the end of the loan term.

Are balloon loans risky?

Balloon loans can be risky if you are not prepared for the large final payment.

Can I refinance my balloon loan?

Yes, refinancing is a common strategy for managing the final balloon payment.

What happens if I can't pay the balloon payment?

Failing to pay the balloon payment could result in defaulting on the loan, which may lead to repossession of the collateral.

Formulas

Fixed-rate amortization:

M = P × [r(1+r)ⁿ] / [(1+r)ⁿ - 1]

  • P: Principal (loan amount)
  • r: Monthly rate (annual rate / 12)
  • n: Total number of payments (term × 12)
