Clothing Size Converter (International)

Convert clothing sizes between US, UK, EU, International, Japan, Australia and more for men, women and kids. Choose a category, select the size you know, and see instant equivalents across regions.

You can either pick a size from the dropdown or type it exactly as shown in the table (e.g. “M”, “10”, “38”, “25.0”).

Important: These are approximate conversions. Always check the brand’s own size chart and your body measurements.

How this clothing size converter works

This tool combines the most common international size charts used by major brands and retailers. Instead of forcing you to read long static tables, it lets you:

  • Pick a category (women, men, kids, shoes).
  • Choose the region where you know your size (US, UK, EU, International, Japan, Australia, or centimeters).
  • Enter or select your size and instantly see equivalent sizes across all other regions.

Under the hood, each category uses a curated table of sizes. When you click “Convert size”, the calculator searches the selected column for your size and highlights the matching row, so you can compare all regions at a glance.

Limitations and fit tips

Clothing and shoe sizes are not fully standardized. Two garments with the same labeled size can fit very differently depending on:

  • Brand and country of origin
  • Cut (slim, regular, relaxed, oversized)
  • Fabric (stretch vs non-stretch)
  • Intended fit (tight, tailored, loose)

Use this converter as a starting point, then:

  • Check the brand’s own size chart and fit notes.
  • Compare your body measurements (bust/chest, waist, hips, inseam, foot length) to the chart.
  • When in doubt between two sizes, consider your fit preference and whether the fabric stretches.

How to measure yourself for better size conversions

Upper body (tops, shirts, dresses)

  • Bust / chest: measure around the fullest part, keeping the tape horizontal.
  • Waist: measure around the narrowest part of your torso.
  • Hips: measure around the fullest part of your hips and seat.

Lower body (pants, jeans, skirts)

  • Waist: where the waistband normally sits (natural waist or low-rise).
  • Hips: again around the fullest part of the seat.
  • Inseam: from the top of the inner thigh to the ankle bone.

Shoes

  1. Place a sheet of paper on the floor against a wall.
  2. Stand with your heel touching the wall and mark the tip of your longest toe.
  3. Measure the distance from the wall to the mark in centimeters.
  4. Use the “Shoes” category and CM column to find the closest size.

FAQ

Are US and UK sizes the same?

No. For most women’s clothing, UK sizes are about 2 sizes larger than US (e.g., US 6 ≈ UK 10). For men’s clothing, the difference depends on the garment type. Always use a converter instead of guessing.

Why do some sizes appear blank in the table?

Not every region uses every size. Some sizes are uncommon or not officially defined in certain markets. Blank cells indicate that there is no widely used direct equivalent.

Can I convert between petite, tall, and plus sizes?

This tool focuses on standard adult sizing. Petite, tall, and plus-size ranges often use the same numeric or letter sizes but with different proportions. Check the brand’s dedicated petite/tall/plus size chart for best results.
