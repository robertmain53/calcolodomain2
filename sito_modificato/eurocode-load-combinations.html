The Formula Explained

ULS Load Combination: 1.35Gk + 1.5Qk

Glossary of Terms

  • Dead Load (Gk): Permanent load acting on the structure.
  • Live Load (Qk): Variable load acting on the structure.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For a dead load of 10 kN/m² and a live load of 5 kN/m²:

Load Combination 1 = 1.35 × 10 + 1.5 × 5 = 20.25 kN/m²

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a load combination?

It is a set of loads that are considered simultaneously for the purpose of structural design.

Why use Eurocode for load combinations?

Eurocode provides a comprehensive set of standards recognized throughout Europe for ensuring safety and reliability in structural design.
