How this North Dakota income tax calculator works

This calculator estimates your North Dakota state income tax using a step‑by‑step approach that mirrors how a simplified state return is computed. It focuses on individual wage earners and small business owners with relatively straightforward situations.

1. Start with your gross income

We combine your wages & salary and any other taxable income you enter:

Gross income = Wages & salary + Other taxable income

2. Subtract pre‑tax deductions

Many North Dakota taxpayers contribute to pre‑tax retirement plans or other benefit programs. These amounts reduce the income that is subject to tax.

Adjusted income = Gross income − Pre‑tax retirement − Other pre‑tax deductions

3. Apply the standard or itemized deduction

You can choose between:

  • Standard deduction (default) – an estimated amount based on your filing status and tax year, or
  • Itemized deductions – if you know your total deductible expenses, you can enter them directly.

North Dakota taxable income = max(Adjusted income − Deductions, 0)

4. Apply North Dakota tax brackets

North Dakota uses a progressive tax system with several brackets. Each portion of your taxable income is taxed at the rate for its bracket. The calculator:

  • Looks up the correct bracket thresholds for your tax year and filing status.
  • Calculates tax owed in each bracket.
  • Sums them to get your total North Dakota tax before credits.

5. Subtract credits

If you enter an amount for ND tax credits, we subtract it from your calculated tax:

ND tax after credits = max(Tax before credits − Credits, 0)

6. Compute effective tax rate and take‑home pay

Finally, we compute:

  • Effective ND tax rate = ND tax after credits ÷ Gross income
  • Take‑home pay after ND tax = Gross income − ND tax after credits

You can view take‑home pay per year, month, 2‑week period, or week using the dropdown in the calculator.

Limitations and assumptions

To keep the tool fast and easy to use, we make several simplifying assumptions:

  • We focus on North Dakota state income tax only – no federal income tax, FICA, or other state/local taxes.
  • We assume your income is primarily wages and salary. Complex items like large capital gains, business losses, or multi‑state income are not modeled in detail.
  • The standard deduction is an approximation based on typical values for your filing status and year.
  • Credits are entered as a single total amount rather than modeled individually.

For official rules, forms, and detailed guidance, always refer to the North Dakota Office of State Tax Commissioner or consult a qualified tax professional.

Frequently asked questions about North Dakota income tax

Who has to file a North Dakota income tax return?

In general, you must file if you are a resident, part‑year resident, or nonresident with North Dakota‑source income and your ND taxable income exceeds the filing threshold for your filing status. Certain taxpayers must file even below the threshold (for example, to claim a refund of withholding or credits).

Does North Dakota have local income taxes?

North Dakota does not impose separate city or county income taxes on top of the state income tax. However, you may still pay local property taxes and sales taxes.

How are North Dakota tax brackets updated?

The North Dakota legislature periodically adjusts rates and brackets. Our calculator is updated when new laws or official guidance are released, but you should always verify current rates with the state tax department if you are making major financial decisions.

Can this calculator help with paycheck withholding?

Yes, it can give you a rough idea of how much North Dakota tax might be withheld from your paycheck over the year. However, employers use official ND withholding tables and forms, which may produce slightly different results. Use this tool for planning, not for exact payroll reconciliation.
