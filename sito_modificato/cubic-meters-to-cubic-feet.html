How to convert cubic meters to cubic feet

Cubic meters (m³) and cubic feet (ft³) both measure volume. Cubic meters are the standard SI (metric) unit, while cubic feet are part of the imperial/US customary system and are common in construction, HVAC, and real estate.

Conversion formulas

From cubic meters to cubic feet:
ft³ = m³ × 35.3146667

From cubic feet to cubic meters:
m³ = ft³ ÷ 35.3146667

Where does 35.3146667 come from?

The conversion factor is based on the relationship between meters and feet:

  • 1 meter = 3.280839895 feet (exact, from 1 inch = 2.54 cm)
  • Volume scales with the cube of length

So:

1 m³ = (1 m)³ = (3.280839895 ft)³ ≈ 35.3146667 ft³

Step-by-step examples

Example 1 – Convert 2.5 m³ to ft³

  1. Write the formula: ft³ = m³ × 35.3146667
  2. Substitute: ft³ = 2.5 × 35.3146667
  3. Calculate: ft³ ≈ 88.2867

Answer: 2.5 m³ ≈ 88.29 ft³

Example 2 – Convert 500 ft³ to m³

  1. Use the inverse formula: m³ = ft³ ÷ 35.3146667
  2. Substitute: m³ = 500 ÷ 35.3146667
  3. Calculate: m³ ≈ 14.158

Answer: 500 ft³ ≈ 14.16 m³

Typical uses of m³ and ft³

  • Cubic meters (m³) – water tanks, shipping containers, gas volumes, engineering calculations.
  • Cubic feet (ft³) – room volume, refrigerators, HVAC airflow, soil and concrete estimates in US projects.

Frequently asked questions

How many cubic feet are in 1 cubic meter?

1 cubic meter is equal to approximately 35.3147 cubic feet.

How many cubic meters are in 1 cubic foot?

1 cubic foot is equal to approximately 0.0283168 cubic meters.

How accurate is this converter?

The calculator uses the exact factor based on the international definition of the inch (2.54 cm), then rounds results to a reasonable number of decimal places for readability. This is more than accurate enough for engineering, construction, and everyday use.

Can I use this for gas or liquid volumes?

Yes. The conversion between m³ and ft³ is purely geometric and applies to any substance: water, air, natural gas, concrete, soil, etc. Just make sure your starting volume is correct.
