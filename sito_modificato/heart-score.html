Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are strictly based on the formulas and data provided by the official HEART Score guidelines.

The Formula Explained

The HEART score is calculated using the sum of the following points: History, ECG, Age, Risk factors, and Troponin levels.

Glossary of Variables

  • History: Patient's medical history.
  • ECG: Electrocardiogram results.
  • Age: Patient's age.
  • Risk Factors: Presence of risk factors such as diabetes, smoking, etc.
  • Troponin: Troponin levels in blood.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Let's consider a patient with the following values: History = 2, ECG = 1, Age = 1, Risk factors = 1, Troponin = 1. The HEART score would be calculated as 2 + 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 = 6.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the HEART Score?

The HEART score is a tool used by clinicians to risk stratify patients with chest pain.

How is the HEART Score calculated?

The score is calculated based on five factors: History, ECG, Age, Risk factors, and Troponin levels.

What is a high HEART Score?

A HEART score of 7-10 indicates a high risk of major cardiac events.

Is the HEART Score reliable?

Yes, it is widely used and validated in clinical settings.

Can I use the HEART Score for self-diagnosis?

No, it is intended for use by healthcare professionals only.
