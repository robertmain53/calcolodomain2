How to Calculate Percentages

Percentages are a way to express a number as a fraction of 100. The word "percent" literally means "per hundred." Understanding how to calculate percentages is essential for everyday tasks like shopping discounts, calculating tips, understanding statistics, and managing finances.

Percentage Formulas

Here are the fundamental formulas for all percentage calculations:

1. Finding X% of a number:

Result = (X / 100) × Y

Example: 25% of 200 = (25 / 100) × 200 = 50

2. Finding what percentage X is of Y:

Percentage = (X / Y) × 100

Example: 50 is what % of 200? = (50 / 200) × 100 = 25%

3. Finding the whole when you know the part and percentage:

Whole = (Part / Percentage) × 100

Example: 50 is 25% of what? = (50 / 25) × 100 = 200

4. Percentage Increase:

New Value = Original × (1 + Percentage/100)

Example: Increase 100 by 15% = 100 × (1 + 15/100) = 115

5. Percentage Decrease:

New Value = Original × (1 - Percentage/100)

Example: Decrease 100 by 15% = 100 × (1 - 15/100) = 85

6. Percentage Difference:

% Difference = |Value1 - Value2| / ((Value1 + Value2) / 2) × 100

Example: Difference between 100 and 150 = |100-150| / ((100+150)/2) × 100 = 40%

Practical Examples

Example 1: Shopping Discount

A $80 shirt is on sale for 25% off. What's the sale price?

  • Discount amount = 25% of $80 = (25/100) × 80 = $20
  • Sale price = $80 - $20 = $60
  • Or directly: $80 × (1 - 0.25) = $60

Example 2: Tax Calculation

If sales tax is 8.5% and an item costs $150, what's the total?

  • Tax amount = 8.5% of $150 = (8.5/100) × 150 = $12.75
  • Total = $150 + $12.75 = $162.75
  • Or directly: $150 × (1 + 0.085) = $162.75

Example 3: Test Score

You got 42 out of 50 questions correct. What's your percentage?

  • Percentage = (42 / 50) × 100 = 84%

Common Percentage Conversions

Here are some frequently used percentage-to-decimal conversions:

  • 10% = 0.10
    • 25% = 0.25
  • 25% = 0.25 or one quarter
  • 33.33% = 0.3333 or one third
  • 50% = 0.50 or one half
  • 75% = 0.75 or three quarters
  • 100% = 1.00 or the whole

Tips for Working with Percentages

  • Converting to decimal: Divide the percentage by 100 (move decimal point two places left)
  • Converting from decimal: Multiply by 100 (move decimal point two places right)
  • Quick mental math: 10% is easy—just move the decimal one place left. Then multiply or divide for other percentages.
  • For discounts: Calculate the discount percentage and subtract from the original, or multiply original by (1 - discount rate)
  • For increases: Add the increase to 100%, then multiply by the original value

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between percentage change and percentage difference?

Percentage change measures the change from an original value to a new value relative to the original (useful for tracking growth over time). Percentage difference measures how different two values are relative to their average (useful for comparing two independent values without one being the "original").

Can a percentage be greater than 100%?

Yes! A percentage over 100% simply means "more than the whole." For example, if sales doubled from $100 to $200, that's a 100% increase. If they tripled to $300, that's a 200% increase.

How do I calculate percentage of percentage?

Convert both percentages to decimals, multiply them, then convert back. For example: What's 20% of 50%? → 0.20 × 0.50 = 0.10 = 10%

Why doesn't a 50% increase followed by a 50% decrease return to the original?

Because the percentages are calculated on different base values. Example: Start with 100 → increase 50% = 150 → decrease 50% of 150 = 75 (not 100). The decrease is calculated on the larger number (150), so 50% of that is larger than the original increase amount.

How do I convert a fraction to a percentage?

Divide the numerator by the denominator, then multiply by 100. For example: 3/4 = 0.75 × 100 = 75%

What's the fastest way to calculate 15% for a tip?

Find 10% (move decimal left one place), then add half of that amount. Example: For $60 → 10% = $6, half of that = $3, total tip = $9
