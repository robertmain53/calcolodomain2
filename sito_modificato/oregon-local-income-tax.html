How this Oregon local income tax calculator works

Oregon does not have city income taxes in most of the state, but the Portland metro area has two major local income taxes on top of Oregon state income tax:

  • Metro Supportive Housing Services (SHS) tax – a regional tax administered by Metro.
  • Multnomah County Preschool for All (PFA) tax – a county‑level tax on high‑income taxpayers.

This calculator lets you enter your income and residency situation and then estimates how much you might owe for each of these local taxes. It is designed for quick planning and education, not for filing a return.

1. Inputs

The tool uses the following inputs:

  • Filing status – Single/Head of Household vs. Married Filing Jointly. Thresholds are higher for joint filers.
  • Metro SHS applicability – whether you are a Metro resident, a non‑resident with Metro‑sourced income, or not subject to the tax.
  • Multnomah PFA applicability – whether you are a Multnomah County resident, a non‑resident with county‑sourced income, or not subject to the tax.
  • Wage & salary income – employment income subject to local tax.
  • Net self‑employment / business income – net income from self‑employment and pass‑through businesses.
  • Adjustments / deductions – optional amount to reduce the base used for local tax (for example, certain losses or adjustments).
  • Non‑resident sourced percentage – if you are a non‑resident, the share of your income that is sourced to Metro or Multnomah.

2. Local taxable income

First, the calculator combines your wage and self‑employment income and subtracts any adjustments you enter:

Local taxable income = max( 0, (Wages + Self‑employment income − Adjustments) )

If you select a non‑resident option, the calculator multiplies this amount by your sourced percentage to approximate the portion of income subject to Metro or Multnomah tax.

3. Modeled Metro SHS and Multnomah PFA brackets

The exact brackets and rates can change over time and may differ for different years. To keep the tool simple and transparent, we use illustrative progressive brackets that roughly mirror recent structures:

Metro SHS – example structure

For illustration, the calculator uses:

  • Single: 1% on income over $125,000; 3% on income over $250,000.
  • Married filing jointly: 1% on income over $200,000; 3% on income over $400,000.

Below those thresholds, Metro SHS tax is treated as $0.

Multnomah PFA – example structure

For illustration, the calculator uses:

  • Single: 1.5% on income over $125,000; 3% on income over $250,000.
  • Married filing jointly: 1.5% on income over $200,000; 3% on income over $400,000.

Again, income below the thresholds is treated as not subject to PFA tax.

Progressive tax calculation (generic)

For each bracket i with rate \( r_i \) and income range \([L_i, U_i)\):

\( \text{Tax}_i = r_i \times \max\big(0,\ \min(\text{Taxable income}, U_i) - L_i\big) \)

Total tax is the sum of all bracket taxes.

4. Combined effective and marginal local rates

The calculator reports:

  • Total local tax = Metro SHS tax + Multnomah PFA tax.
  • Effective local rate = Total local tax ÷ Local taxable income.
  • Top marginal local rate – the highest bracket rate that applies to your income, combining Metro and Multnomah where both apply.

Important limitations and disclaimers

  • This is an educational estimator, not an official calculator.
  • It does not compute Oregon state income tax or federal tax.
  • It does not handle all special cases: part‑year residency, complex sourcing rules, detailed business allocations, credits, or exemptions.
  • Rates and thresholds are simplified and may not match the current year exactly.

For official guidance, always refer to:

  • Oregon Department of Revenue – Personal Income Tax
  • City of Portland / Metro personal tax resources
  • Multnomah County tax authority and official instructions for SHS and PFA returns.

Frequently asked questions

Do I owe these local taxes if I live outside Oregon?

Possibly. Non‑residents can be subject to Metro SHS and Multnomah PFA taxes on income that is sourced to work or business activity in the district or county. The exact sourcing rules are technical; this calculator only approximates them using the percentage you enter.

Are Social Security and retirement income subject to these local taxes?

Many types of retirement income (such as Social Security) may be treated differently from wages and self‑employment income. The treatment can change over time and may depend on specific local rules. This calculator assumes you enter only income that is actually subject to the local taxes.

How can I reduce my Oregon local income tax?

Options may include adjusting where you work or live, managing the timing and form of income, and taking advantage of any deductions or credits allowed by Metro or Multnomah County. Because the rules are complex and can interact with federal and state taxes, you should discuss strategies with a qualified tax professional.
