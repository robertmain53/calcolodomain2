Tangent Line Calculator

Enter a function \( y = f(x) \) and the x-value. We’ll differentiate it, evaluate the slope, and build the tangent line equation step by step. The tool also shows horizontal and vertical tangents explicitly.

Examples: x^2, sin(x), e^x, x^3 - 2x + 1

Formulas used

1. Derivative of \( f(x) \) is \( f'(x) \).

2. Point of tangency: \( x_0 \) is given, \( y_0 = f(x_0) \).

3. Tangent slope: \( m = f'(x_0) \).

4. Point-slope form: \( y - y_0 = m (x - x_0) \).

5. Slope-intercept form: \( y = mx + b \) where \( b = y_0 - m x_0 \).

6. Horizontal tangent: if \( m = 0 \), then \( y = y_0 \).

7. Vertical tangent: if derivative is infinite or undefined, then \( x = x_0 \).

How to use this tangent line calculator

  1. Enter your function in terms of x.
  2. Enter the x-value where you want the tangent.
  3. Click Calculate to get the tangent line.
  4. Copy the slope-intercept form for graphing, or point-slope for algebraic steps.

This calculator is designed to compete with Symbolab, Mathway and WolframAlpha for this specific task, but keeps everything in one screen and shows the actual derivative we used.

FAQs

Why do I sometimes get a vertical line?

Because the derivative there is infinite (the curve has a vertical tangent). In that case the tangent line is x = x₀, not y = mx + b.

Do I need to simplify my function first?

Not necessarily. The underlying engine tries to differentiate symbolic expressions directly.

What if the derivative doesn’t exist?

Then the tangent line does not exist at that point. The tool will alert you if it can’t differentiate the function at the chosen x.
