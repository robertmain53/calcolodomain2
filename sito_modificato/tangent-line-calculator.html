The Formula Explained

Given a differentiable function \( f(x) \) and a point \( x_0 \), the slope of the tangent is the derivative \( f'(x_0) \).

Point-slope form: \[ y = m(x - x_0) + f(x_0), \quad \text{where } m = f'(x_0). \]

Slope-intercept form: \[ y = mx + b,\quad \text{with } b = f(x_0) - m\,x_0. \]

Numerical central difference approximation: \[ f'(x_0) \approx \frac{f(x_0 + h) - f(x_0 - h)}{2h}, \] for a small \( h \).

Glossary of Variables

  • f(x): the input function.
  • x₀: the x-coordinate of tangency.
  • m = f′(x₀): tangent slope at x₀.
  • y = m(x − x₀) + f(x₀): point-slope equation of the tangent.
  • b = f(x₀) − m·x₀: y-intercept; slope-intercept equation is \( y = mx + b \).

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Suppose \( f(x) = \sin x + x^2 \) and \( x_0 = 1 \) (radians). Then \( f(1) = \sin(1) + 1^2 \approx 0.8414709848 + 1 = 1.8414709848 \).

Using a small \( h \) and central difference: \[ f'(1) \approx \frac{f(1+h)-f(1-h)}{2h} \approx 2\cdot 1 + \cos(1) \approx 2 + 0.5403023059 = 2.5403023059. \] (The exact derivative is \( f'(x)=\cos x + 2x \), so \( f'(1)=\cos(1)+2 \).)

Point-slope form: \[ y = 2.5403023059\,(x-1) + 1.8414709848. \] Slope-intercept: \[ b = 1.8414709848 - 2.5403023059\cdot 1 \approx -0.6988313211,\quad y = 2.5403023059\,x - 0.6988313211. \]

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the calculator do symbolic derivatives?

It uses a high-quality numerical central difference by default for robustness and speed. For symbolic work, pair the results with a CAS if needed.

What step size h is used?

An adaptive step proportional to max(1e-5, 1e-5 * (1 + |x₀|)) is used; the tool may shrink it if overflow is detected.

How are degrees handled?

If “Degrees” is selected, trig inputs are converted internally to radians; inverse trig outputs remain consistent with the underlying functions.

What if f(x) is not differentiable at x₀?

The numerical derivative may be unstable or undefined. You’ll see large variations or errors—consider checking left/right derivatives separately.

How accurate is the graph?

The plot uses uniform sampling across the X-range. Increase “Plot Samples” in Advanced for smoother curves on rapidly varying functions.

Can I export the plot?

Use the browser’s Print function to save as PDF. The page includes print styles for clean output.
