How to use

Enter your current savings, yearly contributions, expected return, annual expenses, and withdrawal rate. Calculate to see how many years until you reach FIRE.

Methodology

We compute total savings needed via the safe withdrawal rule (expenses / withdrawal rate) and solve for the time required to reach that target using annual contributions and compounded returns.

  • Withdrawal rate defaults to 4% (the 25× rule).
  • Years solve the future value equation using a binary search on the annual compound growth.
  • Results show total savings target and annual expenses for context.

Full original guide (expanded)

The original page referenced IRS guidelines and outlined formulas for estimating early retirement timelines.

Years = (Target - Current) / Annual Savings

Glossary

  • Current Savings: Funds you already have.
  • Annual Savings: Contributions per year.
  • Expected Return Rate: Annual investment return (%).
  • Annual Expenses: Money needed each year in retirement.

FAQ

What is FIRE?

FIRE stands for Financial Independence, Retire Early.

How do returns affect FIRE?

Higher returns accelerate reaching the target.

Formulas

Target savings: Annual expenses / (withdrawal rate / 100)

Future value equation: FV = Current × (1 + r)^n + AnnualSavings × ((1 + r)^n - 1)/r
