What is a circular segment?

A circular segment is the region of a circle cut off by a chord. It is bounded by:

  • a straight line (the chord), and
  • the corresponding arc of the circle.

If you draw a chord across a circle and shade only the “cap” between the chord and the circle, you get a circular segment. In many applications (fluid levels in tanks, structural design, optics, surveying) we know the radius and one extra measure such as the segment height, chord length, or central angle and need the segment area.

Key geometry and notation

We will use the following notation:

  • R – circle radius
  • h – segment height (sagitta), measured from the chord to the circle along the symmetry axis
  • c – chord length
  • θ – central angle corresponding to the segment (in radians)
  • A_seg – circular segment area
  • A_sec – circular sector area

Formulas for circular segment area

The fundamental formula for the area of a circular segment in terms of radius and central angle (in radians) is:

Segment area from radius and central angle

Given a circle of radius R and central angle θ (radians),

A_sec = (1/2) · R² · θ   (sector area)

A_tri = (1/2) · R² · sin θ   (area of isosceles triangle formed by the two radii and the chord)

A_seg = A_sec − A_tri = (R² / 2) · (θ − sin θ)

From radius and segment height (R, h)

The segment height h is related to the central angle via

cos(θ / 2) = (R − h) / R

so θ = 2 arccos( (R − h) / R ).

Once θ is known, use A_seg = (R² / 2) · (θ − sin θ).

Valid segments satisfy 0 < h < 2R. When h = 0, the segment collapses to the empty set; when h = 2R, it covers the whole circle.

From radius and chord length (R, c)

The chord length and central angle are related by:

c = 2R · sin(θ / 2)

so θ = 2 arcsin( c / (2R) ).

The segment height can also be computed as h = R − √(R² − (c/2)²).

Again, use A_seg = (R² / 2) · (θ − sin θ).

Geometrically, 0 < c < 2R. As c → 0, the segment shrinks away; as c → 2R, it approaches a semicircle.

From radius and angle (R, θ)

When the central angle is known (for example, from geometry or trigonometry), the computation is direct:

Segment quantities from R and θ (radians)

  • A_seg = (R² / 2) · (θ − sin θ)
  • A_sec = (R² / 2) · θ (sector area)
  • c = 2R · sin(θ / 2) (chord length)
  • h = R · (1 − cos(θ / 2)) (segment height)
  • L_arc = R · θ (arc length)

Interpreting the calculator output

For any valid input combination, the calculator reports:

  • Segment area A_seg
  • Sector area A_sec
  • Full circle area πR²
  • Chord length c and arc length L_arc
  • Central angle in both radians and degrees
  • Segment perimeter (chord + arc)
  • Percentage of the full circle occupied by the segment

As long as you keep your length units consistent, you can safely use the results in engineering drawings, volume calculations (for partially filled circular tanks), structural checks, and educational examples.
