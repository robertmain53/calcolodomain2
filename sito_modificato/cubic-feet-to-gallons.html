Which gallon should I pick?

  • US gallon (liquid): water, plumbing, pools, US utility bills.
  • US gallon (dry): older agricultural/crop references.
  • UK/Imperial gallon: UK, some Commonwealth, older engineering texts.

FAQ

1. How many gallons of water in a 2 ft × 2 ft × 2 ft cube?

Its volume is 8 ft³. 8 × 7.480519 ≈ 59.844 gallons (US liquid).

2. Why do some sites show slightly different numbers?

Roundings. Our figures use the NIST exact factors (231 in³ per US gallon, 1 ft = 0.3048 m → 1 ft³ = 0.0283168466 m³).

3. Can I use this for water utility calculations?

Yes—many utilities meter in cubic feet (or CCF = 100 ft³) and customers think in gallons. This tool shows the exact multiplier.
