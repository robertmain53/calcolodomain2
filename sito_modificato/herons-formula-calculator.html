The formula explained

Let \(a, b, c\) be the side lengths and \(s\) the semiperimeter: \[ s=\frac{a+b+c}{2} \] The area \(A\) is: \[ A=\sqrt{\,s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)\,} \] Additional derived quantities: \[ P=a+b+c,\quad r=\frac{A}{s},\quad R=\frac{abc}{4A} \]

How it works: a step-by-step example

Suppose \(a=5\text{ cm}\), \(b=7\text{ cm}\), \(c=8\text{ cm}\).

  1. Check triangle inequality: \(5+7>8\), \(5+8>7\), \(7+8>5\) ✓
  2. Compute \(s=(5+7+8)/2=10\text{ cm}\).
  3. Area: \[ A=\sqrt{10(10-5)(10-7)(10-8)}=\sqrt{10\cdot5\cdot3\cdot2}=\sqrt{300}\approx 17.3205\text{ cm}^2. \]
  4. Perimeter \(P=20\text{ cm}\). Inradius \(r=A/s\approx 1.7321\text{ cm}\). Circumradius \(R=abc/(4A)\approx 8.0829\text{ cm}\).

Frequently asked questions

Can I use decimals or mixed units?

Decimals: yes. Mixed units: no—convert to a common unit first to keep results meaningful.

What if area shows “—”?

Either an input is invalid (non-positive) or triangle inequality is broken. Correct the inputs and try again.

Does the preview preserve exact proportions?

Yes—after solving coordinates from the side lengths, the triangle is uniformly scaled to fit the SVG viewport.

How do rounding and precision work?

The tool computes with full precision, then formats outputs for readability. You can change inputs to see more precise effects.

What’s the difference between inradius and circumradius?

The inradius is the radius of the inscribed circle touching all sides; the circumradius is for the circumscribed circle passing through all vertices.
