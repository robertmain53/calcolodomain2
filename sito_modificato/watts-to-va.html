Formula

\[ \text{VA} = \frac{\text{Watts}}{\text{Power Factor}} \]

Where power factor is the ratio of real power (watts) to apparent power (VA), typically ranging from 0 to 1.

How It Works

In AC electrical systems, apparent power (measured in volt-amps or VA) is the product of voltage and current, while real power (measured in watts) is the actual power consumed. The power factor represents the efficiency of power usage, accounting for the phase difference between voltage and current in AC circuits.

Example Calculation

To convert 1000 watts to VA with a power factor of 0.8:

  1. Divide 1000 W by the power factor 0.8
  2. 1000 ÷ 0.8 = 1250 VA
  3. Result: 1000 W at 0.8 PF = 1250 VA

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between watts and VA?

Watts measure real power (actual work done), while VA (volt-amps) measure apparent power (total power supplied). In DC circuits or purely resistive AC circuits with power factor 1.0, they are equal. In AC circuits with inductive or capacitive loads, VA is always greater than or equal to watts.

What is power factor?

Power factor is the ratio of real power (watts) to apparent power (VA), ranging from 0 to 1. A power factor of 1.0 means all power is being used effectively. Lower power factors indicate less efficient power usage due to reactive components in the circuit.

Why is power factor important?

Power factor affects electrical system efficiency and sizing. Utilities often charge penalties for low power factors because they must supply more current (higher VA) to deliver the same real power (watts), which requires larger infrastructure.

What is a typical power factor?

Resistive loads (heaters, incandescent lights) have PF ≈ 1.0. Inductive loads (motors, transformers) typically have PF of 0.6-0.9. Power factor correction capacitors can improve low power factors.

Data Source and Methodology

The conversion is based on the fundamental relationship: Apparent Power (VA) = Real Power (W) ÷ Power Factor. This is a standard electrical engineering calculation used worldwide.
