Formula

The formula to convert BTU/hr to Watts is:

\[ \text{Watts} = \text{BTU/hr} \div 3.41214 \]

One BTU per hour is equal to 0.293071 Watts. To convert BTU/hr to Watts, divide by 3.41214 (or multiply by 0.293071).

Glossary

BTU/hr (British Thermal Unit per hour)
A unit of power commonly used in HVAC to measure heating and cooling capacity. It represents the energy transfer rate in BTU per hour.
Watt (W)
The SI unit of power, equal to one joule per second. Named after James Watt, it measures the rate of energy transfer or consumption.
Power
The rate at which energy is transferred or converted. Unlike energy (measured in joules or BTU), power measures how quickly energy is used.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert BTU/hr to Watts?

To convert BTU/hr to Watts, divide the number of BTU/hr by 3.41214. Alternatively, multiply by 0.293071.

How many BTU/hr is 1 Watt?

One Watt equals 3.41214 BTU/hr. This means a 1,000-watt device consumes 3,412.14 BTU/hr of energy.

Why is this conversion important?

This conversion helps compare HVAC capacity (BTU/hr) with electrical power consumption (Watts), essential for sizing equipment and calculating energy costs.

Can I use this for air conditioners?

Yes, air conditioners are often rated in BTU/hr. Converting to Watts helps you understand the electrical power required and calculate operating costs.

What's the difference between BTU and BTU/hr?

BTU is a unit of energy, while BTU/hr is a unit of power (energy per time). BTU/hr measures the rate of energy transfer, making it comparable to Watts.
