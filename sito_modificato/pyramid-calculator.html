Formulas used

Volume (general pyramid): \( V = \frac{1}{3} A_{base} \cdot h \)

Square pyramid: \( A_{base} = a^2 \), slant \( l = \sqrt{h^2 + (a/2)^2} \), lateral \( A_L = 2 a l \), total \( A_T = a^2 + 2 a l \)

Rectangular pyramid: \( A_{base} = a b \), slants \( l_a = \sqrt{h^2 + (b/2)^2} \), \( l_b = \sqrt{h^2 + (a/2)^2} \), lateral \( A_L = a l_a + b l_b \)

Regular n-gon base: \( A_{base} = \frac{n s^2}{4 \tan(\pi/n)} \), perimeter \( P = n s \), slant from apex to midpoint of a side: \( l = \sqrt{h^2 + (s/2)^2} \)

Frustum (square): \( V = \frac{h}{3}(a_1^2 + \sqrt{a_1^2 a_2^2} + a_2^2) \), lattice triangles use average base.

FAQs

Which height do I enter?

Always enter the vertical height — the perpendicular distance from the base plane to the apex — not the slanted edge.

Can I mix units?

No. Keep all dimensions in the same unit so that area comes out in unit² and volume in unit³.

Why are there two slant heights for rectangular pyramids?

Because the faces are not all congruent. One slant goes to the long side, the other to the short side. We show both.
