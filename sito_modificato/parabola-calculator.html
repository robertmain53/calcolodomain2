Parabola formulas used

From y = ax² + bx + c: Vertex \( \left( -\frac{b}{2a}, \, c - \frac{b^2}{4a} \right) \)

Vertex form: \( y = a(x - h)^2 + k \) where \( h = -\frac{b}{2a} \), \( k = c - \frac{b^2}{4a} \)

Focus (vertical): \( (h, k + \frac{1}{4a}) \), Directrix: \( y = k - \frac{1}{4a} \), Axis: \( x = h \), Latus rectum: \( \frac{1}{|a|} \)

Horizontal parabola: swap x and y roles.

From focus (xf, yf) and directrix y = d: Vertex \( \left(x_f, \frac{y_f + d}{2}\right) \), \( p = y_f - \frac{y_f + d}{2} = \frac{y_f - d}{2} \), equation \( (x - x_f)^2 = 4p(y - k) \)

FAQs

Why do I sometimes get no real roots?

Because the parabola does not intersect the x-axis. The calculator still gives vertex, focus and directrix.

Can I turn the horizontal parabola into vertical?

Yes — choose the orientation in the relevant tab. Horizontal parabolas use x as the dependent variable.
