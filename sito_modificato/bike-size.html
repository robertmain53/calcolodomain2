The Formula Explained

Frame Size: Frame Size = (Inseam × 0.65)

Glossary of Terms

  • Height: Your total body height measured in centimeters.
  • Inseam: The length from your crotch to the ground, measured in centimeters.
  • Frame Size: The recommended bike frame size based on your measurements.

How It Works: A Step-By-Step Example

Consider a rider with a height of 175 cm and an inseam of 80 cm. The calculator computes the frame size as:

Frame Size = 80 × 0.65 = 52 cm

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What if my measurements fall between sizes?

If your measurements fall between two sizes, consider your riding style and preferences. A smaller frame may be more agile, while a larger frame might offer more comfort.

Can I use this calculator for all bike types?

This calculator is best suited for road and hybrid bikes. For mountain bikes, additional factors such as suspension and frame geometry may influence the size.

How often should I re-evaluate my bike size?

It's a good idea to re-evaluate your bike size if you experience discomfort, change your riding style, or purchase a new type of bike.
