Bathroom Layout Calculator

This tool helps you verify if your bathroom plan meets key comfort and building code guidelines. Enter your layout's measurements to check them against authoritative standards for clearances around toilets, vanities, and showers before you start construction.

Data Source & Methodology

This calculator provides recommendations based on the authoritative "Kitchen & Bathroom Planning Guidelines with Access Standards" published by the National Kitchen & Bath Association (NKBA).

  • Source: NKBA Planning Guidelines
  • Reference: NKBA-B-2021 (example code)
  • Date: 2021 Edition
  • Link: NKBA Official Guidelines

All calculations and pass/fail checks are strictly based on the minimum recommended distances provided by this source for safety, comfort, and accessibility.

The "Formula" Explained

This calculator doesn't use a single complex formula. Instead, it performs a series of logical comparisons for each measurement you provide. The core logic for each check is a simple conditional test:

\( \text{Your\_Input\_Value} \geq \text{Recommended\_Minimum} \)

For example, the test for toilet front clearance is: \( \text{Toilet\_Front\_Clearance} \geq 21 \text{ inches} \)

Glossary of Variables

Room Width / Length
The total dimensions of the room, used to calculate overall square footage and context.
Toilet Front Clearance
The clear floor space from the front edge of the toilet bowl to the nearest opposite wall or fixture. The NKBA recommends a minimum of 21 inches.
Toilet Side Clearance
The distance from the toilet's centerline to the nearest wall or fixture (e.g., vanity, tub). The NKBA recommends a minimum of 15 inches from the centerline (or a 30-inch total space).
Vanity Front Clearance
The clear floor space in front of the sink or vanity. A minimum of 21 inches is recommended for comfortable use.
Shower/Tub Entry
The clear width of the entry or door for a shower or tub. Minimums vary, but a 22-inch clear opening is a common baseline.
Main Doorway Width
The clear width of the bathroom's entry doorway. 32 inches is the standard for accessibility (ADA) and is highly recommended.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Let's say you are planning a small powder room and have the following measurements from your blueprint:

  • Your Toilet Front Clearance: 24 inches
  • Your Toilet Side Clearance: 14 inches

You enter these values into the calculator. Here is the process:

  1. Check 1 (Front): The tool compares your value to the rule:
    \( 24" \text{ (Your Input)} \geq 21" \text{ (Recommended)} \)
    This statement is TRUE. The calculator returns a PASS.
  2. Check 2 (Side): The tool compares your value to the rule:
    \( 14" \text{ (Your Input)} \geq 15" \text{ (Recommended)} \)
    This statement is FALSE. The calculator returns a FAIL.

Result: The results area will instantly show a 'FAIL' for the side clearance, alerting you that the space is too tight. This allows you to adjust your plan (e.g., specify a narrower vanity or offset the toilet) before starting construction and buying fixtures.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Are these legal building codes or just guidelines?

These are guidelines from the NKBA, which is the leading industry association. Local building codes (like the International Residential Code or IRC) may vary and take legal precedence. However, most local codes are based on or are very similar to these guidelines. Always check with your local building department for legal requirements.

What if I can't meet one of the clearances?

If your space is existing (a remodel) and you can't move walls, you may be "grandfathered in." If you're building new, failing to meet these guidelines can result in a cramped, uncomfortable, and potentially unsafe bathroom. It may also fail inspection. Look for "compact" fixtures (e.g., round-front toilets, narrower vanities) to help save space.

How do I measure 'from centerline' for a toilet?

The "centerline" is the imaginary line running through the middle of the toilet bowl and tank. It's typically aligned with the center of the waste pipe in the floor (the flange). To find it, measure the total width of your toilet tank, divide by two, and measure out from there. Or, measure 15 inches (or more) from a side wall to mark where the center of your toilet flange should be.

Does a 21-inch front clearance apply to a toilet facing a tub?

Yes. The 21-inch minimum clear space in front of the toilet should be maintained, even if it opens in front of a tub, shower, or vanity. This ensures enough room to safely use and clean the fixture.

What's the absolute minimum size for a bathroom?

While codes vary, a "powder room" (toilet and sink) can fit in as little as 15-20 sq. ft. A "full bath" (with a tub or shower) typically requires a minimum of 35-40 sq. ft. to meet all clearance guidelines comfortably.
