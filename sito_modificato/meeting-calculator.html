How to use the meeting calculator

1. Find a fair time across time zones

In the Time zone helper tab, choose the meeting date, base time, and the organizer’s time zone. Add participants and select their time zones. The table shows each person’s local time and whether it falls inside typical working hours (09:00–17:00).

Use the Suggest earlier and Suggest later buttons to nudge the meeting time until most participants are in green (within working hours) instead of red (outside hours).

2. Set up duration and participant costs

Switch to Meeting setup to define the duration, working hours per year, and currency. For each participant you can choose:

  • Annual salary – converted to hourly using working hours per year.
  • Hourly rate – used directly.
  • Day rate – divided by 8 hours by default.

Click Copy names from time zone tab to avoid retyping participants. You can still edit or remove people independently in each tab.

3. Estimate and track meeting cost in real time

In the Cost & live ticker tab, the calculator shows:

  • Estimated cost for the full planned duration.
  • Live cost that increases while the meeting is running.
  • Average cost per person for the planned duration.

Press Start live meeting when the meeting begins. You can pause and resume the ticker, or reset it if the meeting is cancelled or restarted.

Formulas used by the meeting cost calculator

1. Hourly cost from annual salary

\(\text{Hourly cost} = \dfrac{\text{Annual salary}}{\text{Working hours per year}}\)

2. Hourly cost from day rate

\(\text{Hourly cost} = \dfrac{\text{Day rate}}{8}\)

3. Cost per participant for a meeting

\(\text{Participant cost} = \text{Hourly cost} \times \dfrac{\text{Duration (minutes)}}{60}\)

4. Total meeting cost

\(\text{Total cost} = \sum \text{Participant cost}\)

5. Live cost ticker

\(\text{Live cost}(t) = \sum \left(\text{Hourly cost}_i \times \dfrac{t}{3600}\right)\)

where \(t\) is the elapsed time in seconds.

Tips for running better, cheaper meetings

  • Invite only decision-makers and contributors. Observers can often get a written summary instead.
  • Shorten default durations. Try 15–25 minutes instead of 60 where possible.
  • Batch status updates. Replace recurring status meetings with async updates plus a shorter Q&A.
  • Use the cost as a sanity check. If the cost looks high for the decision at stake, adjust scope, attendees, or format.

Meeting calculator FAQ

How do I calculate the real cost of a meeting?

Add each participant with their salary or rate, set the duration, and the calculator multiplies each person’s hourly cost by the meeting length, then sums everything. You can also run the live ticker to see the cost accumulate while you are in the meeting.

Can I mix annual salary, hourly rate, and day rate?

Yes. The tool converts all compensation types to an hourly cost internally. Annual salary uses the “working hours per year” field, and day rate assumes an 8‑hour day by default.

Does this meeting calculator support different currencies?

You can choose a display currency (USD, EUR, GBP, CAD, AUD). The calculator does not fetch live FX rates; it assumes all inputs are already in the same currency you select.

How does the time zone helper work?

The helper converts the base meeting time from the organizer’s time zone into each participant’s local time using the browser’s time zone database. It then compares that local time to a default working window (09:00–17:00) and marks it as good, borderline, or outside hours.

Is my salary data stored anywhere?

All calculations run in your browser. If you refresh the page, the data is lost unless you export it as JSON or copy the summary. For sensitive meetings, avoid using names that identify individuals.
