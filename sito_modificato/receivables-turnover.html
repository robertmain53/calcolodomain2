The Formula Explained

Receivables Turnover Ratio: Turnover Ratio = Net Credit Sales / Average Accounts Receivable

Glossary of Terms

  • Net Credit Sales: Total sales made on credit, minus returns and allowances.
  • Average Accounts Receivable: The average amount of money owed to a company by its debtors over a certain period.
  • Receivables Turnover Ratio: A measure of how efficiently a company collects its outstanding credit accounts.

FAQ

What is the ideal receivables turnover ratio?

The ideal ratio varies by industry, but generally, a higher ratio indicates efficient credit and collections processes.

How can I improve my company's receivables turnover ratio?

Consider tightening credit policies, improving collection efforts, and offering discounts for early payments.

Why is the receivables turnover ratio important?

This ratio helps identify how efficiently a company uses its assets and can indicate financial health.
