The Formula Explained

\( M_{c} = \frac{f_{c} \cdot A_{c} \cdot d_{c}}{1 + \frac{A_{s}}{A_{c}}} \)

Glossary of Variables

  • Beam Length: The length of the beam in meters.
  • Steel Grade: The grade of steel used in the beam.
  • Concrete Strength: The compressive strength of the concrete in MPa.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For a beam of length 10m with steel grade S275 and concrete strength of 30 MPa, the calculated composite moment capacity is 230 kNm, and the neutral axis is located at 0.3m from the top.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How reliable are the calculations?

Our calculator is based on AISC standards, which are trusted in the industry for their accuracy and reliability.

Can this calculator be used for all types of composite beams?

While it covers standard cases, for unique beam designs, consult a structural engineer.

What is the significance of the neutral axis?

The neutral axis is crucial for understanding the stress distribution in the beam, which affects its structural performance.
