How this payday loan calculator works

Payday lenders often advertise a simple fee such as “$15 per $100 borrowed for 14 days”. That sounds small, but when you convert it into an annual percentage rate (APR) and include rollovers, the cost can be enormous.

This calculator lets you:

  • Enter the amount you plan to borrow.
  • Model either a flat fee per $100 or a percentage rate for the term.
  • Set the loan length in days.
  • Add up to 6 rollovers (extensions) to see how fees stack up.
  • See total fees, total repayment, cost as a percentage, daily rate, and effective APR.

Formulas used

1. Fee for the first term

If the lender charges a flat fee per $100:

\[ \text{Fee}_\text{term} = \text{Amount} \times \frac{\text{Fee per \$100}}{100} \]

If the lender quotes a rate for the term (not APR):

\[ \text{Fee}_\text{term} = \text{Amount} \times \frac{\text{Rate}_\text{term}}{100} \]

2. Total fees with rollovers

Each rollover usually adds another full fee on the original amount:

\[ \text{Total fees} = \text{Fee}_\text{term} \times (1 + \text{Rollovers}) \]

3. Total to repay

\[ \text{Total repay} = \text{Amount} + \text{Total fees} \]

4. Cost of credit (% of amount borrowed)

\[ \text{Cost \%} = \frac{\text{Total fees}}{\text{Amount}} \times 100 \]

5. Effective APR (approximate)

We convert the cost for the first term into an annualised rate:

\[ r_\text{term} = \frac{\text{Fee}_\text{term}}{\text{Amount}} \]

\[ \text{APR} \approx r_\text{term} \times \frac{365}{\text{Loan days}} \times 100 \]

This is a simple APR approximation widely used for payday loan disclosures.

Example: $400 payday loan at $15 per $100 for 14 days

Suppose you borrow $400 and the lender charges $15 per $100 for a 14‑day loan.

  • Fee for first term: \( 400 \times \frac{15}{100} = \$60 \)
  • Total to repay after 14 days: \( 400 + 60 = \$460 \)
  • Cost of credit: \( \frac{60}{400} \times 100 = 15\% \)
  • APR: \( 0.15 \times \frac{365}{14} \approx 3.911 \Rightarrow 391.1\% \)

If you roll the loan over twice (3 terms total), you pay 3 × $60 = $180 in fees and still owe the original $400. Your total repayment becomes $580.

Understanding rollovers and the debt cycle

A rollover happens when you cannot repay the payday loan on the due date and you extend it for another term. In many cases:

  • You pay another full fee.
  • Your original balance does not go down.
  • New fees may be added for each extension.

Even a small loan can become very expensive after multiple rollovers. Use the slider in this calculator to see how quickly total fees grow.

Payday loans vs. personal loans

Payday loans are usually much more expensive than other forms of credit:

  • Payday loans: often 300%–600% APR or higher.
  • Credit cards: commonly 15%–30% APR.
  • Personal loans: often 6%–36% APR depending on credit profile and country.

A lower APR over a longer term can still be cheaper overall than a short, high‑fee payday loan, especially if you are likely to roll it over.

Safer alternatives to payday loans

Before taking a payday loan, consider:

  • Talking to your creditor about a payment plan or hardship program.
  • Using a lower‑cost personal loan or line of credit.
  • Asking your employer about a salary advance or earned‑wage access.
  • Checking community assistance, non‑profit, or government support schemes.
  • Borrowing from a trusted friend or family member with a clear written plan.

If you already have multiple payday loans or feel trapped in debt, a free, independent debt advice service or financial counsellor in your region can help you review your options.

Limitations & disclaimer

  • This calculator assumes simple fees and interest, not compounding.
  • It does not include late fees, collection costs, or other charges.
  • Actual terms depend on your lender and local regulations.

This tool is for information and education only and is not financial advice, a credit recommendation, or a loan offer. Always read your loan agreement carefully and seek professional advice if you are unsure.

Frequently asked questions

How do I use the payday loan calculator?

Enter the amount you want to borrow, the fee per $100 (or the rate for the term), and the number of days until repayment. If you think you might extend the loan, add the expected number of rollovers. The results update when you click “Calculate cost”.

Why is the APR so high on short‑term loans?

Because APR is annualised, a fee that looks small over 14 days becomes huge when scaled to a full year. For example, a 15% fee for 14 days is equivalent to roughly 391% APR.

What if my lender charges different fees for rollovers?

Many lenders charge the same fee each time, but some may charge more or add extra charges. This calculator assumes the same fee for each term. If your rollover fee is different, you can approximate by adjusting the fee per $100 or the number of rollovers.

Is a payday loan ever a good idea?

Payday loans may be legal and widely available, but they are usually one of the most expensive forms of credit. They can be risky if you are not certain you can repay on time. Carefully compare all alternatives and consider speaking with a qualified adviser before borrowing.
