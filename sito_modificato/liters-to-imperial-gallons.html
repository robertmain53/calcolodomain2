How to convert liters to imperial gallons

The imperial gallon (UK gallon) is used in the United Kingdom and some Commonwealth countries. It is larger than the US liquid gallon. The exact relationship between liters and imperial gallons is:

1 imperial gallon (UK gal) = 4.54609 liters (L)

To convert from liters to imperial gallons, divide by 4.54609:

imperial gallons = liters ÷ 4.54609

Example: 50 liters to imperial gallons

  1. Start with the volume in liters: 50 L
  2. Apply the formula: imperial gallons = 50 ÷ 4.54609
  3. Calculate: 50 ÷ 4.54609 ≈ 11.000

Result: 50 liters ≈ 11.00 imperial gallons (UK gal).

Converting imperial gallons back to liters

To go the other way, multiply by 4.54609:

liters = imperial gallons × 4.54609

Example: 2.5 UK gal × 4.54609 ≈ 11.365 L.

Imperial vs US gallons

A common source of confusion is the difference between imperial gallons and US gallons. They are not interchangeable:

  • 1 imperial gallon (UK) = 4.54609 L
  • 1 US liquid gallon = 3.78541 L

This means 1 imperial gallon ≈ 1.20095 US gallons. Fuel economy figures in the UK (mpg) are therefore not directly comparable to US mpg without conversion.

Typical uses for liters ⇄ imperial gallons

  • Fuel volumes in older UK car manuals and aviation references.
  • Engineering and tank capacities specified in imperial units.
  • Historical or British recipes that use gallons instead of liters.

FAQ: liters to imperial gallons

How many liters are in 1 imperial gallon?

Exactly 4.54609 liters. This is defined by international agreement and is the standard value used in engineering and science.

Is an imperial gallon the same as a UK gallon?

Yes. “Imperial gallon” and “UK gallon” refer to the same unit: 4.54609 liters. It is different from the US liquid gallon (3.78541 L).

What is 1 liter in imperial gallons?

1 liter ≈ 0.219969 imperial gallons. For quick estimates you can use 0.22 UK gal, but for precise work use the exact factor in this calculator.
