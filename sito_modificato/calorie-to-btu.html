Calorie to BTU Converter

Convert small calories (cal), kilocalories (kcal, dietary Calories) and joules to British thermal units (BTU) and back. Includes power conversion (kcal/h ↔ BTU/h ↔ watt) for HVAC and engineering use.

Energy conversion (cal, kcal, BTU, J, kJ)

Reference values: 1 cal = 4.184 J; 1 kcal = 4184 J; 1 BTU (IT) = 1055.056 J. Therefore: 1 cal ≈ 0.0039683 BTU and 1 kcal ≈ 3.9683 BTU.

Heat power conversion (kcal/h, BTU/h, watt)

1 kcal/h ≈ 1.163 W; 1 BTU/h ≈ 0.29307107 W; 1 kcal/h ≈ 3.9683 BTU/h. Use this for HVAC, boiler ratings, heat pumps.

How the calorie to BTU conversion works

Most confusion comes from two facts: (1) people say “calorie” but often mean kilocalorie (kcal, equal to food Calorie), and (2) there are slightly different BTU definitions. Here we use the widely accepted BTU (IT) = 1055.056 J, and the thermochemical calorie = 4.184 J, which is consistent with engineering sources like EngineeringToolbox.

Formulas

cal → J: \( J = \text{cal} \times 4.184 \)

kcal → J: \( J = \text{kcal} \times 4184 \)

BTU → J: \( J = \text{BTU} \times 1055.056 \)

Energy conversion: convert source → joule → target.

Power conversion: convert all to watt, using:

  • 1 kcal/h = 4184 J / 3600 s = 1.163 W
  • 1 BTU/h = 1055.056 J / 3600 s ≈ 0.293071 W

Why use this tool over generic unit converters

This page is tuned specifically to heat/energy contexts. It lets you work in the three most common domains at once: lab/physics (J, kJ), food/thermochemistry (cal, kcal), and HVAC/engineering (BTU, BTU/h, kcal/h, W). You don’t have to guess constants: they are embedded.

FAQ

1. What’s the difference between cal and kcal?

1 kcal = 1000 cal. Food labels use kcal but often write “Calorie” with a capital C.

2. Why are there different BTUs?

Yes, there are slightly different BTU definitions (IT, mean, thermochemical) differing within about 0.1%. We use the IT BTU, common in HVAC.

3. Can I use this for boiler sizing?

Yes, in the power tab you can convert kcal/h (common in European boilers) to BTU/h (common in US specs) or to kW.
