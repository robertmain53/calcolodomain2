Glossary of Terms

Calorie (cal)
A unit of energy commonly used in chemistry and nutrition. The thermochemical calorie is defined as exactly 4.184 joules.
British Thermal Unit (BTU)
A traditional unit of heat energy used in heating, ventilation, and air conditioning industries. One BTU is the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit.
Energy
The capacity to do work or produce heat, measured in various units including calories, BTU, joules, and kilowatt-hours.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a BTU?

A British Thermal Unit (BTU) is a traditional unit of heat energy. It's the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit at sea level.

How do I convert calories to BTU?

Simply multiply the number of calories by 0.00396567 to get the equivalent value in BTU. Our converter does this calculation instantly for you.

Why is this conversion important?

This conversion is crucial for scientific calculations and engineering applications that require precise energy measurements, especially when working with different measurement systems or international standards.

What are common uses for BTU?

BTU is commonly used in heating, air conditioning, and power generation calculations. It's the standard unit for rating furnaces, air conditioners, and water heaters in many countries.

Who can benefit from this tool?

Engineers, scientists, HVAC technicians, students, and anyone working with energy measurements in different unit systems can benefit from this accurate conversion tool.
