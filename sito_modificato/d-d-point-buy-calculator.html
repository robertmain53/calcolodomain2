The Formula Explained

The point buy system assigns a point cost to each ability score, starting at a base value of 8. The cost increases as the score increases. Here is the formula:

Cost = 1 + (Score - 8)

Glossary of Terms

  • Strength: Determines physical power.
  • Dexterity: Determines agility and reflexes.
  • Constitution: Determines endurance and health.
  • Intelligence: Determines mental acuity.
  • Wisdom: Determines perception and insight.
  • Charisma: Determines force of personality.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the D&D Point Buy system?

The D&D Point Buy system allows players to allocate a set number of points to their character's ability scores, offering flexibility and customization.

How many points do I have to spend?

Typically, you have 27 points to spend across your character's abilities.

Can I assign scores below 8?

No, scores start at 8 and can be increased using points.

Is there a maximum score I can assign?

Yes, typically scores cannot exceed 15 before racial bonuses are applied.

How do racial bonuses affect scores?

Racial bonuses are applied after distributing points, enhancing certain ability scores based on race.
