Temperature Conversion Formulas

Converting between Fahrenheit, Celsius, and Kelvin is essential in science and engineering. The most important thing to remember is that there is no direct formula for F to K; you must **convert to Celsius as an intermediate step**.

Formula: Fahrenheit to Kelvin (°F → K)

This is a two-step process:

  1. Step 1: Convert Fahrenheit to Celsius
    °C = (°F - 32) × 5/9
  2. Step 2: Convert Celsius to Kelvin
    K = °C + 273.15

Combined Formula: K = ((°F - 32) × 5/9) + 273.15

Other Conversion Formulas

  • Kelvin to Fahrenheit (K → °F): °F = (K - 273.15) × 9/5 + 32
  • Celsius to Fahrenheit (°C → °F): °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32
  • Fahrenheit to Celsius (°F → °C): °C = (°F - 32) × 5/9
  • Celsius to Kelvin (°C → K): K = °C + 273.15
  • Kelvin to Celsius (K → °C): °C = K - 273.15

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the formula for Fahrenheit (°F) to Kelvin (K)?

The formula is: K = (°F - 32) × 5/9 + 273.15. You must first convert Fahrenheit to Celsius (by subtracting 32 and multiplying by 5/9), and then add 273.15 to the result.

What is 0 Fahrenheit in Kelvin?

0 °F is equal to 255.372 K.
1. °C = (0 - 32) * 5/9 = -17.78 °C
2. K = -17.78 + 273.15 = 255.37 K

What is Absolute Zero (0 K) in Fahrenheit?

0 K (Absolute Zero) is equal to -459.67 °F. This is the lowest possible temperature.
