How this paycheck calculator works

This tool gives you a quick, transparent estimate of your take‑home pay. It is not a full payroll engine, but it follows the same basic steps your employer uses:

  1. Start from your gross pay for the pay period.
  2. Subtract any pre‑tax deductions (401(k), HSA, health insurance, etc.).
  3. Estimate federal income tax based on your filing status and a simplified effective rate.
  4. Calculate FICA taxes (Social Security and Medicare).
  5. Optionally apply a flat state & local tax rate.
  6. Subtract any post‑tax deductions (garnishments, union dues, etc.).

Formulas used

1. Gross pay per period

Salary mode:

\(\text{Gross}_{period} = \dfrac{\text{Annual salary}}{\text{Pay periods per year}} + \text{Additional taxable pay}\)

Hourly mode (assuming 52 weeks/year):

\(\text{Gross}_{annual} = \text{Hourly rate} \times \text{Hours per week} \times 52\)

\(\text{Gross}_{period} = \dfrac{\text{Gross}_{annual}}{\text{Pay periods per year}} + \text{Additional taxable pay}\)

2. Taxable income per period

\(\text{Taxable}_{period} = \max(0,\ \text{Gross}_{period} - \text{Pre‑tax deductions})\)

3. Federal income tax (simplified)

We approximate an effective federal rate based on filing status:

  • Single: 12% on the first $50,000 of annual taxable income, 22% above that.
  • Married filing jointly: 10% on the first $80,000, 20% above that.

4. FICA taxes

Social Security:

\(\text{SS}_{annual} = 6.2\% \times \min(\text{Taxable}_{annual}, 168{,}600)\)

Medicare:

\(\text{Medicare}_{annual} = 1.45\% \times \text{Taxable}_{annual}\)

5. State & local tax (optional)

\(\text{StateTax}_{annual} = \text{State rate} \times \text{Taxable}_{annual}\)

6. Net pay per period

\(\text{Net}_{period} = \text{Gross}_{period} - \text{Pre‑tax} - \text{Federal} - \text{FICA} - \text{State} - \text{Post‑tax}\)

Understanding your paycheck

Gross pay vs. net pay

  • Gross pay is what you earn before any taxes or deductions.
  • Net pay (take‑home pay) is what actually lands in your bank account.

Common paycheck deductions

  • Pre‑tax deductions – retirement contributions (401(k), 403(b)), health, dental, vision, HSA/FSA. These reduce your taxable income.
  • Taxes – federal income tax, Social Security, Medicare, and often state/local income tax.
  • Post‑tax deductions – wage garnishments, some insurance premiums, charitable contributions, etc.

Limitations and disclaimers

  • This calculator uses simplified effective tax rates, not the full IRS withholding tables.
  • It does not model all credits, deductions, or local payroll taxes.
  • Actual withholding depends on your W‑4, employer payroll system, and your full tax situation.

Always consult a qualified tax professional or your payroll department for personalized advice.

FAQ

Can I compare two job offers with this?

Yes. Run the calculator once for each offer (with its own salary, benefits, and state tax rate) and compare the annual net pay results.

How should I choose a state tax rate?

You can start with your state’s top marginal rate or an average effective rate from a reputable source, then adjust until the estimate roughly matches your current paycheck.

Why is my real paycheck different from this estimate?

Employers may withhold based on detailed IRS tables, local taxes, benefit plans, and year‑to‑date information. This tool is designed for clarity and quick estimates, not exact payroll replication.
