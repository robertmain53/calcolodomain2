Step-by-Step Solution

Surface Area vs. Lateral Area vs. Volume

It's important to differentiate between the various 3D measurements:

  • **Volume ($V$):** The amount of space inside the shape (cubic units).
  • **Total Surface Area (TSA):** The total area of all external faces and curved surfaces (square units).
  • **Lateral Surface Area (LSA):** The area of only the sides, excluding the top and bottom bases.

For prisms and cylinders, $TSA = LSA + 2 \times (\text{Base Area})$. For pyramids, $TSA = LSA + 1 \times (\text{Base Area})$.

Surface Area Formulas by Shape

Rectangular Prism / Cuboid

The TSA is the sum of the areas of the 6 rectangular faces ($lw, lh, wh$):

$$\text{TSA} = 2(lw + lh + wh)$$ $$V = lwh$$

Cylinder

The TSA is the lateral area (a rectangle when unrolled) plus the two circular bases:

$$\text{TSA} = 2\pi r h + 2\pi r^2$$ $$V = \pi r^2 h$$

Sphere

Both formulas are elegant and depend only on the radius ($r$):

$$\text{Area} = 4\pi r^2$$ $$V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$$

Square Pyramid

The TSA is the square base area ($s^2$) plus the four triangular faces. The lateral area requires the slant height ($l$):

$$\text{TSA} = s^2 + 2s l$$ $$V = \frac{1}{3} s^2 h$$

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the difference between total surface area and volume?

Total surface area (TSA) is the sum of the areas of all faces and curved surfaces of a 3D object (measured in square units). Volume (V) is the amount of space enclosed within the object (measured in cubic units).

What is the formula for the surface area of a sphere?

The surface area ($A$) of a sphere is $A = 4 \pi r^2$, where $r$ is the radius.

What is the formula for the total surface area of a cylinder?

The total surface area ($TSA$) of a closed cylinder is the sum of the lateral area ($2\pi rh$) and the two circular bases ($2\pi r^2$): $TSA = 2\pi r h + 2\pi r^2$.

What is the lateral surface area?

The lateral surface area ($LSA$) is the area of all the sides, excluding the top and bottom bases. For a prism, $LSA$ is the perimeter of the base times the height ($LSA = P \cdot h$).
