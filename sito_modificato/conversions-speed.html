Formulas we use

m/s is our base.

km/h = m/s × 3.6

mph = m/s × 2.236936

ft/s = m/s × 3.28084

knots = m/s × 1.943844

Mach = m/s ÷ 340.29 (approx. speed of sound at 20°C, sea level)

If you need a different Mach reference (altitude, temperature), convert to m/s here and then divide by the correct speed of sound for your conditions.

When do you need speed conversions?

Speed shows up everywhere: car specs (mph / km/h), running pace (m/s), aviation and nautical navigation (knots), physics problems (m/s), even engineering datasheets. Having a single, reliable tool saves you from memorizing several factors.

Accuracy note

All factors above are standard and widely used. For aviation/nautical work or performance-critical simulations, always check if your organization uses a slightly different knot or Mach reference.

FAQ

1. Are knots the same everywhere?

Yes, 1 knot = 1 nautical mile per hour = 1.852 km/h = 0.514444 m/s.

2. Why do I see a slightly different mph factor elsewhere?

Because sometimes mph is rounded differently. We use 1 m/s = 2.236936 mph, which is standard.

3. Can this convert pace (min/km)?

Not directly — this tool is for speed. Convert your speed to km/h or m/s, then invert to get time per distance.
