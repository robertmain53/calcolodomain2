Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on standard data storage conversion rates. For more information, refer to authoritative sources such as ISO/IEC 80000-13:2008.

The Formula Explained

The conversion from megabytes to gigabytes is calculated using the formula:

GB = MB / 1024

Glossary of Terms

  • Megabyte (MB): A unit of data size equal to 1,024 kilobytes.
  • Gigabyte (GB): A unit of data size equal to 1,024 megabytes.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

To convert 2048 MB to GB:

  1. Divide 2048 by 1024.
  2. The result is 2 GB.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a megabyte (MB)?

A megabyte is a unit of digital information storage equivalent to 1,024 kilobytes.

What is a gigabyte (GB)?

A gigabyte is a unit of digital information storage equivalent to 1,024 megabytes.

Why is the conversion factor 1024?

Computers use binary arithmetic, so the conversion factor is based on powers of two, specifically 2^10, which equals 1024.

Can I convert gigabytes back to megabytes?

Yes, multiply the number of gigabytes by 1024 to convert back to megabytes.

Is there a difference between decimal and binary gigabytes?

Yes, binary gigabytes use 1024 MB per GB, while decimal gigabytes use 1000 MB per GB.
