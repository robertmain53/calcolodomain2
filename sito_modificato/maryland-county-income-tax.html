How Maryland county income tax works

Maryland is one of the few states that imposes a local income tax in addition to the state income tax. Every Maryland resident is assigned a local tax rate based on the county (or Baltimore City) where they live on the last day of the tax year.

On your Maryland return (Form 502), the state calculates:

  • Maryland state income tax – based on progressive state tax brackets; and
  • Maryland local (county) income tax – a flat percentage of your Maryland taxable income.

Formula for Maryland county income tax

County income tax = Maryland taxable income × county rate

Example: If your Maryland taxable income is $80,000 and your county rate is 3.00%, your local tax is:
$80,000 × 0.0300 = $2,400.

Maryland state income tax (simplified brackets)

Maryland uses a progressive state income tax. For this calculator we use a simplified bracket model that approximates current law for most taxpayers. Actual brackets and rates are set by statute and may differ slightly.

Illustrative Maryland state brackets (single / MFS):

  • 2.00% on the first $1,000
  • 3.00% on $1,001 – $2,000
  • 4.00% on $2,001 – $3,000
  • 4.75% on $3,001 – $100,000
  • 5.00% on $100,001 – $125,000
  • 5.25% on $125,001 – $150,000
  • 5.50% on $150,001 – $250,000
  • 5.75% on $250,000 and above

For married filing jointly / head of household, the upper brackets are doubled (e.g., the 4.75% bracket runs to $150,000 instead of $100,000).

These brackets are for estimation only and do not include special surtaxes or phase-outs.

Frequently asked questions

Do all Maryland counties have the same income tax rate?

No. Each county and Baltimore City sets its own local income tax rate within limits set by state law. Some counties are at or near the maximum rate, while others are lower. The Comptroller publishes an official list of county rates each year.

What if I moved to a different Maryland county during the year?

Generally, your local tax is based on where you lived on the last day of the tax year. If you moved between Maryland counties, you usually use the county of residence on December 31. Check the Form 502 instructions for special cases.

How are Maryland tax credits applied?

Most Maryland credits first reduce your state income tax. Some credits also reduce local tax, and some are refundable. Because credit rules are complex, this calculator lets you enter a single combined credit amount and subtracts it from your total Maryland tax for a rough estimate.

Where can I find the official Maryland county tax tables?

The official tables and withholding instructions are published by the Maryland Comptroller. Employers also use state-issued withholding tables and bulletins to calculate paycheck withholding.
