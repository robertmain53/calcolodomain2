Data Source and Methodology

The calculations are based on data and formulas from the Australian Tax Office . All calculations are strictly based on the provided formulas and data.

The Formula Explained

\[\text{Tax Payable} = (\text{Income} - \text{Tax-free Threshold}) \times \text{Tax Rate} + \text{Medicare Levy}\]

Glossary of Variables

  • Income: Your total annual earnings.
  • Tax-free Threshold: The amount of income that is not taxed.
  • Tax Rate: The percentage of income taxed above the threshold.
  • Medicare Levy: An additional tax to fund the public health system.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For an income of $50,000, with a tax-free threshold of $18,200 and a tax rate of 19%, the calculation is as follows:

\[ \text{Tax Payable} = (\$50,000 - \$18,200) \times 0.19 + \text{Medicare Levy} \]

FAQs

What is the Medicare Levy?

The Medicare Levy is a tax to fund the public healthcare system in Australia.

How do I calculate my tax?

Enter your annual income and select whether you pay the Medicare Levy. The calculator will estimate your tax payable.

What is the tax-free threshold?

The tax-free threshold is the amount of income you can earn before you start paying tax.
