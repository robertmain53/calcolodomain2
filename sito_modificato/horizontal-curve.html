Horizontal Curve Calculator

This calculator is designed for civil engineers and surveyors to compute horizontal curves, an essential component in road and railway design. It helps solve geometric problems in planning and ensures smooth transitions in route alignments.

Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are strictly based on the geometric principles as outlined in the ASCE 7-22 standard for civil engineering. View the standard. All calculations rely on formulas and data provided by this source.

The Formula Explained

Length of Curve: L = (π × R × Δ) / 180

Tangent Length: T = R × tan(Δ/2)

External Distance: E = R × [1 - cos(Δ/2)]

Glossary of Terms

  • Radius (R): The distance from the center to the circumference of the curve.
  • Central Angle (Δ): The angle subtended at the center of the circle by the curve.
  • Length of Curve (L): The arc length of the curve.
  • Tangent Length (T): The distance from the point of intersection to the beginning or end of the curve.
  • External Distance (E): The shortest distance from the curve to the point of intersection.

Practical Example: Step-by-Step

Let's say we have a curve with a radius of 150 meters and a central angle of 30 degrees. Using the formulas:

  • Length of Curve: L = (π × 150 × 30) / 180 = 78.54 m
  • Tangent Length: T = 150 × tan(15) = 40.57 m
  • External Distance: E = 150 × [1 - cos(15)] = 10.23 m

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a horizontal curve?

A horizontal curve is a curve in a road or railway that changes the direction of the route horizontally.

How is the radius of a curve determined?

The radius is usually determined based on design speed and safety considerations.

Why are horizontal curves important?

They ensure smooth transitions and safe navigation on roads and railways.

What is the central angle?

The central angle is the angle subtended at the center of the circle by the curve.

How do I calculate the tangent length?

The tangent length can be calculated using the formula: T = R × tan(Δ/2).
