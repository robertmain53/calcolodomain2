Formula

The conversion from meters to feet uses the following formula:

\[ \text{feet} = \text{meters} \times 3.28084 \]

This conversion factor is derived from the fact that 1 meter = 39.37 inches, and since 1 foot = 12 inches, we have 1 meter = 3.28084 feet.

Data Source and Methodology

This converter uses the internationally accepted conversion between metric and imperial units:

  • 1 meter = 3.28084 feet
  • This is derived from: 1 meter = 39.37 inches, and 1 foot = 12 inches
  • Therefore: 1 meter = 39.37 ÷ 12 = 3.28084 feet
  • This conversion is used worldwide in engineering, construction, and scientific applications

Step-by-Step Example

Problem: Convert 5 meters to feet.

Step 1: Write down the formula

\[ \text{feet} = \text{meters} \times 3.28084 \]

Step 2: Substitute the value

\[ \text{feet} = 5 \times 3.28084 \]

Step 3: Calculate the result

\[ \text{feet} = 16.4042 \text{ ft} \]

Answer: 5 meters = 16.40 feet

Glossary of Terms

Meter (m)
The base unit of length in the International System of Units (SI). Commonly used worldwide for measuring distances.
Foot (ft)
An imperial unit of length equal to 12 inches or 0.3048 meters. Commonly used in the United States for construction and everyday measurements.

A number used to convert one set of units to another. For meters to feet, the conversion factor is 3.28084.
