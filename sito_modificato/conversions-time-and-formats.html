How this time & format converter works

This tool keeps a single internal representation of time and updates all other units and formats whenever you change one field. For durations, the internal value is total seconds. For calendar dates, it is a JavaScript Date object (milliseconds since Unix epoch).

Duration conversion formulas

From hours, minutes, seconds to total seconds
\( \text{totalSeconds} = 3600 \cdot h + 60 \cdot m + s \)
From total seconds to h:mm:ss
\( h = \left\lfloor \dfrac{\text{totalSeconds}}{3600} \right\rfloor \)
\( m = \left\lfloor \dfrac{\text{totalSeconds} - 3600h}{60} \right\rfloor \)
\( s = \text{totalSeconds} - 3600h - 60m \)
Decimal hours
\( \text{decimalHours} = \dfrac{\text{totalSeconds}}{3600} \)

Unix time and calendar dates

A Unix timestamp in seconds is related to the internal millisecond count by:

\( \text{unixSeconds} = \left\lfloor \dfrac{\text{msSinceEpoch}}{1000} \right\rfloor \)
\( \text{msSinceEpoch} = 1000 \cdot \text{unixSeconds} \)

Here, epoch is 1970‑01‑01 00:00:00 UTC. Most programming languages (Python, JavaScript, C, Java, .NET) use this convention.

ISO 8601 basics

  • YYYY-MM-DD – calendar date (e.g. 2025-03-01)
  • YYYY-MM-DDThh:mm:ss – local date and time (no time zone)
  • YYYY-MM-DDThh:mm:ssZ – UTC time (Z = Zulu = UTC)
  • YYYY-MM-DDThh:mm:ss+02:00 – local time with numeric offset

The calculator can generate both local and UTC ISO 8601 strings from the same instant in time.

FAQ

What is a Unix timestamp?

A Unix timestamp is the number of seconds since 1970‑01‑01 00:00:00 UTC (ignoring leap seconds). It is a simple integer that many systems use to store and compare times.

What is the difference between UTC and local time?

UTC is a global reference time that does not change with time zones or daylight saving time. Local time is UTC plus or minus an offset depending on your region and DST rules. This calculator shows both where relevant.

Can I use this for programming date/time formats?

Yes. The ISO 8601 strings and Unix timestamps produced here are compatible with most languages and databases. The custom pattern preview helps you design formats similar to those used in libraries like strftime or .NET format strings.
