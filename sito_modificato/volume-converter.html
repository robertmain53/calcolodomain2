Glossary of Terms

  • Gallons: A unit of volume for liquid measure equal to four quarts, in particular.
  • Liters: A metric unit of capacity, formerly defined as the volume of one kilogram of water under standard conditions, now equal to 1,000 cubic cm (about 1.75 pints).
  • Cubic Feet: A unit of volume (symbol ft³) used in the United States and United Kingdom.
  • Cubic Meters: The SI unit of volume, symbol m³, equal to 1,000 liters.

How it Works: A Step-by-Step Example

To convert 10 gallons to liters, the formula is: 10 gallons × 3.78541 = 37.8541 liters .
