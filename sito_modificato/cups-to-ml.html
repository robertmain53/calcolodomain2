Which Cup Are You Using? US vs. Legal vs. Metric

The "cup" is one of the most confusing units of measurement for cooking. The correct conversion depends entirely on where your recipe or measuring cup is from.

  • US Customary Cup (~236.59 mL): This is the standard measuring cup used for cooking in the United States. It is technically defined as half a US liquid pint. When a US recipe calls for a "cup," it means this one.
  • US Legal Cup (240 mL): This cup is defined by the FDA for nutrition labeling in the US. It's slightly larger and rounded for convenience.
  • Metric Cup (250 mL): This is the standard used in most of the rest of the world, including the UK, Australia, New Zealand, and Canada. It is equal to one-quarter of a liter (1000 mL / 4 = 250 mL).

Always check your recipe! A US recipe (like for American pancakes) will use the ~236.59 mL cup, while an Australian or UK recipe (like for scones) will use the 250 mL cup.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is 1 cup in mL?

It depends on which cup you are using:

  • 1 US Customary Cup = 236.588 mL (most common for US recipes)
  • 1 US Legal Cup = 240 mL (used for US nutritional labeling)
  • 1 Metric Cup = 250 mL (common in UK, Australia, Canada, and NZ)

Is 1 cup 240ml or 250ml?

Both can be correct. 1 US Legal Cup is exactly 240 mL, which is used for nutrition and food labeling in the United States. 1 Metric Cup is exactly 250 mL, which is the standard in many countries, including Australia, New Zealand, and the UK. For everyday US cooking, the "US Customary Cup" (236.588 mL) is the standard, which is often rounded to 240 mL for simplicity.

How many mL is half a US cup?

Using the standard US Customary cup, half a cup is 1/2 * 236.588 mL = 118.294 mL. This is often rounded to 120 mL in recipes.

How do I convert 1/3 cup to mL?

For a US Customary cup: 1/3 * 236.588 mL = 78.86 mL.
For a Metric cup: 1/3 * 250 mL = 83.33 mL.
