Pulley speed formula

For a simple belt drive with one driver and one driven pulley, the belt speed is the same on both pulleys (ignoring slip). This gives the fundamental pulley speed relationship:

Belt speed equality:

v = π · D₁ · N₁ = π · D₂ · N₂

Rearranging:

N₂ = N₁ · (D₁ / D₂)
D₂ = D₁ · (N₁ / N₂)

Speed ratio

The speed ratio of a belt drive is the ratio of driver speed to driven speed:

Speed ratio R = N₁ / N₂ = D₂ / D₁

  • R > 1 → speed reduction (driven pulley slower, torque increased).
  • R < 1 → speed increase (driven pulley faster, torque reduced).

Example: motor and compressor pulley

Suppose you have a 1750 RPM motor with a 3 in driver pulley, and you want about 1000 RPM on the compressor.

  1. Given: N₁ = 1750 RPM, D₁ = 3 in, N₂ ≈ 1000 RPM.
  2. Required driven pulley diameter: D₂ = D₁ · (N₁ / N₂)
  3. Compute: D₂ = 3 × (1750 / 1000) ≈ 5.25 in

In practice you would choose the nearest available pulley size (e.g. 5.25 in or 5.5 in) and re-check the resulting RPM with this calculator.

Multi-stage pulley trains

When you have more than one belt stage (for example, a jackshaft), each stage has its own ratio. The overall speed ratio is the product of the stage ratios:

Rtotal = R₁ × R₂ × R₃ × …
Nout = N₁ / Rtotal

The multi-stage tab in this tool lets you enter up to three stages and instantly see the final output speed.

Approximate belt length formula

For two pulleys with diameters D₁ and D₂ and center distance C, an approximate open-belt length is:

L ≈ 2C + (π / 2) · (D₁ + D₂) + (D₂ − D₁)² / (4C)

This is the formula used in the belt length tab. It is accurate when the center distance is reasonably larger than the pulley diameters. For very short center distances or idler pulleys, consult manufacturer data or CAD.

Practical tips for pulley speed design

  • Check the maximum safe RPM for each pulley from the manufacturer.
  • Keep belt speed within recommended limits for the belt type to avoid excessive wear.
  • Allow a few percent margin for belt slip and motor speed variation.
  • After installation, verify actual RPM with a tachometer for critical machinery.

FAQ

How do I slow down a driven machine?

To reduce driven RPM, you can either increase the driven pulley diameter or decrease the driver pulley diameter. Both actions increase the speed ratio N₁ / N₂.

Does belt thickness affect pulley speed?

Yes, slightly. The effective pitch diameter is slightly larger than the bare pulley diameter because the belt rides on top. For most practical calculations, using the pulley outside diameter is sufficient, but precision systems may use pitch diameters from manufacturer data.

Can I mix units for pulley diameters?

No. All diameters in a given calculation must use the same unit (all mm or all inches). RPM is independent of length units, so you can freely enter RPM in any consistent way.
