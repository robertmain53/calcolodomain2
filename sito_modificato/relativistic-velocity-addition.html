The Formula Explained

\( w = \frac{u + v}{1 + \frac{uv}{c^2}} \)

Glossary of Variables

  • u: First velocity, as a fraction of the speed of light (c).
  • v: Second velocity, as a fraction of the speed of light (c).
  • w: Resultant velocity, as a fraction of the speed of light (c).

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Consider two objects moving with velocities \( u = 0.5c \) and \( v = 0.4c \). Using the formula:

w = \frac{0.5 + 0.4}{1 + \frac{0.5 \times 0.4}{1}} = \frac{0.9}{1.2} = 0.75c

The resultant velocity \( w \) is \( 0.75c \).

What is relativistic velocity addition?

It is the formula used to calculate the resultant velocity of two velocities in the context of special relativity.

Why is relativistic velocity addition important?

It is crucial in understanding motion at speeds close to the speed of light.

No, according to Einstein's theory of relativity, the resultant velocity will always be less than the speed of light.
