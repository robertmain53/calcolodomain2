Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the standard formula for Player Efficiency Rating (PER) as provided by official basketball statistics guidelines.

The Formula Explained

The PER formula is a complex calculation that includes a player's positive accomplishments (points, assists, rebounds, steals, blocks) and negative accomplishments (missed shots, turnovers).

Glossary of Variables

  • Points: The total number of points scored by the player.
  • Rebounds: The total number of rebounds secured by the player.
  • Assists: Total assists provided by the player.
  • Steals: The number of times the player has successfully taken the ball from an opponent.
  • Blocks: The number of times the player has blocked an opponent's shot.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Imagine a player who scores 20 points, 10 rebounds, 5 assists, 2 steals, and 1 block. By plugging these values into the PER formula, you can derive the player's efficiency rating.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is PER?

PER is a rating that sums up all of a player's statistical accomplishments in a single number.

How is PER used?

PER is used to compare the overall efficiency of players in a standardized format.

Why is PER important?

PER provides a comprehensive look at a player's performance, accounting for both positive and negative contributions.
