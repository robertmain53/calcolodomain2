A Body Shape Index (ABSI) Calculator

This calculator helps you determine your A Body Shape Index (ABSI), a metric used to assess health risks related to your body shape. It is particularly useful for individuals who want to understand how their body composition affects their health.

Data Source and Methodology

The ABSI calculation is based on the formula developed by Dr. Nir Krakauer. For more detailed information, visit the ABSI Calculator official site. All calculations are based on the formula and data provided by this source.

The Formula Explained

\[ ABSI = \frac{Waist \ Circumference}{BMI^{\frac{2}{3}} \times Age^{\frac{1}{2}}} \]

Glossary of Terms

  • Waist Circumference: The measurement around your waist.
  • BMI: Body Mass Index, a measure of body fat based on height and weight.
  • Age: Your age in years.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Consider a person with a waist circumference of 85 cm, BMI of 24, and age of 30 years. Using the ABSI formula, we calculate the ABSI value as follows:

ABSI = 85 / (24^(2/3) * 30^(1/2)) = 0.0800

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is A Body Shape Index (ABSI)?

ABSI is a metric for assessing the health risk posed by abdominal obesity.

Why is ABSI important?

ABSI helps identify the risk of health issues related to waist size and body shape, beyond what BMI can indicate.
