Anion Gap Calculator

The Anion Gap Calculator is a vital tool for healthcare professionals to assess the balance of electrolytes in the blood, helping to diagnose metabolic acidosis.

Anion Gap: -

Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are rigorously based on clinical formulas provided by authoritative medical resources. Reference: "Clinical Chemistry" by William J. Marshall. View Source

The Formula Explained

The Anion Gap is calculated as:
\[ \text{Anion Gap} = \text{Na}^+ - (\text{Cl}^- + \text{HCO}_3^-) \]

Glossary of Terms

  • Sodium (Na⁺): A key electrolyte in the blood.
  • Chloride (Cl⁻): An electrolyte that helps maintain fluid balance.
  • Bicarbonate (HCO₃⁻): A form of carbon dioxide in the blood.
  • Anion Gap: The difference between primary measured cations and anions.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the anion gap?

The anion gap is a measurement used to identify the causes of metabolic acidosis.

How do you calculate the anion gap?

The anion gap is calculated using the formula: Na⁺ - (Cl⁻ + HCO₃⁻).

Why is the anion gap important?

It helps in diagnosing the underlying cause of metabolic acidosis.

What is a normal anion gap range?

The normal range is typically between 8 and 12 mmol/L.

What does a high anion gap mean?

A high anion gap can indicate conditions such as lactic acidosis, ketoacidosis, or renal failure.
