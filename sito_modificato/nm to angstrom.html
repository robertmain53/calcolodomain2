How to Convert Nanometers to Angstroms

The conversion between nanometers (nm) and angstroms (Å) is a simple factor of 10. This conversion is extremely common in physics, chemistry, and materials science for measuring atomic-scale distances and wavelengths of light.

The Conversion Formula

The relationship is fixed and exact:

1 Nanometer (nm) = 10 Angstroms (Å)

Therefore, to convert from nanometers to angstroms, you **multiply the nanometer value by 10**.

  • Formula: Å = nm × 10
  • Example: 550 nm × 10 = 5500 Å

To convert from angstroms to nanometers, you **divide the angstrom value by 10**.

  • Formula: nm = Å / 10
  • Example: 1 Å / 10 = 0.1 nm

The Scale of Metric Prefixes

Understanding where these units fit in the metric system is key. Each of these units is 1,000 times smaller than the one before it, with the Angstrom as a special case.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How many angstroms are in 1 nm?

There are exactly **10 angstroms (Å)** in 1 nanometer (nm).

Which is smaller, angstrom or nanometer?

An **angstrom (Å)** is smaller. It is one-tenth the size of a nanometer (1 Å = 0.1 nm).

What is 1 nanometer in meters (scientific notation)?

1 nanometer is equal to 1×10-9 meters (or 1e-9 m), which is one-billionth of a meter.
