What does “automotive” mean?

In technical and economic contexts, automotive refers to everything related to motor vehicles: passenger cars, commercial vehicles, motorcycles, buses, trucks and the entire ecosystem that designs, builds, sells, maintains and regulates them.

While dictionaries focus on the definition, this page turns the concept into practice: concrete calculators that help you plan trips, control costs and understand how your vehicle behaves on the road.

Key areas covered by our automotive tools

  • Fuel & efficiency – MPG, L/100 km, fuel cost and trip planning.
  • Vehicle setup – tire size, gear ratios, engine displacement, horsepower.
  • Ownership costs – depreciation, insurance, loans and total cost of ownership.
  • Maintenance – oil change intervals and service planning.

Core formulas used in automotive calculations

Fuel used from distance and MPG

\(\text{Fuel (gallons)} = \dfrac{\text{Distance (miles)}}{\text{MPG}}\)

Fuel cost

\(\text{Cost} = \text{Fuel used} \times \text{Price per unit}\)

Convert MPG ↔ L/100 km

For US MPG:

\(\text{L/100 km} \approx \dfrac{235.215}{\text{MPG (US)}}\)

For UK MPG:

\(\text{L/100 km} \approx \dfrac{282.481}{\text{MPG (UK)}}\)

Engine power from torque

In imperial units:

\(\text{HP} = \dfrac{\text{Torque (lb·ft)} \times \text{RPM}}{5252}\)

In SI units:

\(\text{kW} = \dfrac{\text{Torque (N·m)} \times 2\pi \times \text{RPM}}{60 \times 1000}\)

Best practices when using automotive calculators

  • Always compare results with your vehicle’s owner’s manual and official specifications.
  • For safety-critical decisions (brakes, load limits, tuning) consult a qualified mechanic or engineer.
  • Remember that real-world fuel consumption and performance depend on driving style, traffic, weather and load.

Automotive FAQ

Is this page a dictionary definition of “automotive”?

No. We briefly explain the term, but the main purpose is practical: giving you calculators and tools to work with real automotive data such as fuel, distances, costs and performance.

Can I use these tools for professional work?

Many professionals (mechanics, fleet managers, engineers) use similar formulas daily. Our tools implement standard equations, but they are provided for educational and planning purposes and should be checked against applicable standards, regulations and manufacturer data before professional use.

Do these calculators support metric and imperial units?

Yes. Most automotive tools on CalcDomain support both metric and imperial units (miles/km, gallons/liters, lb·ft/N·m, etc.) and allow quick conversion between them.
