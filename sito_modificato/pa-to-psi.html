How the Pa to psi conversion works

Pascals (Pa) are the SI unit of pressure, defined as one newton per square meter. Pounds per square inch (psi) are commonly used in the US and UK for tire pressure, hydraulics, compressed air and many industrial systems.

Supported pressure units

  • Pa – pascals (base SI unit)
  • kPa – kilopascals, 1 kPa = 1,000 Pa
  • MPa – megapascals, 1 MPa = 1,000,000 Pa
  • bar – convenient engineering unit, 1 bar = 100,000 Pa ≈ 14.5038 psi
  • atm – standard atmosphere, 1 atm = 101,325 Pa ≈ 14.6959 psi
  • psi – pounds per square inch

Worked examples

Example 1 – 1 bar to psi

1 bar = 100,000 Pa. Using psi = Pa ÷ 6894.757:

Example 2 – 30 psi to Pa

Using Pa = psi × 6894.757:

Gauge vs absolute pressure

The converter itself is purely mathematical: it assumes a straightforward unit conversion. In practice, you should distinguish between:

  • Gauge pressure (psig, barg) – measured relative to ambient atmospheric pressure.
  • Absolute pressure (psia, bara) – measured relative to perfect vacuum.

As long as you are consistent (both values gauge or both absolute), the numerical conversion between Pa and psi is the same. What changes is the reference point, not the factor.

FAQ – Pa to psi conversions

How do you convert pascals (Pa) to PSI?

Divide the pressure in pascals by 6,894.757. The formula is: psi = Pa ÷ 6894.757.

How do you convert PSI to pascals (Pa)?

Multiply the pressure in PSI by 6,894.757. The formula is: Pa = psi × 6894.757.

What is the difference between Pa, kPa, bar, and PSI?

Pa, kPa and MPa are SI-based pressure units, bar is a convenient engineering unit, and PSI is a non-SI unit based on pounds of force per square inch of area. They all measure the same physical quantity but on different scales.
