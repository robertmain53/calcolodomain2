How this typing speed calculator works

This typing speed calculator measures how fast and how accurately you type a given passage. It tracks every keystroke in real time and computes:

  • Words per minute (WPM) – your typing speed based on a standard 5-character word.
  • Characters per minute (CPM) – raw speed including spaces and punctuation.
  • Accuracy (%) – how many of your keystrokes match the target text.
  • Error count – total incorrect keystrokes.
  • Backspaces – how often you corrected mistakes.

Formulas used

1. Words per minute (WPM)

We use the standard definition of one word = 5 characters (including spaces and punctuation):

words = total_characters_typed / 5
minutes = elapsed_time_seconds / 60
WPM = words / minutes

2. Characters per minute (CPM)

CPM = total_characters_typed / minutes

3. Accuracy (%)

Accuracy = (correct_keystrokes / total_keystrokes) × 100

What is a good typing speed?

  • < 30 WPM – beginner. Focus on accuracy and proper finger placement.
  • 30–40 WPM – basic everyday typing.
  • 40–60 WPM – average office worker.
  • 60–80 WPM – fast; comfortable for most professional roles.
  • > 100 WPM – very fast; typical of experienced typists and programmers.

How to use the typing speed calculator

  1. Choose a test duration (15 seconds to 5 minutes).
  2. Select Random text or switch to Custom text and paste your own passage.
  3. Click Start test or simply begin typing in the input box.
  4. Type the text exactly as shown. The timer starts with your first keystroke.
  5. When time is up, review your WPM, accuracy, CPM, and error stats.
  6. Click Reset to try again or New random text for a different passage.

Tips to improve your typing speed

  • Prioritize accuracy first. Speed naturally increases as you make fewer mistakes.
  • Use all ten fingers and keep them on the home row (ASDF / JKL;).
  • Look at the screen, not the keyboard. This reduces eye movement and improves rhythm.
  • Practice regularly with short 1–2 minute tests instead of rare long sessions.
  • Vary the content – practice with emails, code, numbers, or language you use at work.

FAQ

Does this test count corrected mistakes?

Yes. Every keystroke (including mistakes) is counted. Backspace itself is not counted as a character, but the incorrect characters you typed before correcting them still affect your accuracy and effective speed.

Why is my WPM different from other websites?

Different typing tests may:

  • Use different word definitions (4, 5, or variable characters per word).
  • Ignore or include corrected mistakes.
  • Use fixed-length texts vs. time-based tests.

This calculator uses the common 5-character word definition and a time-based test, which is comparable to most professional typing benchmarks.

Can I use this for job or data entry practice?

Yes. Use the Custom text mode to paste sample emails, support scripts, or data entry templates. This gives you a more realistic measure of your on-the-job typing speed.
