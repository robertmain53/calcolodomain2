Formula for the volume of a cylinder

The volume of a right circular cylinder is given by the classic formula:

\( V = \pi r^2 h \)

Where:

  • \( V \) = volume
  • \( r \) = radius of the circular base
  • \( h \) = height (length) of the cylinder

If you only know the diameter \( d \), recall that \( r = d / 2 \). Substitute into the main formula to get:

\( V = \pi \left(\frac{d}{2}\right)^2 h = \frac{\pi d^2 h}{4} \)

This is the version most tank / pipe dimensioning tools use.

Example: cylinder volume in liters

Suppose you have a cylinder with diameter 40 cm and height 100 cm.

  1. Radius = 40 / 2 = 20 cm
  2. Volume in cm³: \( V = \pi \times 20^2 \times 100 = \pi \times 400 \times 100 = 125,663.7 \text{ cm}^3 \) (approx)
  3. 1 cm³ = 1 mL, so this is 125,663.7 mL = 125.66 L

The calculator above does these conversions automatically for you.
