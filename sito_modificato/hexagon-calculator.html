The Formulas Explained

All properties of a regular hexagon can be derived from the length of one side, denoted as $s$.

Area (A)

The area is calculated by multiplying the perimeter by the apothem and dividing by two. This simplifies to:

$ A = \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2} s^2 $

Perimeter (P)

The perimeter is the sum of its six equal sides:

$ P = 6s $

Apothem (a)

The apothem is the distance from the center to the midpoint of a side. It forms a 30-60-90 right triangle with the radius and half the side length.

$ a = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} s $

Diagonals (d)

A hexagon has two types of diagonals:

  • Long Diagonal ($d_l$): Connects two opposite vertices and passes through the center. Its length is exactly twice the side length.
  • Short Diagonal ($d_s$): Connects two vertices, skipping one vertex in between.
$ d_l = 2s $
$ d_s = \sqrt{3} s $

Glossary of Variables

  • Side ($s$): The length of one of the six equal sides.
  • Area ($A$): The total two-dimensional space enclosed by the hexagon.
  • Perimeter ($P$): The total length of the hexagon's boundary (the sum of all sides).
  • Apothem ($a$): The perpendicular distance from the center of the hexagon to the midpoint of a side.
  • Long Diagonal ($d_l$): The distance between two opposite vertices. This is the longest possible distance across the hexagon.
  • Short Diagonal ($d_s$): The distance between two vertices that are separated by a single vertex.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Let's say you have a regular hexagon and you only know its side length ($s$) is 10 units .

  1. Select Input: You choose "Side (s)" from the dropdown and enter "10" in the value field.
  2. Perimeter (P):
    $ P = 6 \times s = 6 \times 10 = 60 $ units.
  3. Apothem (a):
    $ a = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \times s \approx \frac{1.732}{2} \times 10 \approx 8.66 $ units.
  4. Area (A):
    $ A = \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2} \times s^2 \approx 2.59807 \times 10^2 \approx 259.81 $ square units.
  5. Long Diagonal ($d_l$):
    $ d_l = 2 \times s = 2 \times 10 = 20 $ units.
  6. Short Diagonal ($d_s$):
    $ d_s = \sqrt{3} \times s \approx 1.732 \times 10 \approx 17.32 $ units.

The calculator performs these calculations instantly, and can also reverse the process. For example, if you enter an Area of 259.81 , it will solve for $s$ first: $ s = \sqrt{\frac{2A}{3\sqrt{3}}} $, find $s \approx 10$, and then calculate all other properties from there.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a regular hexagon?

A regular hexagon is a six-sided polygon (a 2D shape) where all six sides have the same length, and all six internal angles are equal (120°). This calculator only works for regular hexagons.

Can I calculate all properties from just the area?

Yes. The calculator is designed to work from any single known property. If you provide the area, it will first solve the area formula for the side length ($s$) and then use that side length to compute the perimeter, apothem, and diagonals.

What is the difference between an apothem and a radius?

The apothem is the distance from the center to the midpoint of a side . The radius (or circumradius) is the distance from the center to a vertex (corner) . In a regular hexagon, the radius is always equal to the side length ($s$).

How many diagonals does a hexagon have?

A hexagon has a total of 9 diagonals. These are divided into two groups: 6 short diagonals (the $d_s$ value) and 3 long diagonals (the $d_l$ value).

What unit will the results be in?

The calculations are unit-agnostic. The results will be in the same base unit as your input. For example, if you enter a side length in centimeters (cm), the perimeter, apothem, and diagonals will also be in cm. The area will be in square centimeters (cm²).
