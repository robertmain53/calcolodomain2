Data Source and Methodology

This calculator provides an estimate of your daily macronutrient needs based on established scientific formulas. All calculations for Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) are strictly based on the **Mifflin-St Jeor equation**, which is widely regarded as one of the most accurate methods for predicting resting energy expenditure.

  • Authoritative Source: Mifflin, M. D., St Jeor, S. T., Hill, L. A., Scott, B. J., Daugherty, S. A., & Koh, Y. O. (1990). A new predictive equation for resting energy expenditure in healthy individuals. The American journal of clinical nutrition, 51(2), 241–247.
  • Reference: https://doi.org/10.1093/ajcn/51.2.241

The Formulas Explained

Your carbohydrate needs are not calculated in isolation. They are part of your total daily energy expenditure (TDEE). Here is the 4-step process:

  1. Calculate BMR: First, we find your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) using the Mifflin-St Jeor formula.

    Men: $BMR = (10 \times \text{weight in kg}) + (6.25 \times \text{height in cm}) - (5 \times \text{age in years}) + 5$

    Women: $BMR = (10 \times \text{weight in kg}) + (6.25 \times \text{height in cm}) - (5 \times \text{age in years}) - 161$

  2. Calculate TDEE: Next, we find your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) by multiplying your BMR by your chosen activity level.

    $TDEE = BMR \times \text{Activity Factor}$

  3. Adjust for Goal: We adjust your TDEE calories based on your goal (e.g., subtracting 500 calories for 1 lb/week weight loss).

    $Goal \text{ Calories} = TDEE + \text{Goal Adjustment}$

  4. Calculate Carbs: Finally, we calculate your carbohydrate grams based on a percentage of your Goal Calories. We use the fact that 1 gram of carbohydrate = 4 calories.

    $Carbohydrates \text{ (g)} = \frac{Goal \text{ Calories} \times \% \text{Carbs}}{4}$

Glossary of Variables

  • BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate): The number of calories your body needs to function at complete rest (e.g., if you stayed in bed all day).
  • TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure): Your BMR plus all the calories you burn from physical activity, digestion, and daily living. This is your "maintenance" calorie level.
  • Activity Factor: A multiplier that represents your average daily activity level.
  • Goal Adjustment: The number of calories added or subtracted from your TDEE to achieve your goal (e.g., a 500-calorie deficit to lose ~1 lb per week).
  • Macronutrients: The three main nutrients that provide energy: Carbohydrates (4 cal/g), Protein (4 cal/g), and Fat (9 cal/g).

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Let's walk through an example for Alex, a 30-year-old male who is 180 cm tall, weighs 80 kg, is "Moderately Active," and wants to "Maintain Weight."

  1. Calculate BMR (Male):
    $BMR = (10 \times 80) + (6.25 \times 180) - (5 \times 30) + 5$
    $BMR = 800 + 1125 - 150 + 5 = \textbf{1780 \text{ calories}}$
  2. Calculate TDEE:
    The "Moderately Active" factor is 1.55.
    $TDEE = 1780 \times 1.55 = \textbf{2759 \text{ calories}}$
  3. Adjust for Goal:
    The "Maintain Weight" goal has a 0-calorie adjustment.
    $Goal \text{ Calories} = 2759 + 0 = \textbf{2759 \text{ calories}}$
  4. Calculate Carbs (Balanced Plan):
    The "Balanced" plan allocates 40% of calories to carbs.
    $Carbs \text{ (g)} = (2759 \times 0.40) / 4 = 1103.6 / 4 \approx \textbf{276 \text{ g of carbs}}$

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why use the Mifflin-St Jeor equation?

The Mifflin-St Jeor equation, published in 1990, is considered by many nutrition experts and dietitians to be the most accurate formula for estimating Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) in healthy adults, often outperforming the older Harris-Benedict equation.

What is the difference between BMR and TDEE?

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body needs to perform basic, life-sustaining functions at rest. TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is your BMR plus the calories you burn from all other activities, including exercise and digestion. Your TDEE is the total number of calories you burn in a day, also known as your "maintenance" level.

How accurate is this calculator?

This calculator provides a high-quality, scientifically-backed *estimate*. Your true metabolic rate can be influenced by factors not included here, such as body composition (muscle vs. fat), genetics, and hormonal health. Use this number as a starting point and adjust based on your real-world results (e.g., if you aren't losing weight on a "Weight Loss" setting, your TDEE may be slightly lower).

How many carbs should I eat to lose weight?

To lose weight, you must be in a calorie deficit. This calculator first determines your maintenance calories (TDEE) and then subtracts calories based on your weight loss goal (e.g., -500 calories for 1 lb/week). It then calculates your carb intake as a percentage of those *reduced* calories. A balanced or low-carb plan is often used for weight loss, as protein and fat can help with satiety.

What are 'net carbs' and does this calculator track them?

This calculator estimates *total* carbohydrates. 'Net carbs' are total carbs minus fiber and certain sugar alcohols. Net carbs are often tracked on specific diets like the ketogenic (keto) diet. To find your net carb goal, you would need to subtract your daily fiber goal (e.g., 25-30g) from the total carb number provided here.

Why is protein important when calculating carbs?

Carbohydrates are just one piece of the puzzle. Protein is essential for repairing and building muscle, especially when in a calorie deficit for weight loss. Fat is crucial for hormone production and nutrient absorption. This is why we provide full macronutrient plans, so you can see how your carb intake fits with adequate protein and fat.

Is a low-carb or high-carb plan better?

The 'best' plan depends entirely on your goals, activity, and personal preferences.
• High-Carb plans are often favored by endurance athletes who need readily available fuel.
• Low-Carb plans can be effective for weight loss and managing blood sugar.
• Balanced plans are a sustainable, moderate approach for general health.
The most effective diet is one you can adhere to consistently.
