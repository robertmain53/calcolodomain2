What this due date calculator does

This due date calculator lets you quickly add or subtract any combination of years, months, weeks, and days from a starting date. It automatically handles:

  • Different month lengths (28–31 days)
  • Leap years (February 29)
  • Large intervals (e.g., several years at once)

It’s useful for:

  • Finding invoice or payment due dates
  • Calculating contract or warranty end dates
  • Estimating pregnancy due dates (LMP + 280 days)
  • Planning project milestones and deadlines

How the calculation works

Conceptually, the calculator:

  1. Starts from the selected date.
  2. Adds or subtracts years and months using calendar logic.
  3. Then adds or subtracts the total number of days (weeks × 7 + days).

Step 1 – Convert weeks to days

\(\text{totalDays} = 7 \times \text{weeks} + \text{days}\)

Step 2 – Apply operation

If you choose Add:

\(\text{Result Date} = \text{Start Date} + \text{years, months, totalDays}\)

If you choose Subtract:

\(\text{Result Date} = \text{Start Date} - \text{years, months, totalDays}\)

In code, this is done with the built-in date object, which correctly handles month rollovers and leap years.

Example: 90-day payment term

Suppose an invoice is issued on March 15, 2025 with a 90-day payment term.

  • Start date = 15 March 2025
  • Operation = Add
  • Years = 0, Months = 0, Weeks = 12, Days = 6 (12×7 + 6 = 90)

The calculator returns the due date and weekday, so you can see exactly when payment is expected.

Example: Pregnancy due date (LMP method)

A common estimate for pregnancy due date is:

Due date ≈ LMP + 280 days (40 weeks)

If the first day of the last menstrual period (LMP) was 1 June 2025:

  • Start date = 1 June 2025
  • Operation = Add
  • Click the Pregnancy (280 days) preset

The calculator will show the estimated due date and weekday. Remember this is only an estimate; actual delivery can be earlier or later. Always consult a healthcare professional for medical decisions.

FAQ

Does this calculator skip weekends or holidays?

No. This tool uses calendar days, so weekends and holidays are counted. If you need to skip weekends or public holidays (for example, for business deadlines), use a dedicated business days or workdays calculator.

Can I enter negative numbers?

It’s clearer to choose Add or Subtract and keep the interval values non‑negative. The calculator will ignore negative inputs and treat them as zero.

What date format does the calculator use?

The date input uses your browser’s locale settings but internally everything is handled in ISO format (YYYY‑MM‑DD). This avoids ambiguity between day and month.

Is this tool suitable for legal deadlines?

It can help you compute calendar-based dates, but legal rules may define how to count days (e.g., excluding the start day, skipping weekends, or adjusting when the due date falls on a holiday). For legal or contractual matters, always verify the result against the applicable rules or consult a professional.
