        How the New Brunswick tax calculator works

        This tool estimates your New Brunswick provincial income tax together with federal income tax, Canada Pension Plan (CPP) contributions, and Employment Insurance (EI) premiums. It uses a progressive bracket system similar to the official Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) and Government of New Brunswick tables.

        1. Taxable income

        We start from your total income:

        Total income = Employment income + Other taxable income

        Taxable income ≈ Total income − RRSP & other deductions

        In reality, taxable income also reflects many other adjustments (business expenses, capital gains inclusion rate, etc.). This calculator focuses on the most common situation: employment income plus basic deductions.

        2. New Brunswick provincial tax brackets (illustrative)

        New Brunswick uses a progressive system: lower income is taxed at lower rates, and only the portion of income in each bracket is taxed at that bracket’s rate.

        The exact thresholds and rates change over time. The calculator uses approximate 2024–2025 values; always confirm current rates on the Government of New Brunswick and CRA websites.

        3. Federal tax brackets (illustrative)

        Federal income tax is calculated separately using Canada‑wide brackets. The calculator applies those brackets to your taxable income and then subtracts estimated non‑refundable credits.

        4. CPP and EI contributions

        CPP and EI are payroll contributions based on your employment income:

        • CPP: a percentage of pensionable earnings between a minimum and maximum.
        • EI: a percentage of insurable earnings up to a yearly maximum.

        Both CPP and EI generate federal and provincial non‑refundable tax credits. The calculator approximates this by reducing your net tax accordingly.

        5. Average vs. marginal tax rate

        • Average tax rate = Total tax and contributions ÷ Total income.
        • Marginal tax rate = Combined federal + provincial rate on your last dollar of income (excluding CPP/EI).

        Your marginal rate is what matters when you consider extra income or additional RRSP contributions.

        Example: New Brunswick tax on a $60,000 salary

        Suppose you earn $60,000 in employment income, no other income, and no RRSP contributions:

        1. Total income = $60,000
        2. Taxable income ≈ $60,000
        3. Federal tax: calculated using federal brackets, minus basic personal amount credit
        4. New Brunswick tax: calculated using NB brackets, minus NB basic personal amount credit
        5. CPP and EI: based on $60,000 employment income
        6. Net income = Total income − Federal tax − NB tax − CPP − EI

        Use the calculator above and adjust the RRSP field to see how contributions can reduce your tax bill and increase your refund.

        Limitations and important notes

        • This is an educational estimator, not an official tax tool.
        • It does not handle every situation (self‑employment CPP, capital gains, dividends, complex credits, etc.).
        • Rates and thresholds are approximate and may differ from the current year.
        • Always rely on CRA, the Government of New Brunswick, or a qualified tax professional for filing your return.

        Frequently asked questions about New Brunswick tax

        What taxes are included in this New Brunswick calculator?

        The calculator includes:

        • New Brunswick provincial income tax
        • Federal income tax
        • CPP contributions (employee portion)
        • EI premiums (employee portion)

        It does not include HST, property tax, or municipal taxes.

        Does New Brunswick have its own tax brackets?

        Yes. Each province and territory sets its own income tax brackets and rates. New Brunswick’s brackets apply to your taxable income after federal deductions and are calculated separately from federal tax. The calculator applies both sets of brackets to estimate your combined tax.

        How accurate is this New Brunswick tax calculator?

        The calculator is designed to give a reasonable estimate for common employment‑income situations using up‑to‑date brackets and standard credits. However, it cannot capture every rule, surtax, or credit. Treat the result as a helpful estimate, not an official assessment or tax advice.

        Where can I find the official New Brunswick tax rates?

        Official rates and thresholds are published by:

        • Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) – federal and provincial income tax rates
        • Government of New Brunswick – provincial tax information and bulletins

        Always check those sources for the latest numbers before making financial decisions.
