How this credit card validator works

This tool performs client-side validation of card numbers (also called PAN – Primary Account Number). It helps developers, testers, and support teams quickly check whether a card number is structurally valid before sending it to a payment gateway.

For every number you enter, the validator checks:

  • Luhn checksum – detects most typos and invalid numbers.
  • Card brand / network – Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover, JCB, UnionPay, Diners Club, and more.
  • Length rules – whether the number length is allowed for the detected brand.
  • BIN / IIN – the first 6 digits that identify the issuing network and bank family.
  • Formatting – ignores spaces and dashes, warns about non‑digit characters.

Luhn algorithm (mod 10) explained

Most major card networks use the Luhn algorithm to protect against accidental errors. It is a simple checksum over the digits of the card number.

  1. Strip all spaces and dashes from the card number.
  2. Starting from the rightmost digit (the check digit), move left.
  3. Double every second digit (positions 2, 4, 6, … from the right).
  4. If doubling gives a result > 9, subtract 9 from it (equivalent to adding the digits).
  5. Add up all transformed digits.
  6. If the total modulo 10 is 0, the number passes the Luhn check.

Mathematically, if the digits are \(d_0, d_1, \dots, d_{n-1}\) from right to left, then: 

Let f(k) = 2k if 2k < 10, otherwise 2k − 9
Sum = d_0 + f(d_1) + d_2 + f(d_3) + ...
Valid if Sum mod 10 = 0

Card brand detection rules

Card brands are identified by their prefix ranges (IIN/BIN) and allowed lengths. This validator uses commonly published patterns, including:

  • Visa – starts with 4, length 13, 16, or 19 digits.
  • Mastercard – starts with 51–55 or 2221–2720, length 16 digits.
  • American Express – starts with 34 or 37, length 15 digits.
  • Discover – starts with 6011, 65, 644–649, length 16–19 digits.
  • JCB – starts with 3528–3589, length 16 digits.
  • Diners Club – various ranges such as 300–305, 36, 38–39, typical length 14 digits.
  • UnionPay – typically starts with 62, length 16–19 digits.

Note that passing these checks does not mean the card is active or has funds. Only your payment processor or issuer can confirm that.

What this validator can and cannot tell you

This tool can help you:

  • Catch typos before sending data to a gateway.
  • Verify that your form or integration is formatting and sending card numbers correctly.
  • Generate and test against non‑live card numbers in development.

This tool cannot:

  • Confirm that a card is real, active, or not stolen.
  • Check available balance or authorization status.
  • Replace PCI‑compliant processing or tokenization.

Security & privacy

All validation is performed entirely in your browser using JavaScript. The card number you enter is not transmitted to CalcDomain or any third party.

Still, you should avoid entering real customer card numbers into any external tool unless required and compliant with PCI DSS. For production systems, always use your payment provider’s official SDKs and client‑side libraries.

FAQ

Is this credit card validator PCI compliant?

This page does not store or transmit card data, and it is intended for testing and educational purposes. However, PCI DSS compliance applies to your overall environment, not just a single page. For production payments, use PCI‑validated payment gateways and tokenization.

Why does a number pass Luhn but still fail at my payment gateway?

The Luhn algorithm only checks the internal consistency of the number. A gateway may still reject the card because it is expired, blocked, has insufficient funds, or fails additional fraud checks.

Can I use this to generate valid card numbers?

This tool does not generate card numbers. Some test numbers are provided above and are widely used in sandbox environments. Never attempt to generate or guess real card numbers.

Does this check CVV/CVC or expiry dates?

No. This validator focuses on the card number (PAN) only. CVV/CVC and expiry validation must be handled in your checkout flow or by your payment provider.

Can I integrate this validator into my own site?

Yes. The logic is simple to implement in any language. You can adapt the Luhn algorithm and brand detection rules described above into your own forms or backend services.
