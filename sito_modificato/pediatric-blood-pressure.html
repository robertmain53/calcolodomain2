This calculator is designed for healthcare professionals to determine the blood pressure percentile of children and adolescents according to age, gender, and height. It helps in identifying hypertension or hypotension in pediatric patients.

Data Source and Methodology

Calculations are based on the "Fourth Report on the Diagnosis, Evaluation, and Treatment of High Blood Pressure in Children and Adolescents" published by the National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute.

All calculations are strictly based on the methods and data provided by this source. View Source .

The Formula Explained

The calculation uses percentile values to determine where a child's blood pressure falls in comparison to peers. The formula is expressed as:

Percentile Calculation: P = f(age, gender, height, systolic, diastolic)

Glossary of Terms

  • Age: The child's age in complete years.
  • Gender: Biological sex of the child.
  • Height: The child's height measured in centimeters.
  • Systolic BP: The pressure in blood vessels when the heart beats.
  • Diastolic BP: The pressure in blood vessels when the heart rests between beats.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For a 10-year-old male with a height of 140 cm, systolic BP of 110 mm Hg, and diastolic BP of 70 mm Hg, the calculator determines the percentile to help assess the child's blood pressure status.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a blood pressure percentile?

A measurement that compares a child's blood pressure to other children of the same age, gender, and height.

Why is it important to monitor blood pressure in children?

To detect potential hypertension early, which can lead to long-term health issues if untreated.

How accurate is this calculator?

This tool is based on standard tables from authoritative medical guidelines, ensuring accuracy.

Can I use this calculator for adults?

No, this calculator is specifically designed for pediatric blood pressure assessment.

Where can I find more information?

Refer to the National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute's resources for comprehensive guidelines.
