The Formula Explained

The root locus is plotted by varying the gain K in the transfer function:

G(s)H(s) = K * (numerator) / (denominator)

Glossary of Terms

  • Numerator: The polynomial in the transfer function's numerator.
  • Denominator: The polynomial in the transfer function's denominator.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a Root Locus?

Root locus is a graphical method used in control systems to determine the stability of the system as a parameter is varied.

How do I interpret the results?

Look for changes in the plot as gain varies; stability is indicated by poles in the left-half of the complex plane.
