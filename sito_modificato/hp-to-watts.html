How It Works

Horsepower is a unit of power originally defined by James Watt to compare steam engine output to the power of draft horses. The mechanical horsepower (also called imperial horsepower) equals 745.7 watts, while the metric horsepower equals 735.5 watts. This calculator uses mechanical horsepower.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is horsepower?

Horsepower is a unit of power measuring the rate of work. One mechanical horsepower equals 33,000 foot-pounds per minute, or 745.7 watts in SI units.

How many watts are in 1 horsepower?

1 mechanical horsepower (HP) equals 745.7 watts. This is the standard used in the United States and most English-speaking countries for engine ratings.

What's the difference between mechanical and metric horsepower?

Mechanical horsepower (745.7 W) is used in the US and UK, while metric horsepower or PS (735.5 W) is used in Europe. The difference is about 1.4%.

Why do some appliances use watts and others use horsepower?

Electric appliances typically use watts (SI unit), while mechanical engines traditionally use horsepower. Both measure power but in different contexts - electrical vs. mechanical.
