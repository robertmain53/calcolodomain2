Bishop Score Calculator

The Bishop Score Calculator is a medical tool used by healthcare professionals to evaluate the readiness of the cervix for labor. It helps in deciding the necessity of induction and predicting the success of induction procedures.

The Formula Explained

Bishop Score Formula: Sum of Dilation, Effacement, Station, Consistency, and Position scores.

Glossary of Terms

  • Dilation: The opening of the cervix measured in centimeters.
  • Effacement: Thinning of the cervix measured as a percentage.
  • Station: Position of the fetus in relation to the pelvis.
  • Consistency: The firmness of the cervix.
  • Position: The position of the cervix in the pelvis.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the Bishop Score?

The Bishop Score is a system used to assess the readiness of the cervix for labor.

Why is the Bishop Score important?

It helps in determining the likelihood of a successful induction and the readiness for labor.

How is the Bishop Score calculated?

It is calculated by evaluating cervical dilation, effacement, fetal station, consistency, and position.

What does a high Bishop Score indicate?

A high Bishop Score suggests that the cervix is more favorable for labor induction.

Can the Bishop Score predict labor?

While it provides valuable information, it's not an absolute predictor of labor onset.
