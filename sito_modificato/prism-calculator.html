The formulas explained

General right prism with base area \(A_b\), base perimeter \(P_b\), height \(H\):

\[ V = A_b \cdot H,\qquad A_L = P_b \cdot H,\qquad A_T = A_L + 2A_b \]

Rectangular base \(L \times W\): \(\;A_b = LW,\; P_b = 2(L+W)\).

Right triangle base with legs \(b, h\): \(\;A_b = \tfrac{1}{2}bh,\; P_b = b + h + \sqrt{b^2 + h^2}\).

Trapezoid base with bases \(a,b\), height \(h_t\), legs \(c,d\): \(\;A_b = \tfrac{(a+b)}{2}h_t,\; P_b = a+b+c+d\).

Regular \(n\)-gon base with side \(s\): \(\;A_b = \dfrac{ns^2}{4\tan(\pi/n)},\; P_b = ns\).

Glossary of variables

  • H: Prism height (distance between bases).
  • L, W: Length and width of a rectangular base.
  • b, h: Legs of a right-triangle base (perpendicular).
  • a, b, c, d: Two parallel bases and two legs of a trapezoid base.
  • h_t: Perpendicular height of the trapezoid.
  • n, s: Number of sides and side length for a regular polygon base.
  • A_b: Base area, P_b: Base perimeter, A_L: Lateral area, A_T: Total surface area, V: Volume.

How it works: a step-by-step example

Rectangular prism sample

Let \(L=8\), \(W=5\), \(H=10\) (all in cm).

\(A_b = LW = 8 \times 5 = 40\ \text{cm}^2\)

\(P_b = 2(L+W) = 2(8+5)=26\ \text{cm}\)

Lateral area \(A_L = P_b H = 26 \times 10 = 260\ \text{cm}^2\)

Total area \(A_T = A_L + 2A_b = 260 + 80 = 340\ \text{cm}^2\)

Volume \(V = A_b H = 40 \times 10 = 400\ \text{cm}^3\).

Frequently asked questions

When should I choose a regular n-gon prism?

Use it for bases like regular pentagons, hexagons, etc. You specify \(n\) and the side length \(s\); the tool applies \(A_b = \frac{ns^2}{4\tan(\pi/n)}\).

Are inputs validated?

Yes—each field validates on blur and provides actionable guidance below the input, with screen-reader accessible descriptions.

Why do I need both legs for a trapezoid?

Perimeter is needed for lateral area. Without both legs, \(P_b\) is under-specified.
