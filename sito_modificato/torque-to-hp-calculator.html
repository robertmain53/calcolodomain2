Torque to horsepower formula

Power, torque, and RPM are linked by the basic physics relationship:

P = τ × ω
where P is power, τ is torque, and ω is angular speed (radians per second).

For everyday engine calculations we usually work with RPM and either ft·lb or N·m. This leads to the familiar shortcut formulas.

Imperial units (ft·lb and HP)

HP = (Torque(ft·lb) × RPM) ÷ 5252

Rearranged for torque:

Torque(ft·lb) = (HP × 5252) ÷ RPM

The constant 5252 comes from combining:

  • 1 horsepower = 33,000 ft·lb per minute
  • 1 revolution = 2π radians
  • RPM (rev/min) → rad/s conversion

Metric units (N·m and kW)

kW = (Torque(N·m) × RPM) ÷ 9550

Rearranged:

Torque(N·m) = (kW × 9550) ÷ RPM

To convert between kW and HP:

1 kW ≈ 1.34102 HP
1 HP ≈ 0.7457 kW

Unit conversions used

The calculator lets you mix and match torque and power units. Internally it converts using these factors:

  • 1 ft·lb = 1.355817948 N·m
  • 1 in·lb = 0.083333333 ft·lb
  • 1 kgf·m ≈ 7.23301385 ft·lb ≈ 9.80665 N·m
  • 1 HP = 0.745699872 kW

Worked examples

Example 1 – 300 ft·lb at 4000 RPM

Find horsepower:

HP = (300 ft·lb × 4000 RPM) ÷ 5252
HP ≈ 1,200,000 ÷ 5252 ≈ 228.5 HP

Example 2 – 400 N·m at 2500 RPM

Using the metric formula:

kW = (400 N·m × 2500 RPM) ÷ 9550 ≈ 104.7 kW
HP ≈ 104.7 × 1.34102 ≈ 140.4 HP

Example 3 – HP to torque

A 250 HP engine at 6000 RPM. What torque in ft·lb?

Torque(ft·lb) = (250 × 5252) ÷ 6000
≈ 1,313,000 ÷ 6000 ≈ 218.8 ft·lb

Torque vs horsepower: which matters more?

Neither by itself tells the whole story:

  • Torque is a measure of twisting force. High torque at low RPM feels like strong pull from a stop.
  • Horsepower is torque multiplied by RPM – it reflects how quickly work can be done, which relates to top speed and acceleration at higher revs.

For towing and off‑road use, you care about torque in the low‑to‑mid RPM range. For performance cars and bikes, the shape of the HP curve across the rev range is more important.
