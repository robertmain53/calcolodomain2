The Formula Explained

\( \text{Field Goal Distance} = \text{Line of Scrimmage} + \text{End Zone Distance} + 7 \)

Glossary of Terms

  • Line of Scrimmage: The yard line where the ball is placed before the snap.
  • End Zone Distance: The distance from the goal line to the back of the end zone, typically 10 yards.
  • Field Goal Distance: The total yardage the kicker needs to clear to score a field goal.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Suppose the line of scrimmage is at the 20-yard line. The field goal distance would be calculated as 20 (line of scrimmage) + 10 (end zone) + 7 (distance for snap and hold), totaling 37 yards.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the minimum distance for a field goal?

The minimum distance typically starts at around 20 yards, depending on the position on the field.

Why is 7 yards added to the calculation?

The 7 yards account for the distance between the line of scrimmage and where the ball is held for the kick.

How does wind affect field goal distance?

Wind can significantly affect the ball's trajectory and distance, requiring adjustments from the kicker.

What is the maximum recorded field goal distance?

The longest successful field goal in NFL history is 66 yards.

How do I become a better field goal kicker?

Practice consistently, focus on technique, and work on both strength and flexibility.
