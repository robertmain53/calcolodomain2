Formula (extracted text)
For a monthly period loan with gross principal \(L\), monthly rate \(i\), and term \(n\): Serviced interest payment each month: \[ I_m = L \cdot i \] Retained interest deducted upfront: \[ I_{\text{retained}} = L \cdot i \cdot n \] Rolled-up balance at redemption: \[ B_n = L\,(1+i)^n \] Exit fee (percent of gross loan \(e\%\)): \[ \text{Exit} = L \cdot \frac{e}{100} \] APR is the monthly internal rate of return \(r\) that solves: \[ 0 = -\,\text{NetAdvance} + \sum_{t=1}^{n} \text{Pay}_t\,(1+r)^{-t} + \text{Redemption}\,(1+r)^{-n} \] with annualisation \( \text{APR} = (1+r)^{12} - 1 \).
Variables and units
  • P = principal (loan amount) (currency)
  • r = periodic interest rate (annual rate ÷ payments per year) (1)
  • n = total number of payments (years × payments per year) (count)
  • M = periodic payment for principal + interest (currency)
  • T = property tax (annual or monthly depending on input) (currency)
