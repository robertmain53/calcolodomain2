The Formula Explained

LR+: \(\frac{\text{Sensitivity}}{1 - \text{Specificity}}\)
LR-: \(\frac{1 - \text{Sensitivity}}{\text{Specificity}}\)

Glossary of Terms

  • Sensitivity: The probability that a test correctly identifies a condition.
  • Specificity: The probability that a test correctly identifies the absence of a condition.
  • LR+: The likelihood that a positive test result is correct.
  • LR-: The likelihood that a negative test result is correct.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Given a test with 90% sensitivity and 85% specificity, the positive likelihood ratio (LR+) can be calculated as follows:

\(LR+ = \frac{90}{1 - 85} = 6\)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a likelihood ratio?

A likelihood ratio is a value used to determine the accuracy of a diagnostic test.

Why are likelihood ratios important?

They help in understanding how much a test result will change the odds of having a disease.
