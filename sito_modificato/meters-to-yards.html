How to Convert Meters to Yards

The conversion between meters (m) and yards (yd) is based on a single, exact conversion factor defined by international agreement in 1959.

The Exact Formula

The relationship is fixed and exact:

1 Yard = 0.9144 Meters (Exactly)

To convert from meters to yards, you **divide the meter value by 0.9144** (or multiply by ~1.09361).

  • Formula: yd = m / 0.9144
  • Example: 100 m / 0.9144 = 109.361... yd

To convert from yards to meters, you **multiply the yard value by 0.9144**.

  • Formula: m = yd × 0.9144
  • Example: 100 yd × 0.9144 = 91.44 m

How to Convert Meters to Feet and Inches

To get a practical measurement in feet and inches from meters, you first convert meters to total inches.

  1. Convert Meters to Total Inches:
    The exact factor is 1 inch = 0.0254 meters.
    Formula: Total Inches = Meters / 0.0254
  2. Convert Total Inches to Feet and Inches:
    Divide the total inches by 12. The whole number is your **feet** part. The remainder is your **inches** part.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the formula for meters to yards?

The exact formula is: Yards = Meters / 0.9144. You can also multiply by 1.09361 for a close approximation.

How many yards are in a meter?

There are approximately **1.09361 yards** in 1 meter. A meter is slightly longer than a yard.

Which is longer, a meter or a yard?

A **meter is longer** than a yard by about 3.37 inches. 1 yard is defined as 3 feet (36 inches), while 1 meter is about 3.28 feet (39.37 inches).
