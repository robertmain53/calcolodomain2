The Formula Explained

The conversion formula used is:

\( \text{Nm} = \text{ft-lb} \times 1.35582 \)

Glossary of Terms

  • Foot-Pound (ft-lb): A unit of torque representing one pound of force applied at a perpendicular distance of one foot from a pivot point.
  • Newton-Meter (Nm): The SI unit of torque, equivalent to the torque resulting from a force of one newton applied at a perpendicular distance of one meter from the pivot point.

How it Works: A Step-by-Step Example

If you have a torque of 10 foot-pounds and you want to convert it to newton-meters, apply the formula:

\( 10 \, \text{ft-lb} \times 1.35582 = 13.5582 \, \text{Nm} \)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a foot-pound?

A foot-pound (ft-lb) is a unit of torque that represents the amount of force (in pounds) applied at a distance of one foot from a pivot point.

What is a newton-meter?

A newton-meter (Nm) is a unit of torque in the International System of Units (SI) that is equivalent to a force of one newton acting at a distance of one meter from a pivot point.

Why is torque important?

Torque is a crucial concept in physics and engineering because it quantifies the rotational force applied to an object, affecting its angular acceleration.
