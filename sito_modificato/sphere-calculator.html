Sphere formulas used

A sphere is the 3D analogue of a circle – every point on the surface is the same distance (the radius) from the center. These are the standard formulas:

1. Volume of a sphere

\( V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^3 \)

2. Surface area of a sphere

\( A = 4 \pi r^2 \)

3. Diameter of a sphere

\( d = 2r \)

4. Great-circle circumference

\( C = 2 \pi r \)

This is the circumference of the largest possible circle on the sphere (like the equator on Earth).

If you only know the diameter \( d \), remember to convert to radius first: \( r = \frac{d}{2} \), then plug into the formulas above.

Example calculation

Example: A sphere has radius 12 cm. Find its volume and surface area.

  1. Volume: \( V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^3 = \frac{4}{3} \pi (12^3) = \frac{4}{3} \pi \times 1728 = 2304\pi \approx 7,\!238.23 \text{ cm}^3 \)
  2. Surface area: \( A = 4 \pi r^2 = 4 \pi (12^2) = 4 \pi \times 144 = 576\pi \approx 1,\!808.64 \text{ cm}^2 \)

The calculator works through the same steps instantly.
