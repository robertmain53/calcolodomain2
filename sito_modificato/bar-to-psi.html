How to Convert Bar to PSI

Converting from bar to Pounds per Square Inch (PSI) is the most common pressure conversion for drivers, as European and US tire pressure gauges use different units. The conversion is straightforward.

The Conversion Formula

The relationship between bar and PSI is based on their definitions in Pascals:

1 bar = 100,000 Pascals (Pa)
1 PSI = 6,894.757 Pascals (Pa)

From this, we get the direct conversion factor:

  • Bar to PSI: PSI = bar × 14.50377
  • PSI to Bar: bar = PSI / 14.50377 (or PSI × 0.0689476)

For a quick mental estimate, you can just remember 1 bar ≈ 14.5 psi.

Bar vs. atm: What's the Difference?

A common point of confusion is the difference between bar and atm (standard atmosphere). They are very close but not the same.

  • 1 bar is defined as exactly 100,000 Pascals (Pa).
  • 1 atm is defined as exactly 101,325 Pascals (Pa).

Therefore, 1 atmosphere is slightly larger than 1 bar (1 atm = 1.01325 bar). The converter keeps both units in sync for scientific accuracy.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the formula for bar to PSI?

The formula is: PSI = bar × 14.50377.

How many PSI are in 1 bar?

There are 14.50377 PSI in 1 bar. For quick mental math, you can approximate 1 bar ≈ 14.5 PSI.

How many PSI in 2.2 bar?

To convert 2.2 bar to PSI (a common tire pressure), you multiply 2.2 by 14.50377, which equals 31.908 PSI (often rounded to 32 PSI).
