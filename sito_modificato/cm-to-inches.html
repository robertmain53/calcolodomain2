Formula

The conversion from centimeters to inches uses the following formula:

\[ \text{inches} = \frac{\text{cm}}{2.54} \]

Or equivalently:

\[ \text{inches} = \text{cm} \times 0.393701 \]

Data Source and Methodology

This converter uses the internationally accepted definition of the inch, established in 1959:

  • 1 inch = 2.54 centimeters (exact)
  • Therefore: 1 cm = 0.393701 inches (derived)
  • This is the standard used worldwide for converting between metric and imperial units of length

Step-by-Step Example

Problem: Convert 25 centimeters to inches.

Step 1: Write down the formula

\[ \text{inches} = \frac{\text{cm}}{2.54} \]

Step 2: Substitute the value

\[ \text{inches} = \frac{25}{2.54} \]

Step 3: Calculate the result

\[ \text{inches} = 9.84 \text{ in} \]

Answer: 25 cm = 9.84 inches

Glossary of Terms

Centimeter (cm)
A metric unit of length equal to one hundredth of a meter. Commonly used in most countries worldwide.
Inch (in)
An imperial unit of length primarily used in the United States, United Kingdom, and Canada. Equal to exactly 2.54 centimeters.
Conversion Factor
A number used to convert one set of units to another. For cm to inches, the conversion factor is 0.393701 (or divide by 2.54).

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert centimeters to inches?

To convert centimeters to inches, divide the length in centimeters by 2.54 (or multiply by 0.393701). The formula is: inches = cm ÷ 2.54. For example, 10 cm = 10 ÷ 2.54 = 3.937 inches.

What is the exact conversion factor from cm to inches?

The exact conversion factor is 1 inch = 2.54 centimeters (defined). Therefore, 1 cm = 0.393701 inches (1 ÷ 2.54).

How many inches is 30 cm?

30 centimeters equals 11.81 inches (30 ÷ 2.54 = 11.811).

Is the cm to inches conversion exact?

Yes, the conversion is based on an exact definition: 1 inch is defined as exactly 2.54 centimeters. This was established internationally in 1959.

Can I use this converter for negative values?

Yes, the converter works with any numeric value, including negative numbers. This can be useful for coordinate conversions or scientific calculations.
