How this salary to hourly calculator works

This tool converts any salary – annual, monthly, biweekly or weekly – into an hourly rate based on your actual working schedule. Unlike simple converters, it lets you:

  • Set your own hours per week (not just 40).
  • Adjust paid weeks per year (include or exclude unpaid time off).
  • Account for unpaid breaks each day.
  • Optionally include regular overtime with a higher pay multiplier.

Core formulas

1. Convert salary to annual pay

  • Annual salary (if you enter annual): \( S_{\text{year}} = S_{\text{annual}} \)
  • From monthly: \( S_{\text{year}} = S_{\text{month}} \times 12 \)
  • From biweekly: \( S_{\text{year}} = S_{\text{biweekly}} \times 26 \)
  • From weekly: \( S_{\text{year}} = S_{\text{week}} \times 52 \)

2. Working hours per year

First we compute paid hours per week (excluding unpaid breaks):

\( H_{\text{paid/week}} = H_{\text{week}} - H_{\text{break/day}} \times 5 \)

Then hours per year:

\( H_{\text{year}} = H_{\text{paid/week}} \times W_{\text{paid/year}} \)

3. Base hourly rate

\( R_{\text{base}} = \dfrac{S_{\text{year}}}{H_{\text{year}}} \)

4. Effective hourly rate with overtime (optional)

If you enable overtime, we assume your salary already includes overtime pay.

Total hours per week (including overtime):
\( H_{\text{total/week}} = H_{\text{paid/week}} + H_{\text{overtime/week}} \)

Total hours per year:
\( H_{\text{total/year}} = H_{\text{total/week}} \times W_{\text{paid/year}} \)

Effective hourly rate:
\( R_{\text{effective}} = \dfrac{S_{\text{year}}}{H_{\text{total/year}}} \)

Example: $60,000 salary to hourly

Assume:

  • Annual salary: $60,000
  • Hours per week: 40
  • Paid weeks per year: 52
  • No unpaid breaks, no overtime

Hours per year = 40 × 52 = 2,080
Hourly rate = 60,000 ÷ 2,080 ≈ $28.85 per hour.

Common conversions

  • $50,000 per year ≈ $24.04 per hour (40 h/week, 52 weeks).
  • $75,000 per year ≈ $36.06 per hour.
  • $100,000 per year ≈ $48.08 per hour.

Tips for comparing job offers

  • Always compare hourly rates when one job is salaried and the other is hourly.
  • Adjust for different hours per week (e.g., 37.5 vs 40 hours).
  • Consider paid vacation and holidays – more paid time off means higher effective hourly pay.
  • Include benefits (health insurance, retirement match, bonuses) in your decision, even though they are not in this calculator.

Salary to hourly FAQ

How do I convert annual salary to hourly pay?

Divide your annual salary by the number of hours you work per year. With a standard 40-hour week and 52 weeks per year:

\( \text{Hourly} = \dfrac{\text{Annual salary}}{40 \times 52} = \dfrac{\text{Annual salary}}{2{,}080} \)

How many working hours are in a year?

It depends on your schedule:

  • 40 hours/week × 52 weeks = 2,080 hours.
  • 40 hours/week × 50 paid weeks (2 weeks unpaid) = 2,000 hours.
  • 37.5 hours/week × 52 weeks = 1,950 hours.

Should I use gross or net salary?

Use gross salary (before tax and deductions) for consistent comparisons. Net pay depends on your tax situation and benefits, which vary by person and location.

What about overtime and shift differentials?

If overtime or shift premiums are guaranteed and predictable, you can include them by enabling the overtime section and entering your typical overtime hours and multiplier. If overtime is occasional or unpredictable, it is safer to compare offers using the base hourly rate.

Is a salaried job always better than hourly?

Not necessarily. A salaried role may include benefits and stability, but some salaried jobs expect long hours without extra pay. An hourly job may pay overtime and offer more flexibility. Converting salary to hourly helps you compare the real value of your time.
