The Formula Explained

The calculation for child support is based on the formula: Payment = Income × Support Rate, where the Support Rate is determined by the number of children and other factors.

Glossary of Terms

  • Income: Your gross monthly income before taxes.
  • Number of Children: The number of children you are financially supporting.
  • Support Payment: The estimated amount to be paid for child support.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is child support?

Child support is a payment made by a non-custodial parent to support their child financially.

How is child support calculated?

Child support is typically calculated based on the income of both parents and custody arrangements.

Can child support be modified?

Yes, child support can be modified if there are significant changes in circumstances.

What happens if child support is not paid?

Failure to pay child support can result in legal consequences, including fines and imprisonment.

Is child support taxable income?

Child support payments are not considered taxable income for the receiving parent.
