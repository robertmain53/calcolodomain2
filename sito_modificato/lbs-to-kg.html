Methodology

We normalize every input into decimal pounds to keep conversions deterministic. Kilograms are derived through the exact international factor (1 lb = 0.45359237 kg), and ounces are recovered by isolating the fractional pound portion.

Full original guide (expanded)

Exact formula

By international agreement (the International Yard and Pound Agreement of 1959), one pound is defined as exactly 0.45359237 kilograms.

Example: Convert 150 lbs to kg: 150 × 0.45359237 = 68.03885 kg.

Mental math trick

Divide the pound value by 2 to approximate kilograms, then subtract 10% of that result for extra accuracy. The trick mirrors dividing by 2.2 without using a calculator.

  1. Start with the pounds. Example: 180 lbs.
  2. Divide by 2: 180 / 2 = 90.
  3. Subtract 10% of 90 (which is 9): 90 - 9 = 81 kg.
  4. The exact value is 81.64 kg, so the mental math result stays within 1%.

Including ounces

Because 1 pound equals 16 ounces, convert ounces to decimal pounds before multiplying by the kilogram factor.

  1. Convert ounces to decimal pounds: 5 oz / 16 = 0.3125 lbs.
  2. Add to the whole pounds: 150 + 0.3125 = 150.3125 lbs.
  3. Convert to kilograms: 150.3125 × 0.45359237 = 68.179 kg.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 1 kg 2 lbs or 2.2 lbs? 1 kilogram equals approximately 2.2 pounds (exactly 2.2046226218 lbs). Using “2” is a rough shortcut but not precise.

What is the easy trick to convert lbs to kg? Divide by 2, then subtract 10% of that result—e.g., 200 lbs → (200 / 2 = 100) → (100 - 10 = 90 kg). The exact answer is 90.7 kg.
