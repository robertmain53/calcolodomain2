The Formula Explained

Volume \( V = l \times w \times h \)

Glossary of Variables

  • Length (l): The longest side of the prism.
  • Width (w): The shorter side of the base.
  • Height (h): The vertical side of the prism.
  • Volume (V): The amount of space occupied by the prism.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Consider a rectangular prism with a length of 5 cm, width of 3 cm, and height of 4 cm. The volume is calculated as:

\( V = 5 \times 3 \times 4 = 60 \, \text{cm}^3 \)

The volume is calculated by multiplying the length by the width by the height of the prism.
