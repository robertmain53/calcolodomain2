How to convert horsepower to kilowatts

Horsepower (hp) and kilowatts (kW) are both units of power. Horsepower is common in automotive and mechanical contexts, while kW is the SI unit used on spec sheets, electric bills, and engineering documents.

Mechanical horsepower to kW

1 mechanical hp = 745.699872 W ≈ 0.7457 kW

kW = hp × 0.7457
hp = kW ÷ 0.7457 ≈ kW × 1.3410

Example: Convert 150 hp (mechanical) to kW.

kW = 150 × 0.7457 ≈ 111.855 kW
≈ 111.9 kW (rounded)

Metric horsepower (PS) to kW

Metric horsepower (often written as PS, CV, or hk) is slightly smaller than mechanical horsepower.

1 metric hp = 735.49875 W ≈ 0.7355 kW

kW = hpmetric × 0.7355
hpmetric = kW ÷ 0.7355 ≈ kW × 1.3596

Electrical horsepower to kW

Electrical horsepower is defined exactly as:

1 electrical hp = 746 W = 0.746 kW

hp, kW, and torque: how they relate

Power, torque, and rotational speed are linked. Our helper above lets you estimate hp from torque and RPM:

hp = (Torque(lb·ft) × RPM) / 5252
hp = (Torque(N·m) × RPM) / 7127

Once you have hp, you can convert to kW with the formulas above.

When should you care about the hp definition?

For quick everyday estimates, the difference between mechanical, metric, and electrical horsepower is small (about 1–2%). For:

  • Car brochures in Europe: usually metric hp (PS).
  • Older US engines and dyno charts: mechanical hp.
  • Electric motor nameplates: electrical hp or directly in kW.

If you are sizing equipment, motors, or breakers, always check which definition the manufacturer uses and select the matching option in the converter.

HP to kW converter – FAQ

How do you convert hp to kW?

For mechanical horsepower, multiply by 0.7457: kW = hp × 0.7457. For metric horsepower, multiply by 0.7355. Our calculator applies the correct factor automatically based on the horsepower type you select.

What is 1 hp in kW?

1 mechanical hp ≈ 0.7457 kW, 1 metric hp ≈ 0.7355 kW, and 1 electrical hp = 0.746 kW exactly. If you are unsure, mechanical hp is a safe default for many English-language sources.

What is 1 kW in hp?

For mechanical horsepower: hp = kW ÷ 0.7457 ≈ kW × 1.3410. So 1 kW ≈ 1.34 hp, 10 kW ≈ 13.41 hp, 100 kW ≈ 134.1 hp.

Why do car manufacturers sometimes list both kW and hp?

Many markets legally require power in kW (SI unit), but customers are more familiar with hp. Listing both helps with comparison. For example, a car rated at 110 kW will often be advertised as about 150 hp (mechanical) or 150 PS (metric).

Can I use this converter for electric motors?

Yes. Choose Electrical horsepower in the horsepower type dropdown. However, most modern motor nameplates already show power directly in kW, which is usually the preferred value for sizing cables, breakers, and drives.
