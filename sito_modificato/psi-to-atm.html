How to Convert PSI to Atmospheres (atm)

Converting Pounds per Square Inch (PSI) to standard atmospheres (atm) is a common calculation in engineering, diving, and science. The conversion is based on the official definition of 1 atmosphere.

The Exact Formula

One standard atmosphere (1 atm) is defined as the average air pressure at sea level, which is equal to 101,325 Pascals. One PSI is defined as 6,894.757 Pascals. By dividing these, we get the exact factor:

1 atm = 14.695948... PSI

To convert from PSI to atm, you **divide the PSI value by 14.6959**.

  • Formula: atm = PSI / 14.6959
  • Example: 100 PSI / 14.6959 = 6.8046 atm

To convert from atm to PSI, you **multiply the atm value by 14.6959**.

  • Formula: PSI = atm × 14.6959
  • Example: 2 atm × 14.6959 = 29.3918 PSI

The Key Difference: atm vs. bar

A common point of confusion is the difference between `atm` and `bar`. They are very close but **not the same**.

  • 1 bar is defined as exactly **100,000 Pascals (Pa)**.
  • 1 atm is defined as exactly **101,325 Pascals (Pa)**.

Therefore, 1 atmosphere is slightly larger than 1 bar (1 atm = 1.01325 bar). Our calculator includes both for accuracy.
