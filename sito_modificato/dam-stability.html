Dam Stability Calculator (Gravity Dam)

Quickly evaluate the sliding and overturning stability of a 2D gravity dam monolith under hydrostatic water and uplift pressure. Ideal for preliminary design checks, education, and sensitivity studies.

Engineering disclaimer: This tool is for preliminary assessment only. It does not replace a full dam safety analysis per FERC, USACE, ICOLD, or local standards. Final designs must be checked and approved by qualified engineers.

How this dam stability calculator works

This tool models a 2D gravity dam monolith with linear upstream and downstream faces. It computes self-weight, hydrostatic water forces, uplift, optional pseudo-static seismic inertia, and then evaluates sliding and overturning stability about the downstream toe.

1. Geometry model

The dam cross-section is idealized as a quadrilateral with vertical height \(H\) and base width \(B\). Upstream and downstream faces are defined by slopes \(m_u\) and \(m_d\) (H:V).

Upstream face horizontal offset at crest: \( x_u = m_u \, H \)

Downstream face horizontal offset at crest: \( x_d = B - m_d \, H \)

Concrete area (per m length): \( A_c = \frac{(B + x_u - x_d)}{2} \, H \)

2. Forces considered

  • Self-weight W of concrete: \( W = \gamma_c A_c \)
  • Upstream hydrostatic thrust (if water depth \(h_u\) > 0):

\( H_u = \frac{1}{2} \gamma_w h_u^2 \)

Acts horizontally at \( h_u/3 \) above the base.

  • Downstream hydrostatic thrust (if water depth \(h_d\) > 0):

\( H_d = \frac{1}{2} \gamma_w h_d^2 \)

Acts horizontally at \( h_d/3 \) above the base, opposite direction to \(H_u\).

  • Uplift U along the base, linear from upstream to downstream:

Upstream uplift pressure: \( p_u = \gamma_w h_u \)

Downstream uplift pressure: \( p_d = \gamma_w h_d \)

Average uplift pressure (with reduction factor \( \alpha \)):

\( \bar{p} = \alpha \, \frac{p_u + p_d}{2} \)

Resultant uplift force: \( U = \bar{p} \, B \)

  • Seismic inertia He (optional pseudo-static):

\( H_e = k_h \, W \)

Acts at the centroid of the dam section, in the downstream direction.

3. Sliding stability

The normal force on the base is \( N = W - U \). The available frictional resistance is \( R = \mu N \). The total horizontal driving force is:

\( H_{\text{total}} = H_u - H_d + H_e \)

Factor of safety against sliding:

\( FS_{\text{sliding}} = \dfrac{\mu N}{H_{\text{total}}} \)

Many guidelines recommend \( FS_{\text{sliding}} \ge 1.5 \) for normal loading and \( \ge 1.1\text{–}1.3 \) for extreme or seismic loading, but always follow the governing code.

4. Overturning stability

Moments are taken about the downstream toe. Resisting moments are due to dam weight and downstream water; overturning moments are due to upstream water, uplift, and seismic inertia.

Resisting moment: \( M_R = \sum (F_i \, z_i)_{\text{resisting}} \)

Overturning moment: \( M_O = \sum (F_j \, z_j)_{\text{overturning}} \)

Factor of safety against overturning:

\( FS_{\text{OT}} = \dfrac{M_R}{M_O} \)

Typical minimum values are \( FS_{\text{OT}} \ge 2.0 \) for normal loading and \( \ge 1.5 \) for unusual or extreme loading, depending on the standard.

5. Base stress and eccentricity

The resultant vertical force is \( V = N \) and the resultant moment about the toe is \( M = M_R - M_O \). The eccentricity of the resultant from the center of the base is:

\( e = \dfrac{M}{V} - \dfrac{B}{2} \)

Assuming linear stress distribution and no tension, the maximum and minimum base stresses are:

\( \sigma_{\max} = \dfrac{V}{B} \left( 1 + \dfrac{6e}{B} \right) \)

\( \sigma_{\min} = \dfrac{V}{B} \left( 1 - \dfrac{6e}{B} \right) \)

The “middle-third rule” requires \( |e| \le B/6 \) to keep the entire base in compression. If \( |e| > B/6 \), tension is predicted at the heel or toe, which is usually not acceptable for concrete–rock interfaces.

Limitations

  • 2D analysis of a single monolith; no 3D or arch effects.
  • Simple linear uplift distribution; no drains or crack propagation modeling.
  • No detailed foundation bearing capacity or sliding along deeper planes.
  • No dynamic or time-history seismic analysis; only pseudo-static inertia.

For final design or safety review, use this tool only as a starting point and refer to detailed guidance such as USACE EM 1110-2-2200, FERC Engineering Guidelines, and ICOLD bulletins.

FAQ

What input units does the calculator use?

All geometric dimensions are in meters, and forces are computed per meter of dam length. Unit weights are in kN/m³, so resulting forces are in kN and stresses in kPa.

How should I choose the uplift reduction factor?

A value of 1.0 represents full hydrostatic uplift from upstream to downstream. Lower values (e.g., 0.3–0.7) may be used to represent the effect of drains, relief wells, or conservative assumptions in different load cases, as specified by your design standard.

Can I model flood or seismic load cases?

Yes. Increase the upstream water depth to represent flood levels, and set a non-zero horizontal seismic coefficient \(k_h\) to include pseudo-static inertia. For detailed seismic analysis (including hydrodynamic pressures), refer to specialized guidelines.

Is this calculator suitable for arch or buttress dams?

No. The underlying assumptions are for gravity dams where stability is provided primarily by self-weight. Arch, buttress, and embankment dams require different analysis methods.
