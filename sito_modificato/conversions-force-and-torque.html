How the converter works

We normalize to a base unit and then convert to the target unit.

General formula (linear)

\( \text{target} = \text{value} \times \frac{\text{factorFrom}}{\text{factorTo}} \)

For force, the base unit is Newton (N). For torque, the base unit is Newton-metre (N·m).

Force basics

1 kgf is the force due to 1 kg mass under standard gravity: 1 kgf = 9.80665 N. This is common in older manuals.

Torque basics

Torque is force × lever arm. Converting lbf·ft to N·m is common when matching tools to engineering specs.

Good practices

• Always check whether the spec is in SI (N, N·m) or imperial (lbf, lbf·ft).

• kgf is still used in legacy torque tools: convert to N·m for modern sheets.

• Keep at least 4 decimals for torque, then round for publication.
