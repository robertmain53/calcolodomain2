Formula

The formula to convert Joules to BTU is:

\[ \text{BTU} = \text{Joules} \div 1055.06 \]

One Joule is equal to 0.000947817 BTU. To convert Joules to BTU, divide by 1055.06 (or multiply by 0.000947817).

Data Source

All calculations are based on the standard conversion factor where 1 BTU (International Table) = 1055.06 Joules, or 1 Joule = 0.000947817 BTU. This conversion is widely accepted in engineering and scientific applications.

Glossary

Joule (J)
The SI derived unit of energy, work, or amount of heat. One joule equals the energy transferred when applying a force of one newton through a distance of one meter.
BTU (British Thermal Unit)
A unit of heat energy defined as the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit. Commonly used in HVAC systems.
Energy
The capacity to do work or produce heat. Can be measured in various units including joules, BTU, calories, and kilowatt-hours.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert Joules to BTU?

To convert Joules to BTU, divide the number of Joules by 1055.06. Alternatively, multiply by 0.000947817.

What is a Joule?

A Joule is the SI unit of energy. It's named after James Prescott Joule and is used to measure energy, work, and heat in the metric system.

When would I need this conversion?

This conversion is useful when working with international energy standards (SI units) and need to communicate with HVAC professionals or work with equipment rated in BTU.

How many Joules are in one BTU?

One BTU (International Table) equals 1055.06 Joules. This is the standard conversion factor used in most applications.

Is the conversion exact?

The International Table BTU is defined as exactly 1055.05585262 Joules. For most practical purposes, 1055.06 J is sufficiently accurate.
