Frequently Asked Questions

What is BMI and how is it used?
BMI is a screening index that relates weight to height. It does not directly measure body fat but is widely used for population-level assessment.
What are the standard adult BMI categories?
Underweight: < 18.5 · Healthy: 18.5–24.9 · Overweight: 25.0–29.9 · Obesity: ≥ 30.0.
Does BMI apply to athletes or children?
For muscular individuals BMI can overestimate body fat. For children/teens use age- and sex-specific BMI percentiles, not this adult tool.
