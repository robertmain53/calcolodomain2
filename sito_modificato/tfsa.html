The Formula Explained

The future value of your TFSA is calculated using the formula:

FV = P × (1 + r)^n + PMT × [((1 + r)^n - 1) / r]

Glossary of Variables

  • P: Initial Contribution
  • PMT: Annual Contribution
  • r: Annual Interest Rate
  • n: Number of Years
  • FV: Future Value of the TFSA

How It Works: A Step-By-Step Example

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a TFSA?

A Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA) is an account that provides tax benefits for saving in Canada.

How does the TFSA calculator work?

The calculator estimates your savings growth by compounding your contributions and applying an annual interest rate.

Can I exceed my TFSA contribution limit?

No, exceeding the TFSA limit results in penalties. It is important to adhere to your contribution limits.

What happens if the interest rate changes?

If the interest rate changes, the actual growth of your TFSA may differ from the estimate provided by this calculator.

How often should I review my TFSA contributions?

Review your TFSA contributions annually to ensure you are within your limit and optimize your savings strategy.
