Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on data provided by the Australian Taxation Office. For more details, visit ATO Super Guarantee.

The Formula Explained

The formula used is: Future Balance = Current Balance + (Annual Salary × Contribution Rate × Years)

Glossary of Terms

  • Annual Salary: The total salary earned in a year before taxes.
  • Contribution Rate: The percentage of salary contributed to superannuation.
  • Current Super Balance: The current amount in your superannuation account.
  • Years Until Retirement: The number of years you plan to keep contributing before retiring.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is superannuation?

Superannuation is a savings scheme for retirement, mandated by the Australian government to ensure financial security for citizens in their later years.

How are superannuation contributions calculated?

Contributions are calculated as a percentage of your salary, with the current Superannuation Guarantee rate being 10.5%.

Can I make extra contributions?

Yes, employees can make additional voluntary contributions to their superannuation fund.

What happens if I change jobs?

Your superannuation can be transferred to your new employer's super fund, or you can maintain your existing fund.

How can I track my superannuation balance?

You can track your balance through your superannuation fund's online portal or statements.
