Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the standard conversion formula for viscosity measurements. For authoritative data, please refer to the International Engineering Standards.

Formula used: 1 cP = 0.001 Pa·s. All calculations are derived from this base formula.

The Formula Explained

Formula: \( \text{Pa·s} = \text{cP} \times 0.001 \)

Glossary of Terms

  • Centipoise (cP): A unit of dynamic viscosity.
  • Pascal-Seconds (Pa·s): The SI unit of dynamic viscosity.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

To convert 10 cP to Pa·s, multiply by 0.001: \( 10 \times 0.001 = 0.01 \, \text{Pa·s} \).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is viscosity?

Viscosity is a measure of a fluid's resistance to flow.

Why convert cP to Pa·s?

Pascal-seconds is the SI unit, often required in scientific calculations.

Can water viscosity be measured in cP?

Yes, the viscosity of water at 20°C is approximately 1 cP.
