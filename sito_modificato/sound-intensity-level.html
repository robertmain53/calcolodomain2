The Formula Explained

SIL = 10 × log 10 (I/I 0 )

Glossary of Variables

  • I: Sound intensity in watts per square meter (W/m²).
  • I 0 : Reference sound intensity (1×10 -12 W/m²).

How It Works: An Example

To calculate the sound intensity level for a sound intensity of 0.01 W/m², use the formula SIL = 10 × log 10 (0.01/1×10 -12 ), resulting in 100 dB.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is sound intensity level?

Sound intensity level is a measure of the power per unit area of a sound wave.

How is sound intensity level calculated?

It is calculated using the formula: SIL = 10 × log 10 (I/I 0 ), where I is the sound intensity and I 0 is the reference intensity.
