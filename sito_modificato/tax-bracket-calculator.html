The Formula Explained

Tax is computed progressively by bands:

\[ \text{Tax}(y)=\sum_{i=1}^{n}\max\!\bigl(0,\ \min(y,u_i)-l_i\bigr)\cdot r_i \]

where \(y\) is taxable income, each bracket \(i\) has lower bound \(l_i\), upper bound \(u_i\) (or \(+\infty\)), and rate \(r_i\).

Effective rate: \[ \text{EffRate}(y)=\frac{\text{Tax}(y)}{y}\times 100\% \] Marginal rate is \(r_i\) of the highest non-empty band.

Glossary of Variables

  • Taxable income (y): Income after deductions/allowances relevant to the system (e.g., US standard deduction, UK Personal Allowance).
  • Bracket: Income range taxed at a given rate.
  • Marginal rate: Rate applied to your next dollar/pound/euro of income.
  • Effective rate: Total tax divided by taxable income.
  • Breakdown: Per-band tax contribution.

FAQ

Does the calculator include UK National Insurance or AU Medicare levy?

No. It focuses on core income tax. NI (UK), Medicare levy (AU), and Italian regional/municipal add-ons are excluded.

How do I handle deductions?

Enter taxable income. In the US you may tick “Apply standard deduction” to estimate taxable income from gross.

What about Scotland?

Scotland has different bands and rates. This version supports England/NI/Wales bands per GOV.UK.

Are capital gains rates included?

No—this is ordinary income. Capital gains have separate thresholds and may be jurisdiction-specific.

Why are my results different from payroll?

Payroll withholding includes other items (e.g., NI/Medicare levy) and timing differences. This tool calculates annual liability on the chosen system.
