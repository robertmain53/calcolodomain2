The Formula Explained

The conversion between azimuth and bearing involves specific trigonometric calculations:

Azimuth to Bearing: Bearing = (Azimuth + 180) % 360

Bearing to Azimuth: Azimuth = (Bearing - 180) % 360

Glossary of Terms

  • Azimuth: A horizontal angle measured clockwise from a north base line.
  • Bearing: The angle between the reference direction (north or south) and a line.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Let's say you have an azimuth of 120 degrees. To convert this to a bearing, you would use the formula: Bearing = (120 + 180) % 360 = 300. Thus, the bearing is 300 degrees.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is an Azimuth?
An azimuth is a direction expressed as the angular distance from a reference direction, typically north.
How is a Bearing different from an Azimuth?
A bearing is the angle between a line and a reference direction, usually north or south.
Why convert between Azimuth and Bearing?
Conversion is essential in surveying to interpret directional data accurately for navigation and mapping.
How accurate are these conversions?
The conversions are highly accurate as they are based on standard surveying calculations.
Can this tool be used for navigation?
Yes, it can be used for navigational purposes where precise direction measurements are required.
