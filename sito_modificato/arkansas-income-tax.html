How Arkansas income tax is calculated

Arkansas has a progressive state income tax with multiple brackets. Your income is divided into ranges, and each range is taxed at its own rate. This calculator approximates your Arkansas liability using current brackets and standard deductions.

Conceptual formula
Arkansas tax = (Tax on first bracket) + (Tax on second bracket) + ... − Credits

1. Determine your Arkansas taxable income

Start from your federal adjusted gross income (AGI), then apply Arkansas‑specific additions and subtractions to arrive at Arkansas taxable income. This tool assumes you already have an estimate of that number.

  • Arkansas taxable income ≈ Wages + other income − pre‑tax deductions − adjustments.
  • Some types of income (for example, Social Security benefits) may be exempt in Arkansas.

2. Apply the Arkansas standard or itemized deduction

Arkansas allows you to claim either the standard deduction or your Arkansas itemized deductions, whichever is larger. In this calculator:

  • If you leave itemized deductions at $0, we apply an estimated standard deduction based on filing status.
  • If you enter a positive amount for itemized deductions, we use that instead of the standard deduction.

3. Apply Arkansas tax brackets

After deductions, your remaining income is taxed using Arkansas’s progressive brackets. For example, a simplified structure might look like:

  • First portion of income taxed at a low rate
  • Next portion taxed at a higher rate
  • Top portion taxed at the highest rate

The calculator applies each rate only to the income that falls within that bracket, not to your entire income.

4. Subtract Arkansas tax credits

Nonrefundable credits reduce your Arkansas tax liability but cannot reduce it below zero. Examples include certain dependent or low‑income credits. Enter the total amount of credits you expect to claim; the calculator subtracts them from your gross Arkansas tax.

Using the Arkansas income tax calculator effectively

  1. Choose the correct filing status. Your brackets and standard deduction depend on whether you file as single, married filing jointly, married filing separately, or head of household.
  2. Use realistic income estimates. For the best results, use your year‑to‑date pay information or last year’s return as a starting point.
  3. Include Arkansas‑specific deductions and credits. If you know your Arkansas itemized deductions or credits, enter them to refine the estimate.
  4. Compare withholding. Add your Arkansas tax withheld to see whether you are likely to receive a refund or owe additional tax.

Limitations and important notes

  • This tool focuses on Arkansas state income tax only. It does not calculate federal income tax, FICA, or local sales taxes.
  • It uses a simplified model of Arkansas brackets and deductions for planning purposes and may not match your final return exactly.
  • Special rules for capital gains, retirement income, part‑year residents, and nonresidents are not fully modeled.
  • Always refer to the Arkansas Department of Finance and Administration (DFA) instructions or consult a tax professional for filing decisions.

Frequently asked questions about Arkansas income tax

Who has to file an Arkansas income tax return?

Generally, Arkansas residents must file if their gross income exceeds the filing threshold for their status, or if they had Arkansas tax withheld and want a refund. Nonresidents and part‑year residents must file if they earned income from Arkansas sources. Thresholds and rules change, so always check the latest DFA guidance.

Does Arkansas tax Social Security and retirement income?

Arkansas does not tax Social Security retirement benefits. Some other retirement income (such as pensions and IRA distributions) may be partially exempt up to certain limits. This calculator does not model each exemption separately, so if a large portion of your income is retirement income, your actual Arkansas tax may be lower than the estimate.

Are Arkansas income taxes high compared with other states?

Arkansas has a mid‑range state income tax burden compared with other states. The exact impact on you depends on your income level, filing status, and eligibility for deductions and credits. Use this calculator along with your federal estimates to understand your overall tax picture.

When are Arkansas income taxes due?

Arkansas individual income tax returns are generally due on the same date as the federal tax deadline (usually April 15, or the next business day if that falls on a weekend or holiday). Extensions may be available, but an extension to file is not an extension to pay.
