How this mortar mix calculator works

This tool estimates how much mortar you need for brick, block or stone work and breaks it down into bag counts and mix proportions. It is designed to be more flexible than simple “bags per brick” charts by letting you:

  • Choose a standard mortar type (N, S, M, O) or define a custom cement:sand ratio.
  • Estimate mortar from wall dimensions and unit size, or from a known mortar volume.
  • Adjust bag size, yield per bag and waste percentage.
  • See separate cement and sand volumes for site-mixed mortar.

1. Mortar types and typical ratios

Building codes (such as ASTM C270) define several mortar types. This calculator uses typical cement-to-sand ratios for pre-blended masonry cement plus sand mixes:

Type Typical use Approx. ratio (cement : sand)
Type N General above-grade walls, chimneys 1 : 3
Type S Structural, below-grade, patios 1 : 2.25 (rounded to 1 : 2.3)
Type M High-strength, foundations 1 : 2.5
Type O Low-strength, interior, restoration 1 : 4

If you are using separate portland cement, lime and sand, consult the manufacturer or local code for the correct three-part ratio (e.g. 1:1:6 for Type N) and adjust the equivalent cement:sand ratio accordingly.

2. Estimating mortar volume from wall dimensions

When you choose “Estimate from wall size”, the calculator:

  1. Computes the wall area from length and height.
  2. Computes the face area of a single unit (brick or block).
  3. Estimates how many units fit in the wall area.
  4. Approximates the mortar volume per unit based on joint thickness and wall thickness.

The total mortar volume is then:

Net mortar volume ≈ (number of units) × (average joint volume per unit)

This is an approximation, but it is usually more accurate than simple “bags per 100 bricks” rules because it accounts for your actual unit size and joint thickness.

3. From volume to bag counts

Once the net mortar volume is known, the calculator adds your chosen waste percentage:

Gross volume = Net volume × (1 + waste% / 100)

Then it divides by the yield per bag:

Bags required = Gross volume ÷ Yield per bag

You can override the default yield per bag with the value published on your specific product (for example, many 80 lb bags of mortar mix yield about 0.7 ft³ of mortar).

4. Cement, sand and water quantities

For site-mixed mortar, the calculator also shows the split between cement and sand by volume. If the total dry mortar volume is \( V_{\text{dry}} \) and the ratio is \( 1 : r \) (cement : sand), then:

Cement volume = \( \dfrac{V_{\text{dry}}}{1 + r} \)     Sand volume = \( \dfrac{r \cdot V_{\text{dry}}}{1 + r} \)

Water is estimated from the number of bags using a typical range (for example, 4–6 quarts per 80 lb bag). Always follow the manufacturer’s instructions on the bag and adjust on site for temperature, humidity and workability.

Practical tips for mixing mortar

  • Use clean, potable water and well-graded, clean masonry sand.
  • Mix dry ingredients thoroughly before adding water.
  • Add water gradually; it is easy to add more, but impossible to remove excess.
  • Mortar should be workable and hold its shape on a trowel without slumping.
  • Only mix as much as you can place within 2 hours (or the time specified by the manufacturer).
  • Protect fresh mortar from freezing, rapid drying and heavy rain.

Limitations & disclaimer

This calculator is intended for preliminary estimating only. Actual mortar requirements depend on workmanship, unit tolerances, joint profile, retempering, substrate absorption and many other factors. Always verify mix proportions and material quantities with the product data sheet, local building codes and a qualified professional for critical or structural work.

Mortar mix FAQ

What is the standard mortar mix ratio?

For general above-grade brickwork, a common choice is Type N mortar, often mixed at about 1:1:6 (cement:lime:sand by volume) or simplified to roughly 1:3 cement to sand when using masonry cement. Stronger mixes like Type S and Type M use more cement relative to sand. Always follow local codes and the manufacturer’s recommendations for your project.

How many bags of mortar do I need per 100 bricks?

As a rule of thumb, one 80 lb bag of mortar mix will lay about 35–40 standard modular bricks with 3/8 in joints. That works out to roughly 2.5–3 bags per 100 bricks. The calculator on this page refines that estimate using your exact brick size and wall dimensions.

Can I use concrete mix instead of mortar mix?

No. Concrete mix contains coarse aggregate (gravel) and is not suitable for thin joints between bricks or blocks. Mortar mix uses only fine aggregate (sand) and is formulated for bond and workability. Use mortar for laying masonry units and concrete for slabs, footings and structural elements as specified by your design and local code.

How accurate is this mortar mix calculator?

For typical residential projects with standard units and joints, this calculator will usually get you within about 10–20% of actual usage, which is why a waste factor is included. For critical projects, unusual units or very large jobs, confirm quantities with a detailed takeoff or a professional estimator.
