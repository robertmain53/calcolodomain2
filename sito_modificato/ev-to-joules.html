Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is an electronvolt?

An electronvolt is a unit of energy commonly used in physics, particularly in the context of atomic and particle physics.

How do I convert eV to J?

Multiply the energy value in eV by 1.602176634 × 10^-19 to get the energy in joules.

Why use electronvolts?

Electronvolts are useful because they are a convenient unit of energy on the scale of individual particles, making calculations simpler in quantum mechanics.
