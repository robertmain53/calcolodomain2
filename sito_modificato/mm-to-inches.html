How to Convert Millimeters to Inches

Converting from millimeters (mm) to inches (in) is a common task in engineering, manufacturing, and everyday DIY projects. The process is straightforward and relies on a single conversion factor.

The Conversion Formula

The internationally accepted conversion factor is:

1 inch = 25.4 millimeters

Therefore, to convert from millimeters to inches, you simply divide the number of millimeters by 25.4. Alternatively, you can multiply by the reciprocal, 0.0393701.

  • Formula 1 (Division): Inches = Millimeters / 25.4
  • Formula 2 (Multiplication): Inches = Millimeters × 0.0393701

For example, to convert 10mm to inches:

10 mm / 25.4 = 0.3937... inches

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How many mm are in one inch?

There are exactly **25.4 millimeters (mm)** in one inch (in). This definition was standardized in 1959 as part of the International Yard and Pound Agreement.

Is 10mm half an inch?

No, 10mm is not half an inch. A half-inch is 0.5 inches, which is exactly **12.7 mm**.

10mm is approximately 0.3937 inches, which is slightly more than 3/8" (0.375 in) and just under 13/32" (0.406 in).

How do you read mm to inches on a ruler?

Most standard rulers show inches on one side and centimeters (cm) / millimeters (mm) on the other. 1 centimeter is equal to 10 millimeters. To convert, you can visually compare the measurements. You will see that 2.54 cm (or 25.4 mm) lines up exactly with the 1-inch mark.
