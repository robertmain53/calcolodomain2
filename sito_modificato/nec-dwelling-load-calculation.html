Input — Dwelling Unit Data

Used to convert total VA to service amperes.
Per NEC 220.12(A), use 3 VA/ft² for dwelling units. Include habitable area; exclude garages, porches, patios, etc., unless finished and conditioned per local rules.
Per 210.11(C)(1) and 220.52(A), provide at least two 20 A small-appliance circuits in kitchens/dining/pantry. Each counts as 1,500 VA and is included with general lighting for demand.
Per 210.11(C)(2) and 220.52(B), at least one 20 A laundry circuit is required for a dwelling. Each is 1,500 VA and included with general lighting for demand.
Fixed appliances (excluding range, dryer, space-conditioning)
Examples: dishwasher, disposal, microwave, water heater, well pump. Per 220.53, if you have four or more fastened-in-place appliances, apply 75% to their combined nameplate VA; otherwise use 100%.
kW
Per 220.55 Table and notes: for a single household range, use the nameplate at 100%. For multiple ranges, use Table 220.55; multi-range diversity is not implemented in this version.
Per 220.54: a single residential dryer is calculated at 5,000 VA or the nameplate rating, whichever is larger, at 100%.
Space-conditioning (use the larger per 220.60)
Enter both if known. Per 220.60, include only the larger of the noncoincident heating or cooling load (not both).
