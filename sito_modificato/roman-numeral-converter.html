Glossary of Terms

  • Roman Numerals: A numeral system originating from ancient Rome.
  • Subtractive Notation: Using a smaller numeral before a larger one to indicate subtraction, e.g., IV for 4.

To convert 1994 to Roman numerals, break it down: 1000 (M), 900 (CM), 90 (XC), and 4 (IV) to get MCMXCIV.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What are Roman numerals?

Roman numerals are a numeral system originating in ancient Rome, using combinations of letters from the Latin alphabet (I, V, X, L, C, D, M).

How do you convert numbers to Roman numerals?

To convert numbers, break them into thousands, hundreds, tens, and units and convert each into Roman numerals.

How do you write 2023 in Roman numerals?

2023 is written as MMXXIII in Roman numerals.

Can Roman numerals represent zero?

No, Roman numerals do not have a symbol for zero.

Why does the calculator not accept negative numbers?

The Roman numeral system does not support negative numbers.
