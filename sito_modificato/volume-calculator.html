Notes:

Volume formulas used

Rectangular prism: \( V = l \cdot w \cdot h \)

Cylinder: \( V = \pi r^2 h \)

Cone: \( V = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^2 h \)

Sphere: \( V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^3 \)

Pyramid: \( V = \frac{1}{3} A_{base} h \), for rectangular base \( A_{base} = l \cdot w \)

Circular frustum: \( V = \frac{\pi h}{12} (D_1^2 + D_1 D_2 + D_2^2) \)

Horizontal tank (liquid): \( A_{seg} = r^2 \cos^{-1}(\frac{r-h}{r}) - (r-h)\sqrt{2rh - h^2} \), \( V = A_{seg} \cdot L \)

FAQs

Can I use this for landscaping or concrete?

Yes. Enter the dimensions in meters and read the volume in m³ — that’s what most concrete and soil suppliers use.

Why do I get NaN for the tank?

Check that the fill depth is between 0 and the tank diameter. Outside of this range the circular segment formula becomes invalid.
