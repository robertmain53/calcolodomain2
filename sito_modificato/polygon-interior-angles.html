Polygon interior angles – definitions

A polygon is a 2D shape made from straight line segments joined end to end. At each vertex (corner) there is an interior angle inside the polygon and an exterior angle outside the polygon.

  • Interior angle: the angle formed inside the polygon at a vertex.
  • Exterior angle: the angle formed by extending one side of the polygon at a vertex.
  • Regular polygon: all sides and all interior angles are equal.

Formulas for interior and exterior angles

1. Sum of interior angles

Formula

\[ S = (n - 2)\times 180^\circ \]

where:

  • \(S\) = sum of interior angles
  • \(n\) = number of sides (and vertices)

This works for any simple polygon (convex or concave) with \(n \ge 3\). You can think of it as splitting the polygon into \((n - 2)\) triangles, each with 180°.

2. Each interior angle of a regular polygon

Formula (regular polygon only)

\[ A_{\text{int}} = \frac{(n - 2)\times 180^\circ}{n} \]

Because all interior angles are equal in a regular polygon, you just divide the total interior angle sum by the number of sides.

3. Each exterior angle of a regular polygon

Formulas (regular polygon)

\[ A_{\text{ext}} = 180^\circ - A_{\text{int}} \]

or equivalently

\[ A_{\text{ext}} = \frac{360^\circ}{n} \]

For any convex polygon, the sum of one exterior angle at each vertex is always 360°. In a regular polygon, all exterior angles are equal, so each one is \(360^\circ / n\).

Step-by-step examples

Example 1 – Sum of interior angles of a hexagon

Question: What is the sum of the interior angles of a hexagon?

Solution:

  1. A hexagon has \(n = 6\) sides.
  2. Use the formula \(S = (n - 2)\times 180^\circ\).
  3. \(S = (6 - 2)\times 180^\circ = 4\times 180^\circ = 720^\circ\).

Answer: The sum of the interior angles of a hexagon is 720°.

Example 2 – Each interior angle of a regular octagon

Question: Find each interior angle of a regular octagon.

Solution:

  1. An octagon has \(n = 8\) sides.
  2. First find the sum: \(S = (8 - 2)\times 180^\circ = 6\times 180^\circ = 1080^\circ\).
  3. Divide by \(n\): \(A_{\text{int}} = 1080^\circ / 8 = 135^\circ\).

Answer: Each interior angle of a regular octagon is 135°.

Example 3 – Find the number of sides from an interior angle

Question: A regular polygon has interior angles of 150°. How many sides does it have?

Solution:

  1. Let \(A_{\text{int}} = 150^\circ\).
  2. Use the relationship between interior and exterior angles: \[ A_{\text{ext}} = 180^\circ - A_{\text{int}} = 180^\circ - 150^\circ = 30^\circ \]
  3. For a regular polygon, \(A_{\text{ext}} = 360^\circ / n\), so: \[ 30^\circ = \frac{360^\circ}{n} \Rightarrow n = \frac{360^\circ}{30^\circ} = 12 \]

Answer: It is a regular 12-sided polygon (a dodecagon).

Interior vs exterior angles – key facts

  • The sum of interior angles of an n-gon is \((n - 2)\times 180^\circ\).
  • The sum of one exterior angle at each vertex of a convex polygon is always 360°.
  • In a regular polygon, interior and exterior angles at a vertex form a straight line: \[ A_{\text{int}} + A_{\text{ext}} = 180^\circ \]
  • Concave polygons have at least one interior angle greater than 180°, but the same sum formula still applies.

FAQ

What is the formula for the sum of interior angles of a polygon?

For any simple polygon with \(n\) sides, the sum of the interior angles is \((n - 2)\times 180^\circ\). This comes from dividing the polygon into \((n - 2)\) triangles from one vertex.

How do I find each interior angle of a regular polygon?

First compute the sum of interior angles using \((n - 2)\times 180^\circ\), then divide by \(n\): \[ A_{\text{int}} = \frac{(n - 2)\times 180^\circ}{n} \]

Can a polygon have interior angles greater than 180°?

Yes. If a polygon is concave, at least one interior angle is greater than 180°. The formula for the sum of interior angles still holds as long as the polygon is simple (its sides do not cross).

How do I know if a regular polygon is possible from a given interior angle?

If you know each interior angle \(A_{\text{int}}\), compute \[ n = \frac{360^\circ}{180^\circ - A_{\text{int}}}. \] If \(n\) is a whole number ≥ 3, then a regular polygon with that interior angle exists.

What is the interior angle of a regular polygon with 20 sides?

For \(n = 20\): \[ A_{\text{int}} = \frac{(20 - 2)\times 180^\circ}{20} = \frac{18\times 180^\circ}{20} = \frac{3240^\circ}{20} = 162^\circ. \] Each interior angle is 162°, and each exterior angle is 18°.
