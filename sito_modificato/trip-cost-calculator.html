How this trip cost calculator works

This trip cost calculator is designed to give you a realistic, itemized budget for road trips and vacations. Unlike simple fuel-only tools, it includes fuel, tolls, lodging, food, parking and other extras, and shows both the total and per-person cost.

1. Fuel cost

The calculator supports both MPG and L/100 km, and both price per gallon and per liter.

Step 1 – Normalize units

If distance is in km, it is converted to miles:

\(\text{miles} = \text{km} \times 0.621371\)

If fuel economy is in L/100 km, it is converted to MPG:

\(\text{MPG} = \dfrac{235.215}{\text{L/100 km}}\)

If fuel price is per liter, it is converted to price per gallon:

\(\text{price/gal} = \text{price/L} \times 3.78541\)

Step 2 – Fuel needed

\(\text{gallons} = \dfrac{\text{distance (miles)}}{\text{MPG}}\)

Step 3 – Fuel cost

\(\text{fuel cost} = \text{gallons} \times \text{price per gallon}\)

2. Lodging and food

Lodging cost is simply the nightly rate multiplied by the number of nights. Food and other daily costs are based on the estimated number of trip days and the number of travelers.

\(\text{lodging cost} = \text{nights} \times \text{lodging per night}\)

\(\text{food cost} = \text{trip days} \times \text{food per day per person} \times \text{travelers}\)

\(\text{other daily cost} = \text{trip days} \times \text{other daily per person} \times \text{travelers}\)

Trip days are estimated from driving time and nights. Driving time is distance divided by average speed. The tool converts hours to days and then adds nights as full days. If you enter a manual trip length in days, that value overrides the automatic estimate.

3. Tolls, parking and other fixed costs

Tolls, parking and other fixed costs are added directly to the total. These are not multiplied by days or travelers.

\(\text{extras} = \text{tolls} + \text{parking} + \text{other fixed}\)

4. Total and per-person cost

\(\text{total cost} = \text{fuel} + \text{lodging} + \text{food} + \text{other daily} + \text{extras}\)

\(\text{cost per person} = \dfrac{\text{total cost}}{\text{number of travelers}}\)

Tips for more accurate trip budgeting

  • Use real fuel economy: Base MPG on your actual driving or on long-term trip computer data.
  • Check current fuel prices: Use prices along your route, not just at home.
  • Be conservative with lodging: Add a buffer for taxes, resort fees and parking.
  • Include activities: Put tickets, tours and attractions into “Other fixed costs” or “Other daily cost”.
  • Plan for contingencies: Add 5–15% on top of the estimate for unexpected expenses.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use this for international trips?

Yes. Enter distance in miles or kilometers, fuel economy in MPG or L/100 km, and fuel price in your local currency per gallon or liter. All results will be in the same currency you used for fuel and other costs.

How should I estimate tolls?

For the most accurate tolls, use a map or toll-specific service to get a total for your route, then enter that amount in the tolls field. If you only have a rough idea, it is better to overestimate slightly.

Does this include vehicle wear and depreciation?

No. The calculator focuses on out-of-pocket trip expenses: fuel, lodging, food and fees. If you want to include wear and depreciation, you can add an extra amount per mile or per day into the “Other fixed costs” or “Other daily cost per person” fields.

What if my trip has multiple vehicles?

Run the calculator once per vehicle and add the totals, or approximate a combined fuel economy and total number of travelers, then use that single run as a quick estimate.
