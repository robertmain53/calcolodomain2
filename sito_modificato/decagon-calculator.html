The Formula Explained

For a regular polygon with \(n=10\) (decagon) and side length \(s\):

\[ P = n s = 10s \quad,\quad a = \frac{s}{2\tan\!\left(\frac{\pi}{n}\right)} = \frac{s}{2\tan\!\left(\frac{\pi}{10}\right)} \] \[ R = \frac{s}{2\sin\!\left(\frac{\pi}{n}\right)} = \frac{s}{2\sin\!\left(\frac{\pi}{10}\right)} \quad,\quad A = \frac{n}{4}s^2 \cot\!\left(\frac{\pi}{n}\right) = \frac{5}{2}s^2 \cot\!\left(\frac{\pi}{10}\right) \] \[ \text{Equivalently: } A = \tfrac{1}{2} P a = 5 s a = 5 R^2 \sin\!\left(\tfrac{2\pi}{n}\right)=5 R^2 \sin\!\left(\tfrac{\pi}{5}\right). \]
Interior angle \(= 180^\circ - \tfrac{360^\circ}{n} = 144^\circ\), central angle \(= \tfrac{360^\circ}{n} = 36^\circ\).

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Given: side \(s=12\ \text{cm}\).

  1. Perimeter: \(P=10s=120\ \text{cm}\).
  2. Apothem: \(a=\dfrac{s}{2\tan(\pi/10)} \approx \dfrac{12}{2\tan 18^\circ} \approx 18.466\ \text{cm}\).
  3. Area (using \(A=\tfrac12Pa\)): \(A = \tfrac12 \cdot 120 \cdot 18.466 \approx 1{,}107.96\ \text{cm}^2\).
  4. Circumradius: \(R=\dfrac{s}{2\sin(\pi/10)} \approx \dfrac{12}{2\sin 18^\circ} \approx 19.416\ \text{cm}\).

The calculator performs these steps instantly from any single input.

Frequently Asked Questions

What inputs does this decagon calculator accept?

Exactly one of: side \(s\), apothem \(a\), circumradius \(R\), perimeter \(P\), or area \(A\).

Do I need to convert units manually?

No. Pick a unit (e.g., cm). Area is shown in squared units (e.g., cm²) automatically.

How are formulas derived?

From standard regular-polygon geometry: trigonometric relations with central angle \(\tfrac{2\pi}{n}\) and right triangles formed by radii and apothem.

Can I get high precision?

Yes. Set “Decimal places.” Internal math uses full precision; only display is rounded.

What are the decagon angles?

Interior angle = 144°, exterior angle = 36°, central angle = 36°.
