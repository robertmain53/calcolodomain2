Methodology

We multiply body weight by 0.03 to find a baseline water volume in liters, then add a small bonus for higher activity levels. The calculation follows public health guidance to keep hydration goals within a safe range.

Data Source and Methodology

The calculation draws from the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) hydration guidance and common clinical practice. It balances simple inputs with a modest activity adjustment to keep the recommendation practical.

The Formula Explained

Begin with weight × 0.03 L, and add an activity bonus: +0.5 L for moderate activity or +1.0 L for active lifestyles. Sedentary individuals keep the baseline value.

Glossary of Variables

  • Weight: Body mass in kilograms.
  • Age: Age in years; used to qualify completeness of input.
  • Activity Level: Sedentary, Moderate, or Active lifestyle classification.
  • Recommended Water Intake: Total liters of water to drink per day.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is hydration important? Proper hydration regulates temperature, supports joints, and transports nutrients.

Can I drink too much water? Yes. Excessive water intake dilutes electrolytes and can cause discomfort or dangerous conditions. Follow the calculator as a guideline and listen to your body.
