Pig Latin rules used by this translator

English → Pig Latin

  • Words starting with a vowel: add yay or way to the end.
  • Words starting with consonant sounds: move all starting consonants to the end and add ay.
  • Keeps punctuation, numbers, and spacing in place.
  • Preserves capitalization (Title Case, ALL CAPS, etc.).

Pig Latin → English

  • Detects vowel vs consonant forms based on endings (ay, yay, way).
  • Rebuilds the most likely English word and restores capitalization.
  • Leaves unknown tokens (numbers, symbols) unchanged.

How to translate English to Pig Latin

Pig Latin is a playful language game where you disguise English words by moving sounds around. It’s popular in classrooms, games, and puzzles.

This translator automates the rules so you can convert whole paragraphs instantly, without losing punctuation or capitalization.

Core rules (standard Pig Latin)

  1. Words starting with a vowel (A, E, I, O, U)
    Add yay (or way, depending on your setting) to the end.
    Examples:
    • apple → appleyay
    • orange → orangeyay
  2. Words starting with a consonant or consonant cluster
    Move all leading consonants to the end, then add ay.
    Examples:
    • dog → ogday
    • smile → ilesmay (sm + ile)
    • chair → airchay (ch + air)
  3. Capitalization and punctuation
    The translator:
    • keeps commas, periods, and question marks in place
    • preserves ALL CAPS and Title Case
    • leaves numbers and symbols unchanged

Examples

  • Hello, world! → Ellohay, orldway!
  • This is a secret message. → Isthay isyay ayay ecretsay essagemay.
  • PROGRAMMING is fun → OGRAMMINGPRay isyay unfay

Tips for using the Pig Latin translator

  • Choose your dialect: Use the “Vowel rule” dropdown to switch between yay and way styles.
  • Toggle Y as a vowel: When enabled, words like yellow treat y as a vowel in some positions.
  • Round-trip check: Translate English → Pig Latin, then switch direction and translate back to see how reversible your text is.
  • Use for games and teaching: Great for language lessons, secret notes, or just having fun with friends.

Limitations

Pig Latin is not a real language, so there isn’t a single “official” rule set. This tool follows a common schoolyard variant and makes reasonable guesses when translating back to English, but some ambiguous words or invented slang may not reverse perfectly.

FAQ

Can I translate long texts?

Yes. The translator runs entirely in your browser and can handle long paragraphs. Extremely large texts may be slower, but there is no strict character limit.

Does it support other languages?

The rules are designed for English spelling and pronunciation. Text in other languages will still be processed, but the results may not follow any familiar Pig Latin convention.
