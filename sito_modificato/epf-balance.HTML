How EPF contributions are calculated

In standard cases (private sector), the contribution is split between the Employees' Provident Fund (EPF) and the Employees' Pension Scheme (EPS).

  • Employee contribution: 12% of Basic + DA. (Entirely to EPF).
  • Employer contribution: 12% total, split as:
    • 3.67% to EPF account.
    • 8.33% to EPS (Pension) — Subject to a cap of ₹1,250/month (8.33% of ₹15,000).

Example Calculation (Wage = ₹30,000):

If restricted to the statutory ceiling of ₹15,000:

  • Employee EPF = $12\% \times 15,000 = ₹1,800$
  • Employer EPS = $8.33\% \times 15,000 \approx ₹1,250$
  • Employer EPF = $(12\% \times 15,000) - 1,250 = ₹550$

Employer Contrib. Yes (Mandatory) No No

How EPF interest is compounded

Interest is declared annually but calculated on the monthly running balance. The formula for the monthly balance accumulation is:

$$ \text{Balance}_{t} = \text{Balance}_{t-1} \times (1 + \frac{r}{12}) + C_{emp} + C_{employer\_epf} $$

Where $r$ is the annual interest rate, and $C$ represents monthly contributions.

Withdrawal Rules

Full Withdrawal

Allowed only upon retirement (age 58) or if unemployed for 2 months. Full withdrawal before 5 years of continuous service renders the amount taxable.

Partial Withdrawals

Allowed for specific needs like house construction (after 5 years), marriage (after 7 years), or medical emergencies.
