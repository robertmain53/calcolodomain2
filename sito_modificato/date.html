How this date calculator works

This date calculator lets you:

  • Add or subtract any combination of years, months, weeks, and days from a starting date.
  • Instantly see the resulting date and the day of the week.
  • Calculate the number of days between two dates, plus an approximate breakdown in years, months, and days.

Adding and subtracting dates

When you add or subtract time, the calculator:

  1. Converts weeks to days: \( \text{totalDays} = \text{days} + 7 \times \text{weeks} \).
  2. Applies years and months first, then days.
  3. Uses your browser’s built-in Gregorian calendar, so leap years and month lengths are handled automatically.

Conceptual formula

Let \( D_0 \) be the starting date and \( \Delta \) the offset you enter:

  • \( \Delta_{\text{days}} = \text{days} + 7 \times \text{weeks} \)
  • \( \Delta_{\text{months}} = \text{months} + 12 \times \text{years} \)

The resulting date is:

\( D_{\text{result}} = D_0 + \Delta_{\text{months}} \text{ (in calendar months)} + \Delta_{\text{days}} \text{ (in calendar days)} \)

Difference between two dates

For the “Difference Between Dates” tab, the calculator:

  • Computes the absolute difference in days between the two dates.
  • Provides an approximate breakdown into years, months, and days assuming 365 days per year and 30 days per month. This is useful for a quick human-readable duration, but it is not a legal or financial definition of elapsed time.

Typical uses

  • Find a project deadline a certain number of days from today.
  • Work out a due date by adding weeks or months to a start date.
  • Calculate how many days are left until a birthday, anniversary, or event.
  • Check how long has passed between two historical dates.

FAQ

Does the calculator account for leap years?

Yes. All calculations rely on the standard Gregorian calendar implementation in your browser or device, so leap years and February 29 are handled automatically.

What happens if the target month has fewer days?

When adding months or years, if the target month does not have the same day number (for example, adding one month to January 31), the date is adjusted to the last valid day of the target month (February 28 or 29, April 30, etc.).

Is the year/month/day breakdown exact?

The total days between two dates is exact. The breakdown into years, months, and days is approximate, using 365 days per year and 30 days per month. This is usually sufficient for planning and general understanding; for contracts or legal deadlines, always follow the official rules for that jurisdiction.

Which time zone is used?

The calculator uses your device’s local time zone. If you are working with dates across time zones or involving daylight saving transitions, keep in mind that local midnight boundaries may differ.
