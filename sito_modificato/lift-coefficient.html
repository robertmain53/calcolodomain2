The Formula Explained

The lift coefficient (Cl) is calculated using the formula:

Cl = (2 * Lift) / (Density * Velocity² * Area)

Glossary of Terms

  • Velocity: Speed of the aircraft relative to the air.
  • Density: Mass per unit volume of the air.
  • Wing Area: Surface area of the aircraft wing.
  • Lift Force: Force exerted by the air on the wing to lift the aircraft.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For example, to calculate the lift coefficient with a velocity of 50 m/s, density of 1.225 kg/m³, wing area of 20 m², and lift force of 5000 N:

Cl = (2 * 5000) / (1.225 * 50² * 20) = 0.326

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the lift coefficient?

The lift coefficient is a dimensionless number that relates the lift generated by a wing to the density of the air, the velocity, and the area of the wing.

How do I improve the lift coefficient?

Improving the lift coefficient can be achieved by increasing the wing area, optimizing the wing shape, or by increasing the angle of attack.

Is this calculator applicable for all aircraft?

This calculator is most accurate for fixed-wing aircraft under subsonic conditions.

Why is air density important in calculating lift?

Air density affects the amount of lift generated; lower density at higher altitudes results in less lift.

How does velocity affect lift?

Higher velocity increases the lift force because it increases the dynamic pressure over the wing surface.
