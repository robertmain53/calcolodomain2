The Formula Explained

We use the following formula to calculate the ecological footprint:

Footprint = (Electricity × 0.7) + (Transport × 0.2) + (Waste × 0.1)

Glossary of Terms

  • Electricity: The total kWh consumed per year.
  • Transport: Total miles traveled by any means of transportation per year.
  • Waste: Total waste generated in kilograms per year.

Practical Example

For example, if you use 3000 kWh of electricity, travel 10000 miles, and generate 500 kg of waste annually, your footprint would be calculated as follows:

(3000 × 0.7) + (10000 × 0.2) + (500 × 0.1) = 4600

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is an ecological footprint?

An ecological footprint measures how much nature we have and how much nature we use.

How can I reduce my ecological footprint?

You can reduce your ecological footprint by decreasing energy consumption, using public transport, and recycling waste.

Why is it important to calculate my ecological footprint?

Understanding your ecological footprint helps you make informed decisions to reduce your impact on the environment.
