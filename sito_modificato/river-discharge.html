The Formula Explained

The river discharge is calculated using the formula: Q = V × A where:

  • Q: Discharge (m³/s)
  • V: Velocity (m/s)
  • A: Cross-sectional Area (m²)

Glossary of Terms

  • Velocity: The speed of water flow in meters per second.
  • Area: The cross-sectional area of the river in square meters.
  • Discharge: The volume of water flowing per second.

How It Works: A Practical Example

For example, if a river has a velocity of 2.5 m/s and a cross-sectional area of 30 m², the discharge is calculated as follows: Q = 2.5 × 30 = 75 m³/s.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is river discharge?

River discharge is the volume of water flowing through a river channel over a specific period, usually measured in cubic meters per second (m³/s).

Why is calculating river discharge important?

Calculating river discharge is crucial for flood forecasting, water management, and environmental studies.

How can I measure river velocity?

River velocity can be measured using flow meters or by observing floating objects over a known distance and time interval.

What factors affect river discharge?

Factors include rainfall, upstream water usage, and changes in riverbed topography.

Can this calculator be used for any river?

Yes, as long as you have accurate measurements of velocity and cross-sectional area.
