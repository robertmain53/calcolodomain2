The Formula Explained

For an interest-only loan, the monthly payment is calculated using the formula:

Monthly Payment = Loan Amount × (Interest Rate / 100) / 12

Glossary of Terms

  • Loan Amount: The total amount borrowed.
  • Interest Rate: The annual interest rate applied to the loan.
  • Loan Term: The duration over which the loan is expected to be repaid.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Consider a loan amount of $100,000 with an interest rate of 5% for a term of 5 years. The monthly payment calculation would be:

Monthly Payment = 100,000 × (5 / 100) / 12

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is an interest-only loan?

An interest-only loan is a type of loan where you only pay the interest for a certain period, after which you pay both interest and principal.

How do I calculate interest-only payments?

Interest-only payments can be calculated using the formula: Payment = Loan Amount × Interest Rate.

Are interest-only loans risky?

Interest-only loans can be riskier than traditional loans as they don’t reduce the principal balance, potentially leading to higher overall costs.

Can I pay more than the interest on an interest-only loan?

Yes, many lenders allow additional payments to reduce the principal balance, which can lower future interest payments.

What happens when the interest-only period ends?

Once the interest-only period ends, you need to begin repaying both principal and interest, which will increase your monthly payments.
