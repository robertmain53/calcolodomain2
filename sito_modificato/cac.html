The Formula Explained

CAC = \(\frac{\text{Total Marketing Spend} + \text{Total Sales Spend}}{\text{Number of New Customers}}\)

Glossary of Terms

  • Total Marketing Spend: The total amount spent on marketing activities.
  • Total Sales Spend: The total amount spent on sales activities.
  • Number of New Customers: The number of new customers acquired over a specific period.
  • Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): The cost of acquiring a new customer.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Assume your Total Marketing Spend is $10,000 and Total Sales Spend is $5,000, and you acquire 100 new customers. The CAC is calculated as:

CAC = \(\frac{10,000 + 5,000}{100} = 150\). Therefore, your CAC is $150 per customer.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)?

CAC is the cost associated with acquiring a new customer, including marketing and sales expenses.

Why is CAC important?

CAC is crucial for understanding how effectively a company is spending its budget to attract new customers.
