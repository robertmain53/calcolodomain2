Ounces vs. Fluid Ounces: A Critical Difference

The word "ounce" is one of the most confusing units. It's crucial to know if you are measuring **weight (mass)** or **volume**. They are not the same!

  • Ounce (oz): This is a unit of **WEIGHT/MASS**. It is used for dry ingredients like flour, sugar, or nuts. The formula is 1 oz = 28.34952 grams.
  • Fluid Ounce (fl oz): This is a unit of **VOLUME**. It is used for liquids like water, milk, or oil. The formula is 1 US fl oz = 29.57353 milliliters.

A "cup of flour" and a "cup of water" have the same **volume** (8 fl oz) but very different **weights** (mass). Our first calculator is for weight (oz to g), and the second is for volume (fl oz to ml).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How many grams are in 1 ounce?

There are **28.34952 grams** in 1 standard (avoirdupois) ounce. For cooking, this is often rounded to 28.35 g.

How many grams are in 8 oz?

You multiply 8 by 28.34952, which equals **226.796 grams**.

Is a fluid ounce (fl oz) the same as an ounce (oz)?

No. An ounce (oz) measures weight/mass, while a fluid ounce (fl oz) measures volume. 1 cup of water has a volume of 8 fl oz and a weight of about 8.3 oz. 1 cup of flour has the same 8 fl oz volume but only weighs about 4.25 oz.
