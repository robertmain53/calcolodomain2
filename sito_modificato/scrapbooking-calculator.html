What this scrapbooking calculator does

This scrapbooking calculator is designed for practical, everyday use at your craft table. It helps you:

  • Check how many photos and mats will fit on a page.
  • See how much of the page is used vs. left for journaling and embellishments.
  • Plan how many mats or panels you can cut from a full sheet of paper.
  • Switch between inches and centimeters without re‑doing the math.

How the layout calculation works

The calculator treats your photos (plus optional mats) as rectangles arranged in a simple grid. You choose how many photos per row, and it computes how many rows you need.

Step 1 – Mat size (if used)

If you add a mat border of size \(b\) around a photo of width \(w\) and height \(h\):

\[ w_\text{mat} = w + 2b,\quad h_\text{mat} = h + 2b \]

Step 2 – Grid dimensions

With N photos and C photos per row:

\[ \text{rows} = \left\lceil \frac{N}{C} \right\rceil \]

Horizontal span (including spacing \(s\)):

\[ W_\text{layout} = C \cdot w_\text{mat} + (C - 1)\cdot s \]

Vertical span:

\[ H_\text{layout} = \text{rows} \cdot h_\text{mat} + (\text{rows} - 1)\cdot s \]

Step 3 – Page fit

With page width \(W_p\), height \(H_p\) and margin \(m\):

\[ W_\text{usable} = W_p - 2m,\quad H_\text{usable} = H_p - 2m \]

If \(W_\text{layout} \le W_\text{usable}\) and \(H_\text{layout} \le H_\text{usable}\), the layout fits.

How the cutting planner works

The cutting planner assumes straight cuts in a grid pattern. It calculates how many pieces of a given size you can cut from a full sheet, optionally leaving a trim margin around the edges.

Effective sheet size

With sheet width \(W_s\), height \(H_s\) and trim margin \(t\):

\[ W_\text{eff} = W_s - 2t,\quad H_\text{eff} = H_s - 2t \]

Pieces per sheet

For piece width \(w\) and height \(h\):

\[ n_x = \left\lfloor \frac{W_\text{eff}}{w} \right\rfloor,\quad n_y = \left\lfloor \frac{H_\text{eff}}{h} \right\rfloor \]

\[ N_\text{pieces} = n_x \times n_y \]

The calculator also tries the rotated orientation (swap \(w\) and \(h\)) and shows which yields more pieces.

Tips for using this tool effectively

  • Start with your photos. Enter the exact print size (e.g., 3×4, 4×6) before adding mats.
  • Leave realistic margins. Most scrapbookers prefer at least 0.5 in (or 1–1.5 cm) around the page edge.
  • Use spacing to avoid crowding. Even 0.25–0.5 in between photos can make a layout feel more balanced.
  • Use the cutting planner for batches. If you’re making multiple identical pages or cards, it will tell you how many sheets you need.

Common use cases

  • Planning a 12×12 layout with multiple 4×6 photos and journaling blocks.
  • Designing pocket pages with 3×4 and 4×6 cards.
  • Cutting card fronts and layers from 8.5×11 or A4 cardstock.
  • Batch‑cutting photo mats for a mini album or wedding guest book.

FAQ

Can I use this for card making or mini albums?

Yes. Any project that uses rectangular pages and pieces works. For example, set the page size to 4.25×5.5 in for an A2 card front, or to your mini album page size, then enter your panel sizes as “photos.”

Does this calculator handle circles or shaped photos?

Not directly. The math assumes rectangles, but you can still use the tool to estimate how many circular elements will fit by using their bounding box (e.g., a 3 in circle as 3×3 in).

How do I estimate how many sheets of paper I need?

Use the cutting planner to find how many pieces you can cut from one sheet. Then divide the total number of pieces you need by that number and round up to the next whole sheet.

Can I save or print my layout?

This calculator focuses on fast planning and math. For printable sketches or digital layouts, you can transfer the dimensions into your favorite design tool or sketch them on graph paper using the measurements provided.
