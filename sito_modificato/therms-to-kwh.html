How to convert therms to kWh

A therm is a unit of heat energy commonly used for natural gas billing. A kilowatt‑hour (kWh) is the standard unit for electrical energy. To compare gas and electricity usage or costs, you often need to convert between therms and kWh.

Exact conversion factors

  • US therm (100,000 BTUIT): 1 therm = 105.506 MJ ≈ 29.3001111 kWh
  • UK therm: 1 therm = 105.505585262 MJ ≈ 29.3071111 kWh

Therms to kWh formula

For US therms:

\[ \text{kWh} = \text{therms} \times 29.3001111 \]

For UK therms:

\[ \text{kWh} = \text{therms} \times 29.3071111 \]

Example: 10 therms to kWh

Using US therms:

\[ \text{kWh} = 10 \times 29.3001111 \approx 293.0 \text{ kWh} \]

Using UK therms:

\[ \text{kWh} = 10 \times 29.3071111 \approx 293.1 \text{ kWh} \]

How to convert kWh to therms

To go the other way, divide by the same factor.

kWh to therms formula

For US therms:

\[ \text{therms} = \frac{\text{kWh}}{29.3001111} \]

For UK therms:

\[ \text{therms} = \frac{\text{kWh}}{29.3071111} \]

Example: 1000 kWh to therms (US)

\[ \text{therms} = \frac{1000}{29.3001111} \approx 34.13 \text{ therms} \]

Using a custom factor from your gas bill

Many utilities publish a monthly conversion factor that converts gas volume (e.g. CCF or m³) to therms or kWh, based on the actual energy content of the gas. This can slightly differ from the theoretical constant.

Typical examples you might see on a bill:

  • “1 therm = 29.15 kWh”
  • “Conversion factor: 1.034 therms per CCF”

If your bill gives a value like kWh per therm, select “Custom factor” in the calculator and enter that number. All conversions will then match your bill exactly.

Comparing gas vs electric heating costs

Once you convert therms to kWh, you can directly compare the cost of gas and electricity. For example, suppose:

  • Gas price: $1.20 per therm (US therm)
  • Electricity price: $0.18 per kWh

First, find the cost per kWh of gas energy:

\[ \text{Gas cost per kWh} = \frac{\$1.20}{29.3001111} \approx \$0.041 \text{ per kWh} \]

In this case, gas energy is about 4.1¢/kWh, while electricity is 18¢/kWh. However, you must also consider the efficiency of your furnace or boiler versus your electric heater or heat pump.

FAQ

How many kWh are in 1 therm?

Using the US therm definition, 1 therm = 29.3001111 kWh. Using the UK therm definition, 1 therm = 29.3071111 kWh. Many US utilities round this to 29.3 kWh per therm.

Why do some sites show slightly different numbers?

Differences come from:

  • Using US vs UK therm definitions
  • Rounding (e.g. 29.3 vs 29.30 vs 29.3001)
  • Using a real‑world average gas composition instead of the ideal definition

For most household comparisons, these differences are tiny. If you need to match your bill exactly, use the custom factor option.

Is this calculator suitable for solar or heat pump sizing?

Yes. A common use is to:

  1. Take your annual gas usage in therms from past bills.
  2. Convert to kWh using this tool.
  3. Adjust for efficiency (e.g. furnace vs heat pump COP).
  4. Use the resulting kWh to size solar panels or estimate electric bills.

This calculator focuses on the energy unit conversion; you can then plug the kWh into other tools for solar or heat pump analysis.
