How this Nunavut tax calculator works

This Nunavut tax calculator estimates your 2025 income tax as a resident of Nunavut. It combines:

  • Federal income tax (Canada-wide brackets)
  • Nunavut territorial income tax
  • CPP contributions and EI premiums for employment income
  • RRSP and similar deductions you enter

The result is an estimate of your total tax and your net (after‑tax) income for the year, plus an optional per‑pay breakdown.

Assumptions and simplifications

This tool is designed for clarity rather than handling every edge case. It assumes:

  • You are a resident of Nunavut for the full tax year.
  • You have only one main source of employment or self‑employment income.
  • No complex credits (disability, tuition, dividend tax credits, etc.) are applied.
  • Only the basic personal amount and standard CPP/EI rules are used.

Actual tax payable can differ if you have additional deductions, credits, or special income types (capital gains, dividends, stock options, etc.).

Nunavut income tax brackets (territorial)

Nunavut uses a progressive income tax system. For 2025, the territorial brackets can be summarized as:

Example structure (illustrative):

  • Bracket 1: 4% on the first portion of taxable income
  • Bracket 2: 7% on the next portion
  • Bracket 3: 9% on the next portion
  • Bracket 4: 11.5% on the amount over the third threshold

The calculator uses current Nunavut rate sheets and thresholds. Always refer to the Government of Nunavut or CRA for the official tables.

Federal income tax brackets (Canada)

Federal tax is also progressive. Each slice of your taxable income is taxed at the rate for that bracket, and the totals are added together.

The calculator applies the federal basic personal amount and then computes tax using the current‑year federal brackets.

CPP and EI contributions

If you select Employment income, the calculator estimates:

  • CPP contributions – a percentage of pensionable earnings between the yearly basic exemption and the maximum pensionable earnings.
  • EI premiums – a percentage of insurable earnings up to the yearly maximum.

For Self‑employment / other income, CPP and EI are not included in the estimate. Self‑employed CPP and EI rules are more complex and are best handled with professional advice or CRA tools.

Formulas used

1. Gross income

If you enter an annual salary:

\( \text{Gross income} = \text{Annual salary} \)

If you enter an hourly rate:

\( \text{Gross income} = \text{Hourly rate} \times \text{Hours per week} \times 52 \)

2. Taxable income

\( \text{Taxable income} = \max(0,\ \text{Gross income} - \text{RRSP and other deductions}) \)

3. Applying tax brackets

For each of federal and Nunavut tax, the calculator applies the brackets like this:

For each bracket \( i \) with lower limit \( L_i \), upper limit \( U_i \) and rate \( r_i \):

\( \text{Tax}_i = \max\bigl(0,\ \min(\text{Taxable income}, U_i) - L_i\bigr) \times r_i \)

Total tax is the sum of all \( \text{Tax}_i \) across brackets.

4. Net income

\( \text{Total tax} = \text{Federal tax} + \text{Nunavut tax} + \text{CPP} + \text{EI} \)

\( \text{Net income} = \text{Gross income} - \text{Total tax} \)

Average vs. marginal tax rate

  • Average tax rate = total tax ÷ gross income. This tells you what percentage of your total income goes to tax and contributions.
  • Marginal tax rate = the rate that applies to your next dollar of income (combined federal + Nunavut rate at your bracket). This is useful for decisions about overtime, bonuses, or RRSP contributions.

Limitations and disclaimer

This Nunavut tax calculator is for educational and planning purposes only. It does not replace professional tax advice or official CRA tools. It does not handle:

  • Non‑resident or part‑year resident situations
  • Capital gains, dividends, rental, or business income with special tax treatment
  • Detailed credits such as tuition, disability, medical expenses, or northern residents deductions
  • Complex family situations (spousal amounts, dependants, etc.)

For official guidance, consult the Canada Revenue Agency, the Government of Nunavut, or a qualified tax professional.

Frequently asked questions

Does Nunavut have a sales tax?

Nunavut does not have a separate provincial sales tax. The federal Goods and Services Tax (GST) applies to most purchases. This calculator focuses on income tax, not GST.

Can I use this for payroll in Nunavut?

The calculator gives a good high‑level estimate of payroll deductions for a Nunavut employee, but official payroll remittances should be based on CRA payroll deduction tables (T4032NU) or certified payroll software.

How can I reduce my Nunavut income tax?

Common ways to reduce taxable income include RRSP contributions, certain employment expenses, and claiming all eligible credits (basic personal amount, northern residents deductions, etc.). This calculator lets you model the impact of RRSP and similar deductions.
