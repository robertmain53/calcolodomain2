How to convert tonnes to kilograms

A tonne (also called a metric ton and written as t) is defined as exactly 1,000 kilograms. This makes the conversion between tonnes and kilograms very straightforward.

Tonnes to kilograms formula

kilograms = tonnes × 1,000

Example: 2.5 t × 1,000 = 2,500 kg

Kilograms to tonnes formula

tonnes = kilograms ÷ 1,000

Example: 7,500 kg ÷ 1,000 = 7.5 t

Example 1 – Convert 3.2 tonnes to kilograms

Answer: 18,750 kg = 18.75 t.

Tonnes vs tons: what’s the difference?

The words tonne and ton are often confused, but they are not the same:

  • Tonne (metric ton): exactly 1,000 kg. Used in most of the world and in the SI system.
  • US short ton: 2,000 lb ≈ 907.185 kg.
  • UK long ton: 2,240 lb ≈ 1,016.047 kg.

This calculator uses the metric tonne (t) only. If your data is in US or UK tons, convert to kilograms first using the appropriate factor, then you can convert to tonnes if needed.

When are tonnes and kilograms used?

Tonnes and kilograms are both metric units of mass:

  • Kilograms (kg) are used for everyday weights: people, luggage, small packages, etc.
  • Tonnes (t) are used for large quantities: cargo shipments, vehicles, industrial materials, agricultural yields, and CO₂ emissions.

Because 1 tonne = 1,000 kg, using tonnes keeps large numbers more readable. For example, instead of writing 125,000 kg you can write 125 t.

FAQ: tonnes to kilograms

How many kilograms are in a tonne?

Exactly 1,000 kilograms. The tonne is defined that way in the metric system.

How do I quickly convert in my head?

To go from tonnes to kilograms, just add three zeros (multiply by 1,000). To go from kilograms to tonnes, move the decimal point three places to the left.

Does this calculator support decimals?

Yes. You can enter decimal values like 0.75 t or 1234.56 kg. Use the “Decimal places” selector to control how many digits are shown in the result.
