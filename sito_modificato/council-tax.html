How council tax is calculated

Council tax is a local tax on residential properties in Great Britain. Each property is placed in a valuation band, and your local authority sets a yearly charge for a Band D property. Other bands are calculated as fixed fractions of Band D.

Step 1 – Band charge

Band charge = Band D charge × band multiplier

Step 2 – Apply discounts and premiums

Net charge = Band charge × (1 − total discounts + premiums)

Step 3 – Convert to monthly / weekly

Monthly ≈ Net charge ÷ 12
Weekly ≈ Net charge ÷ 52

Standard band multipliers

The statutory multipliers relative to Band D are:

  • England & Scotland (Bands A–H): A 6/9, B 7/9, C 8/9, D 9/9, E 11/9, F 13/9, G 15/9, H 18/9.
  • Wales (Bands A–I): A 6/9, B 7/9, C 8/9, D 9/9, E 11/9, F 13/9, G 15/9, H 18/9, I 21/9.

Northern Ireland does not use council tax bands; instead it has a domestic rating system based on property value and a regional rate. This calculator approximates NI bills by treating the entered figure as a Band D–equivalent charge.

Common discounts and exemptions

Key reductions the calculator can model include:

  • Single person discount (25%) – if only one adult is counted as living in the property.
  • Student exemption (100%) – if all residents are full-time students.
  • Severe mental impairment exemption – if all chargeable adults are severely mentally impaired and meet benefit criteria.
  • Disability band reduction – if your home has been adapted for a disabled person, the bill may be reduced by one band.
  • Council Tax Reduction / Support – income-based help with your bill, set by each council.
  • Empty / second home discounts or premiums – some councils offer discounts for second homes or charge premiums on long‑term empty properties.

The exact rules and percentages vary by local authority. This tool uses typical values to give a quick estimate; always confirm with your council.

Limitations and data sources

Official council tax information, including banding and local charges, is published by:

  • GOV.UK – Council Tax overview
  • Your local council’s website (for example Birmingham, Edinburgh, Cornwall, Buckinghamshire)
  • Citizens Advice and other independent guidance sites

This calculator does not pull live data from councils. For precise figures, refer to your latest bill or your council’s online council tax pages.

FAQ

Is council tax the same across the UK?

No. Each local authority sets its own Band D charge and may add parish, police and fire precepts. Bands and valuation dates also differ between England, Scotland and Wales, and Northern Ireland uses a separate domestic rating system.

Can I spread my council tax payments?

Most councils allow you to pay over 10 or 12 monthly instalments by Direct Debit. Some offer weekly or fortnightly options. The total yearly amount is the same; only the schedule changes.

What happens if I don’t pay?

Councils can issue reminders, court summons and liability orders, and may use enforcement agents (bailiffs), deductions from earnings or benefits, or in extreme cases, apply for committal to prison. If you are struggling, contact your council early to agree a payment plan or apply for Council Tax Reduction.
