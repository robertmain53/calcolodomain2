How to use the Stock Calculator

This stock calculator is designed to help you size your positions and understand the risk and potential return of each trade before you place it. It works for both long and short positions and lets you include trading fees.

1. Fill in your account and risk settings

  • Account size – your total trading capital (for example, 10,000).
  • Risk per trade (%) – the percentage of your account you are willing to lose on this trade (for example, 1–2%).
  • Position type – choose Long if you buy first and hope the price goes up, or Short if you sell first and hope the price goes down.

2. Set entry, stop‑loss and targets

  • Entry price – the price where you plan to open the position.
  • Stop‑loss price – the price where you will exit if the trade goes against you.
  • Target price – your main profit target. You can optionally add a second and third target.

For a long trade, the stop‑loss is normally below the entry and the target is above the entry. For a short trade, the stop‑loss is usually above the entry and the target is below the entry.

3. Add fees (optional)

  • Commission per trade – fixed cost per order (entry and exit each).
  • Fee per share – variable cost per share (for example, ECN fees or stamp duty).

The calculator subtracts fees from profit and adds them to loss so you see net P/L.

Formulas used by the stock calculator

Risk per trade and risk per share

Max risk per trade

\(\text{MaxRisk} = \text{AccountSize} \times \dfrac{\text{RiskPercent}}{100}\)

Risk per share (long)

\(\text{RiskPerShare} = \text{EntryPrice} - \text{StopPrice}\)

Risk per share (short)

\(\text{RiskPerShare} = \text{StopPrice} - \text{EntryPrice}\)

Position size (number of shares)

\(\text{Shares} = \left\lfloor \dfrac{\text{MaxRisk}}{\text{RiskPerShare}} \right\rfloor\)

The floor function means we always round down to the nearest whole share so that your actual risk does not exceed your chosen limit.

Position value and portfolio risk

Position value

\(\text{PositionValue} = \text{Shares} \times \text{EntryPrice}\)

Portfolio at risk (%)

\(\text{PortfolioRisk\%} = \dfrac{\text{MaxRisk}}{\text{AccountSize}} \times 100\)

Profit, loss and risk‑reward ratio

Gross profit per share (long)

\(\text{ProfitPerShare} = \text{TargetPrice} - \text{EntryPrice}\)

Gross profit per share (short)

\(\text{ProfitPerShare} = \text{EntryPrice} - \text{TargetPrice}\)

Gross profit

\(\text{GrossProfit} = \text{Shares} \times \text{ProfitPerShare}\)

Gross loss

\(\text{GrossLoss} = \text{Shares} \times \text{RiskPerShare}\)

Net profit / loss (after fees)

\(\text{NetProfit} = \text{GrossProfit} - \text{TotalFees}\)

\(\text{NetLoss} = \text{GrossLoss} + \text{TotalFees}\)

Risk‑reward ratio

\(\text{R:R} = \dfrac{\text{NetProfit}}{\text{NetLoss}}\)

Example: Long stock trade

Suppose you have a $10,000 account and you want to risk 1% on a long trade:

  • Account size = 10,000
  • Risk per trade = 1%
  • Entry price = 50
  • Stop‑loss price = 47.5
  • Target price = 55

Then:

  • Max risk per trade = 10,000 × 1% = $100
  • Risk per share = 50 − 47.5 = $2.50
  • Shares = floor(100 / 2.5) = 40
  • Position value = 40 × 50 = $2,000
  • Gross profit at target = 40 × (55 − 50) = $200
  • Risk‑reward ratio = 200 / 100 = 2:1

Risk management tips

  • Keep risk per trade small (often 0.5–2% of your account).
  • Always define your stop‑loss before you enter a trade.
  • Use the risk‑reward ratio to filter trades: many traders prefer at least 2:1.
  • Consider total exposure: large positions in correlated stocks increase portfolio risk.

FAQ

What does this stock calculator do?

It calculates how many shares you can trade based on your account size and risk tolerance, and shows your potential profit, loss and risk‑reward ratio at one or more target prices. It also estimates the impact of trading fees.

Can I use it without entering my account size?

You can, but the results will be limited. Without account size the calculator cannot compute your dollar risk or portfolio impact. For best results, fill in both account size and risk percentage.

Is this financial advice?

No. This tool is for educational and planning purposes only. It does not take into account your personal financial situation and is not a recommendation to buy or sell any security.
