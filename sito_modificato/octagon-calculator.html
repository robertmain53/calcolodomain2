The formula explained

Given side \( s \):

\[ \begin{aligned} P &= 8s \\ A &= 2(1+\sqrt{2})\,s^2 \\ r &= \frac{s}{2\,\tan(\pi/8)} = \frac{s}{2}\,\cot(22.5^\circ) \\ R &= \frac{s}{2\,\sin(\pi/8)} = \frac{s}{2}\,\csc(22.5^\circ) \end{aligned} \]

Inversions (solve for \( s \)):

\[ \begin{aligned} s &= \frac{P}{8} \\ s &= \sqrt{\frac{A}{2(1+\sqrt{2})}} \\ s &= 2r\,\tan(\pi/8) \\ s &= 2R\,\sin(\pi/8) \end{aligned} \]

Glossary of variables

  • s — Side length
  • P — Perimeter (8s)
  • A — Area \(= 2(1+\sqrt{2})\,s^2\)
  • r — Apothem (inradius), center to side
  • R — Circumradius, center to vertex

How it works: a step-by-step example

Suppose the known value is the area \(A = 1{,}000\ \text{cm}^2\).

  1. Compute side: \( s = \sqrt{A / [2(1+\sqrt{2})]} \approx \) use the calculator to get \( s \).
  2. Perimeter: \( P = 8s \).
  3. Apothem: \( r = s/[2\tan(\pi/8)] \).
  4. Circumradius: \( R = s/[2\sin(\pi/8)] \).

The tool performs these steps instantly and labels results with your chosen unit.

Frequently asked questions

Does this work for non-regular octagons?

No. These identities assume all sides and interior angles are equal.

Why do you use \( \tan(\pi/8) \) and \( \sin(\pi/8) \)?

Regular n-gons relate side, apothem, and circumradius via central angles. For an octagon, the half-central angle is \( \pi/8 = 22.5^\circ \).

How do units propagate to area?

If you input centimeters, area is in \(\text{cm}^2\). The calculator preserves dimensional consistency.

Numerical stability and rounding
