How to convert ounces to pounds

Ounces (oz) and pounds (lb) are both units of weight in the US customary and imperial systems. The relationship between them is fixed:

1 pound = 16 ounces

pounds = ounces ÷ 16

ounces = pounds × 16

To convert from ounces to pounds, divide by 16. To go the other way (pounds to ounces), multiply by 16.

Examples

  • 8 oz to lb: \( 8 \div 16 = 0.5 \) lb
  • 12 oz to lb: \( 12 \div 16 = 0.75 \) lb
  • 32 oz to lb: \( 32 \div 16 = 2 \) lb
  • 2.5 lb to oz: \( 2.5 \times 16 = 40 \) oz

Working with fractions and mixed numbers

This converter accepts simple fractions and mixed numbers, so you can type values the way you see them in recipes or on packaging:

  • 1/2 → 0.5
  • 3/4 → 0.75
  • 2 1/2 → 2.5

Behind the scenes, the calculator converts the fraction to a decimal and then applies the same 16 oz = 1 lb relationship.

Ounces vs pounds: when to use which

Both units measure weight, but they are used at different scales:

  • Ounces (oz) – smaller items: ingredients, letters, small packages, food portions.
  • Pounds (lb) – larger items: body weight, luggage, big parcels, pets.

Because 1 lb = 16 oz, switching between them is straightforward with the formulas above or the interactive converter on this page.

FAQ

How do you convert ounces to pounds?

Divide the number of ounces by 16. For example, 20 oz ÷ 16 = 1.25 lb.

How many pounds is 8, 12, or 32 ounces?

  • 8 oz = 0.5 lb
  • 12 oz = 0.75 lb
  • 32 oz = 2 lb

How do you convert pounds to ounces?

Multiply the number of pounds by 16. For example, 3 lb × 16 = 48 oz.

Is this converter using US or imperial ounces and pounds?

For ounces and pounds, the US customary and imperial systems use the same definitions: 1 lb = 16 oz. So the conversion factor is the same in both systems.
