How the North Carolina income tax calculator works

North Carolina uses a flat state income tax rate. That means your taxable income is taxed at the same percentage, regardless of how high or low your income is. The calculator:

  • Applies the correct NC standard deduction for your filing status and year.
  • Subtracts any pre-tax deductions you enter (401(k), HSA, etc.).
  • Computes your NC taxable income and multiplies it by the flat rate.
  • Optionally estimates your federal income tax using current brackets and the federal standard deduction.
  • Shows your effective NC tax rate and take-home pay annually and per pay period.

Current North Carolina income tax rate and standard deduction

For recent tax years, North Carolina has:

  • A flat state income tax rate (same for all brackets).
  • A standard deduction that depends on filing status.

The calculator is preloaded with typical values for 2023 and 2024. Always confirm the latest numbers with the NC Department of Revenue.

NC taxable income formula

NC taxable income = max( 0, Gross income − Pre-tax deductions − NC standard deduction − Extra NC itemized deductions )

NC state income tax

NC tax = NC taxable income × NC flat tax rate

Step-by-step example

Suppose you are single in 2024 with:

  • Gross income: $60,000
  • Pre-tax deductions: $3,000 (401(k))
  • No additional NC itemized deductions
  1. Adjusted income after pre-tax: $60,000 − $3,000 = $57,000
  2. Subtract NC standard deduction (single): $57,000 − standard deduction
  3. Result is your NC taxable income.
  4. Multiply by the NC flat rate to get your NC state tax.

Frequently asked questions about North Carolina income tax

Who has to file a North Carolina income tax return?

In general, you must file an NC individual income tax return if you are a resident or part‑year resident and your income exceeds the state filing threshold for your filing status. Nonresidents must file if they have NC‑source income above the threshold. Exact thresholds change over time, so check the latest instructions on the NC Department of Revenue website.

Does North Carolina tax Social Security benefits?

North Carolina does not tax Social Security benefits. However, other retirement income (pensions, IRA distributions, 401(k) withdrawals) is generally taxable at the flat NC rate unless a specific exclusion applies.

Are there local income taxes in North Carolina?

No. North Carolina has a state-level income tax only. Cities and counties do not levy their own local income taxes, although they may impose sales and property taxes.

How does this calculator differ from official NC tools?

This calculator is designed for quick planning and shows both NC and federal estimates in one view. It does not handle every credit, adjustment, or special situation (such as complex itemized deductions, business income, or multi-state returns). For filing, always rely on official instructions or professional tax software.
