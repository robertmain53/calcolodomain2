The Formula Explained

We model North Carolina’s flat tax as follows (LaTeX format):

\(\mathrm{AGI} = \max\{0,\, W - P\}\)

\(D = \begin{cases} \text{SD}(\text{status}, \text{year}), & \text{if Standard}\\ \text{ID}, & \text{if Itemized} \end{cases}\)

\(\mathrm{NC\ Taxable} = \max\{0,\, \mathrm{AGI} + A - S - D\}\)

\(\mathrm{Tax} = \mathrm{NC\ Taxable} \times r_{\text{year}}\)

\(\mathrm{AfterTax} = W - \mathrm{Tax}, \quad \mathrm{EffRate} = \begin{cases}\mathrm{Tax}/W,& W>0\\ 0,& W=0\end{cases}\)

Glossary of Variables

W — Annual Gross Income (USD) entered by the user.
P — Pre‑tax payroll deductions (401(k), HSA, etc.), annual total.
A — NC Additions to income (if applicable).
S — NC Subtractions from income (if applicable).
SD(status, year) — NC Standard Deduction based on filing status and tax year.
ID — NC‑allowed Itemized Deductions total entered by the user.
ryear — Flat NC income tax rate for the selected year (0.045 in 2024; 0.0425 in 2025).
NC Taxable — Taxable income after deductions and NC adjustments.
Tax — Estimated NC state income tax owed.
AfterTax — Income after subtracting NC state tax only.
EffRate — Effective state tax rate (Tax ÷ W).

How It Works: A Step‑by‑Step Example

Scenario: Tax Year 2024, Filing Status Single, W = $85,000, P = $8,000, A = $0, S = $0, Deduction Type = Standard.

  1. AGI = max(0, 85,000 − 8,000) = 77,000.
  2. Standard Deduction SD(Single, 2024) = 12,750.
  3. NC Taxable = max(0, 77,000 + 0 − 0 − 12,750) = 64,250.
  4. Tax = 64,250 × 0.045 = $2,891.25.
  5. After‑Tax Income (state only) = 85,000 − 2,891.25 = $82,108.75.
  6. Effective State Tax Rate = 2,891.25 ÷ 85,000 ≈ 3.40%.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is North Carolina’s state income tax rate?

NC uses a flat rate: 4.50% for 2024 and 4.25% for 2025 (per NCDOR and enacted legislation).

Which standard deduction should I use?

For 2024: $12,750 (Single or MFS), $25,500 (MFJ), $19,125 (Head of Household). The calculator applies these automatically based on your filing status.

Can I itemize in North Carolina?

Yes, but NC itemized deductions differ from federal rules and are limited. If you select Itemized, input your NC‑allowed itemized total.

Does this include Social Security or federal taxes?

No. This tool estimates North Carolina state income tax only. It does not include federal income tax, FICA, or other withholdings.

Do pre‑tax deductions lower NC taxes?

Often yes. NC generally starts from federal AGI, so eligible pre‑tax payroll deductions can lower NC taxable income.

Are credits included (e.g., child credit)?

No. This simplified estimator excludes credits. Consult NCDOR resources or a tax professional for credit eligibility.

Is the 2025 standard deduction final?

Amounts shown reflect the latest published figures available at the time of writing. They may change with new legislation or guidance. Always verify with NCDOR.
