Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are strictly based on the formulas and data provided by the Health Canada PDCAAS guidelines.

The Formula Explained

\[\text{PDCAAS} = \left(\frac{\text{Amino Acid Score} \times \text{Digestibility Coefficient}}{100}\right)\]

Glossary of Terms

  • Protein Content (%): The percentage of protein in the food item.
  • Digestibility Coefficient (%): The percentage of protein digested and absorbed by the body.
  • PDCAAS: A score reflecting the quality of a protein based on human amino acid requirements and its digestibility.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Suppose you have a protein source with a content of 20% and a digestibility coefficient of 90%. The PDCAAS is calculated as follows:

\[\text{PDCAAS} = \left(\frac{1 \times 90}{100}\right) = 0.90\]

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is PDCAAS?

PDCAAS stands for Protein Digestibility Corrected Amino Acid Score. It measures the quality of a protein based on the amino acid requirements of humans and their ability to digest it.

How is PDCAAS calculated?

PDCAAS is calculated using the formula: PDCAAS = (Amino Acid Score x Digestibility Coefficient) / 100.

Why is PDCAAS important?

PDCAAS helps in assessing the protein quality of foods, which is crucial for dietary planning and nutrition labeling.

What foods have a high PDCAAS?

Foods such as eggs, milk, and soy have high PDCAAS values, indicating high protein quality.

Is a higher PDCAAS always better?

Yes, a higher PDCAAS indicates that a protein source meets human amino acid requirements better and is more digestible.
