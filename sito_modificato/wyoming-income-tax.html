The Formula Explained

State tax owed: \( T_{\text{state}} = r_{\text{state}} \times I_{\text{taxable}} \)

For Wyoming: \( r_{\text{state}} = 0 \Rightarrow T_{\text{state}} = 0 \)

Effective state tax rate: \( \text{EffRate} = \begin{cases} \dfrac{T_{\text{state}}}{I_{\text{taxable}}}\times 100\% & \text{if } I_{\text{taxable}} > 0 \\ 0\% & \text{if } I_{\text{taxable}} = 0 \end{cases} \)

Glossary of Variables

Annual income: Your gross yearly earnings before taxes and deductions.
Pre-tax deductions: Amounts like 401(k), HSA, or cafeteria plan contributions typically excluded from taxable income in many jurisdictions.
State taxable income (I_taxable): Income used by a state to compute tax. In Wyoming, the tax rate is 0%, so the output is always $0 regardless.
State tax rate (r_state): The statutory rate applied to taxable income. Wyoming: 0%.
State income tax owed (T_state): The dollar amount of state tax due. Wyoming: always $0.
Effective state tax rate: T_state divided by taxable income, expressed as a percentage. Wyoming: 0%.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Suppose your annual income is $75,000 and you contribute $5,000 to pre-tax benefits.

  1. Compute state taxable income. For explanatory purposes we use: \( I_{\text{taxable}} = \max(0,\; \text{Income} - \text{PreTax}) = \max(0,\; 75{,}000 - 5{,}000) = 70{,}000 \)
  2. Apply Wyoming’s state rate: \( r_{\text{state}} = 0 \Rightarrow T_{\text{state}} = 0 \times 70{,}000 = 0 \)
  3. Effective state rate: \( \text{EffRate} = \dfrac{0}{70{,}000} \times 100\% = 0\% \)

Result: Wyoming state income tax owed is $0. This remains true for any filing status and income amount under current law.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

No. Wyoming does not levy an individual state income tax.

Are there any local (city or county) income taxes in Wyoming?

No. There are no local income taxes in Wyoming either—state, city, and county income tax rates are all 0%.

Which tax year does this calculator support?

It reflects the ongoing policy that Wyoming levies no individual state income tax. The result ($0) is identical for 2024 and 2025 unless state law changes.

Does filing status change the result?

No. Because the state rate is 0%, filing status does not affect the Wyoming state income tax outcome.

Does Wyoming tax retirement income, capital gains, or Social Security at the state level?

No. With no individual state income tax, those are not taxed at the state level in Wyoming.

Does this calculator estimate federal taxes or other withholdings?

No. This tool is focused solely on Wyoming state income tax, which is $0. For federal taxes, use a dedicated federal calculator.
