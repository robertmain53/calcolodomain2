Understanding the dram units

The term dram (or drachm) appears in more than one historical system of units. For mass, the two most commonly referenced are:

  • Avoirdupois dram – part of the everyday imperial system used for goods, where 16 ounces make one pound.
  • Apothecaries dram – part of the historical pharmacy system, where 12 ounces make one pound.

FAQ: dram to grams conversions

Which dram should I use for non-medical technical work?

For most non-medical, non-pharmacy applications, if a “dram” appears without further context, it is usually safer to assume the avoirdupois dram. If you are dealing with historical pharmaceutical recipes or texts, the apothecaries dram is more likely.

Why is there a warning about medical use?

Modern pharmacology uses the metric system (mg, g, mL) with strictly defined dosing protocols. Historical dram-based prescriptions are not meant to be recreated directly. This converter is purely educational and technical, not a substitute for medical judgment.

Why do different websites show slightly different decimal values?

The underlying conversion factors are exact or well-established, but different tools may round at different stages or show fewer digits. This calculator keeps sufficient precision internally and rounds the display to a practical number of decimals for readability.
