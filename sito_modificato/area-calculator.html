Area and Perimeter Formulas for 2D Shapes

Area calculation depends entirely on the base geometry of the shape. The area is always measured in square units ($m^2, cm^2, in^2$), while the perimeter/circumference is measured in linear units ($m, cm, in$).

Rectangle and Square

The simplest area formula involves multiplying the two adjacent dimensions:

  • Area ($\mathcal{A}$): $\mathcal{A} = l \times w$
  • Perimeter ($P$): $P = 2l + 2w$

A square is a special type of rectangle where $l=w$, so $\mathcal{A} = s^2$ and $P = 4s$.

Triangle

The area of a triangle is half the area of a parallelogram with the same base and height. The height **must** be perpendicular to the base.

$$\mathcal{A} = \frac{1}{2} b h$$

If you know all three side lengths but not the height, you must use Heron's Formula.

Circle

For a circle, the perimeter is called the circumference ($C$). Both are calculated using the radius ($r$) and the mathematical constant $\pi$ ($\pi \approx 3.14159$).

  • Area ($\mathcal{A}$): $\mathcal{A} = \pi r^2$
  • Circumference ($C$): $C = 2\pi r$

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the formula for the Area of a Circle?

The area ($\mathcal{A}$) of a circle is calculated by multiplying pi ($\pi \approx 3.14159$) by the square of the radius ($r$): $A = \pi r^2$.

What is the area of a triangle formula?

The area ($\mathcal{A}$) of any triangle is half the product of its base ($b$) and its perpendicular height ($h$): $\mathcal{A} = \frac{1}{2} b h$.

What is the difference between area and perimeter?

Area is the measure of the two-dimensional space enclosed within the boundary of a shape (measured in square units). Perimeter (or circumference for a circle) is the measure of the distance around that boundary (measured in linear units).

What is the perimeter of a rectangle with length l and width w?

The perimeter ($P$) of a rectangle is the sum of all four sides: $P = l + w + l + w$, or simplified: $P = 2(l + w)$.
