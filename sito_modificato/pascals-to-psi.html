The Formula Explained

\( \text{PSI} = \text{pascals} \times 0.000145038 \)

Where pascals is the pressure in pascals (Pa) and PSI is the pressure in pounds per square inch. One pascal equals approximately 0.000145038 PSI.

Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the standard SI conversion: 1 pascal (Pa) = 0.000145038 PSI, or equivalently 1 PSI = 6,894.76 Pa. The pascal is the SI unit of pressure, equal to one newton per square meter.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For example, to convert 101,325 pascals (standard atmospheric pressure) to PSI:

  1. Start with the formula: \( \text{PSI} = \text{pascals} \times 0.000145038 \)
  2. Substitute 101,325 for pascals: \( \text{PSI} = 101{,}325 \times 0.000145038 \)
  3. Calculate: \( \text{PSI} = 14.696 \)

Result: 101,325 pascals = 14.696 PSI (1 atmosphere)

Glossary of Terms

  • Pascal (Pa): The SI unit of pressure, equal to one newton per square meter (N/m²). Named after French mathematician and physicist Blaise Pascal.
  • PSI (Pounds per Square Inch): A unit of pressure commonly used in the United States for measuring tire pressure, hydraulic systems, and industrial applications.
  • SI Unit: International System of Units - the modern form of the metric system used worldwide in science and engineering.
  • Pressure: The force applied perpendicular to the surface of an object per unit area.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How many pascals are in one PSI?

One PSI equals approximately 6,894.76 pascals. Conversely, 1 pascal equals approximately 0.000145038 PSI.

How do you convert pascals to PSI?

To convert pascals to PSI, multiply the pascal value by 0.000145038. For example: 100,000 Pa × 0.000145038 = 14.50 PSI.

Why is the pascal the SI unit of pressure?

The pascal is the SI unit because it's derived from the fundamental SI units: force (newton) divided by area (square meter). This makes it consistent with the metric system.

Is this conversion accurate?

Yes, this calculator uses the precise standard conversion factor of 0.000145038 and displays results to two decimal places for accuracy.

Can I convert PSI back to pascals?

Yes, to convert PSI to pascals, multiply the PSI value by 6,894.76. For example: 14.50 PSI × 6,894.76 = 100,000 Pa.
