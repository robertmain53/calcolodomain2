Methodology

The calculator applies the standard cadence formula used by cycling coaches: cadence equals speed converted to meters per minute divided by the product of wheel circumference and gear ratio. All intermediate steps keep the numbers finite, and the tool rounds every output to two decimal places (cadence) or to aligned precision for auxiliary telemetry.

Glossary

  • Wheel Size: Diameter of the bike wheel, measured in inches.
  • Gear Ratio: Front chainring teeth ÷ rear cog teeth.
  • Speed: Ground speed in kilometers per hour.
  • Cadence: Revolutions per minute of the crank.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is bike cadence?

Cadence is how many times your pedals complete a full rotation in one minute. It impacts how hard you push and your ability to maintain speed.

How can I improve my cadence?

Consistent cadence work, smooth pedaling technique, and gearing adjustments help you pedal more efficiently.

What is the ideal cadence for cycling?

Most road cyclists target 80-100 RPM, but the optimal range depends on fitness, terrain, and riding goals.

How does gear ratio affect cadence?

A higher gear ratio increases the distance per pedal turn, meaning you must pedal more slowly to maintain a given speed.

Why is it important to measure cadence?

Cadence gives insight into your pedaling efficiency, helps prevent fatigue, and informs how to pace efforts and gear selections.

Formulas

Primary cadence formula:

Cadence = (Speed × 1000) ÷ (Wheel Circumference × Gear Ratio)

Wheel Circumference = Wheel Size × π (inches) and speed is in km/h.

  • Cadence output is RPM (revolutions per minute).
  • Speed is converted to meters per minute before dividing.
  • Rounding preserves consistent two-decimal precision for cadence and related KPIs.
