Source of Data and Methodology

The Formula Explained

\( \text{BOD} = \frac{\text{Initial DO} - \text{Final DO}}{\text{Volume}} \)

Glossary of Variables

  • Initial DO: The amount of dissolved oxygen in the sample before incubation.
  • Final DO: The amount of dissolved oxygen in the sample after incubation.
  • Sample Volume: The volume of the sample used for testing.
  • BOD: The calculated biochemical oxygen demand.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Suppose you have a water sample with an initial DO of 9 mg/L and a final DO of 3 mg/L, with a sample volume of 0.5 L. The BOD is calculated as follows:

\( \text{BOD} = \frac{9 - 3}{0.5} = 12 \, \text{mg/L} \)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD)?

BOD is a measure of the quantity of oxygen used by microorganisms in the oxidation of organic matter.

Why is measuring BOD important?

It helps evaluate the effect of wastewater discharges on the oxygen levels of receiving waters.

How do I ensure accurate BOD measurements?

Follow standard methods and ensure samples are incubated under controlled conditions.

What factors can affect BOD results?

Temperature, presence of toxic substances, and sample handling can all impact BOD readings.

Can BOD be zero?

Yes, if the sample is free from organic matter, the BOD can be zero.
