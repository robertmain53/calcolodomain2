What this age calculator does

This age calculator gives you an exact, calendar-accurate age between two dates. Unlike simple “years only” tools, it:

  • Breaks age down into years, months, and days
  • Optionally includes hours, minutes, and seconds
  • Works for past and future reference dates
  • Handles leap years and February 29 birthdays correctly
  • Accepts both date pickers and typed dates in common formats

How to use the age calculator

  1. Enter your date of birth. Use the date picker or type a date like 15/07/1990 or July 15, 1990.
  2. (Optional) Enter time of birth. If you include time, the calculator can show age down to seconds.
  3. Choose the reference date. Click “Use today” or pick any other date to see your age on that day.
  4. Click “Calculate Age”. Your age will appear in multiple formats and units.

How age is calculated (chronological age)

Chronological age is the amount of time that has passed between your date of birth and a reference date. Mathematically, we compute the difference between two date–time values.

Age interval = Reference Date − Birth Date

We then decompose this interval into:

  • Years: full calendar years between the two dates
  • Months: remaining full months after subtracting years
  • Days: remaining days after subtracting years and months

Internally, the calculator:

  • Uses the browser’s calendar rules (Gregorian calendar) for leap years
  • Computes the raw difference in milliseconds for total seconds, minutes, hours, and days
  • Derives weeks as totalDays ÷ 7 and approximate months as totalDays ÷ 30.4375 (average days per month)

Handling leap years and February 29

Leap years occur in years divisible by 4, except centuries not divisible by 400. The browser’s date engine applies these rules, so:

  • People born on February 29 are handled correctly
  • Age is based on the exact number of days between dates, not a fixed 365 days per year

Chronological age vs biological age

This tool calculates chronological age only – the time since birth. In medicine and biology, you may also see:

  • Biological age – how “old” your body appears based on health markers and function
  • Developmental age – stage of physical, cognitive, or emotional development
  • Gestational age – weeks since the start of a pregnancy

Those require clinical data or specialized models and cannot be accurately computed from dates alone.

Common uses of an age calculator

  • Checking eligibility for school, voting, driving, or retirement
  • Filling out forms that require age in years and months (e.g., pediatrics)
  • Planning milestones and anniversaries
  • Research or clinical work where exact age in days or weeks is needed

FAQ

Can I calculate age on a future date?

Yes. Set the reference date to any future date. The calculator will show how old you will be on that day, including the countdown to your next birthday relative to that date.

Why does the months value sometimes look surprising?

Months have different lengths (28–31 days). The “years, months, days” breakdown uses calendar months, while the “total months (approx.)” value uses an average month length to give a smooth decimal estimate.

Does this age calculator support different time zones?

The calculation uses your device’s local time zone. For most everyday uses this is sufficient. If you need sub-day precision across time zones (e.g., for legal or astronomical purposes), you should convert both dates to the same time zone first.

Is this age calculator suitable for medical decisions?

No. This tool is for general information and planning only. It does not replace professional medical, legal, or financial advice. Always consult a qualified professional for decisions that depend on age.
