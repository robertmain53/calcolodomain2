The formulas explained

For a regular heptagon (n = 7), let side \(a\), perimeter \(P\), area \(A\), circumradius \(R\), apothem \(r\), and \(\alpha=\pi/n=\pi/7\).

\[ P = 7a \] \[ A = \frac{7}{4}\,a^2 \cot(\alpha) \;=\; \tfrac12\,P\,r \;=\; \tfrac12\,n R^2 \sin\!\bigl(2\alpha\bigr) \] \[ R = \frac{a}{2\sin(\alpha)}, \qquad r = \frac{a}{2}\cot(\alpha)=R\cos(\alpha) \] Inversions used by the calculator: \[ a = \frac{P}{7} = 2R\sin(\alpha) = 2r\tan(\alpha) = \sqrt{\frac{4A}{7\cot(\alpha)}} \] Angles: \[ \text{interior} = \frac{(n-2)\cdot180^\circ}{n}=\frac{5\cdot180^\circ}{7}\approx128.5714^\circ,\quad \text{central} = \frac{360^\circ}{n}\approx51.4286^\circ \]

Glossary of variables

  • a — side length of the regular heptagon.
  • P — perimeter (sum of all 7 sides).
  • A — area enclosed by the heptagon.
  • R — circumradius (radius of circumscribed circle through vertices).
  • r — apothem/inradius (radius of inscribed circle tangent to sides).
  • Interior angle — angle at each vertex inside the polygon.
  • Central angle — angle subtended at the center by one side.

How it works: a step-by-step example

Example. Known value: side \(a=5\) (units). With \(\alpha=\pi/7\):

  1. Perimeter: \(P=7a=35\).
  2. Circumradius: \(R=\dfrac{a}{2\sin(\pi/7)}\approx \dfrac{5}{2\sin(25.714^\circ)}\approx5.78\).
  3. Apothem: \(r=\dfrac{a}{2}\cot(\pi/7)\approx \dfrac{5}{2}\cot(25.714^\circ)\approx5.19\).
  4. Area: \(A=\dfrac{7}{4}a^2\cot(\pi/7)\approx \dfrac{7}{4}\cdot25\cdot\cot(25.714^\circ)\approx90.62\).
  5. Angles: interior \(\approx128.5714^\circ\); central \(\approx51.4286^\circ\).

FAQ

Is this for any heptagon?

No—this calculator assumes a regular heptagon (all sides and angles equal). Irregular heptagons require different inputs (e.g., coordinates) or triangulation.

Which input is most stable numerically?

Side \(a\), circumradius \(R\), and apothem \(r\) are equally well-conditioned. Area \(A\) inversion uses a square root but remains stable for positive \(A\).

What units do results use?

The calculator is unit-agnostic. Enter any unit for the known value; all derived lengths use that unit and areas use its square.

Can I get exact symbolic values?

For \(n=7\), trigonometric values aren’t expressible with simple radicals. The tool provides high-precision decimal values.

What’s the interior angle of a regular heptagon?

\(\frac{(7-2)180^\circ}{7}\approx128.5714^\circ\).

How is the area formula derived?

Partition the heptagon into 7 congruent isosceles triangles with vertex at the center, each area \(\tfrac12 R^2\sin(2\pi/7)\). Summing gives \(A=\tfrac12 n R^2\sin(2\pi/n)\). Using \(a=2R\sin(\pi/n)\) yields \(A=\frac{7}{4}a^2\cot(\pi/7)\).
