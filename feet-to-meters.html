Formula

The conversion from feet to meters uses the following formula:

\[ \text{meters} = \text{feet} \times 0.3048 \]

This conversion factor is exact and based on the international foot definition established in 1959.

Data Source and Methodology

This converter uses the internationally accepted conversion between imperial and metric units:

Step-by-Step Example

Problem: Convert 10 feet to meters.

Step 1: Write down the formula

Glossary of Terms

Foot (ft)
An imperial unit of length equal to 12 inches or exactly 0.3048 meters. Commonly used in the United States for everyday measurements.
Meter (m)
The base unit of length in the International System of Units (SI). Commonly used worldwide for measuring distances.
Conversion Factor
A number used to convert one set of units to another. For feet to meters, the conversion factor is 0.3048 (exact).

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert feet to meters?

Multiply the length value in feet by 0.3048 to convert it to meters. For example: 20 feet × 0.3048 = 6.096 meters.

Why use meters instead of feet?

Meters are the standard unit of length in the metric system, used in most countries worldwide and in all scientific applications. The metric system is based on powers of 10, making calculations easier.

Is this calculator precise?

Yes, it uses the exact conversion factor (0.3048) defined by international standards. Results are displayed to 2 decimal places for practical use.

What is the formula to convert feet to meters?

The formula is: Meters = Feet × 0.3048. This conversion factor is exact and based on the international foot definition.

Can I convert meters back to feet?

Yes! To convert meters to feet, divide the meter value by 0.3048 (or multiply by 3.28084). For example: 5 meters ÷ 0.3048 = 16.404 feet.
