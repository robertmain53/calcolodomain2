Formulas

\[ r = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2} \]

\[ \theta = \arccos\left(\frac{z}{r}\right) \]

\[ \phi = \arctan\left(\frac{y}{x}\right) \]

Where r is the radial distance from the origin, θ (theta) is the polar angle from the positive z-axis, and φ (phi) is the azimuthal angle in the xy-plane from the positive x-axis.

How It Works

Spherical coordinates represent points in 3D space using a radius and two angles. The conversion from Cartesian to spherical coordinates involves calculating the distance using the 3D Pythagorean theorem, the polar angle using the arccosine function, and the azimuthal angle using the arctangent function.

Example Calculation

To convert Cartesian coordinates (3, 4, 5) to spherical coordinates:

  1. Calculate r: r = √(3² + 4² + 5²) = √50 ≈ 7.07
  2. Calculate θ: θ = arccos(5/7.07) ≈ 45.00°
  3. Calculate φ: φ = arctan(4/3) ≈ 53.13°
  4. Result: (3, 4, 5) in Cartesian = (7.07, 45.00°, 53.13°) in spherical

Frequently Asked Questions

What are spherical coordinates?

Spherical coordinates represent a point in 3D space using three values: radial distance (r), polar angle from the z-axis (θ), and azimuthal angle in the xy-plane (φ). This system is particularly useful for problems with spherical symmetry.

When should I use spherical coordinates instead of Cartesian?

Use spherical coordinates when dealing with spherical objects, radial symmetry, or problems involving angles from a central point. Examples include planetary orbits, atomic orbitals, and gravitational fields.

What are the angles θ and φ measured in?

Our calculator displays both θ and φ in degrees. θ ranges from 0° (positive z-axis) to 180° (negative z-axis), while φ ranges from -180° to 180° measured counterclockwise from the positive x-axis.

What happens when r = 0?

When r = 0, the point is at the origin and the angles θ and φ are undefined. The calculator will handle this case and display appropriate values.

Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on standard mathematical formulas for 3D coordinate system conversions. The arctangent function (atan2) is used to correctly handle all quadrants and the arccosine function handles the polar angle calculation.
