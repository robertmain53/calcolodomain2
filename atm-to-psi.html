The Formula Explained

\( \text{PSI} = \text{atmospheres} \times 14.696 \)

Where atmospheres is the pressure in atmospheres (atm) and PSI is the pressure in pounds per square inch. One standard atmosphere equals 14.696 PSI.

Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the standard conversion factor: 1 atmosphere (atm) = 14.696 PSI. This is the universally accepted conversion based on standard atmospheric pressure at sea level (101,325 pascals).

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For example, to convert 2 atmospheres to PSI:

  1. Start with the formula: \( \text{PSI} = \text{atmospheres} \times 14.696 \)
  2. Substitute 2 for atmospheres: \( \text{PSI} = 2 \times 14.696 \)
  3. Calculate: \( \text{PSI} = 29.392 \)

Result: 2 atmospheres = 29.392 PSI

Glossary of Terms

  • Atmosphere (atm): A unit of pressure based on the average atmospheric pressure at sea level. One standard atmosphere equals 101,325 pascals or 14.696 PSI.
  • PSI (Pounds per Square Inch): A unit of pressure commonly used in the United States for measuring tire pressure, hydraulic systems, and industrial applications.
  • Standard Atmosphere: Defined as exactly 101,325 pascals, used as a reference pressure in many scientific and engineering applications.
  • Pressure: The force applied perpendicular to the surface of an object per unit area.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a standard atmosphere?

A standard atmosphere (atm) is a unit of pressure defined as exactly 101,325 pascals. It represents the average atmospheric pressure at sea level at 15°C (59°F).

Why use PSI?

PSI (pounds per square inch) is widely used in the United States for measuring tire pressure, hydraulic systems, pneumatic tools, and industrial applications. It's a familiar unit for many practical applications.

How accurate is this tool?

This calculator uses the precise standard conversion factor of 14.696 PSI per atmosphere and displays results to two decimal places for accuracy.

Can I convert PSI to atmospheres?

Yes, to convert PSI to atmospheres, divide the PSI value by 14.696. For example: 29.392 PSI ÷ 14.696 = 2 atm.

What's the difference between PSI and PSIA?

PSI typically refers to gauge pressure (relative to atmospheric pressure), while PSIA (pounds per square inch absolute) is measured relative to a perfect vacuum. This calculator converts to absolute PSI.
