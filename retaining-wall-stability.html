Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are strictly based on the principles outlined in the ASCE 7-22 standards. ASCE Official Site. All calculations are based on this source.

The Formula Explained

Stability = Weight × Height Soil + Pressure

Glossary of Variables

  • Wall Height (m): The vertical height of the retaining wall.
  • Soil Type: The type of soil supporting the wall, affecting stability.
  • Stability: A calculated value indicating the wall's ability to remain upright.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Consider a retaining wall with a height of 5 meters on sandy soil. By inputting these parameters, the calculator will provide a stability score using the formula above.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a retaining wall?

A retaining wall is a structure designed to hold or retain soil behind it.

Why is stability important for retaining walls?

Stability is crucial to prevent failure, which can lead to property damage and safety hazards.
