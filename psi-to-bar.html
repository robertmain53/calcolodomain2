The Formula Explained

\( \text{bar} = \text{PSI} \times 0.0689476 \)

Where PSI is the pressure in pounds per square inch and bar is the pressure in bar. One PSI equals approximately 0.0689476 bar.

Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the standard conversion factor: 1 PSI = 0.0689476 bar, or equivalently 1 bar = 14.5038 PSI. The bar is a metric unit of pressure equal to 100,000 pascals.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For example, to convert 100 PSI to bar:

  1. Start with the formula: \( \text{bar} = \text{PSI} \times 0.0689476 \)
  2. Substitute 100 for PSI: \( \text{bar} = 100 \times 0.0689476 \)
  3. Calculate: \( \text{bar} = 6.89476 \)

Result: 100 PSI = 6.89 bar

Glossary of Terms

  • PSI (Pounds per Square Inch): A unit of pressure commonly used in the United States for measuring tire pressure, hydraulic systems, and industrial applications.
  • Bar: A metric unit of pressure equal to 100,000 pascals. Approximately equal to atmospheric pressure at sea level. Widely used in Europe and in scientific applications.
  • Pascal (Pa): The SI unit of pressure, equal to one newton per square meter. 1 bar = 100,000 Pa.
  • Pressure: The force applied perpendicular to the surface of an object per unit area.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How many bar are in one PSI?

One PSI equals approximately 0.0689476 bar. Conversely, 1 bar equals approximately 14.5038 PSI.

How do you convert PSI to bar?

To convert PSI to bar, multiply the PSI value by 0.0689476. For example: 100 PSI × 0.0689476 = 6.89 bar.

Why is bar used instead of PSI?

Bar is a metric unit commonly used in Europe and scientific applications because it's based on the metric system and is close to atmospheric pressure (1 bar ≈ 1 atm).

Is this conversion accurate?

Yes, this calculator uses the precise standard conversion factor of 0.0689476 and displays results to two decimal places for accuracy.

Can I convert bar back to PSI?

Yes, to convert bar to PSI, multiply the bar value by 14.5038. For example: 6.89 bar × 14.5038 = 100 PSI.
