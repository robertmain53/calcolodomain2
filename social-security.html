The Formulas Explained

$$\textbf{AIME (approx.)} = \frac{\text{Average Annual Earnings}}{12}$$ $$\textbf{PIA} = 0.90 \cdot \min(AIME,b_1) + 0.32 \cdot \min(\max(AIME-b_1,0),\, b_2-b_1) + 0.15 \cdot \max(AIME-b_2,0)$$ $$\text{Early Reduction} = \left[\min(m,36)\cdot\frac{5}{9}\% \right] + \left[\max(m-36,0)\cdot\frac{5}{12}\%\right]$$ $$\text{Delayed Credit} = M \cdot \frac{2}{3}\% \quad (\text{per month after FRA, up to age 70})$$ $$\text{Benefit at Claim Age} = \begin{cases} \text{PIA}\cdot(1-\text{Early Reduction}) & \text{if claim age }<\text{FRA}\\ \text{PIA} & \text{if claim age }=\text{FRA}\\ \text{PIA}\cdot(1+\text{Delayed Credit}) & \text{if claim age }>\text{FRA} \end{cases}$$ $$\text{COLA Projection (optional)}: \quad \text{PIA}_{\text{claim}} = \text{PIA}_{y_0}\cdot(1+c)^{(y_{\text{claim}}-y_0)}$$ where $b_1,b_2$ are SSA bend points for the selected year; $m$ is months before FRA; $M$ is months after FRA; $c$ is annual COLA.

Glossary of Variables

  • Date of birth: Used to compute your Full Retirement Age (FRA).
  • Average lifetime earnings: Your estimated average annual earnings across your working years in today’s dollars.
  • AIME: Average Indexed Monthly Earnings. Approximated here as annual earnings divided by 12.
  • PIA: Primary Insurance Amount. Computed from AIME using SSA bend points (b1, b2) and percentages (90%, 32%, 15%).
  • Claiming age: The age when you plan to start benefits (62–70). Benefits are reduced before FRA and increased after FRA.
  • COLA: Cost-of-Living Adjustment applied annually by SSA; optional projection in this tool.
  • Lifetime benefits: Sum of monthly benefits from claim age through your specified life expectancy (nominal, without discounting).

Worked Example

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Assume: Date of birth = 1962-06-15; Average lifetime earnings = $60,000; Bend points year = 2024; Claiming age = 65y 0m; COLA = 0%.

  1. Compute AIME (approx.): AIME = 60,000 / 12 = $5,000.
  2. Get 2024 bend points: b1 = $1,174; b2 = $7,078.
  3. PIA: 0.90×1,174 + 0.32×(5,000 − 1,174) + 0.15×max(5,000 − 7,078, 0) = $1,056.60 + $1,224.32 + $0 = $2,280.92 → rounded down to the next dime ≈ $2,280.90.
  4. FRA for 1962 DOB: 66 years and 10 months. Claiming at 65 is 22 months early.
  5. Early reduction: 22 months all within first 36 months: 22 × (5/9)% ≈ 12.22%. Benefit at claim age ≈ 2,280.90 × (1 − 0.1222) ≈ $2,004/month (rounded down).

This aligns with the formulas above and shows how claiming earlier than FRA reduces the monthly benefit.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is this calculator official?

No. It follows SSA rules and bend points but is for education and planning. Your official estimate is available at SSA my Social Security.

Why do I need to pick a bend-points year?

Bend points change annually. Selecting a known year ensures your PIA is computed from official thresholds for that year.

Why does my estimate differ from SSA’s?

This tool approximates AIME from average annual earnings. SSA uses your top 35 indexed years and other records, which can produce different results.

Can I claim earlier than 62 or later than 70?

Retirement benefits cannot start before 62. Delayed credits stop at age 70.

Does this include Medicare or taxes?

No. Medicare premiums and federal/state taxes are not included in the monthly benefit shown here.

What COLA should I use?

COLA is unknown in advance. Historically it varies by year. Use 0–3% for planning, or leave it at 0% if unsure.

Is the lifetime benefit discounted for inflation?

No. It is a nominal total for illustration. For present value analysis, apply your own discount rate outside this tool.
