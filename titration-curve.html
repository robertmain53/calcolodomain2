Glossary of Terms

  • Titrant: The solution added to the analyte.
  • Analyte: The solution being analyzed.
  • Molarity: Concentration of a solution expressed in moles per liter.
  • Equivalence Point: The point at which the titration is complete.
  • pH: Measure of the acidity or basicity of a solution.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a titration curve?

A titration curve is a graph that shows the pH of a solution as a titrant is added.

How do I use the calculator?

Enter the concentration and volume of the titrant and analyte, then click calculate.

Why is pH important in titration?

pH indicates the acidity/basicity of the solution, essential for determining the endpoint of a titration.

What happens at the equivalence point?

The moles of titrant equal the moles of analyte, indicating the reaction is complete.

Can this calculator handle polyprotic acids?

This version is optimized for monoprotic acids and bases.

Is this tool suitable for educational purposes?

Yes, it provides an interactive way to learn about titration curves and their applications.
