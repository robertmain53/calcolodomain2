The Formula Explained

Electron configuration is determined by the distribution of electrons among the orbitals of an atom. The principle is based on the Aufbau principle, Hund's rule, and the Pauli exclusion principle.

Glossary of Terms

  • Element: A substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances by chemical means.
  • Electron Configuration: The distribution of electrons of an atom or molecule in atomic or molecular orbitals.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Consider the element Carbon (C), which has an atomic number of 6. The electron configuration is determined by filling the orbitals in the order of increasing energy levels: 1s² 2s² 2p².

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is electron configuration?

Electron configuration is the distribution of electrons in an atom's orbitals. It is used to describe the electronic structure of atoms.

How do I find an element's atomic number?

The atomic number is located above the element symbol on the periodic table.

Why is electron configuration important?

Understanding electron configurations helps predict the chemical properties and reactivity of elements.
