Formula

\[ \text{Calories} = \text{Joules} \times 0.239006 \]

Where 1 joule equals approximately 0.239006 calories (thermochemical calorie).

How It Works

The joule is the SI unit of energy, while the calorie is a traditional unit of heat energy. The conversion factor is based on the definition that 1 calorie is the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of 1 gram of water by 1°C.

Example Calculation

To convert 1000 joules to calories:

  1. Multiply 1000 J by the conversion factor 0.239006
  2. 1000 × 0.239006 = 239.006 cal
  3. Result: 1000 J = 239.006 cal

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between joules and calories?

Joules are the SI unit of energy used internationally in science, while calories are a traditional unit of heat energy. 1 calorie equals approximately 4.184 joules.

Are nutritional Calories the same as calories?

No. Nutritional Calories (with capital C) are actually kilocalories (kcal), which equal 1000 calories. So 1 Calorie = 1000 calories = 4184 joules.

Why are there different types of calories?

There are thermochemical calories (4.184 J), International Table calories (4.1868 J), and food calories (kilocalories). Our calculator uses the thermochemical calorie, which is the most common in scientific contexts.

How accurate is this conversion?

The conversion factor 0.239006 is accurate to 6 significant figures, which is sufficient for most practical calculations in science and engineering.

Data Source and Methodology

The conversion factor is based on the internationally accepted definition of the thermochemical calorie: 1 cal = 4.184 J exactly, therefore 1 J = 0.239006 cal.
