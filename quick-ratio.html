The Formula Explained

Quick Ratio: \( \frac{\text{Current Assets} - \text{Inventory}}{\text{Current Liabilities}} \)

Glossary of Terms

  • Current Assets: All assets that are expected to be converted into cash within a year.
  • Inventory: The raw materials, work-in-process products, and finished goods considered part of a business's assets.
  • Current Liabilities: A company's debts or obligations that are due within one year.
  • Quick Ratio: A measure of a company's ability to meet its short-term obligations with its most liquid assets.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the Quick Ratio?

The Quick Ratio measures a company's ability to meet its short-term obligations using its most liquid assets, excluding inventory.

Why exclude inventory in the Quick Ratio?

Inventory is excluded because it may not be as easily convertible to cash as other current assets, making it a less reliable measure of liquidity.

How is Quick Ratio different from Current Ratio?

The Current Ratio includes inventory in current assets, while the Quick Ratio excludes it, providing a more stringent test of liquidity.
