Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are strictly based on the ISO 8601 standard for week numbers. For more information, visit the ISO 8601 documentation.

The Formula Explained

The week number for a given date is determined based on the ISO 8601 standard, which states that the first week of the year is the week with the first Thursday in it.

Glossary of Terms

  • Date: The specific day for which the week number is calculated.
  • Week Number: A sequential number indicating the position of a week within a year, based on ISO 8601.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the ISO 8601 standard?

ISO 8601 is an international standard covering the exchange of date and time-related data.

Why is the first week of the year not always starting on January 1?

The ISO 8601 standard considers the week with the first Thursday as the first week of the year.

How can I use this calculator for project planning?

You can determine the week number of any date to better align your scheduling and planning efforts.

Does this calculator consider leap years?

Yes, the calculator handles leap years by adhering to the ISO 8601 standards.
