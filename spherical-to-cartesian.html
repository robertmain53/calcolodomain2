Formulas

\[ x = r \cdot \sin(\theta) \cdot \cos(\phi) \]

\[ y = r \cdot \sin(\theta) \cdot \sin(\phi) \]

\[ z = r \cdot \cos(\theta) \]

Where r is the radial distance from the origin, θ (theta) is the polar angle from the positive z-axis, and φ (phi) is the azimuthal angle in the xy-plane from the positive x-axis.

How It Works

Spherical coordinates represent points in 3D space using a radius and two angles. The conversion to Cartesian coordinates involves using trigonometric functions to project the radius onto the three Cartesian axes, with θ determining the vertical component and φ determining the horizontal direction.

Example Calculation

To convert spherical coordinates (r=10, θ=45°, φ=30°) to Cartesian coordinates:

  1. Calculate x: x = 10 × sin(45°) × cos(30°) ≈ 6.12
  2. Calculate y: y = 10 × sin(45°) × sin(30°) ≈ 3.54
  3. Calculate z: z = 10 × cos(45°) ≈ 7.07
  4. Result: (10, 45°, 30°) in spherical = (6.12, 3.54, 7.07) in Cartesian

Frequently Asked Questions

What are spherical coordinates?

Spherical coordinates represent a point in 3D space using three values: radial distance (r), polar angle from the z-axis (θ), and azimuthal angle in the xy-plane (φ). This system is particularly useful for problems with spherical symmetry.

When should I use spherical coordinates?

Use spherical coordinates when dealing with spherical objects, radial symmetry, or problems involving angles from a central point. Examples include planetary orbits, atomic orbitals, and gravitational fields.

What are the angles θ and φ measured in?

Our calculator accepts both θ and φ in degrees. θ ranges from 0° (positive z-axis) to 180° (negative z-axis), while φ ranges from 0° to 360° measured counterclockwise from the positive x-axis.

What is the difference between θ and φ?

θ (theta) is the polar angle measured down from the positive z-axis, determining how high or low the point is. φ (phi) is the azimuthal angle in the xy-plane, determining the rotational direction around the z-axis.

Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on standard mathematical formulas for 3D coordinate system conversions. The trigonometric functions (sine and cosine) are used to project the spherical coordinates onto the Cartesian axes.
