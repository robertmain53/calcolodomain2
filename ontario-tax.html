The Formula Explained

Tax Calculation: Provincial Tax = Income × Tax Rate

Glossary of Variables

  • Income: Your total annual earnings before any deductions.
  • Tax Rate: The percentage at which your income is taxed, based on provincial brackets.
  • Provincial Tax: The amount of tax you owe to the provincial government.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For example, if your annual income is $50,000 and the tax rate is 10%, the provincial tax will be calculated as follows: Provincial Tax = 50,000 × 0.10 = $5,000.
