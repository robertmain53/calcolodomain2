Braille Translator

Convert English text into Grade 1 Unified English Braille (UEB) and decode Braille cells back to text. Perfect for educators, accessibility teams, and learners who need fast two-way references.

Text → Braille

Letters A–Z, digits, basic punctuation, and spaces are supported. Numbers automatically receive the numeric indicator ⠼.

Braille → Text

Use Unicode Braille characters (⠁–⣿). Unsupported cells return “?” so you can review the mapping.

Data Source & Methodology

This tool follows Unified English Braille (UEB) Grade 1 mappings: each letter, digit, and symbol is represented by a single six-dot cell. Grade 2 contractions, formatting indicators, and Nemeth math are out of scope. Reference: Braille Authority of North America (BANA) — UEB Rulebook, 2013.

  • BANA: Unified English Braille resources
  • UK Association for Accessible Formats (UKAAF)

Translation Rules

Grade 1 uses direct substitution:

  • Letters A–Z → ⠁ through ⠵
  • Numeric indicator ⠼ preceding letters A–J for digits 1–0
  • Punctuation: comma ⠂, period ⠲, question ⠦, exclamation ⠖, hyphen ⠤, apostrophe ⠄, quotation marks ⠦⠴
  • Space → blank cell (no dots)

Worked Example

Input: Number 123

  1. N → ⠝
  2. u → ⠥, m → ⠍, b → ⠃, e → ⠑, r → ⠗
  3. Space → (blank)
  4. Digits 123 → numeric indicator ⠼, then ⠁ (1), ⠃ (2), ⠉ (3)

Result: ⠝⠥⠍⠃⠑⠗⠀⠼⠁⠃⠉

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this support contractions?

No. Grade 1 is uncontracted Braille. Grade 2 needs linguistic rules beyond straightforward substitution.

How are capital letters handled?

We follow UEB: capital letters use the capitalization indicator ⠠. Entire words receive double caps ⠠⠠.

Can I copy the Braille into embossing software?

Yes. Unicode Braille cells paste cleanly into most embossers and screen readers that understand Grade 1.
