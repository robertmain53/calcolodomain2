The Formula Explained

\( \text{pascals} = \text{PSI} \times 6{,}894.76 \)

Where PSI is the pressure in pounds per square inch and pascals is the pressure in pascals (Pa). One PSI equals approximately 6,894.76 pascals.

Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the standard SI conversion: 1 PSI = 6,894.76 pascals (Pa). The pascal is the SI unit of pressure, equal to one newton per square meter.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For example, to convert 14.7 PSI (approximately 1 atmosphere) to pascals:

  1. Start with the formula: \( \text{pascals} = \text{PSI} \times 6{,}894.76 \)
  2. Substitute 14.7 for PSI: \( \text{pascals} = 14.7 \times 6{,}894.76 \)
  3. Calculate: \( \text{pascals} = 101{,}352.97 \)

Result: 14.7 PSI ≈ 101,353 pascals (1 atmosphere)

Glossary of Terms

  • PSI (Pounds per Square Inch): A unit of pressure commonly used in the United States for measuring tire pressure, hydraulic systems, and industrial applications.
  • Pascal (Pa): The SI unit of pressure, equal to one newton per square meter (N/m²). Named after French mathematician and physicist Blaise Pascal.
  • SI Unit: International System of Units - the modern form of the metric system used worldwide in science and engineering.
  • Pressure: The force applied perpendicular to the surface of an object per unit area.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How many pascals are in one PSI?

One PSI equals approximately 6,894.76 pascals. This is the standard conversion factor used in scientific and engineering calculations.

How do you convert PSI to pascals?

To convert PSI to pascals, multiply the PSI value by 6,894.76. For example: 14.7 PSI × 6,894.76 = 101,353 Pa.

Why is the pascal the SI unit of pressure?

The pascal is the SI unit because it's derived from fundamental SI units: force (newton) divided by area (square meter). This makes it consistent with the metric system.

Is this conversion accurate?

Yes, this calculator uses the precise standard conversion factor of 6,894.76 and displays results to two decimal places for accuracy.

Can I convert pascals back to PSI?

Yes, to convert pascals to PSI, divide the pascal value by 6,894.76 (or multiply by 0.000145038). For example: 101,353 Pa ÷ 6,894.76 ≈ 14.7 PSI.
