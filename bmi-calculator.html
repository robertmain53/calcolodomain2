How BMI is Calculated

Metric: \( \text{BMI} = \dfrac{\text{weight (kg)}}{\text{height (m)}^2} \)

US/Imperial: \( \text{BMI} = \dfrac{703 \times \text{weight (lb)}}{\text{height (in)}^2} \)

BMI Categories

  • Underweight < 18.5
  • Normal 18.5–24.9
  • Overweight 25.0–29.9
  • Obesity ≥ 30.0

FAQ

Is BMI a diagnostic of body fatness?

BMI is a screening measure. It does not directly measure body fatness but correlates with more direct measures.

What’s a healthy BMI range?

For most adults, 18.5–24.9 is considered a healthy range, but individual factors matter.

Does unit choice change my BMI?

No. BMI is unit-independent when formulas are used correctly (703 factor for imperial).
