Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the Kelvin scale?

The Kelvin scale is an absolute temperature scale starting from absolute zero, the point where particles have minimal thermal motion. It's one of the seven SI base units and is widely used in scientific contexts.

Why use Kelvin instead of Celsius or Fahrenheit?

Kelvin is used in scientific contexts because it provides an absolute measure of temperature, eliminating negative values found in Celsius or Fahrenheit scales. This makes thermodynamic calculations simpler and more intuitive.

What is absolute zero?

Absolute zero is the lowest possible temperature (0 K, -273.15째C, or -459.67째F) where nothing could be colder and no heat energy remains in a substance. At this point, all molecular motion theoretically ceases.

Is Kelvin more accurate than Celsius?

Both Kelvin and Celsius are equally accurate. Kelvin is preferred in scientific contexts due to its absolute nature (no negative values), which simplifies many thermodynamic equations and calculations.
