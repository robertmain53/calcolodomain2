The Formula Explained

The formula to convert Fahrenheit (F) to Kelvin (K) is:
K = (F - 32) × 5/9 + 273.15

Glossary of Terms

  • Fahrenheit (°F): A temperature scale used primarily in the United States.
  • Kelvin (K): The SI base unit of thermodynamic temperature used in scientific contexts.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the Kelvin scale?

The Kelvin scale is an absolute temperature scale starting from absolute zero, the point where particles have minimal thermal motion.

Why use Kelvin instead of Celsius or Fahrenheit?

Kelvin is used in scientific contexts because it provides an absolute measure of temperature, eliminating negative values found in Celsius or Fahrenheit scales.

What is absolute zero?

Absolute zero is the lowest possible temperature where nothing could be colder and no heat energy remains in a substance.

How precise is this converter?

Is Kelvin more accurate than Celsius?

Both Kelvin and Celsius are accurate. Kelvin is preferred in scientific contexts due to its absolute nature.
