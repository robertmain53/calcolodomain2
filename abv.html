Data Source & Methodology

Authoritative Data Source: American Society of Brewing Chemists (ASBC), Methods of Analysis. Wort-12: Extract—Specific Gravity and Plato; Beer-4/Beer-6: Alcohol in Beer. Official resource: asbcnet.org — Methods of Analysis. “Tutti i calcoli si basano rigorosamente sulle formule e sui dati forniti da questa fonte.”

ABV is legally defined as volume percent of ethanol at 20 °C. For compliance/labeling, use accredited laboratory methods (e.g., distillation, oscillating U-tube density). The calculator implements widely adopted brewing relationships for practical estimation.

The Formula Explained

LaTeX-like notation is shown; variables are defined below.
ABV_{\%} = (OG - FG)\times 131.25 ABV_{\%} = \frac{76.08 \times (OG - FG)}{1.775 - OG} \times \frac{FG}{0.794} P = -616.868 + 1111.14\,SG - 630.272\,SG^{2} + 135.997\,SG^{3} RE = 0.1808 \times P_{OG} + 0.8192 \times P_{FG} AA_{\%} = \frac{P_{OG} - P_{FG}}{P_{OG}} \times 100 ABW_{\%} \approx ABV_{\%} \times \frac{FG}{0.794} SG_{corr} = SG_{obs} \times \frac{CF(T_{sample})}{CF(T_{cal})} CF(T) = 1.00130346 - 1.34722124\cdot10^{-4}T + 2.04052596\cdot10^{-6}T^{2} - 2.32820948\cdot10^{-9}T^{3} V_2 = V_1 \times \frac{ABV_1}{ABV_2}\quad\Rightarrow\quad \text{Water to add} = V_2 - V_1

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

  1. Inputs: OG = 1.060, FG = 1.012, sample 20 °C, calibration 20 °C (no temp bias).
  2. Choose formula: Advanced brewing equation.
  3. Compute ABV: \( 76.08(1.060-1.012)/(1.775-1.060) \times (1.012/0.794) \approx 6.3\% \).
  4. ABW: \( \approx 6.3\% \times (1.012/0.794) \approx 5.0\% \).
  5. Attenuation & °P: Using Plato conversion and Balling relationships.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Which ABV method should I choose?

The Advanced equation is preferred for accuracy, especially at higher OG. The Standard factor is simple and close for moderate gravities.

Do I need hydrometer temperature correction?

Yes, when sample temperature differs from hydrometer calibration (often 20 °C/60 °F). Enable correction and enter both temperatures.

Can FG be higher than OG?

No. If FG ≥ OG, re-check readings and temperature compensation; fermentation may not have started or measurements may be off.

Does it handle refractometers?

Yes. Use the Refractometer tab with Original and Final Brix and a wort correction factor (typ. 1.02–1.06).

How can I dilute to a target ABV?

Use the Dilution tab: V2 = V1 × (ABV1/ABV2); water to add = V2 − V1, in your chosen unit.
