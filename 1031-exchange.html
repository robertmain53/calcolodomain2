How the 1031 exchange math works

For a high-level estimate, we model realized gain and split it into deferred vs. recognized (taxable) portions.

  • Adjusted Basis = Purchase Price + Improvements
  • Net Sales Proceeds = Current Value − Selling Costs − Mortgage Payoff
  • Realized Gain = Current Value − Selling Costs − Adjusted Basis
  • Recognized (Taxable) Boot = max(0, Cash Received + Debt Relief − New Debt − New Cash Invested)
  • Deferred Gain = Realized Gain − Recognized Boot
  • Estimated Tax Deferred = Deferred Gain × Tax Rate

Example

Purchase $200,000 + improvements $50,000 → adjusted basis $250,000. Sell at $400,000 with $24,000 selling costs and $150,000 mortgage payoff. With a 20% aggregate rate: 

Adjusted Basis = 200,000 + 50,000 = 250,000
Realized Gain  = 400,000 − 24,000 − 250,000 = 126,000
Recognized Boot (assume 0) = 0
Deferred Gain  = 126,000
Estimated Tax Deferred = 126,000 × 0.20 = 25,200

Timing rules (overview)

  • Identify replacement property within 45 days of the sale.
  • Acquire the replacement property within 180 days of the sale.
  • Use a Qualified Intermediary; proceeds cannot be received directly by the taxpayer.

FAQs

What is a 1031 exchange?

A like-kind exchange lets you defer capital gains on real property used for investment or business by reinvesting in qualifying real property.

Who qualifies?

Taxpayers holding real property for investment or business use. Personal-use property doesn’t qualify.

Are there strict deadlines?

Yes—45 days to identify and 180 days to close, measured from the sale date of the relinquished property.
