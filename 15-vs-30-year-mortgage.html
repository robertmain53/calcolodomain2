Formulas used

  • Monthly rate \(i = r/12\)
  • Payment (principal & interest) \(PMT = P \cdot \dfrac{i}{1-(1+i)^{-n}}\)
  • Total interest = \(PMT \cdot n - P\)
  • PITI = \(PMT + escrow\)

What to consider

  • 15-year loans carry lower rates but higher payments; you save significant interest.
  • 30-year loans free monthly cash flow but accumulate more interest across time.
  • Extra principal payments reduce term and interest on both options.

FAQ

Is the 15-year always better?

Financially, interest is lower, but affordability and cash flow needs can make the 30-year preferable for some borrowers.

What about refinancing later?

If rates drop or income rises, refinancing a 30-year into a 15-year can capture future savings.
