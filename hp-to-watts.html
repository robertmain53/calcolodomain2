Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the conversion factor that 1 horsepower equals 745.7 watts. For more detailed information, refer to authoritative resources such as the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST).

The Formula Explained

The conversion formula used in this calculator is:

Watts = Horsepower × 745.7

Glossary of Terms

  • Horsepower (HP): A unit of power that indicates the rate at which work is done.
  • Watts (W): The SI unit of power, equivalent to one joule per second.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

To convert 5 HP to watts, multiply 5 by 745.7. The result is 3728.5 watts.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is horsepower?

Horsepower is a unit of power measurement that was originally developed to compare the output of steam engines with the power of draft horses.

Why convert horsepower to watts?

Watts is the standard unit of power in the International System of Units (SI), and converting to watts is necessary for scientific calculations and engineering specifications.

Is this conversion accurate?

Yes, this calculator uses the precise conversion factor of 1 HP = 745.7 W as recognized by international standards.

Can I convert watts to horsepower?

Yes, the reverse conversion is possible by dividing the number of watts by 745.7.

Where can I learn more about power units?

Refer to scientific literature or resources such as the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) for more information on power units.
