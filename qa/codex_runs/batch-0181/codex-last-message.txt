            <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-gray-800">How to Use This Calculator</h2>
            <p>Enter your bust, waist, and hip circumferences. Hips should be measured at the fullest point of the seat, waist at the natural indentation, and bust at its most prominent location. High-hip and shoulders are optional but help distinguish similar categories.</p>
            <div>
                <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-gray-700">Measurement guidelines</h3>
                <ul>
                    <li>Use a flexible tape measure and stand straight without sucking in the stomach.</li>
                    <li>Record each value to the nearest millimeter/decimal to keep precision consistent.</li>
                    <li>For imperial units, convert your tape readings into the calculator using the toggle above.</li>
                </ul>
            </div>

            <div>
                <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-gray-700">Methodology</h3>
                <p>The calculator derives ratios inspired by the Female Figure Identification Technique (FFIT). Ratios are dimensionless and compare each measurement against the hips so you can see how bust, waist, shoulders, and optional high-hip values align with apparel research.</p>
                <p>Pay attention to the waist-to-mean(B,H) ratio and the difference between bust and hips: together they indicate whether the shape is balanced, top-heavy, pear-shaped, or somewhere in between.</p>
            </div>

            <div>
                <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-gray-700">Step-by-step example</h3>
                <ol>
                    <li>Inputs: Metric units, Bust 94 cm, Waist 70 cm, Hips 96 cm, High-hip 90 cm, Shoulders 104 cm.</li>
                    <li>Ratios: WHR = 70/96 = 0.729; B/H = 94/96 = 0.979; W to Mean(B,H) = 70/((94+96)/2) = 0.737; S/H = 104/96 = 1.083.</li>
                    <li>Neither shoulders nor high-hip destabilize the basic hourglass rule, so the shape resolves to Hourglass.</li>
                </ol>
            </div>

                <div class="faq-item">
                    <h3>Is this calculator accurate?</h3>

                    <p>Let B = bust, W = waist, H = full hip, HH = high-hip, S = shoulders (all in the same unit). Ratios:</p>
