      <section class="card content-card space-y-6 mt-10" id="howToUse" aria-label="How to use and methodology">
        <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold">How to Use This Calculator</h2>
        <p>This professional-grade tool estimates your body fat percentage using two validated approaches: the U.S. Navy circumference method (measurement-based, recommended) and the Deurenberg BMI-based estimate (useful when tape measurements are unavailable). It’s designed for accuracy, accessibility, and mobile use.</p>

        <div>
          <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold">Data Source &amp; Methodology</h3>
          <p class="text-sm text-gray-600">Authoritative Data Sources:</p>
          <ul class="list-disc list-inside text-gray-700">
            <li>Hodgdon JA, Beckett MB. <em>Prediction of Percent Body Fat for U.S. Navy Men and Women from Body Circumferences and Height.</em> NHRC Report 84-11, Naval Health Research Center, San Diego, 1984. <a href="https://apps.dtic.mil/sti/pdfs/ADA144168.pdf" class="text-blue-600 hover:underline" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Direct PDF</a>.</li>
            <li>Deurenberg P, Weststrate JA, Seidell JC. <em>Body mass index as a measure of body fatness in adults.</em> Br J Nutr. 1991;65(2):105–114. <a href="https://doi.org/10.1079/BJN19910073" class="text-blue-600 hover:underline" target="_blank" rel="noopener">DOI:10.1079/BJN19910073</a>.</li>
          </ul>
          <p class="text-sm text-gray-700"><strong>All calculations are strictly based on the formulas and data provided by these sources.</strong></p>
        </div>

        <div>
          <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold">Measurement Guidance</h3>
          <ul class="list-disc list-inside text-gray-700 space-y-2">
            <li><strong>Height:</strong> Stand tall without shoes. For US units, enter height in inches (e.g., 71). Use the tip of a ruler or wall-mounted tape for accuracy.</li>
            <li><strong>Neck:</strong> Measure just below the larynx (Adam’s apple) with the tape slightly angled down across the front. Keep the tape snug but not compressing the skin.</li>
            <li><strong>Waist:</strong> Measure horizontally at the level of the navel, with the abdomen relaxed and at the end of a normal exhale.</li>
            <li><strong>Hips (women):</strong> Wrap the tape around the fullest part of the buttocks with the tape level and snug.</li>
            <li><strong>Age:</strong> Required only for the BMI-based Deurenberg estimate; it is not used in the Navy method.</li>
            <li><strong>Weight:</strong> Optional but necessary to calculate fat mass, lean mass, and a BMI estimate.</li>
          </ul>
        </div>

        <div>
          <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold">Methodology</h3>
          <p class="text-gray-700">The Navy equations compute body fat percentage from circumference data, converting all measurements to inches before applying the formulas below. When only height, weight, and age are available, the Deurenberg BMI-based estimate provides a reasonable alternative (especially for quick tracking), though it may be biased for muscular builds.</p>
        </div>

        <div>
          <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold">How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example</h3>
          <p><strong>Scenario:</strong> Male, Metric units, Height 180 cm, Neck 38 cm, Waist 85 cm, Weight 80 kg.</p>
          <ol class="list-decimal list-inside space-y-2 text-gray-700">
            <li>Convert to inches for the Navy formula: height 180 ÷ 2.54 ≈ 70.87 in; neck 38 ÷ 2.54 ≈ 14.96 in; waist 85 ÷ 2.54 ≈ 33.46 in.</li>
            <li>Compute logarithms: log₁₀(33.46 - 14.96) ≈ 1.267; log₁₀(70.87) ≈ 1.851.</li>
            <li>Apply the formula: %BF = 86.010 × 1.267 - 70.041 × 1.851 + 36.76 ≈ 15.3%.</li>
            <li>Fat mass: 0.153 × 80 kg ≈ 12.2 kg; Lean mass: 80 - 12.2 ≈ 67.8 kg.</li>
          </ol>
        </div>
