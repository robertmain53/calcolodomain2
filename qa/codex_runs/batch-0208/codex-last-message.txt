        <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold">How to Use this Calculator</h2>
        <p>Enter your height and weight, choose a unit system, and select the clinical formula you prefer. The calculator normalizes inputs internally to metric units and updates instantly whenever you hit Calculate or change an input.</p>
        <p>The formula comparison ensures you can review the Mosteller result alongside Du Bois &amp; Du Bois, Haycock, Gehan &amp; George, and Boyd values so you can pick the approach used in your setting.</p>
        <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold mt-6">Methodology</h2>
        <p>The calculator converts imperial inputs to centimeters and kilograms, then applies the chosen α–β exponent formula. Rounding is applied only for display; internal calculations keep full double-precision accuracy.</p>
        <ul class="list mt-4">
          <li>Mosteller is fast and widely used for chemotherapy dosing and clinical trials.</li>
          <li>Du Bois &amp; Du Bois and Haycock offer slightly different exponent weights for height and weight.</li>
          <li>Gehan &amp; George as well as Boyd provide alternative exponent combinations derived from large datasets.</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="text-sm text-gray-600 mt-3">The shared inputs update the URL automatically, letting you bookmark or share the exact scenario if needed.</p>

        <p><strong>Inputs:</strong> Height = 170 cm, Weight = 70 kg, Formula = Mosteller.</p>

        <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold mt-10">Frequently asked questions</h2>
        <section aria-label="FAQ">
          <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold mt-4">What is Body Surface Area (BSA)?</h3>
          <p>BSA is the total external surface area of the human body. Clinical dosing, normalization of clearance, and fluid resuscitation often rely on this metric.</p>
          <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold mt-2">Which formula is best?</h3>
          <p>No single formula fits everyone. Mosteller is easy and precise, but some institutions prefer Du Bois &amp; Du Bois, Haycock, Gehan &amp; George, or Boyd based on protocol.</p>
          <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold mt-2">Do I need to switch to metric?</h3>
          <p>No. Use inches and pounds if that matches your measurement system; the calculator converts to centimeters and kilograms.</p>
          <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold mt-2">Is this tool suitable for pediatrics?</h3>
          <p>Yes. Haycock and Mosteller are widely used for infants and children, but always follow pediatric dosing guidelines.</p>
          <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold mt-2">Can I rely on it for chemotherapy dosing?</h3>
          <p>This calculator is educational. Use it to cross-check dosing but always confirm with official protocols.</p>
          <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold mt-2">How accurate is BSA?</h3>
          <p>Each equation is an estimate from reference populations. Human variation means these are tools, not definitive measurements.</p>
          <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold mt-2">Why do formulas differ?</h3>
          <p>Different datasets and statistical models produce minor variations. Compare values to understand the range.</p>
        </section>

        <details id="formulaDetails">
          <summary class="cursor-pointer font-bold text-gray-800">Formulas</summary>
          <div class="mt-3 p-3 bg-white border border-gray-200 rounded-lg text-sm space-y-2">
            <p>Most formulas operate on Height in centimeters (H_cm) and Weight in kilograms (W_kg). The calculator normalizes imperial units before applying the exponents below.</p>
            <div class="formula-box">
              <strong>Mosteller</strong>
              <p class="latex">$$\mathrm{BSA} = \sqrt{\frac{H_{\mathrm{cm}} \times W_{\mathrm{kg}}}{3600}}$$</p>
              <strong>Du Bois &amp; Du Bois</strong>
