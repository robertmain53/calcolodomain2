        <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold">How to Use This Calculator</h2>
        <p>This professional-grade beer bottling calculator helps homebrewers and small breweries determine the exact priming sugar to add, the number of bottles and caps required, and the per-bottle sugar dose.</p>
        <p>Start by entering the finished batch volume and temperature, choose a carbonation target or preset, and confirm the fermentable and bottle size that match your process.</p>

        <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold mt-6">Methodology</h2>
        <p>The tool estimates residual dissolved CO₂ using a polynomial fit for temperature, subtracts it from your target volumes, and multiplies the delta by batch liters and the sugar yield constant.</p>
        <ul class="list-disc pl-5 space-y-2 text-gray-700">
          <li>Residual CO₂ is evaluated in °F; lower temperatures retain more gas and reduce priming sugar.</li>
          <li>Sugar mass tracks the required grams to reach your target volumes for the whole batch.</li>
          <li>Per-bottle sugar is simply the total mass divided by the rounded-up bottle count after converting the batch into your container size.</li>
        </ul>
        <div class="mt-4 bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200 rounded-lg p-4 text-sm text-gray-700">
          Outputs are estimates. Use a scale for accuracy, add a safety margin when filling, and consult trusted brewing resources before scaling to commercial runs.
        </div>

        <h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold mt-8">Full original guide (expanded)</h2>
        <p>The original beer bottling page included a complete audit, formulas, source list, and verification notes. The following guidance preserves that context while following the canonical CalcDomain layout.</p>
        <ol class="list-decimal pl-5 space-y-2 text-gray-700">
          <li>Set the batch volume (e.g., 19 L) and bottling temperature (21 °C / 70 °F) so the calculator can estimate residual CO₂.</li>
          <li>Pick a style preset or enter your own carbonation target in vols CO₂ (American Ale at 2.3 volumes is the default).</li>
          <li>Choose your fermentable — corn sugar is the baseline, but table sugar, DME, or honey are supported.</li>
          <li>Confirm bottle size through presets (12 oz / 355 mL) or enter a custom milliliter value.</li>
          <li>Click Calculate to see sugar mass, per-bottle dosing, bottle count, and CO₂ delta information.</li>
        </ol>
        <div class="mt-4 space-y-2">
          <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold">FAQ Highlights</h3>
          <ul class="list-disc pl-5 text-gray-700 space-y-2">
            <li><strong>Which priming sugar should I use?</strong> Corn sugar (dextrose) offers a neutral flavor and predictable yield; sucrose needs a slightly lower weight, while DME adds malt character.</li>
            <li><strong>Why does bottling temperature matter?</strong> Cooler beer retains more fermentation CO₂; the calculator subtracts that residual gas to prevent over-carbonation.</li>
            <li><strong>Will honey or DME change flavor?</strong> Yes. Honey and DME can contribute subtle aromatics, so use dextrose or sucrose for the cleanest profile.</li>
            <li><strong>Is volumetric measuring (cups) reliable?</strong> No. Densities shift with sugar type and packing; weighing in grams ensures consistency.</li>
            <li><strong>How many bottles do I need?</strong> Enter the batch and bottle sizes, and the calculator reports the total bottles and caps required along with a packaging loss option.</li>
            <li><strong>Can I prime per bottle instead of batch priming?</strong> Yes. Use the per-bottle sugar output to dose individually, preferably with tiny funnels and a jeweler’s scale for best accuracy.</li>
          </ul>
          <p class="text-sm text-gray-600">Audit status: Complete. Verification performed by Ugo Candido on 2026-01-19.</p>

        <details id="formulaDetails">
          <summary>Formulas</summary>
          <div class="mt-3 p-4 bg-white border border-gray-200 rounded-xl text-sm text-gray-700 space-y-3">
            <div class="formula-box">
              Residual CO₂ (volumes) from temperature (°F): V_res(T_F) = 3.0378 - 0.050062·T_F + 0.00026555·T_F²
              <br>
              Required sugar mass (grams): m_sugar = (V_target - V_res) × V_beer,L × k_sugar
              <br>
              Per-bottle sugar (grams): m_per_bot = m_sugar / N_bottles, where N_bottles = ceil(1000 × V_beer,L / V_bottle,mL)
            </div>
            <p class="text-xs text-gray-500">All sugar calculations assume the chosen fermentable yield constants listed in the inputs.</p>
