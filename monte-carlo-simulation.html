The Formula Explained

Monte Carlo Simulation: Value = InitialInvestment × (1 + ExpectedReturn ± Volatility)

Glossary of Terms

  • Initial Investment: The starting amount of money invested.
  • Expected Return (%): The anticipated percentage increase in investment.
  • Volatility (%): The degree of variation in returns.
  • Time Horizon: The duration over which the investment is expected to grow.
  • Iterations: The number of simulation runs to predict outcomes.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a Monte Carlo Simulation?

A Monte Carlo simulation is a mathematical technique that allows people to account for risk in quantitative analysis and decision making.

How accurate are the results?

The accuracy depends on the number of iterations and the quality of input data.

Can this calculator predict market crashes?

No, it provides probabilistic outcomes based on input scenarios, not predictions of specific events.

Why use a Monte Carlo Simulation?

It provides a range of possible outcomes and the probabilities they will occur, helping to manage uncertainty.

What inputs are critical?

Initial investment, expected return, volatility, and time horizon are crucial for simulation accuracy.
