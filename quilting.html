The Formula Explained

The formula to calculate fabric needed is:

\[ \text{Fabric Needed (yards)} = \frac{\text{Quilt Width} \times \text{Quilt Length}}{\text{Fabric Width} \times 36} \]

Glossary of Variables

  • Quilt Width: The width of the quilt.
  • Quilt Length: The length of the quilt.
  • Fabric Width: The width of the fabric you are using.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Suppose you have a quilt that is 60 inches wide and 80 inches long, and you're using fabric that is 42 inches wide. The fabric needed would be calculated as follows:

\[ \text{Fabric Needed} = \frac{60 \times 80}{42 \times 36} = \text{Approximately 3.17 yards} \]
