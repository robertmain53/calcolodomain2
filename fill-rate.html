The Formula Explained

\[ \text{Fill Rate} = \left( \frac{\text{Fulfilled Orders}}{\text{Total Orders}} \right) \times 100 \]

Glossary of Variables

  • Fulfilled Orders: The number of orders completely fulfilled.
  • Total Orders: The total number of orders placed.
  • Fill Rate: The percentage of orders fulfilled from available stock.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Assume you have 80 fulfilled orders out of 100 total orders. The fill rate would be calculated as follows:

\[ \text{Fill Rate} = \left( \frac{80}{100} \right) \times 100 = 80\% \]

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a fill rate?

Fill rate is a metric used in supply chain management to measure the percentage of customer orders fulfilled from available stock.

How is the fill rate calculated?

The fill rate is calculated by dividing the number of fulfilled orders by the total number of orders.

Why is fill rate important?

Fill rate is crucial for assessing the efficiency of inventory management and customer satisfaction.

What is a good fill rate percentage?

A fill rate of 95% or higher is generally considered good in the industry.

How can I improve my fill rate?

Improving your fill rate can be achieved by optimizing inventory management, improving supply chain processes, and forecasting demand accurately.
