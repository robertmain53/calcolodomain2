Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the Epley formula, a reliable method used by professionals to estimate 1RM:
1RM = Weight × (1 + Reps / 30)

Glossary of Terms

  • Weight Lifted: The amount of weight you successfully lifted.
  • Reps Completed: The number of repetitions you completed at that weight.
  • Estimated 1RM: The maximum weight you can lift for one repetition, calculated using the Epley formula.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is One Rep Max (1RM)?

One Rep Max (1RM) is the maximum weight you can lift for one repetition of a given exercise.

Why calculate 1RM?

Calculating your 1RM helps you tailor your strength training regimen to maximize muscle gains and improve performance.

How accurate is the Epley formula?

The Epley formula provides a good estimate of 1RM, although individual factors like technique and fatigue can influence precision.

Can this calculator be used for all exercises?

Yes, this calculator can be applied to most resistance exercises, including bench press, squat, and deadlift.

How often should I recalculate my 1RM?

It's advisable to recalculate your 1RM every 4-6 weeks to track progress and adjust your training plan accordingly.
