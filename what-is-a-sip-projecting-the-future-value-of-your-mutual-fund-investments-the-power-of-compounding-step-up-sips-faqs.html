Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on industry-standard compound interest formulas. For precise data, consult reliable financial resources.

The Formula Explained

Future Value = P × ( (1 + r/n)^(nt) - 1 ) / (r/n)

Where P is the monthly investment, r is the annual interest rate, n is the number of compounding periods per year, and t is the number of years.

Glossary of Terms

  • Monthly Investment: The amount invested every month.
  • Investment Period: The total duration of investment in years.
  • Expected Return Rate: The anticipated annual growth rate of the investment.
  • Future Value: The estimated value of the investment at the end of the period.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a SIP?

A SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) allows for regular, disciplined investment in mutual funds.

How does the SIP calculator work?

The calculator projects future value based on regular investments and compounding returns over time.
