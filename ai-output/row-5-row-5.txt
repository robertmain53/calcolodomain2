1) Thinking Process (UX & IA)

- UX archetype: Cockpit layout. Unit conversion is simple but used repeatedly; showing inputs and results side-by-side on desktop gives “pro tool” feel and instant feedback. On mobile, inputs stack with a sticky result bar.
- Structure: Single-screen tool (no wizard). Progressive disclosure used only for secondary options (e.g., precision/rounding) under an “Advanced options” toggle.
- Inputs: Two main numeric fields:
  - Pounds (lb) → primary input.
  - Kilograms (kg) → secondary, editable for reverse conversion.
  Both autocalculate on change, with a “Calculate” button for accessibility and explicit control.
- Microinteractions:
  - Result panel highlights briefly when values change.
  - Contextual validation messages instead of generic “Error”.
  - Tooltips for “pounds” and “kilograms” explaining definitions and typical use.
- Mobile:
  - `inputmode="decimal"` on both fields.
  - Sticky bottom bar summarizing the main result when the form is scrollable.
- SEO & IA:
  - URL: `/en/science-and-mathematics/unit-conversion/lbs-to-kg`.
  - Rich content sections: theory with formula in LaTeX, real-world example, and 3 targeted FAQs.
  - JSON-LD: `WebApplication`, `FAQPage`, and `BreadcrumbList`.
  - Application category: `ScienceApplication` (unit conversion in science/math context).
- Reuse:
  - Uses `Layout.astro` and `CalculatorIsland.astro` as the interactive island.
  - Right rail preserved via layout slots/props for ads.
