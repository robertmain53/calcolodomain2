1) Thinking Process

- UX archetype: Cockpit layout. BTU ↔ kWh is a simple, fast unit conversion but often used in technical contexts (HVAC, energy bills), so side‑by‑side inputs and results work best. No multi‑step wizard needed.
- Layout: On desktop, inputs on the left, live results and key summary on the right. On mobile, stacked with a sticky result bar at the bottom once the user starts typing.
- Progressive disclosure: Basic mode shows the core BTU → kWh conversion. An “Advanced options” toggle reveals reverse conversion (kWh → BTU) and a small “energy cost” helper (optional) to keep the main UI clean.
- Interactions:
  - Autocalculate on input change with debouncing; a “Calculate” button is still present for accessibility and explicit control.
  - Input tips/tooltips explaining BTU and kWh, and typical ranges.
  - Numeric keypad on mobile via `inputmode="decimal"` and `inputmode="numeric"` where appropriate.
  - Microinteractions: subtle highlight of result when it changes; focus styles on inputs.
- Trust & SEO:
  - Hero mentions that the conversion is based on the standard definition \(1 \text{ kWh} = 3412.14163 \text{ BTU}\).
  - “Verified by” note referencing standard energy unit definitions.
  - Rich content: explanation of formulas with LaTeX, real‑world example (e.g., converting AC energy), and 3 targeted FAQs.
  - JSON‑LD: `WebApplication`, `FAQPage`, and `BreadcrumbList` embedded in the page.
