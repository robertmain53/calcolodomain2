The Formula Explained

\( \text{grams} = \text{ounces} \times 28.3495 \)

Where ounces is the weight in imperial units and grams is the weight in metric units.

Data Source and Methodology

The conversion is based on the standard formula where 1 ounce (oz) is equivalent to 28.3495 grams (g). This is the internationally recognized avoirdupois ounce used in the United States and other countries. All calculations adhere strictly to this conversion factor.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For example, to convert 5 ounces to grams:

  1. Start with the formula: \( \text{grams} = \text{ounces} \times 28.3495 \)
  2. Substitute 5 for ounces: \( \text{grams} = 5 \times 28.3495 \)
  3. Calculate: \( \text{grams} = 141.7475 \)

Result: 5 oz = 141.75 g

Glossary of Variables

  • Ounces (oz): A unit of weight in the imperial and US customary systems. One ounce equals 28.3495 grams or 1/16 of a pound.
  • Grams (g): A metric unit of mass. One gram is one-thousandth of a kilogram and is commonly used worldwide for measuring small weights.
  • Avoirdupois Ounce: The standard ounce used in the US for everyday measurements, different from the troy ounce used for precious metals.

Is there a difference between ounces and fluid ounces?

Yes! Ounces (oz) measure weight/mass, while fluid ounces (fl oz) measure volume. This converter deals with weight ounces, not fluid ounces.
