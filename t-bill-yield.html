The Formula Explained

Yield Formula: \( \text{Yield} = \left( \frac{\text{Face Value} - \text{Purchase Price}}{\text{Purchase Price}} \times \frac{365}{\text{Days to Maturity}} \right) \times 100 \)

Glossary of Terms

  • Face Value: The amount paid to the holder at maturity.
  • Purchase Price: The price paid to buy the T-Bill.
  • Days to Maturity: The number of days until the T-Bill matures.
  • Annual Yield: The return on investment expressed as a percentage per annum.

Practical Example

Consider a T-Bill with a face value of $1000, purchased for $950, and maturing in 90 days. Using the yield formula, the calculated annual yield would be approximately 20.42%.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a Treasury Bill (T-Bill)?

A Treasury Bill is a short-term government security with maturity ranging from a few days to one year.

How is the yield on a T-Bill calculated?

The yield is calculated based on the difference between the purchase price and the face value, adjusted for the time to maturity.

Why invest in T-Bills?

T-Bills are considered a safe investment due to the backing of the U.S. government and provide a predictable return.

Are T-Bills subject to state taxes?

T-Bills are exempt from state and local taxes, but interest income is taxable at the federal level.

Where can I buy Treasury Bills?

T-Bills can be purchased directly from the U.S. Treasury or through a broker.
