Data Source and Methodology

Authoritative data source: IRS Publication 590‑A (Contributions to Individual Retirement Arrangements), 2024; Publication 590‑B (Distributions from Individual Retirement Arrangements), 2024. Direct links: IRS Pub 590‑A and IRS Pub 590‑B.

Tutti i calcoli si basano rigorosamente sulle formule e sui dati forniti da questa fonte.

This tool applies IRS age-based annual contribution limits and Roth IRA income phase-out thresholds by filing status for the selected tax year. Compounding is annual with end-of-year contributions. Inflation is used only to express purchasing power at retirement.

The Formula Explained

Annual limit with phase-out (simplified proportional method):
$L_{eff} = \begin{cases} L, & \text{if } \text{MAGI} \le \text{Lower} \\ 0, & \text{if } \text{MAGI} \ge \text{Upper} \\ L \cdot \dfrac{\text{Upper} - \text{MAGI}}{\text{Upper} - \text{Lower}}, & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$

Future value of current balance after n years at return r:
$FV_{balance} = P \cdot (1+r)^{n}$

Future value of a growing annual contribution $C_0$ with growth g (end-of-year payments):
$FV_{contrib} = \begin{cases} C_0 \cdot \dfrac{(1+r)^n - (1+g)^n}{r-g}, & r \ne g \\ C_0 \cdot n \cdot (1+r)^{n-1}, & r = g \end{cases}$

Total future value (nominal):
$FV_{nominal} = FV_{balance} + FV_{contrib}$

Inflation adjustment to today’s dollars with inflation i:
$FV_{real} = \dfrac{FV_{nominal}}{(1+i)^{n}}$

Glossary of Variables

  • Age: Your current age in years.
  • Target retirement age: The age at which you plan to retire; determines the number of years to project.
  • Filing status: IRS category used for Roth IRA income phase-out thresholds.
  • MAGI: Modified Adjusted Gross Income used by the IRS for contribution eligibility.
  • Current balance (P): Existing Roth IRA balance today.
  • Planned annual contribution (C0): Amount contributed at the end of year 1. If IRS enforcement is on, it’s capped by $L_{eff}$.
  • Annual contribution growth (g): Year‑over‑year increase in your annual contribution, as a percentage.
  • Expected annual return (r): Yearly portfolio return assumption (decimal form in formulas).
  • Inflation (i): Annual inflation assumption (decimal form in formulas) used to convert nominal future dollars to today’s dollars.
  • Allowed contribution (this year): Age‑based limit reduced by income phase‑out for the selected tax year.

How It Works: A Step‑by‑Step Example

Inputs: Age 30; retire at 65; Single; MAGI $90,000; IRS enforcement ON (2024); Current balance $20,000; Planned annual contribution $7,000; Contribution growth 0%; Expected return 7%; Inflation 2.5%.

  1. Years to retirement: n = 65 − 30 = 35.
  2. IRS limit (2024 Single): $L = $7,000 (age under 50); MAGI is below phase‑out, so $L_{eff} = $7,000.
  3. Future value of balance: $FV_{balance} = 20{,}000 \cdot (1+0.07)^{35}$.
  4. Future value of contributions (constant, g = 0): $FV_{contrib} = 7{,}000 \cdot \dfrac{(1+0.07)^{35} - 1}{0.07}$.
  5. Total nominal: $FV_{nominal} = FV_{balance} + FV_{contrib}$.
  6. Real (today’s dollars): $FV_{real} = \dfrac{FV_{nominal}}{(1+0.025)^{35}}$.

The calculator automates these steps and caps contributions when enforcement is enabled.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What income limits apply to Roth IRA contributions?

For 2024, the MAGI phase‑out ranges are: Single $146,000–$161,000; Married filing jointly $230,000–$240,000; Married filing separately $0–$10,000. Below the range you may contribute the full limit; above, you cannot contribute directly to a Roth IRA.

What are the annual contribution limits?

For 2024, the limit is $7,000 if you’re under age 50, and $8,000 if you’re 50 or older (catch‑up). This calculator uses IRS limits for 2023 and 2024 and will be updated as new limits are released.

Does the calculator handle backdoor Roth strategies?

No. The tool focuses on direct Roth IRA contributions. Backdoor strategies involve separate steps and potential tax considerations.

Are results guaranteed?

No. Market returns and inflation are uncertain. Use this as a planning estimate and consult a licensed professional for personalized advice.

When are contributions assumed to occur?

At the end of each year with annual compounding. You can model rising contributions with the contribution growth field.

Why is my allowed contribution lower than I entered?

With IRS enforcement enabled, the tool caps your entry using age‑based limits and the MAGI phase‑out for your filing status.
