Data Source & Methodology

Authoritative data source: IRS Publication 590‑A/590‑B (2024). Pub 590‑A and Pub 590‑B.

Annual phase‑out (simplified):
\[ L_{eff}= \begin{cases} L & \text{if } MAGI \le Lower \\ 0 & \text{if } MAGI \ge Upper \\ L \cdot \dfrac{Upper - MAGI}{Upper - Lower} & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \]
Future value of balance: \( FV_{balance} = P(1+r)^n \)
Future value of growing contributions: \( FV_{contrib} = C_0 \cdot \frac{(1+r)^n - (1+g)^n}{r-g} \) (if \( r \ne g \))
Total (nominal): \( FV_{nominal} = FV_{balance} + FV_{contrib} \)
Real (today’s $): \( FV_{real} = \dfrac{FV_{nominal}}{(1+i)^n} \)

Compounding annual, contributions end‑of‑year. Inflation only for purchasing‑power adjustment.

FAQ

What income limits apply to Roth IRA contributions?

2024 phase‑out: Single $146k–$161k; MFJ $230k–$240k; MFS $0–$10k.

What are the annual contribution limits?

2024: $7,000 (under 50) and $8,000 (50+ catch‑up).

When do contributions occur?

End of each year with annual compounding.

Are results guaranteed?

No. Markets and inflation vary. Use as planning guidance and consult a professional.
