The Formula Explained

The formula to calculate NPV is:

\[ NPV = \frac{TN}{TN + FN} \]

Glossary of Terms

  • True Negatives (TN): Number of actual negatives correctly identified.
  • False Negatives (FN): Number of actual positives that were incorrectly identified as negative.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Suppose a test identified 90 true negatives and 10 false negatives. The NPV would be calculated as follows:

  • NPV = 90 / (90 + 10) = 0.9 or 90%

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is NPV?

NPV is the proportion of negative test results that are true negatives, indicating the accuracy of a test in identifying non-disease cases.

Why is NPV important?

NPV is crucial for understanding the reliability of a test in ruling out a disease, which is especially important in high-risk health conditions.

How can I improve NPV?

Improving the sensitivity of the test and reducing false negatives can enhance NPV.

What factors affect NPV?

Prevalence of the disease and the test's sensitivity and specificity affect NPV.

Is NPV the same as specificity?

No, specificity measures the proportion of actual negatives correctly identified, while NPV focuses on the probability of a negative test being correct.
