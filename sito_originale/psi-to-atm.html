The Formula Explained

\( \text{atmospheres} = \frac{\text{PSI}}{14.696} \)

Where PSI is the pressure in pounds per square inch and atmospheres is the pressure in atmospheres (atm). One standard atmosphere equals 14.696 PSI.

Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the standard conversion factor: 1 atmosphere (atm) = 14.696 PSI. This is the universally accepted conversion based on standard atmospheric pressure at sea level (101,325 pascals).

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For example, to convert 29.392 PSI to atmospheres:

  1. Start with the formula: \( \text{atmospheres} = \frac{\text{PSI}}{14.696} \)
  2. Substitute 29.392 for PSI: \( \text{atmospheres} = \frac{29.392}{14.696} \)
  3. Calculate: \( \text{atmospheres} = 2 \)

Result: 29.392 PSI = 2 atmospheres

Glossary of Terms

  • PSI (Pounds per Square Inch): A unit of pressure commonly used in the United States for measuring tire pressure, hydraulic systems, and industrial applications.
  • Atmosphere (atm): A unit of pressure based on the average atmospheric pressure at sea level. One standard atmosphere equals 101,325 pascals or 14.696 PSI.
  • Standard Atmosphere: Defined as exactly 101,325 pascals, used as a reference pressure in many scientific and engineering applications.
  • Pressure: The force applied perpendicular to the surface of an object per unit area.

Is this conversion accurate?

What's the difference between PSI and PSIG?

PSI typically refers to absolute pressure, while PSIG (pounds per square inch gauge) is measured relative to atmospheric pressure. PSIG = PSI - 14.7 (approximately).
