Glossary

BTU (British Thermal Unit)
A unit of heat energy commonly used in the United States for heating and cooling systems. One BTU is the energy needed to raise one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit.
Kilowatt-hour (kWh)
A unit of energy equal to one kilowatt of power sustained for one hour. The standard unit used by electric utilities to measure and bill for electricity consumption.
Energy Conversion
The process of converting energy measurements from one unit to another, essential for comparing different energy sources and understanding utility bills.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert BTU to kWh?

To convert BTU to kWh, divide the number of BTU by 3,412.14. Alternatively, multiply by 0.000293071.

How many BTU are in one kWh?

One kilowatt-hour equals 3,412.14 BTU. This is the standard conversion factor used in energy calculations.

Why is this conversion useful?

This conversion helps compare heating/cooling capacity (often measured in BTU) with electrical energy consumption (measured in kWh), making it easier to understand utility bills and energy efficiency.

Can I use this for my HVAC system?

Yes, this conversion is commonly used to compare HVAC system capacity (BTU) with electrical consumption (kWh) to calculate energy efficiency and operating costs.

Is the conversion exact?

The conversion factor 3,412.14 BTU per kWh is based on the precise relationship between joules and BTU. Results displayed to two decimal places are accurate for most applications.
