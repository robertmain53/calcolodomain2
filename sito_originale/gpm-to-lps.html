How the conversion works

GPM is a volumetric flow rate: how many gallons pass in one minute. To express it as L/s we need two things:

  • convert gallons → liters
  • convert minutes → seconds

US gallons:

1 US gal = 3.785411784 L

1 minute = 60 s

So: L/s = gpm × 3.785411784 ÷ 60 = gpm × 0.0630901964

Imperial gallons:

1 Imp gal = 4.54609 L

L/s = gpm × 4.54609 ÷ 60 = gpm × 0.0757681667

FAQ

1. Which “gallon” does my device use?

North America: almost always US gallon. UK / some offshore / some legacy data: Imperial gallon.

2. Can I get L/min too?

Yes — the tool above shows L/min and L/h automatically.

3. Why convert to L/s?

Many SI-based engineering formulas (nozzles, orifices, pump curves) use L/s or m³/h, so converting from GPM makes designs comparable.
