Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the Maryland county income tax?

The Maryland county income tax is a local tax applied to your income, calculated based on your residency within a specific county.

How can I find my county's tax rate?

County tax rates are available on the Maryland Comptroller's website or you can contact your local county office for specific rates.

Why is my county income tax different from state income tax?

County income taxes are additional local taxes that supplement state taxes based on county-specific rates.

When is the county income tax due?

The tax is typically due at the same time as your state income tax return, on April 15th each year.

Can I deduct county income tax on my federal return?

Yes, state and local taxes, including county income tax, can be deducted on your federal tax return, subject to certain limits.
