The Formula Explained

\( \text{cups} = \frac{\text{tablespoons}}{16} \)

Where tablespoons is the volume in tablespoons and cups is the volume in cups. There are 16 tablespoons in 1 cup.

Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the standard US cooking conversion: 1 cup = 16 tablespoons. This is the universally accepted conversion factor in American cooking and baking.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For example, to convert 32 tablespoons to cups:

  1. Start with the formula: \( \text{cups} = \frac{\text{tablespoons}}{16} \)
  2. Substitute 32 for tablespoons: \( \text{cups} = \frac{32}{16} \)
  3. Calculate: \( \text{cups} = 2 \)

Result: 32 tablespoons = 2 cups

Glossary of Terms

  • Tablespoon (tbsp): A unit of volume used in cooking, equal to 3 teaspoons or approximately 15 milliliters. Abbreviated as "tbsp" or "T".
  • Cup: A larger unit of volume commonly used in culinary measurements. One US cup equals 16 tablespoons or approximately 237 milliliters.
  • US vs Metric Cup: The US cup (236.6 ml) is slightly different from the metric cup (250 ml) used in some countries.
