Classification rules for conics

A general second-degree equation in two variables is

\( Ax^2 + Bxy + Cy^2 + Dx + Ey + F = 0 \)

To classify, compute the discriminant

\( \Delta = B^2 - 4AC \)

  • Δ < 0 → ellipse (or circle if additionally \(A = C\) and \(B = 0\))
  • Δ = 0 → parabola
  • Δ > 0 → hyperbola

To detect degenerate cases, we use the determinant of the augmented matrix:

\( \Delta_0 = \begin{vmatrix} A & \frac{B}{2} & \frac{D}{2} \\ \frac{B}{2} & C & \frac{E}{2} \\ \frac{D}{2} & \frac{E}{2} & F \end{vmatrix} \)

If this determinant is 0, the conic is degenerate (it may represent a point, a line, two parallel/orthogonal lines, etc.).

Rotated conics

If B ≠ 0 the axes of the conic are rotated with respect to the x and y axes. Our classification still works because Δ is invariant under rotation.

FAQ

Why is this better than a static PDF table?

Because you can paste any equation, even after algebraic manipulations, and the tool reclassifies it for you. No need to complete the square manually first.

Can I use this in exams?
