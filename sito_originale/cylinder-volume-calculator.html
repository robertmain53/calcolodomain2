The Formula Explained

The volume ($V$) of a cylinder is calculated by multiplying the area of its circular base (Area = $\pi r^2$) by its height ($h$). This formula applies to any right circular cylinder.

$$ V = \pi r^2 h $$

Glossary of Variables

  • $V$ (Volume): The total amount of three-dimensional space enclosed by the cylinder.
  • $r$ (Radius): The distance from the center of the circular base to any point on its edge.
  • $h$ (Height): The perpendicular distance between the two circular bases.
  • $\pi$ (Pi): A mathematical constant, approximately equal to 3.14159...

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Let's calculate the volume of a water tank with a radius of 2 meters and a height of 5 meters.

  1. Identify the formula:
    $V = \pi r^2 h$
  2. Input the values:
    $r = 2 \text{ m}$
    $h = 5 \text{ m}$
  3. Calculate the base area:
    $\text{Area} = \pi \times (2 \text{ m})^2 = \pi \times 4 \text{ m}^2 \approx 12.566 \text{ m}^2$
  4. Calculate the final volume:
    $V \approx 12.566 \text{ m}^2 \times 5 \text{ m} = 62.83 \text{ m}^3$

Result: The volume of the tank is approximately $62.83$ cubic meters.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What if I have the diameter instead of the radius?

The radius is simply half of the diameter. Divide your diameter by 2 and use that value in the 'Radius' field.

How do I calculate the volume of a hollow cylinder (a pipe)?

To find the volume of a hollow cylinder, you must calculate two separate volumes:

  1. Calculate the volume of the outer cylinder using the outer radius ($R$).
  2. Calculate the volume of the inner (hollow) cylinder using the inner radius ($r$).
  3. Subtract the inner volume from the outer volume: $V = \pi R^2 h - \pi r^2 h = \pi h (R^2 - r^2)$.
How many liters are in one cubic meter?

There are exactly 1,000 liters in 1 cubic meter ($1 \text{ m}^3$). This calculator provides this conversion automatically.

What if my cylinder is lying on its side?

The orientation does not change the formula. The 'height' ($h$) is always the perpendicular distance between the two circular bases. If a cylinder is on its side, its 'height' is simply its 'length'.

Can I use different units for radius and height?

To ensure accuracy, this calculator requires you to select a single unit for both input dimensions. If your radius is in inches and height is in feet, you must convert one of them first (e.g., convert 2 feet to 24 inches) before using the tool.

What is the formula for the surface area of a cylinder?

The total surface area ($A$) is the area of the two circular bases plus the area of the curved side. The formula is: $A = 2\pi rh + 2\pi r^2$.
