The Formula Explained

\( \text{meters} = \text{nanometers} \times 10^{-9} \)

Where nanometers is the length in nanometers (nm) and meters is the length in meters (m). One nanometer equals 0.000000001 meters (10-9 m).

Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the SI (International System of Units) definition: 1 nanometer = 10-9 meters. The nanometer is a standard metric unit prefix meaning one billionth.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

For example, to convert 1,000,000 nanometers to meters:

  1. Start with the formula: \( \text{meters} = \text{nanometers} \times 10^{-9} \)
  2. Substitute 1,000,000 for nanometers: \( \text{meters} = 1{,}000{,}000 \times 10^{-9} \)
  3. Calculate: \( \text{meters} = 0.001 \)

Result: 1,000,000 nanometers = 0.001 meters (1 millimeter)

Glossary of Terms

  • Nanometer (nm): A unit of length equal to 10-9 meters. One billionth of a meter. Widely used in nanotechnology, physics, and chemistry.
  • Meter (m): The base unit of length in the SI system, defined as the distance light travels in vacuum in 1/299,792,458 of a second.
  • SI Prefix: Standard prefixes like nano- (10-9), micro- (10-6), and milli- (10-3) used to express multiples or fractions of base units.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How many nanometers are in a meter?

There are 1 billion (1,000,000,000 or 109) nanometers in one meter.

How do you convert nanometers to meters?

To convert nanometers to meters, divide the number of nanometers by 1,000,000,000 (or multiply by 10-9). For example: 500 nm = 0.0000005 m.

What is a nanometer used for?

Nanometers are used to measure very small distances such as wavelengths of light, atomic dimensions, semiconductor features, and molecular structures.

Is this conversion accurate?

Yes, this calculator uses the exact SI conversion factor (10-9) and displays results in scientific notation or decimal format for precision.

Can I convert meters back to nanometers?

Yes, to convert meters to nanometers, multiply the number of meters by 1,000,000,000 (or by 109). For example: 0.001 m = 1,000,000 nm.
