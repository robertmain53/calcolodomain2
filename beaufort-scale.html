Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the Beaufort scale?

The Beaufort scale is an empirical measure that relates wind speed to observed conditions at sea or on land.

How is wind speed converted to the Beaufort scale?

Wind speed is converted to the Beaufort scale using a specific range of speeds that correspond to each Beaufort number.

Why is the Beaufort scale important?

It helps in understanding and predicting the impact of wind conditions, especially in maritime and meteorological contexts.

Can the Beaufort scale be used on land?

Yes, while originally developed for maritime use, the Beaufort scale is applicable on land to describe wind effects.

What is the highest Beaufort number?

The highest Beaufort number is 12, which represents hurricane-force winds.
