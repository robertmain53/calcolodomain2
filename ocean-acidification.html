The Formula Explained

The calculator uses the following formula to estimate the pH level of ocean water:

\[ pH = \frac{CO_2}{Alkalinity} \times Constant \]

Glossary of Terms

  • CO2 Concentration (µatm): The amount of carbon dioxide in the water measured in microatmospheres.
  • Alkalinity (µmol/kg): A measure of the water's capacity to neutralize acid.
  • pH Level: The acidity or basicity of the water, which affects marine life.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is ocean acidification?

Ocean acidification refers to the process by which the ocean becomes more acidic due to increased CO2 levels in the atmosphere.

Why is pH important for marine life?

The pH of ocean water affects the health and survival of marine organisms, particularly those with calcium carbonate shells or skeletons.

How accurate is this calculator?
