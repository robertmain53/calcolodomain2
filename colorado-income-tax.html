The Formula Explained

Glossary of Variables

  • Annual gross income: Total income before pre-tax deductions.
  • Pre-tax deductions: Amounts excluded before federal tax (e.g., traditional 401(k), HSA).
  • Standard deduction: Federal standard deduction based on filing status and year.
  • Itemized deductions: Alternative to standard; enter your total if itemizing.
  • Colorado taxable income (TI): AGI minus deductions (not below zero).
  • Colorado flat tax rate (r): 4.40% for TY 2024‚Äì2025.
  • Credits: Colorado credits that reduce tax due (not refundable in this simplified model).
  • Effective state tax rate: Final tax owed divided by gross income.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is the Colorado state income tax flat?

Yes. Colorado levies a flat tax rate (4.40% in 2024‚Äì2025) on Colorado taxable income.

How does the calculator treat the standard deduction?

It mirrors the federal approach: you select Standard or Itemized. The standard deduction is based on your year and filing status.

Can I model itemizing vs. the standard deduction?

Yes. Toggle the deduction method and enter your itemized total to compare outcomes.

Do TABOR sales tax refunds affect this estimate?

Potential TABOR refunds are not part of the state income tax computation and are not included here.

Are payroll taxes or federal taxes included?

No. The calculator focuses on Colorado state income tax only.

What if my credits exceed the pre-credit tax?

The tool caps credits so tax owed doesn‚Äôt go below zero. Some credits may be refundable in reality; consult official guidance.
