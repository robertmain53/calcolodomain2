Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on standard mathematical formulas for determining fuel efficiency. Ensure accuracy by using precise measurements for miles traveled and fuel consumed.

The Formula Explained

The formula used to calculate MPG is:

MPG = \frac{\text{Miles Traveled}}{\text{Gallons Used}}

Glossary of Terms

  • Miles Traveled: The total distance covered by the vehicle.
  • Gallons Used: The total amount of fuel consumed.
  • MPG: Miles per gallon, a measure of fuel efficiency.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Suppose your car traveled 300 miles and used 10 gallons of fuel. The MPG would be calculated as:

MPG = \frac{300}{10} = 30

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is gas mileage?

Gas mileage is the distance a vehicle can travel per unit of fuel, usually expressed in miles per gallon (MPG).

How do I improve gas mileage?

Improving gas mileage can be achieved by maintaining your vehicle properly, driving efficiently, and reducing weight and drag.
