The Formula Explained

The churn rate formula is given by:

Churn Rate = (Initial Customers - Remaining Customers) / Initial Customers × 100%

Glossary of Variables

  • Initial Customers: The number of customers at the start of the period.
  • Remaining Customers: The number of customers at the end of the period.
  • Churn Rate: The percentage of customers who have left the service over the period.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Suppose you start with 200 customers and end with 180 after a month. The churn rate is calculated as:

Churn Rate = (200 - 180) / 200 × 100% = 10%

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is churn rate?

Churn rate is the percentage of subscribers to a service who discontinue their subscriptions within a given time period.

Why is churn rate important?

Churn rate is critical for businesses to understand customer retention and to improve service and marketing strategies.

How can I reduce churn rate?

Improving product features, enhancing customer support, and tailoring marketing efforts can help reduce churn rate.

What is a good churn rate?

A good churn rate varies by industry, but generally, lower rates indicate better customer retention.
