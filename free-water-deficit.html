FAQ

What is hypernatremia?

Too much sodium in the blood, usually due to water loss/dehydration.

Why is water deficit correction important?

It prevents the risks of rapid shifts (brain injury, edema, seizures). Careful staged correction is lifesaving.

Does this apply to children?

Yes—use actual body weight and consult a pediatrician for the rate of sodium change. Pediatric guidelines may vary.

Can I use this for hyponatremia?

No, the formula and risks are different for sodium deficiency.
