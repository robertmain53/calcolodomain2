Data Source and Methodology

Authoritative Data Source: OpenStax (2016), Algebra and Trigonometry — Section 6.8 “The Mathematics of Finance” (Amortization formula). Available at: openstax.org. For regulatory context on HELOCs, see CFPB: What is a HELOC and how does it work?.

The Formula Explained

Maximum line: \( L_{\max} = \max(0,\ \text{CLTV}_{\max}\cdot V - M) \)
CLTV after draw: \( \text{CLTV}_{\text{after}} = \dfrac{M + D}{V} \)
Interest-only during draw: \( I_{\text{draw,m}} = D \cdot \dfrac{r_d}{12} \)
Repayment payment: \( P = \dfrac{(r_r/12)\,B}{1 - (1 + r_r/12)^{-n}} \); with \(B=D\) at repayment start
Total interest: \( I_{\text{total}} = I_{\text{draw,m}}\cdot n_d + (P\cdot n_r - B) \)

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

  1. Maximum line: \( L_{\max} = 0.85\cdot 500{,}000 - 300{,}000 = 125{,}000 \)
  2. CLTV after draw: \( (300{,}000 + 50{,}000)/500{,}000 = 70\% \)
  3. Draw payment: \( 50{,}000 \times 0.0825/12 \approx \$343.75 \)
  4. Repayment payment: \( \approx \$427.39 \) for \(r=0.0825, n=240\)
  5. Total interest: \( \approx \$93{,}823.60 \)

FAQs

Does this include changing rates or caps? No—use different draw/repay APRs to stress test.

Is the draw payment always interest-only? Often yes; check your agreement.

Are fees or taxes included? Not in this model.
