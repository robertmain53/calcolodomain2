The Formula Explained

The law of sines formula is \( \frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C} \).

Glossary of Terms

  • Angle A: The angle opposite side a.
  • Side a: The side of the triangle opposite angle A.
  • Side b: The side of the triangle opposite angle B.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the law of sines?

The law of sines is a formula that relates the lengths of the sides of a triangle to the sines of its angles.

How do I use the law of sines?

To use the law of sines, you need at least one side length and its opposite angle.

Can the law of sines be used for any triangle?

Yes, the law of sines can be applied to any triangle, but it is most useful for non-right triangles.
