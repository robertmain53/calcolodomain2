The Formula Explained

Weight = Specific Gravity × Volume × Density of Water (1000 g/L)

Glossary of Variables

  • Specific Gravity: The ratio of the density of the food to the density of water.
  • Volume: The volume of the food item in milliliters.
  • Weight: The calculated weight of the food item in grams.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Imagine you have a food item with a specific gravity of 0.92 and a volume of 250 ml. The weight is calculated as follows: Weight = 0.92 × 250 × 1,000 = 230 grams.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is food-specific gravity?

Food-specific gravity is a measure of the density of a food item compared to water.

Why is understanding food density important?

Understanding food density helps in analyzing nutritional content and managing portion sizes.

How accurate are the calculations?

Calculations are based on data from the USDA FoodData Central and should be accurate for standard conditions.

Can I use this for all types of food?

Yes, as long as you have the specific gravity and volume of the food item.

Do I need special equipment to measure specific gravity?

Specific gravity can be measured using a hydrometer or obtained from reliable food databases.
