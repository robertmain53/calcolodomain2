Data Source and Methodology

All calculations are based on the guidelines provided by the MDCalc Pediatric Maintenance Fluids Calculations.

The Formula Explained

The daily maintenance fluid requirement is calculated using the following formula:

Fluid Requirement (mL) = 100 mL/kg/day for first 10 kg + 50 mL/kg/day for next 10 kg + 20 mL/kg/day for each kg over 20 kg

Glossary of Terms

  • Weight (kg): The weight of the child in kilograms.
  • Fluid Requirement: The total volume of fluid needed per day, calculated in milliliters.

Example Calculation

For a child weighing 15 kg, the daily fluid requirement is calculated as follows:

  • First 10 kg: 100 mL/kg/day = 1000 mL
  • Next 5 kg: 50 mL/kg/day = 250 mL
  • Total Fluid Requirement = 1250 mL/day

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the purpose of this calculator?

This calculator helps determine the fluid needs for pediatric patients based on their weight.

Who should use this calculator?

Healthcare professionals such as pediatricians and nurses.

How often should I recalculate the fluid requirements?

Fluid requirements should be recalculated with any significant change in the child's weight.

What if the child is overweight?

Consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice.

Can this calculator replace medical advice?

No, it is a tool to assist professionals and should not replace professional medical judgement.
