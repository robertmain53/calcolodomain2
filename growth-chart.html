How this growth calculator works

  • Percentiles and Z-scores use CDC weight-for-height tables (CDC official data).
  • Values are precise for children 2–20 years (24–240 months).
  • Interpretations are in parent-friendly language.

Frequently asked questions

What is a percentile?

A percentile tells you how your child ranks compared to children of the same sex and height. E.g., 60th percentile means heavier than 60 of 100 peers.

What is a Z-score?

A z-score expresses how far a measurement is above or below average in standard deviations (0 is average).

Are the results medical advice?

No. Always discuss growth concerns with your pediatrician, especially for results below the 3rd or above the 97th percentile.
