The Formula Explained

The conversion from cylindrical to Cartesian coordinates relies on the following formulas:

\( x = r \cdot \cos(\theta) \)

\( y = r \cdot \sin(\theta) \)

\( z = z \)

Glossary of Variables

  • Radius (r): The distance from the z-axis to the point.
  • Angle (θ): The angle in the xy-plane from the positive x-axis.
  • Height (z): The height above the xy-plane.
  • X, Y, Z: Cartesian coordinates.

How It Works: A Step-by-Step Example

Suppose you have a cylindrical coordinate (r = 5, θ = 30°, z = 10). Converting to Cartesian coordinates involves:

  1. Calculate \( x = 5 \cdot \cos(30°) = 4.33 \)
  2. Calculate \( y = 5 \cdot \sin(30°) = 2.5 \)
  3. The z-coordinate remains the same: z = 10.

Thus, the Cartesian coordinates are (4.33, 2.5, 10).

FAQ

What is a cylindrical coordinate system?

A cylindrical coordinate system is a three-dimensional coordinate system that specifies point positions by the radial distance, the azimuthal angle, and the height from a chosen reference plane.

How do I convert degrees to radians?

To convert degrees to radians, multiply the number of degrees by π/180.
