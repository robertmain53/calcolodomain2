Formula

mph = Mach × 761.207

1 Mach at sea level (standard conditions) = 761.207 mph.
Speeds will vary with altitude, temperature, and humidity, but this is the US/ICAO reference value.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Mach?

Mach is a dimensionless number representing the ratio of speed to the speed of sound in air. Mach 1 = speed of sound.

Does Mach 1 always equal 761.207 mph?

No, that’s the value at sea level, standard pressure, and 15°C. Actual speed of sound changes with conditions (e.g., altitude, temp).

Can I use this for high-altitude/spaceflight?

For ballpark estimates, yes. For precise calculations in rarefied/ionized air or space (where “sound” is undefined), please use environment-specific values.
